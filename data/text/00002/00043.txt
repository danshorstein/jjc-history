HENRY HERZENBERG
Executive Secretary Y. M. H. A.
Active Community Worker
About twenty-five years ago a
group of Jewish young men met to¬
gether in Jacksonville for the pur¬
pose of organizing a Young Mens
Hebrew Association. At this meet¬
ing a constitution and set of by-laws
were proposed and adopted and
plans formulated for the raising of
funds sufficient to erect a building
and home so that the principles and
purposes of the organization might be
realized. In a comparatively short
time sufficient funds were raised and
soon the building was completed. It
stands to-day at 71.2 West Duval
Street in the same condition as when
first constructed, with no alterations
and no additional improvements.
The building occupies a plot of
ground approximating 70x115 feet
and consists of an office, a small
meeting room, an auditorium, a bal¬
cony and showers and lockers in the
basement. The auditorium is used
for the presentation of plays, gym¬
nasium work, readings, musical con¬
certs, dances, basket ball and other
miscellaneous purposes. At the time
of the erection of the building the
purposes of the organization as set
forth in the Preamble were as fol¬
lows:
“We, the Jewish people of this
community, in order to promote the

President T. M. H. A.
general welfare, to promulgate moral
and educational principles, for a
higher and systematic physical de¬
velopment, ana to establish a meet¬
ing place for the free discussion of
the fundamental principles of man¬
hood, do ordain and provide this
Constitution for the Young Mens
Hebrew Association of Jacksonville,
Florida.”
There were then only a compar¬
atively few Jewish young men in the
City and the Y. M. H. A. building
and its facilities were adequate for
its purposes.
To-day the Preamble to the Con¬
stitution of the Y. M. H. A. and
its principles, aims and purposes have
not changed,—nor has the building.
The population of Jewish young men
in the City has doubled itself many
times over since the organization of
the Y. M. H. A., the present build¬
ing and its facilities are now far
too inadequate. The Y. M. H. A.
has grown. Its members totaling
approximately three hundred are in
urgent need of a new and bigger and
better building and home. An audi¬
torium for plays, meetings, concerts,
dancing, etc., a gymnasium, swim¬
ming pool, reading room, meeting
rooms and office, showers and lock¬
ers are needed.
