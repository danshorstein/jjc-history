THE JEWISH CENTER FROM THE
Y. M. H. A. POINT OF VIEW.
by Henry Herzenberg
Most cities in the Unted States
with a Jewish population of 500
families or more are the proud pos-
sessors of buildings known as “Jew-
ish Community Centers” in which
young and old find the facilities
to satisfy ther religious, social, cul¬
tural, and physical needs.
From the educational standpoint
too such buildings fill a vital need
in the community. The child, af¬
ter completing his Hebrew or Sun¬
day School training, and in the ado¬
lescent stage which is the most po¬
tent in moulding the man and
women of to-morrow, is Jewishly
permitted to drift for himself and
finds no institution to interest itself
in his needs. The Jewish Center
obviates this difficulty and solves
this important problem in a most
satisfactory manner.
The classrooms and clubrooms of
the Jewish Center afford the oppor¬
tunity to conduct study circles in
a pleasant environment at hours
suitable even for the high school
boy and girl. The auditorium be¬
comes a center of attraction and
influence through dramatic presen¬
tations, oratorical contests, debates,
mock trials and at times even
through its use as a dance hall.
The present Y. M. H. A. structure
does not permit the conducting of
any of these activities in an orderly
and well regulated manner. For
the whole building consists practic¬
ally of one large hall which, when
used for one purpose, most often
for athletics, becomes entirely im¬
possible, for the time being, for
any other activity.
To my regret I must confess
that Jacksonville Jewry has shown
an utter indifference to the needs
of our youth, and despite my fre¬
quent appeals in the Y. M. H. A.
News and through the spoken word
that we should erect a community
center nothing was done. Now,
thanks to the Almighty, a leader
has come into our city who has
brought the necessary inspiration to
invigorate our apathetic community.
Through the fervent appeal of Dr.
Samuel Benjamin, esteemed rabbi of
the B’nai Israel Congregation, the
necessary funds have already been
raised for the purchase of a suitable
lot for the Jacksonville Jewish Com¬
munity Center.
It is my sincerest hope that every
Jew both orthodox and reform will
unite in this grand effort to save
our youth for Judaism. Let us drop
the “shibolet” that divides us,
meaninglessly into opposing groups
and factions and let us all join in
the effort to erect a Jewish Com¬
munity Center which will weld us
together into a harmonious homo¬
geneous body proud of our past
and eager to work out our future
as beseems Americans and Jews.
