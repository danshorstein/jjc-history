Daily Minyan Captains
Traditionally, public worship requires a minyan, a
quorum of ten. Only in the presence of a minyan may the
Torah be read or the Kaddish prayer be recited.
Individual prayer is of course commanded in Judaism,
but communal prayer reflects the covenant of peoplehood.
God is fully hallowed only in the assembly of the people.
Our dedicated minyan captains insure that there is a
minyan twice every single day of the year. It is often a hard
and thankless job. It requires hours of phoning to remind
regular minyan-goers, to find additional men to make up the
quorum and often to arrange transportation.
It is because of them that you can recite kaddish when
you mourn or observe yarhzeit. We owe them not only our
thanks, but also our help, to continue providing a minyan.
Please call any minyan captain and become a “regular” for at
least one minyan a week.

Valued Staff & Volunteers
At the Center’s Annual Meeting which was held on June 20, 1996, the following
special honors were bestowed on three very deserving people:
Inez Achord
Special Staff Recognition
The strong and capable hand
of Inez Achord has been maintaining
order in the Center bookkeeping
office for more than two decades.
Accurate record keeping, timely
follow-up and Inez’s penchant for
numbers have added up to 23 years
as an invaluable staff member. She
is always available for conversation
and telling a joke or two while
simultaneously completing the most
complicated tasks with unparalleled
dedication. Asking Inez for anything
extra is never a problem for she gives
a 110% on an average day. Over the
years Inez has operated by a standard
that puts the interests of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center and its
congregants above everything else.
She is an office dynamo, a true
“mensch” and an invaluable staff
member. Inez - We appreciate you!
Renee Gordon
Valued Volunteer
Through the years Renee has
contributed much of her time to the
Center’s Annual Raffle, Yearbook,
and other projects. Her dedication
and efforts distinguish her as a
volunteer who has given her heart
and soul to the congregation. Renee
operates on positive energy and
always strives to raise more money
and more awareness of the Center
programs “for the children”. Her
efforts are professional and delivered
with style and distinction. We wish
to honor her specifically for her
continued dedication to Education
and the grace with which she
performs this undertaking year after
year.
William Rose
Valued Volunteer
William is a long standing
member of the Center congregation
and an avid volunteer who gives of
his time and devotion. Over the years
he has taken the chapel and sanctuary
as his personal responsibility to ensure
that our places of worship are in top
condition. He is the person who
stocks the synagogue with prayer
books, pamphlets and other
appropriate items that are necessary
for observance. His efforts, week
after week and especially during the
High Holidays, are greatly appreciated
by our entire congregation. The
Center congratulates William on this
honor and thanks him for his
continued service.
