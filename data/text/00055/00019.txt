Simhat Torah Honorees
Norman Moss, Z”L
Hatan Torah
Norman Moss
was a model
Jew, volunteer,
and leader in the
Jacksonville
community. He
and his loving
wife Raela were
both killed in an
automobile accident this spring. The
congregation mourns the loss of these
dear friends and dedicated leaders. We
are grateful to have had the opportunity
to honor him as Hatan Torah, a
distinction he richly deserved.
For over 50 years, Norman Moss
devotedly served the Jacksonville
Jewish Center by initiating programs
and bringing high standards to existing
procedures. One of his earliest efforts
was co-chairing the first Center family
picnic, over thirty years ago. This
wonderful family-oriented tradition
still occurs annually at Lag B’omer
A gentleman of many interests
and talents, Norman had also served
as chairman of the Cub Pack and Boy
Scout Troop 14 committees. He had
been an active member of the Center
Board, Men’s Club, Finance and
Religious Committees. For ten years,
he provided a much needed mitzvah
to the Center, as the Thursday Minyan
captain.
For thirty-one years, Norman
brought high standards to the solemnity
of the synagogue by volunteering as
Head Usher for the High Holidays at
Third and Silver Street and the Center’s
present location.
Norman served on the Board
and was treasurer of Jewish Family
& Community Services, for over
thirty years. He also lent a helping
hand to transients by making sure they
had a place to stay and money for
groceries until they were settled.
Throughout his many years of
service, Norman Moss always strove
for excellence and worked toward the
goals of the Center, often initiating
new programs , while upholding the
sanctity of traditional values. We
honor the memory of Norman Moss,
and his consistent devotion to Judaism.
Ellen Freedman
Woman of Valor
Ellen Freedman
was honored as
a Woman of
Valor for her
many years of
volunteer service
to the Jackson¬
ville Jewish Cen¬
ter. Ellen grew up
at the Center and has always appreci¬
ated the role that the synagogue played
and continues to play in her life. Her
parents, devoted Center members Bea
and Bud Grossman, are strong role
models. For more than 25 years Ellen
has worked for the Center and its
many programs. In addition to being
a former Board member, she has
served on and co-chaired the Educa¬
tion Committee, taught in the Supple¬
mental School, and moved through
the ranks of Sisterhood from Donor
co-chair to Ways and Means VP and
then President from 1976-78.
Ellen’s background as an
educator with the Duval County
Public Schools, and her commitment
to high standards in education
motivated her to seek full
accreditation for the Center’s
Solomon Schechter Day School, as
well as the pre-school. In the process
top professional educators were
hired. They implemented a well-
rounded curriculum in Judaica and
general studies, enriching and
strengthening the existing program.
Ellen’s foresight and knowledge were
instrumental in the early development
of the Solomon Schechter Day School
helping to make it the excellent
educational facility that it is today.
Ellen believes it is important to
have experienced advisors who will
guide new synagogue leaders to take
their places. “That assures continuity
and future leadership.” Ellen fondly
recalls that her father often expressed
the same sentiment.
Ellen’s son, Michael, a former
Solomon Schechter Day School stu¬
dent and Wharton graduate, joined his
mother to celebrate her honor, last Fall.
Ellen truly exemplifies a Woman of
Valor. The Center congratulates her and
expresses its gratitude with this honor.

Hatan Bereshit

wife Jane feels that her husband
“taught his family well the impor¬
tance of being involved.” Their
daughters, Beth and Renee, are presi¬
dents of their respective youth
groups, and share in their dad’s dedi¬
cation to their Jewish roots. Ira’s
parents, May and David Grann, are
also visibly active Center members.
For the past ten years, Ira has
“religiously” attended Minyan, but
his most important personal
achievement is being a regular Torah
reader at services. Ira is inspired “to
read God’s words” and that is why
you will see him on the pulpit many
Saturdays a month.
An active member, Ira serves on
the Religious Committee, House Com¬
mittee, and as Secretary for the Center
Executive Board. He was a member of
the Middle School Parent Advisory
Board of the Solomon Schechter Day
School. Currently, Ira is Vice President
of the Chevra Kaddisha, which is
committed to Jewish burial tradition
in Jacksonville. A valued volunteer in
the community, Ira’s many accom¬
plishments include co-chairing the
Jacksonville Jewish Federation’s Su¬
per Sunday Event for the past three
years.
The Center Men’s Club owes its
current success in part to the efforts of
Ira Grann. He was instrumental in
reactivating the Men’s Club and served
as tits first President. Ira is a member
of the National Board of Men’s Clubs
and Vice President of the Regional
Men’s Club. He is also an official
trainer for the Federation of Men’s
Clubs. The highly successful Yom
HaShoah Yellow Candle program was
initiated under Ira Grann’s
chairmanship. For providing strength
in character and devoted leadership, we
honor Hatan Bereshit, Ira Grann.
