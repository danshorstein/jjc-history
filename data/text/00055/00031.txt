Men’s Club New American B’nai Mitzvah
June 21 and 22, 1996/5 Tammuz 5756
Once in a while, a congregation experiences a
moment that touches each individual in a very personal
way, and at the same time stirs a community pride that
runs deeper than anyone had
imagined.
Our congregation had
many such moments during
services June 21 and 22, as
twelve inspired adults from
the former Soviet Union cel¬
ebrated their discovery of our
Jewish heritage with a B’nai
Mitzvah ceremony of incom¬
parable significance. Three
men read Torah; two women
chanted Haftorah; they had
aliyot, recited prayers and led
singing - expressions of a faith
they had been denied in their
homelands. They spoke
poignantly of their lives and
their gratitude to those who
helped them reach this occa¬
sion. They spoke of their
awakened Jewish souls. Even
the most jaded of us was over¬
whelmed with emotion. Our
Jewish souls were reawakened.
The weekend was en¬
hanced by lovely receptions following services and a mag¬
nificent Saturday evening gala which featured authentic
Russian delicacies and a Russian band.
The Center Men’s Club pioneered this program of
intensive Jewish education with a curriculum outlined
by Rabbi Gaffney and taught by lay leaders of our
congregation. The ten month
course included Hebrew
literacy, as well as the study
of festivals and holidays,
rituals and customs of life
cycle events, laws and
mitzvot, synagogue skills and
Jewish history. It was a
rewarding experience for
students and teachers.
This was one of the
most positive and far-
reaching experiences to occur
in the Jewish community in
many years. The program
received the praise and
support of the Jewish
Federation and the Simon
Selber Fund of Jewish Family
and Community Services,
which presented the B’nai
Mitzvah with gifts to enrich
their Jewish observances.
The success of this new
program is encouraging. With
the full involvement of the
entire Jewish community, the New American B’nai
Mitzvah Program will become an ongoing educational
resource.
