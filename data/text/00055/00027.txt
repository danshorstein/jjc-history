Performing Artist Series
The Performing Artists Series is devoted to promoting and enhancing Jewish life and culture in
Jacksonville. The 1995-96 Series featured three different but equally exciting performances by exceptionally
talented artists. The intimate setting of each presentation allowed the audience to establish a rapport with the
entertainers. The series was one of the most successful ever, thanks to
co-chairs Joan Levin and Merle Selevan and their hardworking
committees.

Safam
Monday, February 12,1996
The new wave in Jewish music
came from the Boston based six
man band - SAFAM. SAFAM
reinvented the world of Jewish
music with its creative blend of
contemporary and traditional
melodies and themes. Their
unique approach to Jewish music
was lively and fun to see and hear.
Whether it was rock & roll, pop,
folk, Latin, Chassidic, cantorial, or other musical styles, SAFAM’s
eclectic approach to its music had everyone tapping their toes and
singing. To the audiences delight, the group members signed CD’s and
autographs during intermission, and were very sociable as well as
entertaining.
Gitit Shoval
Sunday, April 28,1996
To coincide with the celebration
ofYom Ha’Atzmaut, Gitit and her
husband, composer Ron Druyan
accompanied each other on guitar,
keyboard and percussion, creating
a very special atmosphere of
closeness with their audience
through songs and stories which
captured the spirit of modern Israel. In a festive Kumzitz-Israeli style,
the audience enjoyed the intimacy and camaraderie of seating on pillows
or at tables and enjoying delicious Israeli noshes during Gitit’s
performance. The evening was a wonderful finale to a highly successful
Performing Artist Series.
