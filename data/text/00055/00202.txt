well as performing the usual tasks
carried out by Red Cross Societies
throughout the world.

BBYO - B ’nai B’rith Youth Organization
Israel no longer needs to rely
on blood products processed or
imported from abroad with the
opening of a three story Blood
Fractionation complex located within
the MDA National Blood Center on
the ground of Tel Hashomer Hospital
in Ramat Gan. This breakthrough in
medical services is the culmination
of an 8-year campaign that raised
$14.5 million by ARMDI.
Within minutes, MDA’s rescue
units arrive on the scene: for medical
emergencies, accidents, and
unfortunately, for terrorist bombings.
Trained paramedics administer
immediate help and then transport the
patient to the hospital.
While the symbol of Magen
David Adorn, a red star of David, is
recognized by the American Red
Cross, efforts are still going on to
achieve international recognition.
For only $18 (Chai) you can
show your support for Israel and do
the mitzvah of pikkuach nefesh.
Th e B’nai B’rith Youth Organ¬
ization (BBYO) is the largest youth
organization in the world. It consists
of two unique chapters, Aleph Zadik
Aleph (AZA ) for young men and
B’nai B’rith Girls (BBG) for young
women.
AZA, which stands for
fraternity and harmony, focuses on
building Jewish fraternal bonds while
taking part in projects that benefit the
community.
BBG focuses on sisterhood and
social action and encourages
members to take active roles in the
community, as Jews and as concerned
citizens.
Each group is actively involved
in the Jewish community,
volunteering at local synagogues and
attending regularly scheduled
programs on Jewish ethics and
modern values. BBG and AZA also
work together to make positive
changes in their communities.
BBYO also promotes a feeling
of unity , togetherness and belonging
through chapter programs,
conventions, retreats and summer
programs.
