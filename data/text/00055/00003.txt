To Our Advertisers: Our constant gratitude, not
only for making this publication possible, but also
for the support you lend to our many activities. We
urge our members to reciprocate with their patronage.
About THE Cover: In Afghanistan, the Torah is
decorated with an unusual combination of three pairs
of finials, consisting of a pair of flat ketarim (crowns)
and two pairs of globular rimonim (pomegranates).
Between the finials is a fine silk malbush (garment).
Colorful scraves are tied to the finials and draped
around the case.
This set of Torah ornaments, which was brought
to Israel by Afghan immigrants, was traced in the
course of a documentation project by the Center for
Jewish Art of the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.
