GIRL SCOUTS

. . . for the greatest program available to girls between
the grades of 4-12. We thank the Center Sisterhood for
continuing to supply excellent leadership and offering
Scouting to so many girls for a period of almost 50 years. The
Center troop was the second troop started in Jacksonville
and originally carried the number ”2”.

Troop 162, the present number, participated in many
neighborhood Scout activities, field trips, requirements for
proficiency badges, arts and crafts, and just the good times of
getting together with friends.

Leaders for 1976-77 will be Mrs. Milton Mann and Mrs.
Allen Gray. Meetings will be held each Tuesday at 3:30 p.m.
at the white house on the Campsite.

BROWNIES

The Brownies had a most successful year. Our Brownie
Troop was increased in number to twenty.

We had the Brownie Investiture in October and the
Brownies participated in many functions such as the
Brownie Games Day, Outing at Girl Scout Camp, picking
oranges at the Schnippers, nature walks, many arts and
crafts projects, Scout Sabbath and a Brownie cook-out to
culminate a most enjoyable year for all.

Our mothers were a wonderfully supportive group,
especially Mrs. Allen Balotin who assisted with Arts and
Crafts.
