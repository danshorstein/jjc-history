PESAH (Passover), the Feast of Unleavened Bread — an eight-day celebration of the deliverance of the Jews from
Egyptian slavery. During the traditional Seder meal, on the first two nights of Passover, Jewish families read the Hag-
gadah (a ritual recitation of the story of the Exodus). Throughout the Passover periods, foods are selected according
to special dietary laws. Instead of bread, Jews eat matzos (unleavened bread) to recall the hastv flight from Egypt-

when there was not time to allow the bread to rise.
