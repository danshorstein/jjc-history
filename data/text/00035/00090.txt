JEWISH CENTER PLANS INNOVATIVE EDUCATIONAL PROGRAM

The Educational Program of the Jacksonville Jewish
Center is in the process of undergoing a major
transformation under the dynamic leadership of Rabbi Efraim
Warshaw, newly appointed Educational Director. In an
attempt to create a school which is uniquely joyful,
contemporarily meaningful, and educationally significant to
children, the curriculum of studies and activities has
undergone major revision during the summer months.

At the core of the educational philosophy which informs
the school’s program is the conviction that Judaism is the
evolving religious civilization of the Jewish people, whose
great ethical teachings, spiritual values, and social concerns
have demonstrated that they possess the ability to enhance
the quality of life for the individual, the Jewish community and
general society.

Rabbi Warshaw noted that the curriculum at the
Jacksonville Jewish Center is based on the view that the
classical texts of the Judaic-Hebraic genius, including the
Bible, the Talmud, the Midrash, the Siddur, and Chassidic
lore, have the power to’transform lives, clarify moral values,
and elicit ethical commitment of the highest order.

The rabbi further elaborated on this philosophy of
education. “] know that Judaism is best taught children by
example, not by preachment, and through the experiential,
as well as the theoretical. I don’t believe that coercion and
force have any place in the educational process.”
