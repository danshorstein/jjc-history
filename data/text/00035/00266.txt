Sunday: School offers joyful, creative Judaic-Hebraic
learning experiences for children in the kindergarten, first
and second grades. A well-trained staff of teachers involve
the children in the delightful activities of Shabbat, Holidays,
Bible Stories, Hebrew Language, Israeli Singing and Dancing
and Prayers. All these activities provide a smooth transition
to afternoon Hebrew School in the third grade.

 

Rabbi David Gaffney prepares Stephanie Katz and
Michael DuBow for the high holidays. Each of the festivals of
the Jewish calendar, including the Sabbath, play a primary
role in the Center’s curriculum. Synagogue skills are taught,
enabling students to chant from the Torah, blow Shofar, and
lead services in a natural and involved manner. The Sabbath
and Festivals provide the perfect time to explore the ethic of
Jewish ritual and Torah study in order to develop the moral
sensitivities, character and Jewish loyalties of the young
people.

 

High school students join Rabbi Warshaw, Educational
Director, in planning new courses of study. Student-
centered, the youth themselves select their program from a
list of more than a dozen electives. Teachers emphasize
student questions and concerns, exploring together the
issues which the young people consider relevant to their lives
and times. Classes include such divergent topics as Who isa
Zionist?, Cycle of the Jewish Year, Jews around the World,
and The Bible for Today. In addition, Israeli Folk-dancing,
Modern Jewish Philosophy, Comparative Religion, and
Conversational Hebrew Ulpan are scheduled.

The staff of ten specialists views its role as providing the
teenagers with an opportunity for encountering greatness in
the Jewish past, for finding personal meaning in Jewish
practices, and for sharing the intellectual insights of the
tradition.
