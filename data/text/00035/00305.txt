Be. He PELTON DAVID MOSCOVITZ
For nearly 20 years President President

On April 1st, 1926, Dr. Samuel Benjamin was elected rabbi. Dr. Benja-
min is a graduate of the Jewish Theological Seminary of America. He holds the
A. B. degree from Brown University, Providence, and the degree of Dr. Juris
from New York University. Immediately upon graduation from the Seminary
Dr. Benjamin was called to the pulpit of the Anshe Emes Congregation, Cleve-
land. In one year he reorganized that congregation into an institution known as
the Cleveland Jewish Center which owns the largest structure of this kind in the
world and which was erected at a cost exceeding one million dollars.

Dr. Benjamin also occupied the pulpit of the Oheb Zedek Congregation,
Cleveland, from which he resigned to go to Palestine in June 1923. In Pales-
tine, Dr. Benjamin played a prominent role in the civic, economic and political
life of the country. He was the president of the American Organization in
Palestine, president of the American Palestine Bank, member of the Administra-
tive Committee of the Jewish Community of Jaffe, and Tel Aviv, and a member
of the Municipal Council of Tel Aviv.

Upon his return from Palestine, Dr. Benjamin undertook at the request of
the Jerusalem Synagog-Center Fund a tour of the South on behalf of this
undertaking. Almost one thousand dollars were subscribed by the members of
the B'nai Israel Congregation to this cause in response to his appeal.

In the short time that Rabbi Benjamin has been here the Congregation has
made tremendous strides forward. Its budget has increased from a bare $5,000
annually to $17,000. - The membership has already reached 200 and is constantly
growing. The Hebrew School has been completely reorganized. An able prin-

JACKSONVILLE HEBREW SCHOOL

The Jacksonville Hebrew School was founded simultaneously with the
B’nai Israel Congregation but has undergone many changes and developments
since that time. At first it was in the form of the typical European “Cheder”
with the ‘“‘shochet” or cantor acting as the teacher. In 1922 the Daughters of
Israel assumed the responsibility for maintaining the Talmud Torah and with
extraordinary zeal and painstaking care succeeded in introducing a modern
system and methods.

With the coming of Rabbi Samuel Benjamin to Jacksonville the Talmud
Torah was completely reorganized. Mr. Joseph Schenkerman, an able and ex-
perienced pedagogue, was engaged as principal. He introduced the curriculum
adopted by the best Hebrew Schools in the country, and arranged classes to
enable every child desiring a Jewish training to find a proper place.

At present the school has the following classes: (1) A Preparatory Class,
(2) First Class for Beginners, (3) Second Class for Advanced Pupils, (4) Bar

 

Mitzva Class for boys about to become confirmed. In the first two classes
the natural method i.e. Ivrit b'Ivrit is used but in the last two classes due to
the children’s earlier training the Hebrew-English translation system is em-
ployed.
