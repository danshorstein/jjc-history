Boys between the ages of eight and ten find many and
challenging experiences in the Cub Scout program. As they
advance from Wolf to Bear and then on to Weblos, they look

forward to the time that they will be ready for the great out-of-
doors offered in the scouting program of Troop 14.

The Cubs in each Den become a close unit as they work
together to develop new hobbies, take field trips, work on
craft projects, skits, and explore nature.

Annual events are the Rocket and Pinewood Derbies,
with rockets and cars designed by Cubs emphasizing the fact
that the entire program is parent-child oriented.

Each monthly pack meeting features a different theme,
such as Musical Jamboree, Hanukkah, Indian lore, hobbies
and skits. Scout month is honored with participation in a
Friday Sabbath Service and the Blue and Gold Banquet.

The Larry Hacker Award, given to the Cub who scores
the highest in attendance, achievement, and all around good
will, was awarded at the closing picnic to Dorone Manasse.
An individual plaque is given to the recipient, and his name is
added to the perpetual plaque that remains at the Center.

It is with sincere appreciation that the Youth Commis-
sion of the Center extends thanks to the aforementioned,

whose dedication to youth has made the 1975-76 Cub Scout
program so successful.
