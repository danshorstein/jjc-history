MEYER LEIBOWITZ POST #199

The Jewish War Veterans this year celebrate the 81st
year of their founding. This veterans organization is the
first so founded and is the only Jewish organization
authorized to speak before the Congress of the United
States.

In addition to its many other activities, Israel is the
number one concern of J.W.V. working for peace and
security in the Middle East. No organization works harder
in the halls of Congress towards this end.

The 3 million Soviet Jews, separated from their natural
homeland, receive constant attention and support from all
J.W.V. members; we are active at the national as well as the
community planning and implementation levels.

One local project this past year has been an attempt to
locate and mark all graves in Jacksonville, of deceased
Jewish veterans, regardless of affiliation. This worthwhile
project has resulted in a new attempt by the local post to
establish a new marker for all military personnel that would
be a permanent and decorative attachment to the headstone
for easy identification. Hopefully this will be adopted ona
national level.

All persons who know of a Jewish War Veteran
cemetary unlocated are requested to contact any of the
officers of Post 199.

The above photograph shows the marble tablet which
was removed from the old B’nai Israel Synagogue and
placed on the wall in the Center Synagogue, 3rd & Silver
Streets.

Those whose names appear on the Plaque have devoted
many years of zealous work for the Jewish community.
Their efforts were not in vain. The Jacksonville Jewish
Center, which was a direct outgrowth of the B’nai Israel
Synagogue, has placed this plaque within the walls of its
synagogue as a tribute of love and honor to these fine men
whose Jewish interests and loyalty will ever be an
inspiration to the Jewish people of Jacksonville.
