About the Cover:
The cover was created by Susan Krinsky Lubliner,
who has been creating art all throughout her life. She
graduated from Bennington College, having majored in
Painting and Printmaking.
Susan saw her first ketubah at a wedding she and
her then-future husband, Jonathan, attended in — of
all places — Bennington, Vermont. Subsequently, she
turned to the study of calligraphy, both independently and
with various teachers, including Christopher Calderhead,
Eleanor Winters, and David Moss. During Jonathan’s first
pulpit in Connecticut, Susan opened a studio, specializing
in ketubot and related lettering arts. Among her special
artistic loves are papercutting and collage.
Susan has enjoyed being part of the Jacksonville
Jewish Center community since she and her family arrived
in 2004. During that time, she has taught Hebrew
Calligraphy at Camp Ramah Darom, workshops in
papercutting, and has been proud to be called “Picture Lady”
at the Martin J. Gottlieb Day School.
She lives with her husband, Rabbi Jonathan Lubliner,
and their three sons, Avichai, Elior, and Itamar.
