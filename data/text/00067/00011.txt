A Message from the Hazzan
Shana Tova Umetuka
Hazzan Jesse Holzer
Quoting Job 25:2, our liturgy suggests that in the
chaos of our daily lives, we should ask God for protection
and order; for peace. God, referred to here as “the One who
brings peace”, holds an orderly court on high where peace
reigns. Our partnership with the divine grants us a daily
dialogue with God. Each Oseh Shalom means something
different, whether it is comforting those closest to us or
healing the entire world. Creating peace, creating wholeness
in ourselves and in our relationships, is a theme of our day-to-
day lives and, in particular, our lives at the time of our Jewish
new year. May God grant us peace in the coming year, a year
filled with laughter, joy and song.
—Hazzan Jesse Holzer
