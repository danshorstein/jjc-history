Honor Roll
We are happy to present the following list of Jewish men from Jacksonville who have served or are now serv-
ing their country in World War 2. Although great pains have taken in the compilation of names over a period of
months, we are quite certain that this list is not complete. If there are omissions or inaccuracies, readers will
be rendering a distinct service to the Community if they will call these to the attention of the Jacksonville Jew-
ish Community Council, 7F Atlantic Bank Annex, phone 50060־. These names will form the basis for a communi-
ty־wide survey to be conducted this fall, in which full data will be sought, so that the participation of our local
men and women in the armed forces will be recorded in permanent form.
It would appear that already the percentage of those of Jewish faith from Jacksonville who serve Uncle Sam
is nearly nine per cent of the total Jewish population of Jacksonville. Since Jews constitute only two percent of the
total population of the City of Jacksonville, obviously our contribution to the armed forces, on a proportionate basis,
is very heavy. In the country at large, the overall percentage of the national population now in uniform is only
72/ו per cent.
From time to time in Jacksonville, as in other parts of the country, Nazi-inspired voices of hate have whispered
the lie that Jews are shirking their war responsibilities. Several times there has been distributed “the first Amen-
can״ propaganda, which slanders our honored dead. These calumnies make rolls of honor like the following even
more important. They represent irrefutable evidence that in the world-wide struggle against a Nazi tyranny that
has debased every principle of social justice for which Judaism traditionally stands, those of the Jewish faith, in
common with all other Americans, are ever ready to give the last full measure of devotion. The next time that
someone challenges Jewish patriotism when you're around, let him have these local facts. W. I. B.
