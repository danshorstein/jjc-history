Dewey Speaks on Palestine
"As the success of our fighting forces bring us
closer to ultimate victory, the significance of Pal-
esdne becomes greater every month. We also come
closer to the necessity to remember well and hard
the lessons of the First World War. The peace
which followed World War I failed to solve the
problems confronting the minorities, and that fail-
ure contributed to bringing about the present cruel
conflict. The methods followed by Axis propa-
ganda in the Middle East showed clearly how the
problem of minorities and the dilemma of Pales-
tine became a strategic factor for the forces of
hate and oppression.
"It is essential that a way be found to open the
doors of Palestine, especially with so many doors
elsewhere which remain closed. This question has
ceased to be the concern of merely one or two na-
tions. It is the concern of men of good will all
over the world. There will be no easy solution.
At the same time, if no home is found for hundreds
of thousands of helpless and wandering Jews, the
problem will be magnified throughout the entire
world. That is a spectacle which no Jew could
tolerate and no Christian could observe with any-
thing but abhorrence.
"The doors of Palestine must be permanently
opened.” — Thomas E. Dewey, Governor of New
York.
