A large delegation attended the Southeastern Zion-
ist conference at Savannah, November, 1943. The
Jacksonville Zionist District was signally honored for
having gained the largest increase in membership dur-
ing 1942-43. Following the conference, the local dis-
trict entered upon an intensive program against the
White Paper. This work was carried on through the
Emergency Committee for Palestine. The committee
caused thousands of letters and cards, hundreds of tele-
grams, and many resolutions to be sent to Washington,
calling upon the President of the United States and his
Cabinet, to use their good offices toward the abrogation
of the White Paper. In February, members of the Ameri-
can Palestine Committee were guests of the Zionist Dis-
trict at a luncheon in the Roosevelt Hotel.

1944, members of the Zionist District and Hadassah
