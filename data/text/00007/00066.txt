Beverly Safer and Sabina Reiser were chosen to rep-
resent Troop 2 at the: city-wide week-end at Camp
Chowenwah, Girl Scout Camp at Green Cove Springs.
They brought back numerous ideas for games, songs and
out-of-door cooking . . . On January 14, Arbor Day, a
very interesting program was presented, during which a
dogwood tree was planted on the east side of the Cen-
ter . . . During the past year the troop secured the
services of two new leaders, Miss Rachel Rothstein and
Mrs. Sarabelle Holbert. The final meeting of the year
was in the form of an all-day hike and cookery event on
the shores of the St. John’s River.

Girl Scouts of America, Troop 2, is sponsored by the
Daughters of Israel. Its membership includes Jewish
girls between the ages of 10 and 14 . . . Besides their
regular activities of camping, homemaking, arts and
crafts, etc., the girls last year sold over $30,000 worth
of U. S. War Bonds and stamps during the Fourth War
Loan Drive, receiving a citation from the Surgeon Gen-
eral of the U. S. Army for this achievement.
