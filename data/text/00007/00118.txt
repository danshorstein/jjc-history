National Jewish Welfare Board
Army and Navy Committee
Ira Stein
Despite many handicaps, the Home Hospitality Com-
mittee has been placing 150 servicemen a month in pri-
vate homes . . . The Social Affairs Committee has con-
tinued to sponsor Saturday evening dances and Sunday
open house at the Center or Temple. Each Wednesday
evening the Military Maids entertain . . . Through the
JWB extension program at the YMHA, sleeping accomo-
dations are available on weekends . . . The JWB office
also renders assistance to wives of servicemen who find
themselves hard pressed for living quarters or employ-
ment. — I. S.

The JWB Army and Navy Committee is the recognized
civilian agency serving Jewish military personnel away
from camp. The religious elements in its program al-
ways have been of prime importance. During Rosh
Hashonah arrangements were made in local homes, for
more than 250 men; on Yom Kippur they attended ser-
vices at the Temple and the Center, after which they were
guests at a YMHA break-fast and at the traditional
Yom Kippur dance in the Center.
During Chanukah, Chaplain Shaukman officiated at
YMHA services . . . Over 400 attended the joint Con-
gregational Seder on the first night of Passover. A second
Seder was held in the Center the following evening; simi-
larly the Temple acted as hosts to 350 men in the May-
flower Hotel. At Camp Blanding 1500 men took part in a
huge Seder . . . Throughout the year invitations were ex-
tended to servicemen to attend Friday evening services . . .
During Shovuos the YMHA held open house, serving a
buffet supper of traditional dishes ... A large quantity
of Bibles, prayer books and other religious material
regularly have been distributed to the men.
