A survey of the 1943-44 activities of the Brotherhood
reveals a year highlighted by several important episodes.
The monthly meetings consisted mainly of speakers who
brought to the members a message of interest. Among
the guest speakers were Chaplain Jacob K. Shankman of
the Naval Air Station, L. M. Rhodes of the State Mar-
keting Bureau and Dr. Lewis J. Velte of the USO.
The joint evening session with the Temple Sisterhood
proved to be a noteworthy event. Another project spon-
sored by the members included the Passover Seder at the
Mayflower Hotel for those in the armed forces, with 350
in attendance. The annual observance of the Temple
Brotherhood Sabbath was marked by an increased attend-
anee on Friday evening, May 5th. Lt. Col. Chaplain
Edwin V. Carter, Senior Chaplain at Camp Blanding,
delivered the sermon on "Brotherhood in Action.”
