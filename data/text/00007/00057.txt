National AZA Sabbath was observed by the local
chapter in November. Beryl Weinstein gave the ser-
mon which received honorable mention in the National
AZA Sermon Contest. A Friday service was given by
the chapter in honor of Captain William Katz, alumnus
of the chapter, who was home on leave. Life member-
ship cards were presented to the mothers of the mem-
bers in service . . . The social highlight of the year was
the dance held in February, 1944, at which Miss Miriam
Portnoy was crowned "AZA Sweetheart.” In April
the chapter staged its seventh anniversary dinner at the
Seminole Hotel.
In line with the National War Service program of
AZA, the chapter sponsored several USO nights at the
Young Judea
There are two senior Young Judea clubs: the Henri-
etta Szold Club, under the guidance of Miss Raela Fink
and Miss Eunice Davis, and the Daughters of Judea, led
by Mrs. Sidney Stillman . . . The Daughters of Judea
originated and sponsored a successful bazaar, the pro-
ceeds going to the Keren Hanoar Fund. The Meyer
Levin and Henrietta Szold clubs each conducted a beau-
tiful Sabbath service at the Center. All clubs participated
in the Balfour Day celebration and in several Hadassah
programs.
The Yeung Judea movement strives to develop Jew-
ish boys and girls of adolescent age for Jewish leader-
ship and an active interest in Palestine . . . During the
past season two new Young Judean Clubs were organiz-
ed: the Meyer Levin Club, under the leadership of Miss
Marion Freed, is a junior club composed of 21 boys and
girls, ranging in age from 12 through 14 years;
the Theodor Herzl Club, originally sponsored by Mrs.
Nathan Newman, later by Mrs. Joseph Cohen, has the
10 to 12 year age group. Mrs. M. J. Reiser led the Rebec-
ca Gratz Club, the third junior group of boys and girls.
