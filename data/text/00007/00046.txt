Smile of Achievement
This young refugee farmerette proudly holds the first fruits of
her labors as a tiller of the soil. Behind her is a wall of the van in
which her family’s worldly possessions were brought to Palestine
from Czecho-Slovakia before the war broke out. That van was
converted into their home in a Palestine settlement until funds
provided by American contributors to the United Jewish Appeal
made possible the construction of new living quarters.
The Racket of Anti-Semitism
by WILLIAM DRAPER LEWIS
Director, American Law Institute, Philadelphia
Can America be protected from the use of anti-
Semitism as a racket, as a means of helping Fase-
ism, as an instrument for destroying the spirit of
unity for the war effort and so prolonging the per-
iod for the destruction of lives? America is today
confronted with many vital questions but none
more vital than this. The evil results of the spirit
of race prejudice and hatred threaten our future
and our children’s future. It is the darkest cloud
on our national horizon today because it is a di-
rect denial of those principles, even our imper-
feet recognition of which has made possible the
strength of our nation and, what is more import-
ant, a way of life that gives us a high average
of happiness.
The consequences of anti-Semitism are clear;
clear also the lines along which it should be com-
bated. Little can be done by legislation. You
cannot legislate out of a man his hate and con-
tempt of another, or, except within narrow limits,
compel him to associate on terms of equality with
those he dislikes. To attempt by legislation to
suppress the public expression of a dislike for
Jews, except where such expression threatens pub-
lie disorder, sets a bad precedent: Freedom of ex-
pression is the cornerstone of individual liberty.
To be combatted, anti-Semitism must be attacked
at its root. The root is the denial of the sacred-
ness of personality, the denial of democratic ideals
based on the equality of man. It is the denial of
the basic teachings of Jesus. The Jews them-
selves can doubtless do much but it is principally
by non-Jews that this battle will be won or lost.
