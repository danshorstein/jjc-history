Jacksonville Jewish Community Council
1944 Beneficiaries (Continued)
NATIONAL JEWISH HOSPITAL—Treats people suffering from pulmonary tuberculosis. Non-sectarian.
NATIONAL JEWISH WELFARE BOARD—Conducts special activities for Jewish men in the armed forces; serves
YMHA’s and community centers.
RABBI ISAAC ELCHANAN THEOLOGICAL SEMINARY—Promotes study of Talmud; prepares students for ortho-
dox rabbinate and trains teachers.
YESHIVOTH—Six miscellaneous American yeshivoth, training students for the rabbinate along traditional orthodox
lines, derive funds from the Community Council campaign.
YIDDISH SCIENTIFIC INSTITUTE—Records, analyzes and publishes materials related to Jewish life and trains stu-
dents for Jewish research.
AMERICAN JEWISH CONFERENCE—Aims through joint consultation to arrive at decisions affecting problems of
rescue, post-war reconstruction and Palestine.
AMERICAN ASSOCIATION FOR JEWISH EDUCATION—Stimulates coordination of Jewish educational facilities.
AMERICAN ACADEMY FOR JEWISH RESEARCH—Fosters and promotes Jewish learning and research.
DROPSIE COLLEGE!—Offers graduate courses in higher Hebrew and cognate learning.
UNION OF AMERICAN HEBREW CONGREGATIONS AND HEBREW UNION COLLEGE—Former stimulates
Reform Judaism; latter trains rabbis for Reform pulpits.
Local and Regional Causes
ARMY-NAVY COMMITTEE (JWB)—Renders recreational, social and religious services to Jewish men in uniform
in the Jacksonville area.
FLORIDA STATE STUDENT UNION—Help to make possible Bnai Brith Hillel activities of Florida campuses.
HEBREW ORPHANS’ HOME—Located in Atlanta, this institution cares for dependent and destitute children of the
Southeastern area.
JEWISH EDUCATION—A fund has been earmarked to promote Jewish education in Jacksonville.
PUBLIC RELATIONS COMMITTEE—A specific activity of the Community Council, this committee works toward ef-
fecting better relations between the local Jewish and non-Jewish community.
TRANSIENT FUND—Gives financial assistance to religious itinerants passing through Jacksonville.
