Jacksonville Jewish Community Council
1944 Beneficiaries
Overseas Causes
AMERICAN FRIENDS OF HEBREW UNIVERSITY—Supports the Hebrew University in Jerusalem, a permanent home
for the traditions of science and learning among Jews.
AMERICAN FUND FOR PALESTINIAN INSTITUTIONS—Supports 52 cultural, educational and social service in-
stitutions which do not receive funds from any other American source.
AMERICAN ORT FEDERATION—Aids vocational retraining of Jews through educational and technical schools, agri-
cultural colonies, cooperatives, etc.
EZRAS TORAH—Grants assistance to orthodox rabbis and religious scholars in need.
EUROPEAN AND PALESTINIAN INSTITUTIONS—24 traditional charitable and educational institutions receive
allocations from an annual miscellaneous fund.
HEBREW SEIELTERING AND IMMIGRANT AID SOCIETY—Conducts immigration activities on a world-wide
scale in collaboration with numerous local committees.
JEWISH TELEGRAPHIC AGENCY—Collects and distributes news about Jews all over the world.
NATIONAL LABOR COMMITTEE FOR PALESTINE—Provides funds for cooperative industries and labor settle-
ments in Palestine.
UNITED JEWISH APPEAL—Comprises the following three agencies:
(1) JOINT DISTRIBUTION COMMITTEE—Major American Jewish agency for world-wide relief.
(2) NATIONAL REFUGEE SERVICE—Assists newcomers to this country.
(3) UNITED PALESTINE APPEAL—Central instrument for building the Jewish National Home in Palestine
through the Jewish National Fund and Palestine Foundation Fund.
HADASSAH YOUTH ALIYAH—Brings children to Palestine from countries of Jewish persecution, and places them
in agricultural and vocational schools.
AMERICAN JEWISH CONGRESS—Fights against anti-Semitism and economic discrimination; endeavors to safe-
guard the rights of Jews everywhere.
National Causes
BNAI BRITH WIDER SCOPE—Maintains Hillel Foundations on college and university campuses, Vocational Serv-
ice Bureau and AZA youth activities.
COUNCIL OF JEWISH FEDERATIONS AND WELFARE FUNDS—Coordinates activities of welfare funds and
offers services in research and planning.
HEBREW THEOLOGICAL COLLEGE OF CHICAGO—Trains religious leaders for orthodox synagogues and instruc-
tors for religious schools.
HISTADRUTH IVRITH—Promotes knowledge of Hebrew language and literature in the United States.
JEWISH LABOR COMMITTEE—Through labor and fraternal organizations fights to protect religious, political and
civil rights of Jews.
JEWISH CONCUMPTIVES’ RELIEF SOCIETY—Maintains non-sectarian sanatorium for free treatment and care of
tuberculous patients.
JEWISH CONSUMPTIVE AND EX-PATIENTS’ RELIEF ASSOCIATION—Treats tuberculous patients who come to
Southern California; non-sectarian.
JEWISH PUBLICATION SOCIETY—Promotes, prints and circulates books of general interest on Jewish subjects.
JEWISH TEACHERS’ SEMINARY—Trains men and women for Jewish teaching, research and community service.
JEWISH THEOLOGICAL SEMINARY—Prepares men for the conservative rabbinate and teachers for religious
schools.
JOINT DEFENSE APPEAI. OF AMERICAN JEWISH COMMITTEE AND ANTI-DEFAMATION LEAGUE—
Raises funds for combatting anti-Semitic activities.
LEO N. LEVI MEMORIAL HOSPITAL—Gives care and treatment to indigent persons suffering from rheumatism,
sciatica and related ailments; non-sectarian.
NATIONAL FARM SCHOOL—Prepares city boys for careers as practicing farmers.
