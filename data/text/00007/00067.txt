The Facts Are ..
• At least one third of the Jewish
physicians of the nation are serving
with the armed forces.
• Five hundred and twelve Jewish
families in America have given a to-
tal of 1791 sons to the armed forces.
Fifteen of these families have 98 sons
in the service.
• The Distinguished Service Cross,
the second highest Army award for
heroism, has been given to 29 men of
Jewish faith.
• Thirty-four percent of the Jewish
male refugee population of the Unit-
ed States are bearing arms for their
adopted country.
Twenty-five percent of the Jewish
inductees from a typical industrial
city are serving in what is commonly
regarded as the most dangerous
branch of the service^the Army Air
Forces.
1 House-to-house canvasses in a num-
ber of representative cities show that
the percentage of Jewish men in serv-
ice is in excess of the ratio of Jews to
the general population.
1 Studies by The Bureau of War Rec-
ords indicate that more than 400,000
men of Jewish faith are serving in the
armed forces of the United States; or,
in military terms, more than 25 divi-
sions.
• More than 5,000 Jewish men in uni-
form have received citations for val-
or and merit, often posthumously.
• Among Jewish servicemen from
New York City alone over 1,100
casualties were already recorded and
many more remain to be authenticat-
ed.
• Fifty percent of all rabbis in Ameri-
ca have volunteered for military duty
as chaplains.
• Thirty-seven Jewish men have receiv-
ed ten or more awards of valor and
among them hold an aggregate of
405 such awards.
