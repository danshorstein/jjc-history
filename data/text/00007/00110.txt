A. B. Weil Lodge No. 800
Bnai Brith
Sidney J. Brown
To mark the founding of Bnai Brith, A. B. Weil
Lodge had two outstanding meetings. The first, in
January, took the form of a joint service with the Jewish
Center and was held at the Temple. Rabbi Amram
Prero of the Hillel Foundation at Gainesville was the
principal speaker. In March, a second joint service was
arranged, this time at the Center and a large class of new
members was initiated by a degree team composed of
Abe Newman, Philip Selber, Nat Shorstein, Harry Katz
and M. J. Reiser. Julius Fisher, secretary of the Fifth
District, gave the main address to the class which was
dedicated to David Davis. A joint meeting with the
Bnai Brith Women also was held in June at the home
of Mr. and Mrs. Morris Witten.
Another achievement to the credit of the lodge was
the organization of Bnai Brith Girls, a junior order of
Bnai Brith Women, with a charter membership in Jack-
sonville of about 35.
At present the lodge, already the largest men’s group
in the city, is striving to bring its membership up to 500
as a result of a drive headed by Leon Springer.—S. J. B.

The one hundredth anniversary year of the establish-
ment of Bnai Brith finds the local lodge concentrating
upon a war service program. Although 20% of the
membership is now in service (June 15), activities have
continued without interruption, including donations to
the Blood Bank, a war bond drive headed by David Har-
ris and the raising by David Davis of a $500.00 quota for
the National War Service Fund. Also included within
the category of war work has been the sponsorship of
Saturday and Sunday affairs for the men in service. Held
at the Temple and the Center in cooperation with the
USO, these have received the aid of Bnai Brith Women
and especially of Mrs. Morris Witten who was in charge.
Under the chairmanship of Harry Jaffe the lodge’s
AZA committee has made unusual efforts to assist its
junior order, the Jacksonville AZA chapter, in every pos-
sible manner.
