The Daughters of Israel functions as the Jewish Cen-
ter auxiliary and its chief purpose is to aid the institution.
Its special project is to maintain the Hebrew and the
Sunday Schools, providing every Jewish child with an
opportunity to receive a Jewish religious education. In
order to encourage the children and give them a special
incentive, the organization offers prizes each year for
outstanding achievement and arranges suitable observ-
anees of the Jewish holidays. As an extension of its in-
terest in youth, the Daughters of Israel sponsors a Girl
Scout troop and tries to interest the sons of Center mem-
bers in joining the Boy Scout troop.
The Daughters of Israel have taken an ever-increasing
interest in the general life of the community. They
have sought to become extremely active in civic work.
