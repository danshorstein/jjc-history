Community Organizations
Jacksonville Mitzvah Clown Corps
The goal of the
Jacksonville Mitzvah
Clowns is to create
a self perpetuating
corps of local Mitzvah
Clowns available for
periodic visits and special programs at local facilities, as well
as participation in local programs which serve the Jewish
community and the general community. The Mitzvah Clown
program seeks to bring smiles, companionship and cheer to
sick and aged people while at the same time enhancing self-
confidence and creating a joy of doing mitzvot among the
Mitzvah Clown participants. The program also seeks to instill
in the participants a level of comfort when performing the
mitzvah of bikkur holim.
The Jacksonville Mitzvah Clowns have participated
in various community events during the past year including
bringing laughter with their crazy antics to Center
Preschoolers, River
Garden and Mount
Carmel residents,
recipients of shelach
manot delivered by
the Martin J. Gottlieb
Middle School Mitzvah Program for Purim, Community
Yom HaAtzmaut celebration and Family University, as well
as other community events.
Many thanks are owed to the many volunteers who
continue to give freely of themselves to make this program
so successful.
The group is always looking for new trainees and
hopes to sponsor additional training sessions for both adults
and teens in the sixth grade or older during the coming year.
