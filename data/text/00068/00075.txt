It is good to thank the Lord and sing your name on high!
Hazzan Jesse Holzer
As we celebrate our 1st anniversary here in Jacksonville,
Leora and I want to take this opportunity to thank
everyone who has made us feel so welcome, so
gratified and so loved. Working with an energetic and
focused leadership team has been especially gratifying to me
as I grow into the role of your Hazzan. Our clergy team and
our lay leaders have given me the room to create innovative
new programs and even more innovative puns.
As a “Minister of Music”, I find it remarkable that
I have the privilege of singing Shabbat songs with pre¬
schoolers one moment and Yiddish folk songs with our
“former pre-schoolers” the next. We’ve been able to celebrate
times of great joy together while being there in times of great
need. Your attendance and support at recurring workshops
like Cantors and Cocoa and Monday Night Musical show a
strong desire not only for continuing your Jewish education,
but for breathing new melodies into your Jewish spirits.
Enough cannot be said about our musical volunteers - those
who give their talents and their time to productions like our
synagogue choir, the Octet and our band: Kol-train. The
installation weekend in April still brings goosebumps to our
family.
In this coming year, I’d like to continue developing
these programs along with our choral program here at the
Center. Our youth can hopefully be involved through choir as
well as through our incentive laden programs. The Chaizman
Trophy, unveiled in March, will hopefully create a strong
connection to davening skills. Our motto “Pray 2 Play”
will hopefully encourage teens to come to Shabbat morning
services prior to playing a pick-up game against Hazzan’s
Hoopsters.
We hope and pray for a year of happiness and joy,
fruitfulness and laughter. Shana tova.
—Hazzan Jesse Holzer
