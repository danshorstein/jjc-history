Honoring Raymond Cohen
for his lifelong contributions of time and talent in service to our synagogue
Jacksonville Jewish Center Groundbreaking, December 20, 1987
“And all thy children shall be taught the Lord, and great shall be the peace of thy
children.” - Isaiah. Rabbi Hanina said of that passage, “Read not banayich, thy
children, but bonayich, thy builders, for the children are the builders of our future.”
Dad, you have built a rich life, for yourself and your family, based on the wisdom
of Judaism and a heartfelt calling to use your talents in service to our congregation.
You taught us by your example and we have tried to teach our children, in turn.
The Torah tells us that when the Sanctuary was to be built, God “called” the artist
and filled him with wisdom, understanding and knowledge that his works might
inspire and uplift others.
Dad, you have been blessed with an exceptional talent, both for the visual arts and
for music, which you used to fashion a fulfilling creative career and rewarding vocal
avocation. But by answering the call, using your gifts for the benefit of the synagogue,
you have achieved far greater success — you are an inspiration. “The highest artist,
in the eyes of Jewish teachers of all generations, is not the master of self-expression
but of self-control — he who fashions himself into a sanctuary.” You are that artist.
Dad, you and Mom have our love, our respect and our gratitude, always,
Peggy, Ken, David and Rachel
