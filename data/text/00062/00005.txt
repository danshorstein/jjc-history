A Tribute to Raymond Cohen
for his lifelong contributions of time and talent in service to our synagogue
Art, music and love of Judaism have always
been intertwined in the life of Raymond Cohen, and
he has never hesitated to share his talents with
enthusiasm and grace. He is a longtime friend to
many in our congregation, and exemplifies the best
that the Jacksonville Jewish Center has to offer.
Raymond’s personal history is tightly woven
into the history of this congregation. His life spans
the generations and is a bridge to the future.
Bom in Jacksonville on
January 4, 1921, Raymond
was the youngest of the four
children of Ida and Joseph
Cohen. The couple, like other
Pusheloters, helped start this
congregation and were
founding members of B’nai
Israel, as our synagogue was
then named.
Jewish education was especially precious to
Raymond’s mother, whose own father had been a
Yeshiva student in Lithuania. Naturally, Eddie, Macy,
Bessie and Raymond were enrolled in Hebrew
school at the synagogue on Jefferson and Duval.
Raymond would later become Bar Mitzvah on the
pulpit of the new Jacksonville
Jewish Center at 3rd and
Silver. It was there, that he
learned to daven with devotion
and began to develop his
unparalleled hazzanuut.
As a teen in the 1930s,
Raymond joined one of the
youth groups that were bringing
the spirit of Zionism to
communities like ours. In
Rothchild Young Judea,
Raymond experienced the
satisfaction of volunteering for Jewish causes, that
would carry over into adulthood.
