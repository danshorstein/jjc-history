Simhat Torah Honorees
The High Holiday season reaches its joyous climax on Simhat Torah, the last day of the festival
of Sukkot. Because of our perennial love of the Torah, it has been referred to as Israel’s “bride”. On
Simhat Torah, we bring to a close the annual cycle of the reading of the Torah and immediately begin
anew. An ancient tradition evolved of honoring worthy men by calling them to the Torah for the last
reading, Hatan Torah, and the beginning portion, Hatan Bereshit. In our synagogue tradition, we
also honor the contributions made by a “Woman of Valor”.
David Schmerer
Hatan Torah
David
Schmerer is one
of the nicest guys
you’ll ever meet.
Being affable is
also a quality that
Dave draws upon
to enlist the aid
of others. He’s
both a devoted volunteer and a very
effective leader.
David Schmerer was born in
Miami Beach in 1952. He received a
BA in government from FSU and a BT,
cum laude, in industrial technology
from UNF. He is a Safety & Health
Consultant with Zenith Insurance.
When the Schmerers moved to
our community in 1981, Dave found
that the Center offered just the right
mix of traditional Jewish observance
and a wide variety of activities. He
immediately became active in Family
Council, and youth would become a
focal point for his volunteer efforts in
the years that followed.
At the heart of it all is Dave’s
deep love of Judaism. He expresses it
in his own observance, and in the
attention he gives religious life at the
Center. David has taught in our
Religious School, Hebrew High and in
Junior Congregation. He is a minyan
captain, Shabbat usher, gabbai, bass
soloist in the Center Choir and past
chairman of the Religious committee
For five years, Dave was
president of the Center Men’s Club. He
was the driving force behind the
creation of the Horty T. Gendzier
Summer Scholarship Fund, as well as
many other successful fundraising,
social and communal projects that
benefit our congregation.
Fred Pozin
Hatan Bereshit
Fred Pozin
has a real head for
business and a
genuine heart for
the welfare of our
congregation.
This winning
combination has
made him
invaluable as the
Center’s Financial Vice President.
Beginning in 2000, and continuing with
this administration, Fred’s tireless
efforts have maintained the fiscal
strength of our synagogue.
Fred Pozin has the hotel business
in his blood. He was bom in Miami
Beach, and at a young age, relocated
to Gainesville, where his family built
and operated the University Motel.
Later in Orange Park, they built and
operated the Holiday Inn. After
attending the Hotel Restaurant School
at Florida State University, Fred began
his career with the Hyatt Hotel
Corporation and served in numerous
management positions.
In the early 80s, Fred returned to
Jacksonville to open and operate the
Ramada Inn Mandarin and Comedy Zone.
He met and married Wendy Margol and
became active at the Center.
With Fred’s business acumen and
dedication to Jewish education, he was
a natural choice to co-chair the 1997
Center Raffle for Education, and other
fundraising events. Most notably, Fred
lent his Comedy Club for an exclusive
performance to benefit Hadassah.
Active on our Board of Directors, Fred
served on the house and finance
committees before accepting the
position of Financial Vice President,
which he still holds.
Phyllis Goldberg
Woman of Valor
Phyllis
Goldberg is an
empowered
woman, dedicated
to Jewish life and
education. She is
poised, assured
and self-confident.
She is a loyal
friend and delightful companion. A
strong sense of propriety guides her
every action. She is the essence of the
Woman of Valor “robed in strength and
dignity; abounding in wisdom; modest,
her work proclaims her praises.”
Born in Chicago, Phyllis was
raised in Jacksonville from age 4. She
received a bachelor’s degree from the
University of Pennsylvania, and a
master’s degree at Columbia. She
married Larry Goldberg and began a
family in New York, but was soon drawn
back to Jacksonville.
Phyllis retired from the computer
profession to become a “professional
volunteer.” The Center has been the
beneficiary of her time and talent for
organization for over 20 years. She is a
past VP of Membership, long-standing
Board member, and has served on the
Adult Education, Performing Artists
and Rabbi Search committees, among
others.
Over the years, Phyllis has been
a leader and driving force for women’s
issues. In Center Sisterhood, she served
as Education VP, Torah Fund chair and
President. She distinguished herself in
numerous positions for the Florida
Branch of Women’s League for
Conservative Judaism and sits on the
International Board. Phyllis has served
Hadassah as chapter co-chair, and
earned its Chai Award.
