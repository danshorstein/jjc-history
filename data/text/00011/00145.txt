The lodge was chartered in 1950 and has a present
membership of over fifty members, entirely Jewish. The
first Noble Grand of the Lodge was David E. Freedman.
The IOOF holds to no creed, but all who become mem¬
bers must believe in the existence of a Supreme Being, the
Creator and Preserver of the Universe. While the mem¬
bership is largely in the United States and Canada, there
are lodges throughout the world. The history of the IOOF
can be traced back about two centuries. The first United
States Lodge dates back to 1819.
MOE B. SAFER
Teaching forgiveness and tolerance, the Order pro¬
motes benevolence and charity and has established Homes
for the Aged and Indigent, the Widows and Orphans.
In Jacksonville there are eight Odd Fellow and their
sister Rebekah Lodges. There is in Jacksonville also the
Encampment Canton and two auxiliary lodges, which
lodges bestow additional degrees of the Order. Odd Fel¬
lowship in the subordinated lodges is composed of three
degrees in addition to the initiatory degree.
Israel Lodge No. 11 will take an active part in local
affairs and will aid in Youth Movements. The Lodge is
making plans for an increased membership and arranging
for social affairs.
