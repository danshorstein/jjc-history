STANLEY KANTOR
Fifteen years ago three Jewish boys began our club.
From a simple social club Esquire has developed into an
outstanding youth organization of Jacksonville. This year
the club enjoyed its most active and successful season.
Our monthly social functions provided many pleasant
times and memorable occasions for the young people of
this city. Esquire also showed great interest in athletics.
Our teams participated in basketball and baseball compe¬
tition.
Although a social and athletic schedule has always
been the basis of our program—this year the interests of
Esquire branched out in many new directions. Under the
supervision of our religious committee our members con¬
ducted a Friday evening service. In March a party was
given for the crippled children at Hope Haven. Under
our new cultural program, Esquire enjoyed listening to
informal talks by outstanding citizens of our community.
A debate on the subject of Jewish Parochial Schools was
also a part of this program.
We feel certain that next year’s club will have an
equally successful year and will continue with the expanded
program initiated in 1951.

The junior auxiliary of the Esquire Club, known as
Junior Esquire, followed the general program of the par¬
ent club. Social events were staged monthly and athletic
programs were held every two weeks.
The club boasts of a membership of nineteen boys.
Trophies were presented to Sidney Setzer and Danny
Rosenberg as outstanding pledges and an appropriate
trophy was awarded to Byron Selber as the outstanding
boy of Junior Esquire.
