30 Rosh Hodesh
Hamisha Asar B’Shevat (Fifteenth of Shevat) is Holy Land Arbor Day.
Also called the New Year of the trees. In Israel school children use the
day for planting thousands of trees each year. In the Diaspora it is an
occasion for stressing the significance of the upbuilding of Israel.
Shabbat Shekalim the Sabbath when every adult Jew in the days of
the Temple was reminded to pay his poll tax of a half a shekel on the
first day of Adar. In our day, a shekel is paid by every adult Jew,
entitling him to vote for delegates to the Zionist Congress.
