CLIFFORD J. BODARKY
The Jewish Welfare Society has served the needy in
Jacksonville for many years. It is a member agency of
the Jacksonville Community Chest and the Jewish Com¬
munity Council. The agency’s functions are divided into
various categories.
The Society works with the National Council of
Jewish Women and the United Service for New Americans
in helping New Americans adjust themselves to our com¬
munity. It administers the funds furnished by the Jewish
Community Council which make their resettlement
possible.
Local families receive financial assistance and receive
casework help to enable them to strengthen themselves and
find a more satisfying way of life.
Transients are counseled and helped wherever possible
either with food, lodging, employment, or contact with
their families.
The Welfare Society works in close relationship with
River Garden in processing applications for the Home and
furnishing a casework service to the residents of the
Home to make their initial adjustment to group living
easier, and their continuous residence richer.
With the financial help of the Levy Fund, the Jewish
Welfare Society has inaugurated, to a limited extent, the
operation of the Family Consultation Center. It serves
families where there are problems due to parent-child re¬
lationships. Under the auspices of the Jewish Welfare
Society, the Family Consultation Center is non-sectarian.
The Center has completed a year of highly successful
operation gaining wide community and professional recog¬
nition.
Mr. Bodarky is Executive Director of the Jewish Wel¬
fare Society and Director of its Family Consultation
Center. 'Dr. Sidney Halpern, President of the Jewish
Welfare Society, also serves as chairman of the Admini¬
strative Committee of the Family Consultation Center.
The Society is a member of the Florida Conference of
Social Welfare and the National Conference of Jewish
Social Welfare.
