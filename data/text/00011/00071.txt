The two Brownie Troops and one Girl Scout Troop
sponsored by the Center Sisterhood, Daughters of Israel
had a very successful year.
In the fall of 1950 they participated in Girl Scout
Sabbath and in the city-wide International Friendship
program.
Among their many service activities were: collection
and shipment of three boxes of clothing to Israel; making
and filling book bags which were sent to children overseas;
contributions to Red Cross, Community Chest and Flope
Haven Hospital; folding newspapers for the Visiting Nurses
Association; stuffing envelopes for the Easter Seal Drive
Heart Campaign.
All troops made Mother’s Day gifts and had programs
in honor of their mothers. The beautiful corsages pre¬
sented to mothers at the Center Mother’s Day Service were
made by Brownies.
Also enjoyed by the girls were folk dances, sports and
games and cook-outs.
Arts and crafts covered the making of woven pot hold¬
ers, sit-upons, string holders, aprons and the painting of
plaques and figurines.
The closing luncheon was held on June 7th in the
Center auditorium with approximately 125 Scouts and
guests attending. Special guests were Mrs. J. E. Ferrell,
Publicity Chairman for the Girl Scout Council, Mrs. Albert
S. Lawton, Girl Scout Commissioner, Miss Bonnie Beth
Wimpy, Executive Secretary, Girl Scout Council, Mrs.
Ralph Mizrahi, President of Center Sisterhood and Rabbi
Sanders A. Tofield.
A formal dance given by Scouts of Troop 35 under the
supervision of Mrs. Al Gross and Mrs. Barney Margolin
terminated the season.
