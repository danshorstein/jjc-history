NOTES OF TISHR1
L. C. “Light Sabbath or Festival candles.”
Rosh Hashanah marks the beginning of the Jewish New Year and
lasts two days.
Shabbat Shuva the Sabbath occuring between Rosh Hashanah and
Yom Kippur, takes its name from the first words of the Haftorah,
“Shuvah, Yisrael—Return, O Israel.”
Yom Kippur the Day of Atonement, is a day of fasting and prayer,
a day for confession of sin and supplication for forgiveness. “For trans¬
gressions between man and God, repentence on Yom Kippur brings
atonement. For transgressions between man and man, Yom Kippur
brings no atonement until amends are made.”
Sukkot the Feast of Tabernacles commemorates the period when our
forefathers dwelt in booths (Sukkot) during their wanderings through
the wilderness of Sinai after their Egyptian bondage.
Simhat Torah (Rejoicing in the Torah) celebrates the completion of
the reading of the five books of Moses. On this day, the reading is
begun anew for the coming year.
