One of the most important functions of the Jacksonville
Jewish Center is its service to the Jewish community in
providing appropriate and dignified burial facilities for
the members of the congregation.
Under the chairmanship of Max Rubin, the Center
Cemetery Committee during the past year continued its
efforts to appropriately care for the burial requirements of
Center membership as well as to provide adequately for
future needs. The new Memorial Park, developed during
the past few years, has begun to relieve the overtaxed
facilities of the old Evergreen Cemetery7, the remaining

During the past year a complete audit and analysis of
the financial structure of the Cemetery’s fund revealed the
necessity for an adjustment in the schedule of perpetual
upkeep as well as in the schedules for annual maintenance.
Maintenance costs due to increased costs of labor and
other necessities, necessitated the adjustment which was
the first increase in maintenance charges in over twenty
years. Rates have been adjusted in keeping with this
analysis and the splendid cooperation of the membership
in accepting these new schedules has been gratifying.
The committee has continued its unselfish devotion to
the needs of the community and is on call at all times.
