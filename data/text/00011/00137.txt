In the past year, A. Z. A. participated fully in their
five fold and full program of Community Service, Re¬
ligious, Cultural, Athletics, and Social.
The Community Service program was carried out by
performing various services to the River Garden Hebrew
Home For the Aged and the Community Council. On the
Religious program we had our annual service at the
Center, and a talk was given by our Chaplain on each
Jewish holiday. Five prayer books were presented to the
Center in memory of Roland Krantz. On the Cultural
program, Ted Chapeau gave a talk on the coaxial cable,
Leigh Culley spoke on the A. D. L. and we had several
round table discussions and debates between fellow Alephs
and different clubs. Between football, softball, basketball,
and pingpong our athletic calendar was easily filled.
Under the guidance of our sweetheart, Miss Carol Kantor,
our social program was more than filled.
HARRY KATZ, JR.
As if these activities weren’t enough, we also had a
wonderful banquet and dance, attended a convention in
Miami, put on a play for B’nai B’rith Women, and of
course—the minstrel. A. Z. A. is also holding the annual
Florida Region Convention here this winter.
For assisting us in carrying out all phases of our
program so successfully this past year, many thanks are
due our advisors, Hyman Selber, Morris Chonin, and Allie
Goldstein.
