Cubbing, which is nationally accepted as the finest
organized activity for boys aged 8 to 11, completed a
satisfactory year at the Center. The quota of 32 boys was
almost filled and the activities were interesting to parents
as well as to the boys. Among the activities for the boys
were a trip to the Coca Cola Bottling Plant and a trip on
the St. Johns River aboard the City Fire Boat. There were
several outdoor picnics in which the entire Cub Pack
family participated.
The Den Mothers who are the real backbone of cub¬
bing did an excellent job under the leadership of Mrs.
Meyer Chepenik. Ted Freedman, who was Institutional
Representative and also acted as Cubmaster, due to the
illness of Ben Chepenik, takes this opportunity of thanking
all those mothers who served.
Irvin Rothstein handled the Chairmanship in his usual
efficient manner. Sam Rosenblum did an excellent job as
Treasurer, while Ben Lasris served as Secretary and in
addition worked with Dr. Carl Hackel in preparing various
Cub ceremonies.
Appreciation for cooperation goes to Sam Fishier who
acted as Assistant Cubmaster and Jack Blattner who con¬
tributed goodies for our picnics. A special vote of thanks
goes to Nat Cohen, an alumni of Cubbing, whose son
Larry Cohen graduated into scouting. He contributed
materially to the pleasure of our outings.
All the cubs did good jobs in completing their achieve¬
ments. Special commendation goes to George Glossman,
Stewart Goldberg, and Larry Rosenblum for winning prize
flashlights from the North Florida Council for selling a
large number of tickets to the Boy Scout Circus.
Applicants for membership in the Cub Scouts should
contact the Center Office or a member of the committee.
All interested parents doing their share and working with
the committee will make the new season an outstanding
one.
For the third year our Cubs have been under the
guidance and direction of its genial Cubmaster Ted Freed¬
man. Words of appreciation hardly suffice, but we offer
him our sincerest thanks.
