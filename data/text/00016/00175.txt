The Girl Scouts and Brownies, under the sponsorship of the Center Sisterhood,
have had a very busy and successful year.
The troops under their able leaders participated in all phases of the Girl Scout
program. The Girl Scouts did volunteer work at the Public Library, Cumberland
Nursery, hospitals, the Jewish Community Council and help with the Sisterhood
Bazaar. They were given the honor of being Color Guards at the city-wide Fun Day
at the Gator Bowl. They served as members of the City Council of the Girl Scouts.
Rene Slott was in charge of the Senior Girls Banquet held at the Center and Bar-
bara Ann Stillman was elected Treasurer of the Senior Girls of the Gateway Coun-
cil. The senior girls attended the Senior Scout weekend held at the Girl Scout camp.
All of the Girl Scout and Brownie troops took part in Girl Scout Sabbath and
served as hostesses after services. Arrangements were made by Mrs. Irving Kramer,
who was formerly Girl Scout Chairman, and her committee.
The Brownies were active in all neighborhood events. They presented an Is-
raeli Day at the Arlington Methodist Church. They danced at the city-wide Fun
Day at the Gator Bowl and took part in the "Massing of the Colors." The Brownies
also had a program of First Aid and learning about Scouting. They had a grand
closing party at the Birthday House at Storyland.
