Scouting
Boy Scout Troop 14
Boy Scout Troop 14 continued the scouting tradition at the
Jacksonville Jewish Center with another year of outdoor
adventure.
Living up to the scout motto “Do a Good Turn Daily,”
(at right, 1 to r) Andrew Vining, Eddie Norton, Kyle Vining,
and Adam Schwartz, along with Scout Master, Louis Schwartz,
volunteered to help clean up after Hurricane Katrina.
They spent four days at Salmon Scout Reservation in Gulf Port,
Mississippi, removing downed trees and brush.
As in the past, the scouts participated in campouts at the Riverbend
Camporee, Camp Thunder, in Georgia. Also, Cory Brenenson, Harrison
Safer, Adam Schwartz, Ben Gatewood and Max Rogozinski spent a
week at Camp Baden Powell Scout Reservation. They took part in shooting
sports: shotgun, rifle and black powder rifle and archery. They also worked
on advancement in astronomy, cooking, painting, home repair, weather,
citizenship and sports.
All the scouts made great strides in
advancement this year. Andrew Vining, son of
Elissa & Scott Vining, and Adam Schwartz,
son of Jeri & Louis Schwartz, earned Boy
Scouting’s highest honor, the rank of Eagle.
Among the rigorous requirements, these scouts
earned 21 merit badges, held leadership
positions and completed community projects.
Benjamin Hecht and Jacob Heclit, sons of Rose & Larry Hecht, were
awarded the Jewish Boy Scout religious emblem, the Ner Tamid.
Troop 14 looks forward to continuing its proud tradition of providing Jewish
youth with a Boy Scouting experience. If you enjoy camping, sports and a
variety of outdoor activities, and are between the ages of 11 and 18, we
invite you to attend our meetings at the Center on Thursday nights.
For more information, call me at 268-8717.
— Louis Schwartz, Scoutmaster
