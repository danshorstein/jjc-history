“It Will Be An Historic Weekend”
It is an event that seldom
occurs, with tradition
playing an essential part.
“We will recaptivate our
yesterdays and affirm our
tomorrows at this historic,
once-in-a-lifetime week¬
end,” said Rabbi David
Gaffney, spiritual leader of
the Jacksonville Jewish
Center.
“It will
be
an
historic
transition.

“There
are rare
an
unduplicated moments,
extraordinary occasions in
which all the tenses - past,
present and future - seem to
be compressed. We are at
such a moment.”
That moment is the
relocation of the Jewish
Center from Third and
Silver streets to its new
synagogue at 10101 San
Jose Blvd.
“Our weekend of transi¬
tion will give us the
opportunity to renew the old
and consecrate the new,”
Rabbi Gaffney said.
The old will be renewed in
a Shabbat service at 9 a.m.
today at the synagogue on
Third Street. A highlight of
the service * will be a
ceremony in which the
Torah Scrolls are removed
from the Ark in preparation
of their relocation to the new
synagogue.
“For almost 50 years the
Jewish Center at Third and
Silver has been an integral
part of our lives,” Rabbi
Gaffney said. “These
memories will forever be a
part of us.”
Traditionally, when a
synagogue moves to a new
location, the focal point of
everyone’s attention is the
transfer of the Holy Torah
Scrolls, the rabbi said.
At 1:30 p.m. Sunday, a
caravan of automobiles will
leave the center on Third
Street with the Torah
Scrolls, the Prophet Scrolls
and silver ornaments. The
procession will go to the
Beauclerc Shopping Center
on San Jose Boulevard in
Mandarin, where members
of the congregation will join
in a walking procession to
Crown Point Road.
An open flat-bed truck
will lead tne procession. On
the truck/, Rabbi Gaffney,
Cantor Abraham Marton
and Charles Mizrahi, Center
president, will each hold a
Torah Scroll, and members
of the congregation, also
carrying scrolls, will walk
behind the truck.
The procession will begin
at 2:30 p.m. and will reach
the new synagogue at
approximately 3:15 p.m.
When the congregation
has assembled at the
entrance of the new
synagogue, a ceremony will
be held for attaching a
mezuzah on the front door of
the synagogue. The
congregation will then enter
the synagogue and witness
the arrival of the scrolls and
their placement in the Ark.
The formal opening of the
new center will be heralded
by the sound of the Shofar.
Throughout the afternoon
there will be singing and
dancing, and at the
conclusion of the ceremony
a reception will be held for
the congregation.
