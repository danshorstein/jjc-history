GIRL SCOUTS
Daisy Troop 1367 earned all of our ten petals and two financial leaves. In addition, the girls earned all three
journey badges, so they were awarded with a special summit pin. The girls earned the “my faith, my promise”
religious pin. Some of our activities included a trip on a bank tour, a visit from a dog rescue group, sewing lessons,
planting a flower, playing money bingo, picking up trash, learning how to conserve our natural resources, exploring
the butterfly life cycle, visiting the preschool butterfly garden, writing letters to our Jewish pen pals in Colorado,
exchanging “swaps” with a Canadian and West Virginia troop, baking cupcakes as a thank you for the maintenance
staff, and first aid safety pin.
Brownie Troop 1371 had an awesome year! The Brownies earned a number of badges, including first aid,
money manager, nature hike, computer expert, and dancer! Our great year was capped off with a group performance
in the Martin J. Gottlieb Day School Talent Show, where the girls performed “It’s A Hard-Knock Life” from Annie.
The 4th and 5th grade girls that make up Junior Troop 1372 had a blast this year! We went on a “journey”
and discovered through role playing, imagination and prop box skits that girls can be anything they want to be.
Reach for the stars! We sold over 1000 boxes of Girl Scout cookies and used some of the money to buy gifts for
underprivileged children. We delivered the presents ourselves and shared the Chanukah story, songs and played
dreidel with them. We went back to the United Methodist Children’s Village and delivered 40 boxes of donated
cookies and shared the Passover story with them. We also went to River Garden and shared prop box skits with the
residents. Our year capped off with a fun trip to Adventure Landing!
We’re proud to be offering Girl Scout troops next year for kindergarten through 8th grade! For more information
on Girl Scouts, contact Rebecca Fixel at info@beccasbling.com.
