This past year, the Center’s Social Action Committee has been quite busy keeping our members aware
and involved in doing Mitzvot.
We continue to prepare and serve dinner the second Sunday of every other month to those at I. M.
Sulzbacher Center. We are fortunate to have a very dedicated corps of people who continue to do this.
Our monthly drive for the Teacher Supply Depot and World Relief Organization continue to be supported
by our members.
Food for Operation Isaiah and our magazine drives continue to get much support. We are able to supply
the food pantry at JFCS with needed items and have been able to provide magazines for a number of locations,
including River Garden and the Mandarin Senior Center.
Our Christmas project with Congregation Ahavath Chesed continues to grow in scope and in support.
In 2014, we delivered approximately 225 Meals On Wheels that had been cooked and packaged at the
Temple. More than 4,000 cookies had been donated and then delivered to First Responders and local Police
Substations. Fifteen volunteers cooked and served dinner to those who were spending their holiday at Ronald
McDonald House.
We continue to educate our members with many other ways to do social action activities. Each week
both the Mah Koreh and our Shabbat Handout is filled with ways to help others in our community and in the
world. “A Package from Home” for IDF soldiers, Hubbard House Thrift store, and Dignity U Wear are just a
few of the ways that we have reached out and helped others who otherwise would “go without”.
Our committee and congregation need continued support with all of our projects. We hope that YOU will
be one of our supporters!
