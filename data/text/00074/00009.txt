The Ark in the Main Sanctuary was built in 1982
at a local motel. An added bonus at the newly purchased
property was a two story, 13 room house, known as The
White House, situated on the front of the property that
was to be used as the indoor and office space for the
camp. Over the course of that year, the Olympic sized
swimming pool and bath house, two handball courts,
basketball courts, volley ball court, two baseball fields
and an archery range were created.
On June 25,1973, the Board of Directors held a special
meeting to approve the hiring of a contractor to begin
work on the new home for the Jacksonville Jewish Center.
President Charles Mizrahi signed the formal contract on
July 18th for the construction of the synagogue and school
complex with a target completion of September 1975. On
Sunday, September 16, 1973, 800 people attended the
ceremonial ground breaking.
The day had finally come. On Sunday, January 11,1976,
the Torahs were removed from the Ark at 3rd and Silver
and marched out of the building and onto an open flat
bed truck led by Rabbi Gaffney and Cantor Marton z’l.
Charles Mizrahi leads the caravan of cars to Mandarin.
Upon reaching the new campus, a ceremony was held to
attach a mezuzah to the door and place the Torahs in the
new ark. The sounds of the Shofar heralded the event’s
closing.
However after the celebration, came the reality
of a congregation constructing a $2.5 million dollar
facility. New President Sheldon Gendzier requested in
his yearbook message the additional participation of
the membership in the building campaign. “There are
financial problems. We have failed to finance the debt of
this new building. Even as I write this, a last campaign is
underway to bring our debt into manageable proportions.”
