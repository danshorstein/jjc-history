JEWISH MUSIC MONTH
Maxwell Street
Klezmer Band
On Sunday, January
11, the world renowned
Maxwell Street Klezmer
Band wowed our
community with an
evening of music and
dancing. Based out of
Chicago, the group
brought tons of energy
that excited over 300
synagogue members.
The music ranged
from Klezmer to Old
Standards to Israeli Folk
songs. One of the highlights of the evening
was watching the crowd mesmerized by
the artistry of every musician. The band
captivated the audience with their spirit and
sound. You could see the gratitude on the
faces of everyone in the room. This concert
was generously underwritten by Jeff and
Doris Goldstein, to which we owe a debt of
thanks!

Ani V’ata Shabbat Shira
Superbowl weekend was kicked off by a wonderful Shabbat of A Capella music featuring Ani V’ata, the premier
Jewish A Capella group of New York University. Hazzan Holzer, as a group alumnus, invited this large ensemble
of 18 members to visit Jacksonville for our second ever A Capella shabbaton. Our Setzer Youth and their families
enjoyed a ruach filled Friday night
service and dinner. The group split
into smaller clusters to help facilitate
our Shabbat morning davening
experiences throughout the building.
The trip culminated with our annual
Torah Fund experience honoring
Robin Morris, a music lover in her
own right. The group, in honoring
Robin, packed our social hall as they
shared their musical interpretations
of everything from the classics to
contemporary Israeli pop.
