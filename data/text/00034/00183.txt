The Jacksonville Jewish Community Council is a
viable, unifying force that encompasses the entire
Jewish community. Its structure reaches out to every
Jewish organization in this city and brings them to¬
gether in a policy-making group to help and aid the
Jewish people in our community, in our nation and
in Israel.
The Jewish Community Council was founded in
the mid 1930’s. The original aim of the Council was
a means of consolidating independent fund-raising
efforts by many outside agencies. It has grown and
flourished into a dedicated group of men and women
who have as their goal' the broadening of Jewish life
in many areas. This is obvious by the far-reaching
aspects of its committees.
In the .area of fund-raising, the annual Combined
Campaign has excelled in providing funds for the
United Jewish Appeal and local and national agencies.
Our Community Relations Committee has brought
together the Jewish and general communities through
the efforts of an active Speaker’s Bureau, significant
mailings of pertinent materials, and an Interfaith
pilgrimage to Israel in conjunction with the Main
Street Baptist Church to be held in November, 1975.
An Educational Series throughout the year fea¬
tures prominent speakers in all areas of Jewish life
and concern.

Through the Community Programming Commit¬
tee, programs are planned for Israel Independence Day
as well as for other events during the year.
For the first time in 1975, the Council will spon¬
sor a Sunday Night Spotlight Series with four out¬
standing performers and speakers.
A Young Leadership Division has educational
meetings,1 and encourages future development and
leadership in the community.
An active year-round Women’s Division has been
a vital area of the Council’s program, featuring edu¬
cation as well as a successful fund-raising Campaign.
A Shalom Committee has been organized to meet and
greet newcomers to Jacksonville.
The Council also keeps a calendar for the Jewish
community for all organizations, which helps to avoid
conflict of activities.
Last, but not least, the aim and goal of the
Council is to extend the horizon of Jewish identity
and to intensify a desire for Jewish education to a
more significant depth on a community wide basis. We
endeavor to eliminate duplication, fragmantation and
its consequences. A united effort in this particular
field benefits all segments of the community.
