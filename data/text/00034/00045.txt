“A New Heart and a New Spirit” from the 18th
chapter of the Book of Ezekiel, is our theme for a
second year. The hope, the comfort, and the consola¬
tion that is contained in the message of this theme is
still very much needed in this troubled era.
The New Spirit promises that it will be a stead¬
fast one. It is this spiritual regeneration that is essen¬
tial in the contributing challenge of educating EVERY
member of the Jewish family, through the woman
who is OUR member.
We are told that Ezekiel regarded it as his mis¬
sion to educate individuals both as committed Jews
personally and as responsible members of the com¬
munity. Our call is for renewed Spirit, much as the
“Spirit of ’76” embodied the determined beliefs of the
founders of the United States of America. They worked
for their beliefs with steadfast determination, as we
must do in these equally challenging times.
In our education programs for the year; they will
help us to focus on the spiritual regeneration which is
the urgent need of our contemporary society.
