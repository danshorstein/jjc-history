Cub Scouting, as an introduction to the Boy
Scouts, continues to be one of the outstanding youth
programs offered at the Center. Boys between the ages
of 8 and 10 find many new and challenging experiences
in Cubbing.
The Cubs in each Den become a close unit as
they work together to develop new hobbies, take field
trips, explore nature and work on craft projects and
skits. The annual Rocket and Pinewood Derbies give
the boys the opportunity to design their own rockets
and cars and enter them in competitive racing.
In honor of Boy Scout Week the Cubs participated
in the late Friday Sabbath Services and the annual
Blue and Gold Banquet. To close a most successful
year all Cubs and their families gathered at the Center
Southside Site for an evening of swimming, sports,
games and cooking over an open fire.
The highlight of the year was the presentation
of the Larry Hacker Award to the Cub scoring the
highest in attendance at Pack Meetings, achievements
and good will. The recepient for 1975, a Cub most
deserving of this yearly award, was Andrew Ringel.
The name of the winner is recorded on a perpetual
plaque that remains at the Center and a smaller plaque
is given to the Cub to keep. The Committee extends
thanks.to the Donor who made the perpetuation of this
award possible.
