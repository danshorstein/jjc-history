This year has been so active for Troop 162. The
girls presented our sponsorship certificate at a Sister¬
hood meeting. They marched in the Veterans’ Day
Parade and had a Campfire with Troop 330 at Camp
Chowenwaw. A fun learning experience was the joint
Skills Day we had with the Center Boy Scouts.
Did you know that we sold more cookies per girl
than any other troup in the Lake-Man-Jose area?
The cookie booth at Lakewood did help. For Inter¬
national Day at Phillips Mall, the girls sewed their
own costumes, decoupaged bottles, and performed a
dance to represent Switzerland. They had a ball at the
neighborhood Games Day competing on teams made
up of all neighborhood girls. Our girls even got to
try their hand at sand art with Sisterhood help.
Have I left things out? Probably. The girls and
I would like to thank the mothers who pitched in to
help on various projects.
What are our plans for next year? Well, for start¬
ers, there’s a rescheduled neighborhood campout. We’d
like to see more participation of the parents so all
the girls could gain from their “expertise.” We’ll repeat
some of the fun things of last year and we’ll try to
explore many new and exciting interests.
Wendy Honigman, Leader
