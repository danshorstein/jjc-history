U.S.Y. Regional Board Meeting November 16, 1952
What has this year been, in retrospect to the past
years of U.S.Y.? Why open with a question? Maybe
because this is the question of many parents and of
our own contemporaries as well.
The answer? Quite simple — We have accomplish¬
ed one main factor: Remember that so well known
phrase, “We, not me.” This is what I’ve tried to ac¬
complish, and it has finally become a reality. This is
what I feel is the main objective of the presidency, to
coordinate with his/her board, in order that those
people will extend the policy of “We not me” to the
overall membership. It was difficult for me, being in¬
different to grasp this concept at first, and still is, but
with the help of our advisors who were constantly
saying, “You’ll have no club if you intend on doing
it on your own, Karen.” How true it all was.
One thing that our group has realized is the need
for one another. You can’t do anything alone now¬
adays. You need to depend on others, which really is
not all that bad. There is a well know proverb, “A1
tifrosh minha-tzibor” — Don’t separate yourself from
the community.” Basically, we have incorporated this
theme as well to many of our activities. For example,
service to the Center on the High Holidays, River
Garden services, social action at River Garden, setting
up for Sukkot and various other programs and social
action projects, as well as showing a great amount of
courtesy to other synagogues and youth groups.
