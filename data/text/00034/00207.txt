MEYER LEIBOWITZ POST #199
The Jewish War Veterans this year celebrate the
80th year of their founding. This veterans organization
is the first so founded and is the only Jewish organi¬
zation authorized to speak before the Congress of
the United States.
In addition to its many other activities, Israel is
the number one concern of J.W.V. working for peace
and security in the Middle East. No organization works
harder in the halls of Congress towards this end.
The 3 million Soviet Jews, separated from their
natural homeland, receive constant attention and sup¬
port from all J.W.V. members; we are active at the
national as well as the community planning and imple¬
mentation levels.
One local project this past year has been an
attempt to locate and mark all graves in Jacksonville,
of deceased Jewish veterans, regardless of affiliation.
This worthwhile project has resulted in a new attempt
by the local post to establish a new marker for all
military personnel that would be a permanent and
decorative attachment to the headstone for easy
identification. Hopefully this will be adopted on a
national level.
All persons who know of a Jewish War Veteran
cemetary unlocated are requested to contact any of
the officers of post 199.
