Shabbat Shekalim the Sabbath when every adult Jew in
the days of the Temple was reminded to pay his poll tax of
a half a shekel on the first day of Adar. In our day, a shekel
is paid by every adult Jew, entitling him to vote for delegates
to the Zionist Congress.
Sabbath Zakhor. On this S'abbath before the Festival of
Purim, a special portion of the Torah is read beginning with
the word “Zakhor”—“Remember Amaelk.” Haman is presum¬
ed to have been an Amalekite.

Taanit Ester commemorates the day when Queen Esther and
the Jews of Persia fasted and prayed that God would save
them from their threatened annihilation plotted by Haman.
Purim commemorates the day when, through the interven¬
tion of Mordecai and Queen Esther, the Jews of Persia nar¬
rowly escaped being victims of Haman’s plot to exterminate
them.
Shabbat Par ah. A special portion of the Bible concerning
the ashes of the red heifer is read, reminding us of the ritual
of purification of our ancestors in preparing for the Passover
Festival.
Shabbat Hadodesh. A special portion of the Bible is read,
emphasizing the significance of the month of Nisan in which
Israel’s great deliverance occured.
