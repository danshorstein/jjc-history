Shabbat Hazon this S'abbath which precedes Tisha B’Ab
derives its name from the first words of the Haftoran “Hazon”
—the vision of Isaiah, which fortells the destruction of
Jerusalem.
Tisha B’Ab (ninth day of Ab) is a fast day which marks
the destruction of the first Temple in Jerusalem by the Baby¬
lonians (586 B.C.E.) and also of the second Temple by the
Romans (70 B.C.E.).
Shabbat Nahamu (Sabbath of Consolation), derives its name
from the first words of the Haftorah “Nahamu, nahamu,
Comfort ye, My People.”
