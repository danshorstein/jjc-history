Rosh Hodesh is the term for the beginning of a new month.
As a Jewish year is based on the lunar calendar, the beginning
of the new month coincides with the new moon. When a
month has thirty days, the last is considered the first of the
two days which constitute Rosh Hodesh of the month following.
