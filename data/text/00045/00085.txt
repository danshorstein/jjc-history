Jewish families today look to the synagogue as the center for Jewish activity: religious, education, social and recreational. A
major goal of the synagogue and its school is the education of youth with a firm base of knowledge, Jewish identification of youth, a
sense of pride in their heritage, and a commitment to Judaism. The goal of Kadima is to bring about the internalization of Jewish
values, attitudes and behavior patterns by supplementing the child’s formal religious education with informal Jewish learning
experiences.
Charles Palot
