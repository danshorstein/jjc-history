MRS. SAMUEL J. THIER
Sisterhood looks back with pride to its accomplish¬
ments of the past year. Our membership, due to the val¬
iant efforts of our Membership Committee, now stands
at the highest mark in our history. However, we are not
only striving to improve our position, but to consolidate
all the gains made in the past.
It has always been our firm belief that in order to
get the full cooperation of our membership, they should
be brought to realize what Sisterhood really stands for
and that fund-raising is only incidental to the fulfillment
of our over-all plan.
Why all these projects? Donor Affair, Funds for
Youth Dance, Year Book, Rummage Sale, etc. For in¬
stance, the funds realized from our solicitation of adver¬
tisements for the Center Year Book, our largest source
of revenue, are used primarily for the rebuilding of the
Synagogue. Our youth projects include financial assist¬
ance for the Center Youth League, such as defraying ex¬
penses for delegates to national conventions, leadership
training institutes and regional conventions. What this
has done for our teen agers is amply illustrated by the
favorable comments received after our local C.Y.L. were
hosts to the Southeast Regional Convention of United
Synagogue Youth in February. It was surely a thrilling
sight to witness hundreds of youngsters participating in
a Jewish activity in our Center.
