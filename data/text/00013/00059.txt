The Lt. Meyer Leibovitz Post 199, Jewish War Veter¬
ans, was organized in Jacksonville, in 1939 and was
known at that time as Jacksonville Post 199. Its name
was changed to Lt. Meyer Leibovitz Post in 1946, after
Lt. Meyer Leibovitz, of this city, who gave his all for his
country.
The Post has always followed a course of giving serv¬
ice to our fellow-men, regardless of race, creed, or color,
service to our country and all its citizens and service to
the cause of peace, of brotherhood, and of humanity.
Many people ask why join the Jewish War Veterans:
One answer amongst many is, that JWV’s speak out
against bigotry, prejudice and totalitarianism; it devotes
its principal energies to positive, creative efforts for the
preservation and advancement of the freedoms of oppor¬
tunities that are vital to the security and well being of
America, and the Jewish people. It has demonstrated its
capacity for useful service to the Country and to Jewry
since its formation by Jewish Veterans of the Civil War,
fifty-eight years ago; Its members understand the needs
of service men, and veterans and are best situated to de¬
tect minority inequalties therein.
Jews have fought, bled and died in every war of our
republic. We seek no special pleading for ourselves as
veterans; However, when needed by our fellow Jews, we
are available, for we have earned the right to fight on
their behalf to preserve our constitutional privileges as
Americans, and JWV Posts have so done; Nor must we
ever forget our obligation to fight for these same privil¬
eges for non-Jews as well. JWV gives its members an
opportunity for all, young or old, to train in its ranks in
the ever rising echelons for useful citizenship and leader¬
ship on local and national levels, to watch and help where
needed in man’s fight against bigotry.
The above principles are the aims and principles of
your JWV Post, here, and our course of action is directed
towards those ends.
