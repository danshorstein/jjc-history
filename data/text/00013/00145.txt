HAROLD HAIMOWITZ
Y. P. L. is the link between the young people and the
Jacksonville Jewish Center, bringing to the young adults
of our community, cultural, religious and social activities.
This year Y.P.L. resumed its closer affiliation with
the national Young People’s League of the United Syna¬
gogue of America. Two delegates attended the National
Convention held in Atlantic City, N.J. The organization
selected two of its outstanding leaders, Barbara Hirsh¬
field and Sol Proctor. These delegates returned from the
convention and imparted their enthusiasm to the group.
Y.P.L. continually opens its doors to the Jewish Ser¬
vice men and women stationed nearby. Our Tuesday
evening meetings are often frequented by large groups
of service personnel.
Under the guidance of Rabbi Tofield, a cultural-edu¬
cational program is planned for the coming year, which
promises to be another successful one for Y.P.L. The
aim of Y.P.L. is to bring inspiration to the youth of our
community and encourage them to participate in the ac¬
tivities of the Jacksonville Jewish Center.
