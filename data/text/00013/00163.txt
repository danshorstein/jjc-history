MISS MYRA THIER
WAYNE SIEGEL
During its fifth year of organized activity in Jacksonville, the Center Youth
League has continued along the important lines of cultural, social, and religious
development. We have not only doubled our membership, but have instilled
into our members the definite aims and objectives of National U.S.Y. (United
Synagogue Youth).
Many important projects were completed, of which the Southeast Regional
Convention was outstanding. We continued our interesting by-weekly post-
Confirmation Study Group which proved very informative.
Other projects included a Mid-Winter Dance in December and our Second
Annual Spring Dance.
The financial assistance of the Center Sisterhood, Center Men’s Club and
the Palot Memorial Fund enabled us to send delegates to the Leadership Train¬
ing Institute in Hendersonville, North Carolina, held during the Spring of
1953, to the National U.S.Y. Convention in Atlantic City last December, and
to our Regional Board Meeting in Atlanta, Georgia. We of the Center Youth
League are indeed grateful to these organizations and to the Center Youth Ac¬
tivities Committee for their wonderful cooperation.
The Center Youth League looks back upon the past year as a very success¬
ful one and considers it a challenge for the future. With the help and guidance
of Rabbi Tofield and our Advisor, Miss Myra Thier, we of C.Y.L. look for¬
ward to the coming year eagerly.
