Girl Scout Troop 29 and Brownie Troops 25, 197 and 212
completed another most successful year under the capable leadership
of Mrs. Irving Kramer, Chairman of the Center Sisterhood Girl
Scout Committee. These troops participated in many civic, religious
and outdoor activities. Trips to various nearby places of interest were
taken. Outdoor cooking, hiking and overnight camping were made
possible through the proceeds of the annual Cookie Sale.
Favors and napkins were made for the trays at the County
Hospital. Clothes were collected and sent to Israel and also to
Brewster and County Hospitals. The Girl Scouts took part in a
city-wide art exhibit. All troops participated in Girl Scout Sab¬
bath and Boy Scout Sabbath Services, Thinking Day and Court of
Awards. This year’s arts and crafts program included the making
of aprons, bun warmers, pot holders, wire-craft, chalk drawing and
flower planters. Corsages for Mother’s Day were also made.
The annual luncheon climaxed the most successful year. Mrs.
Charles Norton was the guest speaker and Miss B. Wimpee, Mrs. B.
Y. Abel and Rabbi Tofield were among the guests present. Mothers
of Girl Scouts and Brownies, under the direction of Mrs. Kramer
and Mrs. Robert L. Block were in charge of the luncheon.
