We are proud to present on this page the accomplish¬
ments of our Building Program during the past year. In
the last two editions of the Year Book our Membership
was promised a graphic demonstration that continued sup¬
port of the Building Fund can achieve. Here it is.
With grateful hearts and gratitude to the Almighty we
may view not only the photographs that appear on this
page but also experience the heightened inspiration to
worship when we come to the Synagogue.
In the upper center we see the remodeled Sanctuary.
Structural changes of windows and ceiling designs, pulpit
area, oak-paneled walls and modern lighting fixtures are
quite evident. Air-conditioning and the remodeled bal¬
cony, a new seating arrangement in radius, upholstered
seats can only be appreciated on a visit to the Synagogue
and eludes photographic portrayal.
Flanking this picture on both sides are photographs of
the first wedding held in the renovated Sanctuary. On the
left is a view from the balcony, and on the right, one
taken from the center aisle from floor level, of the Setzer-
Cohen wedding on July 11th, 1954.
Two "before and after" scenes appear in the lower
position. On the left is a photograph published in the
Jacksonville Journal of December 12th, showing the scaf¬
folding used in rebuilding the windows and the front of
the building before the coat of stucco was applied. The
picture on the right appeared in the same newspaper on
July 12th. (Photograph by courtesy of Ephrem). The
small insert gives our two buildings, The Activities Build¬
ing in the foreground and Synagogue alongside, as seen
from the park looking East.
