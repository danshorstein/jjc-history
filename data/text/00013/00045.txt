Young Judaea has in Jacksonville 7 Clubs for boys and girls from
the ages of 9 through 16. The Senior Clubs meet weekly on Wednesday
evenings at 7:30 o’clock, either in the Center or at homes of members;
Junior Clubs meet in the Center every other Sunday afternoon at 2 P.M.
All activities center around the four-fold program, which consists
of: Jewish living, group activities, American affairs and Israel and Zion¬
ism.
Outstanding affairs during the past year consisted of: Sabbath Serv¬
ices sponsored by Judaea at the Jewish Center during December; Senior
Conclave in Daytona Beach in December; Program for joint Zionist and
Hadassah meeting at the Temple in January; Israeli Fair to raise funds
for the Tsofim held at River Garden Home in April; and concluded with
the Bikkurim Festival in the Jewish Center, which climaxed the year of
working on the Point System with a banquet honoring Judaean mem¬
bers.
Camp Judaea for 10-12 year olds, was sponsored for the first time
this year at Blue Star in Hendersonville, N.C. Also held there in June
was Camp and Convention for Senior Judaeans and a Leaders’ Institute.
Jacksonville sent a delegation to the National Judaean Camp, Tel Yehudah
in Barryville, N.Y. during August and also one delegate to Israel on the
Summer-In-Israel program.
Young Judaea is sponsored jointly by Hadassah and the Zionist Or¬
ganization of America, under the direction of the local Youth Commis¬
sion, headed by Dorothy Marx, Chairman, and Mrs. Irving Brukner, Di¬
rector. Young Judaea is a member of the Jacksonville Jewish Community
Council.
