The Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah boasts a mem¬
bership of over 750. Basically, membership in Hadassah
is an act of faith. It represents for the American Jewish
woman not only a concrete expression of a positive Ju¬
daism, but also a deep belief in the program and objec¬
tives of Hadassah.
Every member is a part owner in Israel—of a vast
network of hospitals, medical and nurses schools, health
welfare stations and vocational training schools. She is a
director of the Youth Aliyah program, and may feel
proud of her efforts, through the Jewish National Fund,
of redeeming and building the land in Israel.
Through our package fund drive and an allocation
to Youth Aliyah from our Jewish Community Council our
local unit raises its quota to maintain these projects.
This season Hadassah will take an active part in the
Tercentenary together with other organizations in the
city.
The local chapter claims one large day group and
one evening group.
