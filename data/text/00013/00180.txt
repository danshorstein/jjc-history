BUDDY SHORSTEIN
The Esquire Club is happy to announce that 1954 was
a most successful year for the organization, under the
capable leadership of its president, Buddy Shorstein.
However, much credit is due to Richard Hollins for de¬
voting so much of his time and effort to the affairs of
the group.
Without the cooperation of all the officers and mem¬
bers, the "Esquires” could not have grown to the out¬
standing organization that it is today. Credit is due also
to Miss Carol Chepenik, Mascot, who assisted so gracious¬
ly in all of the Club’s functions.
This year the Esquires contributed to the Charles H.
Palot Memorial Fund and to the Center Building Fund.
One of the highlights of the season was the Esquire Sab¬
bath held at the Jewish Center.
Our entire membership was saddened by the tragic
death of Allan Bernstein, who was an active member and
served as treasurer of Esquire. We mourn his passing
and will always cherish his memory.
