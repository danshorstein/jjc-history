MRS. MORTON B. TURBOW
The National Council of Jewish Women, organized
in 1893, is one of the oldest women’s organizations in
America. It now has 250 Sections throughout the country
with an enrollment of over 100,000 members. Council, in
the spirit of Judaism, is dedicated to furthering human
welfare in the Jewish and general communities, locally,
nationally, and internationally. Through an integrated
program of education, service, and social action, it edu¬
cates the individual and the community toward the demo¬
cratic way of life.
Council’s overseas projects are threefold: the support
of the School of Education of Hebrew University in Jeru¬
salem; maintenance of graduate scholarships to students
from Israel and European countries for study in American
universities; shipments of clothing, play and study mater¬
ial to Jewish orphanages in the Middle East.
Local services include Council’s Medical Loan Shelf,
Golden Age Club, sewing for the less fortunate, Service
to Foreign Born, and parties for hospitalized servicemen
at the Naval Air Station.
