MRS. IKE WITTEN
The beautiful River Garden Hebrew Home on the
banks of the St. Johns River has been a source of pleasure
and inspiration to the entire community. It is a source of
pride to all of us that all the functions and the care of the
residents have continued to be accomplished in a very
satisfactory and homelike atmosphere.
In evaluating the services rendered by us we are ever
mindful of the aims of our organization to meet the needs
of the residents as well as those of the Home. The needs
vary from sponsorship of a hobby program—the provide
ing of linens by our linen shower—a new movie projec¬
tor—refurnishing the sun parlor—as well as the main¬
tenance of the new summer pavillion, where picnics and
outdoor suppers are held throughout the summer.
The Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society has always ac¬
cepted the responsibility of providing the Home with
these special needs which could not be met through its
operating budget.
The financing of our many projects has been made
possible through the wonderful cooperation of our fund¬
raising committees. They consist of our napkin and sta¬
tionary project—the annual donors fund—and the yearly
Tombola bazaar, conducted both in and out of the city,
as well as our annual dues collection. This year all mon¬
ices raised from the various funds will give us a good start
towards the furnishing of the proposed new infirmary.
We extend our deepest appreciations and heartfelt
thanks to all the many friends of the River Garden Home
and Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society for their loyal and
devoted efforts to our dedicated cause.
