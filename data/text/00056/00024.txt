Simhat Torah Honorees
The High Holiday season reaches its joyous climax on Simhat Torah, the last day of the festival
of Sukkot. Because of our perennial love of the Torah, it has been referred to as Israel’s “bride”. On
Simhat Torah, we bring to a close the annual cycle of the reading of the Torah and immediately begin
anew. An ancient tradition evolved of honoring worthy men by calling them to the Torah for the last
reading, Hatan Torah, and the new beginning portion, Hatan Bereshit. In our synagogue tradition,
we also honor the contributions made by a “Woman of Valor”.

Upon moving to Jacksonville,
Sonny and Joan Waitz joined the
Center in 1964. They enrolled their
children in the Solomon Schechter Day
School and the Afternoon Hebrew
School and began a “career” of
synagogue volunteerism, which they
passed on, Tdor v’dor, to their
children.
Sonny is a regular at the morning
minyans and served as a member of
the Board of Directors. With degrees
in Microbiology, Civil Engineering
and a Masters Degree in Sanitary
Chemistry, Sonny offers not only his
time, but also his highly qualified
assistance to enhance the physical
surroundings of our community’s
Jewish institutions — the synagogues,
JCA and River Garden. He continues
to work for the improvement of our
cemetery, as well as the upgrading of
the irrigation system on the Center site.
Sonny shared this honor with his
wife Joan; mother, Bella; his children
Linda and Philip Bloom, Risa Schneider,
Ira Waitz; and grandchildren Lauren
and Sasha Bloom and Jodi Schneider.
The Center expresses its gratitude with
this honor.

Center By-Laws. He served as Secretary

devoted Chairman of the Religious
Committee since 1990. Ed is proud of
his achievements as leader in a search
for a cantor for our synagogue.
The Jacksonville Jewish Center is
truly a central part of the Tannen
Family. Ed often leads Shabbat services
and has attended Sunday morning
minyan for 16 years. Sons Sam and
Jonathan have grown up in our
schools, youth activities and camp.
Their family belongs to an active
Center Havurah.
It is with pride and appreciation
that the Center honored Edward
Tannen as Hatan Bereshit. Ed, Millie,
Sam and Jonathan are a family who
demonstrate Jewish living in a
beautiful and inspiring light.
Wendy Honigman
exemplifies the
Woman of Valor.
She is a social
activist, champion
of Jewish causes
and is committed
to the highest
ideals of Judaism.
Among the issues for which Wendy is
a vocal advocate are women’s rights
in the synagogue, accurate reporting
of Israeli soldiers missing in action and
the plight of Jews still living in the
former Soviet Union.
Wendy’s concern for the welfare
of others is rooted in Hillel’s teachings
of Pirkey Avot, “separate not yourself
from the community”. Wendy is a
founder of the Hevra Kaddisha, the
current president of the Mitzvah
Chapter of ARMDI, the Jewish rescue
organization, and active at River
Garden. Wendy and Joe Honigman are
recognized for their hospitality,
hosting visitors to the Center over
Shabbat and holidays.
The Center has been the
beneficiary of years of Honigman
involvement. Joe is a past president of
the Center. Wendy is a past president
of the Center Sisterhood, as well as
chair of several committees at the
Branch level. She has served on the
Board of Directors as Recording
Secretary, on the Youth Commission,
PTA, Adult Education and Religious
Committees.
We honored Wendy for her
steadfast loyalty to her synagogue,
family, friends and those in need.
Mazel Tov to Wendy’s husband, Joe,
daughters, Andrea and Michele, and
their families, who shared this special
honor with her.
