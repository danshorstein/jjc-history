Solomon Schechter Day School
This fall, Solomon Schechter Day School opened its doors with
the second highest enrollment increase since its beginning 36 years ago.
One reason for the growing numbers is the marked improvement
Schechter has shown in retaining students. At the same time, the
enrollment base has continued to grow by adding an additional
kindergarten class for Fall 1997.
Willard M. Kennedy
Principal
Quality of education is our hallmark. I am pleased to note an
overall confidence in the mission of the school and in its ability to make
that mission a reality.
After much evaluation and deliberation, over the last few years, our Judaica faculty endorsed the adoption
of a new Hebrew curriculum which begins with the TaL Am program. This curriculum is a scoped and sequenced
language program. In preparation for this undertaking, members of our staff have received intensive in-service
education from the Jewish Education Council of Montreal.
We have planted the seeds of family education at Schechter with the Family University program. The roots
are growing and the plant is blooming, but it is not a full-fledged tree yet.
Another area of successful parent participation is our hard-working PTA. The school is truly enriched by all
the parent volunteers who provide many support services during the school year. A special thanks is due the PTA
for its generous gift which enabled us to computerize the Henry Kramer Library and
Resource Center and expand its services to our student population.
It is my hope that people will continue to feel free to come and visit me, to share
with me their concerns, hopes and dreams for their children. I look back on a year of
successes for individuals and for the school as a whole. I look ahead to a year that
will be equally successful, and at the same time filled with challenges.
School Secretary
Evelyn Borstein
