Debbie Friedman
Sunday, October 27, 1996
Debbie Friedman, internationally
acclaimed singer and songwriter
who pioneered the development of
contemporary Jewish music, kicked
off the 1996-97 Performing Artist
Series. Folk-singer extraordinaire,
Friedman’s music presented a
jolting charge to our Jewish
batteries. Her music and her message was a mixture of spirituality,
intelligence, sensitivity, and soul. In some circles Friedman has come to
be known as the “high priestess of Jewish healing”. Often compared to
the late Rabbi Shlomo Carlbach, she was enthusiastically embraced by
the entire audience, young and old alike.
Wasting Time With
Harry Davidowitz:
The Musical Journey of a Jewish Soul
Sunday, March 30, 1997
Dani Maseng’s one-man show
about life with, and after, his
grandfather was a most wonderful,
richly detailed and happiness-
inducing performance for all ages.
Performing in a variety of languages,
Maseng mixed music with humorous, yet sometimes painful anecdotes.
His melodic voice took the audience through his personal voyage from
Shabbat dinners with his grandparents, to a beatnik camp in Elat, to 14
years with a Zen teacher and back to Judaism. Dani Maseng was incredibly
touching and spiritually enlightening.
The Parvarim
Sunday, May 11,1997
Yossi Hury and Oro Harpaz are
Israel’s most popular folk duo.
Their distinctive and highly
polished repertoire of traditional
and contemporary songs has won
them thousands of admirers from
all over the world. Celebrating
thirty years after embarking on the
Israeli music scene, the Parvarim are still one of the most sought after
and in demand figures in the entertainment world. Their concert, which
included songs in Hebrew, Yiddish, Ladino and English, was an exciting
experience, which encouraged the audience to participate in the show.
