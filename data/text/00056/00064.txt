Men’s Club New American B’nai Mitzvah
June27 and 28,1997/23 Sivan 5757
Following the success of
last year’s groundbreaking
educational program for New
American adults, a second
class began studying early in
the Fall of 1996. Of that group,
four dedicated students
completed the nine month
course of classes held every
Sunday morning from 10 a.m.
to noon. The curriculum which
was outlined by Rabbi David
Gaffney, included Flebrew
literacy, as well as the study of
festivals and holidays, rituals
and customs of life cycle
events, laws and mitzvot,
synagogue-skills and Jewish
history, and was taught by
Center professionals and learned
laity.
Boris and Tamara
Kufman, husband and wife,
their sister-in-law Svetlana
Kufman, and Israel Turovsky
participated fully in Shabbat
services, Friday evening and Saturday morning. They were
joined by members of last year’s class, who found the
experience almost as rewarding as their own ceremony,
and a lot more relaxing!
The congregation was
deeply moved by the personal
accounts of Jewish life in the
former Soviet Union, as related
by each of the four candidates.
Their warmth and humor was
engaging, and their sincere
determination to live a Jewish
life was inspiring.
The gala reception
Saturday night, in honor of this
joyous occasion, was an
exuberant celebration with
non-stop dancing to a variety
of Jewish music provided by
Kavvanah, Jacksonville’s
authentic klezmer band. A
sumptuous buffet featured a
special variety of Russian
delicacies.
Again this year, the
Jacksonville Jewish Federation
and the Simon Selber Fund of
Jewish Family and Community
Services presented the B’nai Mitzvah celebrants with
gifts to enhance their Jewish observance.
The entire weekend was made possible by a long list
of generous sponsors and through the efforts of
hardworking committees.
