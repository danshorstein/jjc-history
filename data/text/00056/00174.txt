Cantor Heller’s Message
Rosh Hashanah — the start of a
New Year — what an appropriate time
to make a new beginning! It is the time
when we reflect on our lives and thank
the Almighty for our blessings, ask
forgiveness for our sins, and pray that
He grant us a new year of health,
happiness and prosperity.
It is with those thoughts that I
approach my first Rosh Hashanah in
the United States of America, and more
importantly, at the Jacksonville Jewish
Center. I have been blessed to realize
my aspirations of bringing me and my
wonderful family to this warm community. This is a
congregation that has a deep appreciation for Jewish
values and traditions, a congregation that places a
premium on teaching its children the importance of a
Jewish lifestyle.
The greatest challenge that faces Jewish parents
and educators can be summed up by these words of the
Shema: “... v’shinantam Vvanecha, v’dibartah bam...
Thou shalt teach them diligently unto thy children.”
Nothing is more gratifying to me
than to be part of the team teaching
the leaders of tomorrow — your
wonderful children.
Together, let us join in song
and prayer over the High
Holidays to set the foundation of
a spiritual new beginning for the
year ahead, as well as those to
come.
I look forward to meeting
many more members of this
unique congregation during the
months to follow. On behalf of me and my family, I
would like to take this opportunity to wish each and
everyone of you a Shanah Tovah Umetukah. May we
all be inscribed for a year blessed with good health,
happiness and prosperity.
Best wishes,
Cantor Aron Heller
