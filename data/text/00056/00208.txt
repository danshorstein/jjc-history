Happy 50th Birthday Israel
50 years seen through the eyes of our congregation
a time line researched by Wendy Honigman
continued from page 5
1972: June: JJC awarded Cantor Abraham and Pearl Marton a trip to celebrate 25 years at the Jacksonville Jewish Center. In
Munich, massacre of Israeli athletes shook the Olympics. Phyllis Garbose was impressed with the Kennedy Memorial on her first
trip to Israel. Saralee Newman went on Hadassah Year Course and met Iri Kassel. Dina Saroff (Mirsky) attended B’nai B’rith Israel
Summer Institute, worked on Moshav Mahalal where Moshe Dayan grew up. Earl Berman was counselor for AZYF tour. Kate
Robbins toured Israel with Hadassah. Rabbi David Gaffney was installed.
1973: Ben-Gurion died at Sede Boker. Summer: Sheila Jacobson, Sam, Erica and Janet visited Sheila’s sister on Kibbutz Bet
HaEmek and worked in the laundry; Edith Gendzier (Horovitz) went to Ramah in Israel; Robin Schechtman (Shorstein) went on USY
Pilgrimage. 6 October: Wendy Brezen was walking to the Wall when she heard planes, sirens and Reshet Bet from radios. Jack Becker
toured Army Encampments after Yom Kippor War. 25th Anniversary of Israel and ORT in Israel. Ruth Becker Robins toured. ORT Israel
Scholarship Award presented to Jack and Sue Becker. Robin Saliman (Morris) studied at Hebrew University.
1974: Architects plans for new synagogue were presented. Groundbreaking ceremony took place in Fall. Earl Berman
studied at Yeshiva Or Samayach; Martin Berman was a rabbinical student at Jewish Theological Seminary in Israel. Janet Goldstein
participated in Young Judaea Year Course. Summer: Rabbi Gaffney led congregational tour to Israel. Michael Shorstein and Lenny
Maiman went on USY Pilgrimage; Jeff Morris finished backpacking in Italy and came to Isreal for week with Robin Saliman
(Morris) as tour guide; Beverly and Bill Goldstein visited Mt Scopus facilities of Young Judaea Year Course.
1975: New building erected; building fund campaign offered tributes and memorials. Earl Berman made aliyah with his
bride. Chana, while serving in the IDF, Wendy Brezen made contact with family - Holocaust survivors who were lucky to get exit
visas from the Soviet Union. Sheila Jacobson started the tradition of visiting her sister every 2 years. July - August: Jeff Goldstein
did a clinical clerkship at Hadassah Hospital working under Dr Eli Davis. Janice Goodman met Steven Meisel on Kibbutz Yiron.
October: ZOA/JNF Banquet in honor of Albert Meyerhoff planted a Grove at Lehav.
1976: January - November: Janet Goldstein volunteered thru Sherut L’Am in the development town of Yaracham. Summer:
Louise Berman attended High School in Israel and the bris of nephew Eleazer Berman; Lynn Goldstein took part in BBYO Israel
Summer Institute. October: Ricki Gross made aliyah. November: ZOA/JNF Banquet in memory of Leo Meizlik planted a Grove at
Lahav. December: Dr Edwin and Harriet Safer, children Steven and Marcia, and mother Mary Safer went on a Hadassah tour.
