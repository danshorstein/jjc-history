National Council of Jewish Women is an organiza¬
tion which, in the spirit of Judaism, is dedicated to
furthering human welfare in the Jewish and general
communities, locally, nationally, and internationally.
Through an integrated program of education, service,
and social action, it provides essential services and
stimulates and educates the individual and the com¬
munity toward their responsibilities in advancing
human welfare and the democratic way of life.
Our Community service projects include such
varied activities as:
1. Pre-School Vision Screening.
2. Supplying sick-room equipment to the indigent of
our community by our Medical Loan Shelf.
3. Supervising the sewing of layettes by residents of
River Garden for distribution by G.J.E.O. to needy
mothers.
4. Tutorial help in three Southside Elementary schools
by our Volunteers in Public Schools Program.
5. Coordinating and presenting the Mental Health
Forum in March each year.
