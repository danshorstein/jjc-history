by Frances Gunther
“All the wonderful things in life are so simple that one
is not aware of their wonder until they are beyond touch.
Never have I felt the wonder and beauty and joy of life
so keenly as now in my grief that Johnny is not here to
enjoy them. Today, when I see parents impatient or tired or
bored with their children, I wish I could say to them,
But they are alive, think of the wonder of that! They may
be a care and a burden, but think, they are alive!
You can touch them — what a miracle! All parents
who have lost a child will feel what I mean. Others,
luckily, cannot. But I hope they will embrace them with
a little added rapture and a keener awareness of joy.”
In Loving Memory of Neal Portnoy
