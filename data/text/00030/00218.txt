Proclamation
WHEREAS:
the world-wide commemoration of Human Rights Day falls
on December 10, 1970, celebrating the United Nations
Universal Declaration of Human Rights, subscribed to
both by the United States and the U.S.S.R. , which states s
(Article 18), "Everyone has the right to freedom of
thought, conscience and religion; this right includes
freedom to change his religion or belief, and freedom,
either alone or in community with others and in public
or private, to manifest his religion or belief, in
teaching, practice, worship or observance;" and
during the eight day period of December 22-30, 1970,
American Jews, together with Jews throughout the world,
observe Hanukkah, the Festival of Lights, commemorating
an ancient victory over oppression;
NOW, THEREFORE,
I, Hans G. Tanzler, Jr., Mayor of the City of Jacksonville
do hereby proclaim the Month of December, 1970, as
HUMAN RIGHTS FOR JEWS OF THE SOVIET UNION MONTH
in Jacksonville, Florida.
