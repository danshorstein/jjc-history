1970-71 was a good year for our Brownies.
The girls gave a pantomime play of the “Brownie
Story” for their investiture. The girls presented the
play October 30 after which refreshments were served
and stars were given the girls who were in the Troop
last year.
In November they decorated and filled a Thanks¬
giving box which was given to a needy Jewish family.
In December the girls made Hanukah decorations for
the Hanukah Supper at the Center and held a Latke
Party. During school vacation they went to Marine-
land and had a picnic at Anastasia Park on the way
home.
In January they planted a tree at the Campsite
and were guests of Popeye on T.V., Channel 12. They
also took a trip to the Children’s Museum and the
Planetarium. They participated in the Purim Carni¬
val by “manning” a booth. March 12 was Girl Scout
Sabbath and the Brownies took part as well as at the
Father-Daughter Banquet at the Center when the girls
served their fathers and presented their World Day
program.
To wind up a wonderful year the Brownies gave
a Mother’s Day program and later held their Fly-Up
for the girls going into Troop 167 of the Girl Scouts,
