JUNIOR TROOP #162
Leaders: Mrs. Albert Levy, Mrs. Bernard Nachman,
Mrs. Leonard Saraga. Meetings are each Friday, 3:00
to 4:30 p.m. at the Center Southside Site.
Troop Committee: Chairman, Mrs. Harry Berman;
Mesdames Sidney Backer, Irwin Gendzier, Eli Becker,
Philip Shapiro and Earl Horowitz.
Girl Scouting is a very wide range program offering
the maturing girl the opportunity to grow and develop
new ideas and interests by helping others and partici¬
pating in out-of-door activities.
The year started with a re-dedication program in
the form of a cook-out at the Center Southside Site.
At this time the Scouts made plans for the coming
year and received pins and badges earned during the
summer.
Other activities during the past year included par¬
ticipation in the Veterans Day Parade and group at¬
tendance at the Rodeo held at the Jacksonville Coli¬
seum. December included a week-end camp-out and a
Hanukah celebration. The annual Father-Daughter
Banquet was held in February. The Scouts entertained
their fathers at a delicious dinner, presented a program
and received merit badges earned throughout the year.
During National Girl Scout Week, the Troop par¬
ticipated in Friday evening services at the Center and
gathered with all the Scouts of the Lakewood Man¬
darin district for a day of good fellowship and sports
activities at the Center Site.
The year ended with a week-end Camp-out, a visit
to a local radio station, a family outing and cook-out
and a weekend trip to Rainbow Springs as a reward
for selling the fabulous amount of 2568 boxes of Girl
Scout cookies.
