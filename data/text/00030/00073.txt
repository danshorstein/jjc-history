Sisterhood Purim Carnival
Sisterhood women have been challenged by our
theme this year, “Ve’ahavta” “And Thou Shalt Love”
to live lives of commitment; lives devoted to learning,
teaching, sharing and giving.
They have carried on the Jewish tradition of hos¬
pitality by welcoming new members. Through our
social action meeting and our panel discussion on
abortion, we have shown our interest in our commu¬
nity and in current issues. Following the path of Ma-
thilde Schechter, the founder and first president of
National Women’s League, we have shown our love
of Torah with our successful campaign for the Jewish
Theological Seminary and the Women’s Residence Hall.
We have demonstrated our love for our own Synagogue
at our Donor meeting where we provided funds for
Sisterhood Hanukah Program
furnishings and kitchen equipment, and previewed the
Center’s proposed new plans. Our pride in modern Is¬
rael led us to study its culture and industry, and enjoy
its products at a wine tasting meeting.
Many of our projects are aimed at and for our
youth. Sisterhood funds provide Sunday School teach¬
ers salaries, Ramah scholarships, and the purchase of
modern teaching aids. We finance the youth programs
of USY and ATID, as well as Scouting.
As always, we are interested in tomorrow as well
today. Thus we end each year looking forward to the
next. We look to a year of service to our synagogue,
to the community and to Judaism.
