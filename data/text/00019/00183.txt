BARRY SINOFF
The Esquire Club, primarily a social organization, has completed another outstanding year and looks forward to even greater heights
in the future.
Led capably by President Barry Sinoif, Esquire proved during the past year that it was proficient in serving the community in cul¬
tural and religious aspects. An example of this was the Club’s successful trip to the Duval Medical Center to entertain the children. Every
member of the Club volunteered when called upon to help with the Cerebral Palsy Telethon. Aside from the boys’ tireless work through¬
out the night, the club also gave a generous contribution to this worthy cause.
The outstanding social events of the year were the annual Holiday Dance at the Beauclerc Country Club and the Esquire Weekend at
the Beach. The club began the Weekend by turning out in mass for Sabbath services at the Center. The club plans to make this a tradi¬
tional part of our program.
The Esquire Club wishes to thank our sweetheart, Marcia Rothstein, and our Advisor, Charles Zimmerman, for their untiring efforts.
