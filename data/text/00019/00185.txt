The National Council of Jewish Women is a volunteer service organization which
through a program of service, education and social action helps to meet human needs.
Council’s local activities include recreational activities at the Juvenile Shelter, the Naval
Hospital and at River Garden Home for the Aged. It tested the hearing of over 9,000 Duval
County school children, continued to provide wheelchairs, crutches and hospital beds through
our Medical Loan Shelf and sewed layettes for needy families.
The Jacksonville Section was honored this year by the Northeast Florida Chapter of Social Workers as one of the local organizations
nominated for the Jane Adams Centennial Award in outstanding community services.
Council continues its support to Israel by training leaders for welfare and educational work through scholarships and by sending toys
and clothing to children in Israel. It has also expanded its services by building a new Hebrew Univ. High School.
Council provides a strong, unifying cord of mutual interests and aims for all Jewish women, regardless of a diversity of thinking
and background, so that they may work together in the spirit of Judaism for the good of alJ humanity.
The Jacksonville Section has both a daytime and evening grouo, meeting every third Wednesday and fourth Thursday respectively.
