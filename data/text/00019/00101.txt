YOUTH ACTIVITIES
With a full time Director for the Activities Department,
we have been able to provide a well rounded program for all
ages. Utilizing all of our facilities we now offer the following:
CENTER YOUTH ORGANIZATIONS
For High School students—a U.S.Y. Chapter that func¬
tions with the National Organization, meeting bi-weekly with
a varied program; Explorer Scouts organized for boys who
wish to continue their Boy Scout training; Center Youth
Board composed of presidents of all the youth clubs meeting
at the Center that acts as a governing body for all youth
problems.
For Junior High School—a similar U.S.Y. group for
the younger teen-agers also affiliated with the national move¬
ment of U.S.Y.; Boy Scout Troops for this same age group
meeting weekly with many extra activities such as hikes, out¬
ings and jamborees.
For 5th, 6th and 7th Graders—Social clubs with monthly
functions; Boy Scouts and Girl Scouts.
For the Younger Set—Brownie and Cub Scout programs
affiliated with the local Scout headquarters.
Youth activities sponsored by the Center include teen-age
canteens with music, refreshment and floor shows; athletics
for boys and girls in all sports and a bowling league for teen¬
agers.
Charles Palot Youth Fund
This Fund finances all transportation charges to the Region¬
al U.S.Y. Conventions and the annual Leadership Training
Institute for C.Y.L. and C.P.C. members. The soul source of
income is through donations tothis Fund.
