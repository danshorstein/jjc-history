Camp Ramah
The Camp Ramah movement provides our youth with a unique
summer camp experience. It is a dynamic and unique program de¬
signed to enrich educationally, religiously, culturally and socially
the lives of boys and girls in the Conservative Movement.
Each phase of the camp’s program is most unusual.
Devoted and dedicated laymen composing the Board of Direc¬
tors have the responsibility of setting the camp’s policies.
It is said of the faculty of over 400 men and women who ad¬
minister the camp to more than 1400 boys and girls that a sum¬
mer at Ramah is more than a job—it is a calling, a way of life, a
way in which to teach boys and girls to walk in the paths of God.
Children pray in Hebrew and play in Hebrew. The maxim "The
study of the law is equal in value to all else” is practiced by cam¬
pers, Directors, Counsellors and the athletic specialist.
Jewish observance, based in the framework of traditional prac¬
tice but interpreted to our modern way of life, is practiced daily—
the entire camp "davans” three times a day, make the blessings be¬
fore and after meals, sing Zemirot in celebration of the Sabbath—
religion is meaningful and relevant in the daily routine of all.
And just as important—that which makes a camp experience
adventurous and exciting—swimming, athletics, nature study, arts

with the creative life of American Jewry must make it our task to
develop into righteous people as many boys and girls and young
men and young women as we can find—and help! It is to this
proposition that Ramah is dedicated and, with your help, Ramah
will go from strength to strength!”
