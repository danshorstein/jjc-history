Several significant improvements were introduced this year and innovations adopted
previously were strengthened.
Our Hebrew School furthered its objective of classifying pupils into compatible age
groups and scholastic ability. Hebrew School classes have almost become parallel with
public school classes. Two sessions each day and extensive and intensive divisions make th s
possible. With the help of an additional full time professional teacher, we were able to in¬
stitute an integrated program for nine year old beginners who meet with the same teacher
three times a week. This year’s beginners’ class thus numbers 72 pupils. Among the inno¬
vations is the 8th grade Hebrew School class that meets from 5:00 to 7:30 p.m. with a half
hour for supper served in our Cafeteria.
Jr. Congregation and Pre-Jr. Congregation attendance at Sabbath Services continued
to grow and the unit leader system introduced last year has proved most helpful.
On Sunday mornings following Minyan Club services the breakfasts have been put on
a self-sustaining basis, each boy pays $1.50 a month. The Men’s Club continues to cater the
breakfast, its financial resources being directed to other projects like Camp Ramah scholar¬
ships.
The new Classroom Building in addition to fulfilling its primary function has enabled
us this year to re-activate the Arts and Crafts department and initiate a full scale Library
program. The Assembly Hall and convertible Chapel helped immeasurably with children’s
services.
