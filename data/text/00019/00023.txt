President s Message
It is an honor for me to be able to greet all of our Center
members and friends through this Year Book, which contains
the history of our congregation during the past year.
Unfortunately, we must in this issue record the death of our
late rabbi, Dr. Sanders A. Tofield "of blessed memory". Our
congregation was the beneficiary of his devoted and selfless
service for thirteen years. We inherited from him a deep appre¬
ciation of the high ethical and moral principles of our faith.
It is incumbent upon our congregation to keep alive the mem¬
ory of Rabbi Tofield by incorporating into our lives the Love of
Torah which was so deeply imbedded in his life. The congrega¬
tion’s dedication of the Tofield Memorial Library will be a last¬
ing reminder to us of his zeal for learning and his zest for
teaching.
It is imperative that our congregation remains true to the
ideals that Rabbi Tofield deemed so important, and which are
the cornerstones of our institution, named "Torah — Avodah —
G1 miles Che sed" which represent Study—Worship—Doing of
Good Deeds.
LOUIS SAFER, President
Your administration will work harder than ever to continue
the meaningful program which Rabbi Tofield organized and nurtured. Our staff has assumed greater responsibility than ever before.
I am confident that our membership will continue its loyal support of all our Center activities.
On behalf of the Officers, Directors and Trustees, I extend to each of you best wishes on the occasion of this New Year. May
we all be blessed with good health, spiritual enlightenment, peace and prosperity. May the coming year crown with success all our
efforts and aspirations. "Le Shanah Tovah Tikatevu"
