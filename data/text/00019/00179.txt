Of major importance to the local Jewish community is the need for higher education
for its youth. In this field the A. B. Weil Lodge No. 800 of B’nai B’rith is second to none.
To assist deserving students our lodge, as we have done for the past 25 years, is continuing
its scholarship loan fund. A need for additional funds for this purpose prompted a highly
successful fund raising dance at the Beauclerc Country Club. IOEL L GOLDMAN
In the area of recreational activity the Lodge is going all out to promote a bowling
league for its members. State, regional and national tournaments are planned for B’nai B’rith bowlers.
Meetings are held every second and fourth Thursday of the month at the George Washington Hotel at 12:30 noon. Interesting
guest speakers are on the program and all persons are invited to attend.

This past year has been a rewarding and gratifying year for B’nai B’rith women.
The chapter overscribed its goal of allocations by contributing generously to the worthy jjg| |¡|¡ —
services of B’nai B’rith. The National Jewish Hospital, Leo N. Levi Hospital, Bellefaire Chil¬

local drives. They send the daily Jewish newspapers to River Garden residents. The Birthday ladies go each month to Pinecastle School
and give the children a birthday party.
The youth programs reach out to all high school and college youth through B’nai B’rith Girls, A.Z.A. and Hillel groups.
The Anti-Defamation League this year went overboard with the purchase of five of the Dolls for Democracy and the distribution
of B’nai B’rith’s memo books throughout the local schools and churches.
The local chapter had a gain of 98 new members and all of this was helped with the marvelous programs and publicity and full
cooperation of membership, thus winning the district-wide membership award for second largest gain.
In a spirit of friendship B’nai B’rith Women attempts to render a service to mankind in a fine social atmosphere and these achieve¬
ments are possible only through the zealous efforts of the Board and membership.
