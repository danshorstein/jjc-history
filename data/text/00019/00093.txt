Ritual and Worship
As this Year Book goes to press the Jacksonville Jewish Center is like a ship without
its captain. Shortly after Shavuot our beloved Rabbi, Dr. Sanders A. Tofield, was called to
his Eternal Reward. May his memory ever be a source of inspiration and a blessing to us.
The years of Rabbi’s guidance and indoctrination produced a goodly seasoned crew of
staff, officers and board members for the Center. This training stood us in good stead during
Rabbi’s illness and since his passing. Relying heavily upon the charts and instructions Rabbi
left us, we were able to sail the good ship Center through the turbulent seas of year 5720.
All of our services, daily, Sabbath and Holiday have continued to adhere as closely as pos¬
sible to those of previous years.
The daily Minyans have been strengthened by members of the Board who volunteered
to observe the Kaddish for Rabbi. The Sabbath services are led by laymen or by guest
rabbis when available. The attendance at these services continues good.
For the High Holidays this year we were most fortunate to have secured the services
af Rabbi Yaakov Rosenberg. We look forward to his messages of inspiration. As in past
years Aliyahs and Honors will be distributed as fairly and as widely as possible throughout
our membership.
NATHAN FREIDLIN
Our goal remains as always to promote and encourage attendance and participation in all our services.
We pray the Almighty send us a new captain soon to guide us in the spiritual forces of our tradition towards greater love and re¬
spect for the Jewish way of life. AMEN
