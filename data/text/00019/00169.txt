The Ladies’ Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society was founded thirty-seven years ago as an
aid society to help the needy of Jewish faith. Even at that time, the founders envisioned a
Home for the Aged in Jacksonville. It was through their perseverance that River Garden
Home for the Aged became a reality fourteen years ago.
When this goal was attained, the role became that of auxiliary to the Home, help¬
ing to nurture its growth and development through the years.
Another successful year has been completed by Ladies Aid, the 618 members playing a special part in serving River Garden in pro¬
viding extra-budgetary needs such as furnishings, redecorating, air conditioning and kitchen and infirmary equipment. Funds for these needs
were raised through Tombola, Donor and Social Printing projects as well as through annual dues and Life Memberships in Ladies’ Aid.
Linens were provided through the Linen Shower. ¡
In the realm of service, Ladies’ Aid sponsors activity and volunteer programs at the Home. Since its inception three years ago, over
14,000 hours of service has been given to the residents through the Gray Ladies program. Ladies’ Aid also sponsors regularly scheduled
entertainment and parties through Special Holidays and Friendly Visitors programs. River Garden residents participate in projects served
by volunteers of the auxiliary, such as the residents’ magazine, the Rhythm Band, ceramic classes and the Hobby Shop.
This year a new project was added at River Garden, a weekly Senior Citizens Day which serves the residents as well as many older
people from the community. They look forward to an afternoon of varied programs including book reviews, lectures on art and music,
films, rhythm band and community singing, followed by an enjoyable coffee hour a nd competitive games.
The members of the Ladies’ Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society renew their pledge of continued devotion and service to River Garden
and its residents in the hope that they can help bring some measure of comfort and meaning to their lives.
