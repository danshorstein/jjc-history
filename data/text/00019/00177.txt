Young Judaea
Young Judaea is a youth organization composed of Senior and Junior groups. In the South summer conventions and winter con¬
claves are held for the Senior and Camp Judaea in Hendersonville, N. C. is held each summer for two weeks for the junior members.
The aims of Young Judaea are fourfold: to promote better understanding of Jewish living and historical background; to foster love
for Zion by aiding in the development of the land of Israel; to actively participate in American affairs of the local community; to fulfill
the ideals of brotherhood in club life and activities.
Miss Sybil Horovitz, City Director of Young Judaea, resigned her duties this spring and is now in Israel, continuing her studies
under the Jewish Agency Year in Israel Program.
The Zionette Senior Club was directed by Susan Margolin the first part of the season. Under her direction many interesting pro¬
grams were presented and she organized the Dance Team which participated at the Conclave in Jekyll Island in December, 1959. This
team presented their annual program of Israeli dances at the Stephen Foster Memorial Festival in White Springs, Fla. Officers of this
club were President, Judy Bossen; Vice-Pres., Elaine Brukner; Secy., Marilyn Berkowitz; Treasurer, Rose Zager.
The Junior Clubs are the Sabrim Club and the Tova Club. Under the direction of Elaine Brukner and Byron Kolitz, these clubs
celebrate Purim with masquerades and games. Another club for girls was organized by Claire Lynn Fleet and held various social events.
Three members of the Zionette Club were awarded scholarships to the national Young Judaean Camp, Tel Yehudah: Judy Bossen,
Marilyn Halpern and Elaine Brukner. All will attend the second session in the month of August and hope to return with increased zeal
to continue their work.
Young Judaea operates under the local Youth Commission which is composed of members of Hadassah and Z.O.A., with Mrs.
Charles Kluglose, as the Chairman.
