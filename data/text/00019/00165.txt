Serving the entire Jewish community of Jacksonville, the Jewish Community Council is the central Welfare Fund and Community
Relations body of the local Jewish community. An association of individuals, synagogues, organizations and social service agencies, the
program encompasses several key activities which give unity, cohesion and coordination to local Jewish communal life.
A community-wide annual Welfare Fund Campaign provides support ¿o significant local, national and overseas organizations. A key
recipient, the National United Jewish Appeal, has expressed American Jewry’s responsibility for the welfare and rehabilitation needs of
Jewish communities the world over for over 20 years. River Garden Hebrew Home for the Aged and the Jewish Welfare Society are
among the local beneficiaries.
The Community Relations Committee, designed to cultivate amicable, positive and cooperative ties with civic, social and church organi¬
zations in the general community, focuses a spotlight on Church-State problems, the influence of Israel as the rapidly emerging center off
vibrant democracy in the Middle East, inter-cultural relations, civil liberties and civil rights and internal affairs within the Jewish com-;
munity itself. When the seeds of overt anti-Semitism erupt, the Council’s Community Relations Committee is thus equipped to act forcefully.
The "Commentator”, the Council’s monthly newspaper, is a major communal project. Involved in presenting communal affairs objec¬
tively are the professional staff in cooperation with the publicity chairman who reflect the community’s diverse groupings.
