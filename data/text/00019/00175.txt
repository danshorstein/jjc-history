The Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah is composed of approximately 825 Jewish women
who meet the second Tuesday of each month to serve together in the ranks of the American
Zionist Movement. Hadassah’s members join in efforts to effect the national Hadassah pro¬
gram of health, vocational and education services to Israel and the dedication of our local
women to active participation in the life and expression of American community affairs.
With more than 315,000 members in 1250 chapters and units in the United States and Puerto Rico, Hadassah is the world’s largest
Zionist organization. While its pioneering efforts in medicine and social welfare have permeated every facet of Israeli life, it has con¬
ducted significant activities on the American scene to foster creative and intelligent living through educational programs designed to
stimulate understanding of the Jewish cultural heritage.
To date, Hadassah has raised more than $14,000,000 toward the completion of the new Hadassah Hebrew University Medical Center
which is to be dedicated on August 3, I960 in Israel. This is a tremendous contribution, one that was made possible through the self¬
less devotion of Hadassah’s dedicated members. Together with the Zionist Organization of America, Hadassah supports the Jewish Na¬
tional Fund for the rehabilitation of the land of Israel and Zionist youth work through Young Judaea clubs.
Outstanding events of the coming year will be the opening luncheon on October 11, the Israeli Showcase in December and the
Membership Tea, which will be the featured attraction of the month of January. The spring will be devoted to programs honoring the
donors to Hadassah’s projects. The closing meeting and installation of officers will be held in May.
