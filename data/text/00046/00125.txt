Thirteen members of the Jacksonville Jewish Center family - 10 teen agers
and 3 adults took off for five days to the mountains of North Carolina for a
wonderful Judaica retreat and nature appreciation. After camping in Hot
Springs National Forest, the group went rafting on the French Broad River and
rock repelling. Every morning and evening, services were conducted and Jewish
rituals were totally observed. Discussion groups were led by Rabbi Kentof on
the meaning of the dietary laws and the passages of prayers that the youngsters
recited. On the fourth day of the trip, the youngsters tubed down a river flow at
Deep Creek, North Carolina. All in all, it was a wonderful trip which will remain
for many months in the minds of those who participated. Many thanks go to the
advisors, Mr. Allan Cohen and Mr. Allan Silberberg and Rabbi Dov Kentof, who

led the trip.

Campers: Greg Balotin, Bobby & Teddy Clayman, Joe Cohen, Jeffrey
Meyers, Nicole Portnoy, David Schnipper and Brent Trager.
