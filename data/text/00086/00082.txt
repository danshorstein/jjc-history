Another phase of the Center’s
program of character and citizen¬
ship building are the Girl Scout
and Brownie Troops sponsored by
the Daughters of Israel.
Last year the Girl Scout Troops
showed marked progress in all
phases of their program. Four in¬
dividual troops were organized and
each undertook projects in keep¬
ing with their age level.
Troop 2 had as their main pro¬
ject the making of stuffed toys to
be presented to the children of the
Hope Haven Association. Another
project undertaken by the troops
was the adoption of several fami¬
lies in Palestine.
The joint Hanukah party was
very successful in its two-fold pur¬
pose. The girls were shown that
their sponsoring organization has
their interest at heart and the lead¬
ers were inspired by the coopera¬
tion of the parents, evidenced by
the large attendance. Mrs. Leo
Michelson acted as master of cere¬
monies at this affair.
The Brownie Troops under ex¬
cellent leadership advanced con¬
siderable. Theirs was mostly handi¬
craft projects and outdoor past-
times.
Further demonstrating that the
Girl Scout movement in the Cen¬
ter is closely connected with its
kindred groups, the troops parti¬
cipated in the annual closing ban¬
quet given by the Boy Scout Com¬
mittee. Many of the girls and lead¬
ers appeared in uniform.
Members of the troops are also
students of our Hebrew and Sun¬
day Schools and take part in the
religious activities of the Schools.
Aside from attending services as
individual they also appeared of¬
ficially twice when the annual
Sabbath dedicated to the Girl
Scouts of America was observed
on October 24th and at the annual
service dedicated to Mothers when
they presented floral momentos to
all mothers present.
