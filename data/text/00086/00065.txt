On June 11th Y. P. L. honored
members of the 1948 Confirmation
Class at Sabbath services in the
Center. Morty Rosenkranz and
Herbert Sohn addressed the con¬
gregation and other members par¬
ticipated in the service and ar¬
ranged the refreshments.
During the summer a social was
held at the home of Mr. and Mrs.
I. H. Edwards. Part of the evening
was devoted to a discussion of a
Y. P. L. High Holiday Service.
Leaders of the Center have con¬
ferred with Y. P. L. officers to plan
a separate High Holiday service
for the youth of the Center.
The officers and member of the
Young People’s League have high
hopes for the success of the latest
addition to the Center family.
In keeping with the progressive
ideas pervading Conservative Ju¬
daism in our time, the men of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center over
three years ago felt the need for
a Men’s Club to act as an adjunct
in its full program of communal
activity. During these in which the
Men’s Club has progressed from in¬
fancy to maturity, the things which
have contributed most to its suc¬
cess have been the diversified ac¬
tivity of its interests and the in¬
sistence upon Jewish content and
education in its program.
Our organization undertook
many worthwhile projects during
this past year which kept the chair¬
man and their committeemen un¬
der pressure for the entire season.
The religious committee headed by
Vice President Louis Safer, direct¬
ed the efforts of the Men’s Club in
ushering for the high Holy days,
distribution of fruit and candy for
the Simchath Torah celebration,
the awarding of prizes for the best
class competition in Sunday School
and sponsorship of Shlichoth
serving.
The Committee for Institutional
projects headed by Julius Levin,
aided the House committee of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center in
maintenance of the procurement of
special projects or items which
have had no mention in the bud¬
get. We also carry an item in our
budget which contributes to the
salary received by the Sunday
School teachers.
The Athletic program guided by
Benjamin Cohen provided every
phase of sports for those who are
either interested in strenuous or
light exercise. The Center Men’s
Club provided two top teams in
the Bowling league sponsored by
the Community Council and one
of the teams carried off champion¬
ship honors. Monday night remains
bowling night for many of the
Center Men’s Club members. The
softball team captained by the ath¬
letic chairman, carried away
championship honors and the first
place cup in the Center Sunday
Morning softball league. Also as a
special project the Men’s Club
sponsored a Jewish Center team in
the city-wide Sunday School
league.
The Monte Carlo night which
has become an annual project was
headed by Treasurer, Herbert
Sandler and David Friedman, as
in the past, a financial achieve¬
ment as well as a social success
and enabled the club to continue
its usual finance consuming pro¬
jects.
Besides those projects promoted
by the various chairmen, the Men’s
Club shouldered many other
responsibilities. Our President se¬
lected Bill Goldberg to help in the
formation of the Center’s Young
People’s League and the club pro¬
vided the expenses for a delegate
to the national convention. All
prospective members of the YPL
were invited to dinner at one of
the meetings and the entire pro¬
gram was designed to aid the for¬
mation of the League.
