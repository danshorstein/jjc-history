30 Rosh Hodesh Elul
Shabbat Hazon this Sabbath which precedes Tisha B’Ab derives
its name from the first words of the Haftorah “Hazon”—the
vision of Isaiah, which foretells the destruction of Jerusalem.
Tisha B’Ab (ninth day of Ab) is a fast day which marks the
destruction of the first temple in Jerusalem by the Babylonians
(586 B. C. E.) and also of the second Temple by the Romans
(70 B. C. E.) (*When the fast occurs on a Saturday, it is ob¬
served on Sunday)
Shabbat Nahamu (Sabbath of Consolation), derives its name
from the first words of the Haftorah “Nahamu, nahamu, Comfort
ye, My people.”
