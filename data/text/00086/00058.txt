BOY SCOUTS
Troop 14 began with the 1947-
48 season last September with 15
Scouts on the roster, ending the
year with 25 Scouts on the roles,
including 5 associate Scouts.
Advancement in rank, as well
as the acquirement of Merit Badges
was consistent by Troop members.
As outstanding Scout of the
Sioux District, Senior Patrol Lead¬
er and Eagle Scout, Jack Eff, was
awarded a one month camping trip
in the far west at Camp Philmont.
The Scout Sabbath Service on
February 13th was carried out en¬
tirely by the Scouts, with the ex¬
ception of the sermon. Also dur¬
ing February, a Parent’s Night
was held when the Troop was pre¬
sented its new charter by the late,
beloved Mr. Joel Richard, District
Commissioner.
Troop 14 won second place in
the Sioux District Field Meet in
March. Each Patrol in the Troop
entered the District Camporee. The
Flaming Arrow Patrol, led by Irv¬
ing Proctor, qualified for the Coun¬
cil-wide Camporee but could not
participate because of the Pass-
over Holiday.
The Troop held its first attempt
at a Scout Circus on May 23rd. This
proved to be successful enough to
warrant it becoming an annual
affair. Quite a substantial sum
was raised on this affair held in
Springfield Park and the entire
proceeds were given to the Com¬
munity Council for the 1948 drive
of the United Jewish Appeal.
The Year was concluded with a
large banquet for all of the Cen¬
ter’s Scout organizations. The
Troop was privileged to have Mr.
Horace Williamson, Chief Scout
Executive of the J acksonville
Council, as the principal speaker.
At this time the A. Z. A. award
to the Troop’s best all-around
Scout was presented to Life Scout,
Irving Spevak. An award for the
high score in the Troop Individual
Point Contest was made to Life
Scout, Irving Proctor.
The Troop Committee is com¬
posed of the following members
led by Chairman Robert J. Gor¬
don: Ben Chepenik, Harry Katz,
Irvin Rothstein, Hyman Selber,
Philip Selber and Rabbi Tofield,
the Institutional representatives.
