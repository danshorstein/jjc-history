TROOP 14 BOY SCOUTS OF AMERICA
Troop 14 is sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Center, under the capable
leadership of Abe Diamond and the troop committee. The troop has just com¬
pleted its 17th year as a member of the Greater Jacksonville Boy Scout Council.
The purpose of the troop has been, and always will be, to develop in its members
a sense of loyalty to the high ideals expressed by the Scout Laws, to build good
citizenship, and to instill in the boys reverence for their religion and the tra¬
ditions of their people.
During the year, the troop offered its services to organizations affiliated
with the Center, at various social functions. The highlight of the season was the
special Mother’s Day service held on Friday night sponsored by Troop 14. The
troop also entered in the Boy Scout Circus parade and made a very fine showing.

GIRL SCOUTS OF AMERICA. TROOP 2
Troop 2 Girl Scouts of America sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Center,
had a very successful year under the leadership of Miss Edith Wolf son, and Miss
Rosalyn Magezis. The troop met every Tuesday evening at the Center and had
many enjoyable and interesting meetings. The troop now consists of seventeen
girls, the majority of whom have passed the tenderfoot tests. The year’s activities
were closed with a banquet at Begal’s Restaurant, where the girls received their
registration cards.
Recently the Center assigned the Girl Scouts, troop 2 room number 2 on the
third floor of the Center building. The girls are eagerly looking forward to the
task of decorating the room in scout fashion during the coming year.
Patrol leaders of the Girl Scouts are Peggy Schemer and Pearl Haber. Char¬
lotte Rose is treasurer of the troop and Bernice Kassover is scribe.

TROOP 14 CUB SCOUTS OF AMERICA
Under the capable leadership of Melvin M. Margol, Troop 14 Cub Scouts of
America, sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Center, has completed the first
year of cubbing. During the first year of activities the troop has shown promise
of becoming one of the best cub troops in the city. The main purpose of the
troop is to develop good citizenship among its members and imbue them with a
sense of loyalty and reverence for their religion and traditions . The troop
entered in the Boy Scouts Circus Parade and made such a fine showing that the
cubs drew rounds of applause from the spectators lined along the streets. The
troop has grown in membership during the year, so that it now counts twenty- *
three boys. Boys between the ages of 9 and 12 are invited to join.
