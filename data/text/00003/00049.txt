Another successful season was record¬
ed at the completion of the Jewish Cen¬
ter Diamond Ball League’s second year
of play. More than sixty Jewish ath-
letej took part in the games which were
held every Sunday morning at the For¬
est and Palm street diamond over a
span of three months.
Much of hte credit for the interest
maintained throughout the season should
go to the officers of the Jewish Center
League who worked untiringly to bring
about Cohesion between the four teams
competing in the circuit. The organi¬
zation was headed by Dr. Sam Witten
for the second consecutive year. Isadore
Moscovitz served as secretary and pub¬
licity director, and Robert Baker was
treasurer and general quartermaster of
equipment.
The Y. M. H. A. ten won the first
half of the circuit’s play, and the A.Z.A.
fraternity captured the second half. In
the playoff to determine the champion¬
ship the latter team emerged victorious.
The Florida Jewish News won second
place during the second half, while the
Jewish Progressive Society completed
the circuit.
The same four teams are again ex¬
pected to comprise the Center League,
although two additional teams may join
to make the circuit a six-team league.
