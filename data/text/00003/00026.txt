A Message From The President
a.
I feel that the members of the Jacksonville Jewish Center may look back with con¬
siderable pride upon the accomplishments of the last few years, and especially the year
5696.
Under the spiritual leadership of our able Rabbi who is beginning now his fifth
year of service to the Jewish Center, which is in itself a record achievement for our
community, our organization has made notable progress.
Our beautifully conducted modern services, our fine daily Talmud Torah and Sun¬
day School where the children have acquired considerable Hebrew knowledge, our im¬
pressive celebrations of holidays and festivals, our fine entertainments, the constant
buzz of activities within the walls of the Center, are things of which every Jew should
be justly proud.
The successful refinancing of the Center building, which we were in danger of los¬
ing last year, speaks volumes for the splendid cooperation which the members of the
Center have given me and the officers, during the past year. This accomplishment is a
source of courage to me and will enable me to plan with confidence for the ultimate
complete ownership of the Center building, so that we may be able to devote all our
energies to the spiritual and cultural progress of our beloved institution.
May the New Year find us united in Jewish fellowship and working together to¬
wards the achievement of the highest ideals of our religion and humanity.
May God inscribe us all in the book of life, prosperity and happiness.
MAX RUBIN, President
