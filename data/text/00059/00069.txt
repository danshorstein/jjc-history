Rabbi Kentof’s Message
As we enter the school year 2000/
2001, our Education & Youth
Department is taking a new look at an
old problem: How can we be sure our
young people will retain their Judaism
and excel in Judaica?
I agree with the group of
educators who believe the way to do
this is by offering young people diverse
and challenging opportunities to
experience their Judaism, both in
formal as well as informal situations.
Through a variety of hands-on
activities, designed to capture their imaginations and
encourage leadership and independence, we can help our
youth create memory banks which they can use to help
make the tough decisions they will face later on in their
lives - the right Jewish decisions.
Experiencing Judaism in a variety of ways - some
in the formal classroom setting, some informal - like
clubs and synagogue holiday celebrations, combined with
summer USY, Ramah, and Day Camp activities - helps
to create well-rounded individuals, who return to Judaism
because they choose to and because they are in love with
its traditions.
With this in mind we design, and
redesign, our Hebrew School and
Youth Programs. Every year we
endeavor to add new curriculum
materials, programs, and staff members
to keep our Hebrew School fresh and
exciting. And every year we bring in
new advisors and/or create new
programs and social action projects to
keep our youth clubs moving forward
and brimming with excitement. Of
course, we include plenty of camp outs
and conventions!
I hope and pray that you will take advantage of the
many opportunities offered at the Center to enrich your
family and keep them in the fold of Judaism, so that we
may grow “from generation to generation.”
I, my wife Alina and our children, Gil and Tali, join
together to wish you and yours a Happy, Sweet, and
Prosperous New Year.
- Rabbi Dov Kentof
Rabbi Dov Kentof
Director of Education & Youth
