Scouting
Boy Scout Troop 14
Troop 14 continued the scouting tradition at the
Jacksonville Jewish Center with another year of outdoor
fun, adventure and activities in 1999-2000. Bob Fleet
served as Scoutmaster, and was assisted by Assistant
Scoutmasters Louis Schwartz and
Billy Wilensky. Youth leadership
was provided by Justin Jackral,
Junior Assistant Scoutmaster, Jared
Fleet, Senior Patrol Leader, and
Michael Demri, Assistant Senior
Patrol Leader.
This year, the troop went on
campouts to Goldhead Branch State
Park, Laura Walker State Park near
Waycross, Georgia, Florida Caverns State Park near
Marianna, where they enjoyed spelunking. The trip to
Skidaway Island Sate Park in Savannah, Georgia, was
especially exciting. One half of the scouts traveled to
Skidaway Island on Amtrak, and the other half traveled
back in a stretch limousine.
Other activities the scouts enjoyed included laser
tag, go-kart racing, playing miniature golf, and attending
the Jacksonville Suns Opening Game.
Once again, the scouts purchased food for the Food
Bank for Thanksgiving. They also donated prizes they
earned from selling Entertainment Books to the Day
School’s “adopted” holiday families.
A lot of scouts made strides
in advancement this year. Leonard
Beir became the most recent
member of Troop 14 to earn Boy
Scouting’s highest honor, the rank
of Eagle. Fourteen scouts attended
summer camp with Troop 14 at
Camp Grimes in North Carolina.
They were led by Assistant
Scoutmaster Louis Schwartz.
Troop 14 looks forward to continuing its proud
tradition of providing Jewish youth with a Boy Scouting
experience. If you enjoy camping, sports, a variety of
outdoor activities, and are between the ages of 11 and
18, we invite you to come to our meetings at the Center
on Thursday nights. The troop also welcomes participation
from adults interested in providing volunteer assistance.
For more information call Bob Fleet at (H) 268-2746
or (W)730-8103.
