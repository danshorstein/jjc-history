Reflections on life with our father Ted Gross
by Carol Gross and Ricki Gross Manne
Growing up... our father wasn't
ours. Mom used to say she "lost us"
all to the synagogue—that we spent
more time there than at home. It was
true. So many hours were spent there
waiting—praying, studying, learning
. . . and waiting. We were always
waiting for Dad to do whatever he
had to do with his students, or his
scheduling. And then, we'd take his
students home — and they'd sit in the
front seat of the car and we, in the back!
Dad gave so much respect to each and
every one of his students and their families. I
guess I felt then, that we came second — that
we were taken for granted.
Then I grew up . . . and found myself
treating the children and families
I worked for with the same
respect; arid I suppose my
personal relationships came
second, too, for a while.
Today, I believe, as I feel I
always did, that my father is one of
the "righteous men" of the world.
And I know how lucky l am to feel his
love and his blessings upon me and my family
— from the bottom of his bottomless heart.
I know I speak for all of us when I say we
have all been blessed to have this man in our
midst and active in our lives.
We love you Pa,
Ricki and Ed,
Tsurit, Ariel, Aviad and Yaniv
Carol and Jesse
