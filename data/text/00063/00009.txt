A Message from the Director of Education
The education of our
preschoolers, youth, young adults and
families is a top priority of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center. Jewish
education at the Center is strong and
growing. Camp Neshama has just
completed a banner year. The
Preschool, Solomon Schechter Day
School and the Religious School are
off to another year of learning and
excitement.
I look forward to leading the educational
programs of the Center with the partnership and
assistance of Willard Kennedy, Dale Schemer and
Lois Tompkins, an outstanding team of professional
administrators, along with the excellent faculty and
staff, and a strong group of dedicated volunteers.
Together we will provide continuity and a cohesive
vision throughout all educational components of the
Center by implementing a team approach to
coordinating programming, curriculum, professional
development and marketing.
Michael and Dr. Judy Stoner
Cheryl Plotkin and Sue Levine
will co-chair the Education
Committee comprised of
representation from all the Center
Schools. As a team, we will identify
and determine priorities, research
areas for development and explore
opportunities for institutional growth.
Together, we will perpetuate the
culture of menschelkeit and learning
that makes the Center so special. The
notion that kol yisrael arivim zeh
lizeh (all Israel is responsible one for the other)
thrives in our schools. This is the unique culture of
the Jacksonville Jewish Center.
As we begin this New Year with great energy
and excitement, I look forward to open
communication through regular parlor meetings and
other avenues as we make this journey together.
On behalf of my husband and my children,
I wish you a Shanah Tovah U’Metukah, a Happy
and Healthy New Year. May you be inscribed in the
Book of Life.
B ’Shalom,
— Dr. Judy Stoner
