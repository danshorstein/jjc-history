Simhat Torah Honorees
The High Holiday season reaches its joyous climax on Simhat Torah, the last day of the festival
of Sukkot. Because of our perennial love of the Torah, it has been referred to as Israel’s “bride”. On
Simhat Torah, we bring to a close the annual cycle of the reading of the Torah and immediately begin
anew. An ancient tradition evolved of honoring worthy men by calling them to the Torah for the last
reading, Hatan Torah, and the beginning portion, Hatan Bereshit. In our synagogue tradition, we
also honor the contributions made by an Ayshet Hayil, “Woman of Valor”.
Fred Kupperman
Hatan Torah
Fred Kupperman
is a fixture at daily
minyan. Not only
does he attend
every day, but he
is the Minyan
Captain for both
Monday and
Friday afternoons.
Fred spends many hours each week calling
individuals to make up the necessary ten
worshippers. His commitment began in his
youth and continues in retirement, but
now he has time to enjoy kibbitzing each
day, as well.
Fred grew up in an Orthodox home
in New Orleans, Louisiana, in the 1940s.
where he was active in AZA and B’nai
B’rith. He made a commitment to attend
daily minyan that continued throughout
his life.
After graduating from LSU with a
degree in Geological Engineering. Fred
served as a 1st Lieutenant in the U.S.
Army in Germany. Upon his discharge,
Fred went back to New Orleans, where
he met Marilyn Fletcher, from
Jacksonville, whom he married in 1959.
Raising a family in Jacksonville,
Fred worked hard, first at Jax Metal
Shredding, and then as a contractor, but
he still found time to become involved
at the Center. He often picked up older
members of the congregation and drove
them to 3rd and Silver to attend minyan.
Over the years, Fred has served on the
Center’s Board and on Religious and
Cemetery Committees. He currently
serves the community as the Vice
President of the Hevrah Kaddisha.
Fred is a devoted husband, father,
grandfather and friend to so many. Mazel
tov to Fred, Marilyn and their children
Greg Kupperman, Keith & Katherine
Kupperman and grandson A.J.
Moti Demri
Hatan Bereshit
Moti Demri has
the enthusiasm
and can-do spirit
one would expect
of a youthful
synagogue leader.
When a volunteer
is needed, Moti
says yes, first,
and asks questions later. His warm smile
and hardy handshake are readily offered
to all. He revels in the simchas of
others and takes Israeli dancing to a
whole new level.
Moti was bom in Safed, Israel. He
served for six years in the Israeli
Defense Force, rising to the rank of
Master Sergeant. Later, he completed
his education, receiving a degree in
computer science.
After Moti met and married
Cindy Proctor, the couple moved to her
native Jacksonville, where he quickly
became active in Jewish community
life. On any given Shabbat morning,
Moti can be seen walking between
synagogues on his way to read Torah
or participate as needed. An expert in
computer software design, Moti is a
manager at Citicorp, where he heads the
software testing department.
A member of the Center Board,
Moti has been a member of the
Religious, Education, Youth and
Recreation Committees. He has been
involved with Boy Scout Troop 14,
taught Hebrew school and coached
basketball. Moti works closely with the
Israeli Consulate to bring information
and guest speakers to our community.
Moti was honored last year as the
Center Men’s Club Man of the Year, a
well-deserved distinction. Mazel tov to
Moti, Cindy and their sons, Michael
and Ori, who shared this honor.
Robin Morris
Woman of Valor
Robin Morris is
the very model of
a modem Ayshet
Hayil, Women of
Valor. She is
bright, educated,
charming, a
devoted mother
and wife, a
popular Judaica teacher, avid volunteer
and dedicated Jew. Her home is always
open to newcomers in our community.
Bom in Denver, Colorado, Robin
received a Bachelors degree in Jewish
Studies from Newcomb College of
Tulane University in New Orleans. She
was the first student to receive a
Master’s degree in Jewish Education
from Spertus College of Judaica in
Chicago and she attended Hebrew
University in Jerusalem.
Robin met husband, Jeffery, in
New Orleans and came to his native
Jacksonville to begin not only married
life, but a long commitment to Jewish
life in our synagogue. A much loved
Judaica teacher in Center schools for
26 years, Robin adds a humorous
American twist to Hebrew programs to
make them memorable. Her happy
outlook and generous nature make her
a natural ambassador for our synagogue.
Attuned to women’s issues, she
has served on the boards of Center
Sisterhood and National Council of
Jewish Women, and was co-President
of Shir Li Hadassah and the
Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah.
Active in community concerns, Robin
is on the Advisory Council of the Jewish
Healing Network, a member of
Wolfson’s Children’s Hospital Auxiliary
and an active Mitzvah Clown.
Mazel tov to Robin, Jeffery and
their children, Shira, Aliza and Zach.
