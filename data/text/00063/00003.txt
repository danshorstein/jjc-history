After each sounding of the shofar on Rosh Hashanah, we recite a
brief prayer that begins with the words, “Today the world came into
being..The theme that the world is renewed each year is central to the
spirit of the season. Just as nature renews itself, every human being has
the opportunity on Rosh Hashanah to begin anew - to go forward and
dream dreams of the future. Rosh Hashanah is ultimately about how to
make things better for ourselves, our community and the world.
As we begin this New Year, the theme of renewal is especially
meaningful to all of us at the Jacksonville Jewish Center, because we are
welcoming new leadership to our synagogue. Rabbi Jonathan Lubliner
brings a magnetic warmth and enthusiasm as well as a broad spectrum of
knowledge, creativity and leadership to his position as Spiritual Leader.
Dr. Judy Stoner, Director of Education/Head of Schools, has extensive
experience in the field of Jewish education and an ability to bring
continuity, unity and a cohesive vision to our programs. Our new
synagogue president, Seeman Zimmerman, and his Officers and Board
of Directors, are inspired to invigorate all aspects of congregational life.
As visualized on the cover of this Yearbook, it is with continued
commitment to our Jewish traditions and hope for the future that we
greet the New Year 5765. May we all be inspired to strengthen our
dedication to Jewish life and pledge renewed participation in our
synagogue so that this will be a year of sweetness, success and blessing for
all of us.
