About the Cover:
We are proud to feature a mixed-media work
by one of the regions best known artists, Helene
Baker. A variety of her pieces was exhibited in the
Center’s Yergin Galley last May. This one stood
out for its exceptional beauty and meaningful
content.
Mrs. Baker has united her affinity for flowers
and plants with her outlook on the fragility of life
to create a message of hope and renewal, as well as
a tribute to her Jewish heritage. The draped tallit,
focal point of the composition, honors the memory
of her father, Sam Shelter, who was a longtime
member of the Jacksonville Jewish Center as well
as a tenor in the Center Choir. Her children, Merle
and Harris Rittenberg have continued his
committment to the Center in their own involvement
as officers and committee members.
About her work, the artists explains, “Some
viewers who see my work, choose to see the surface
beauty alone; however I intend my works to have
layers of content in them. The cycle of plants
reflects our own patttem of birth, growth and death.
If the observer is able to feel something spiritual,
that is enough.”
