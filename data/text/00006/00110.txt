Dorothy Davis, President
1 lie Henrietta Szold Young Judean Club has made remarkable progress dur¬
ing the past year. To beg in the season, the club held a joint membership tea
with the other Judean clubs of the city, through which many new members were
gained.
1 he club meetings are held at the homes of its members. First there is a
business session foil owed by a cultural program. During the informal discussions
which are sometimes held after the meetings the members write questions on
slips of paper. Then the sponsors lead the members in a discussion on the quest¬
ion raised.
The club paid all its dues and filled all the voluntary and required quotas
J he largest money raising project of the year was a penny raffle which netted a
profit of over thirty doll ars. The club also donated fifteen dollars to the Jackson¬
ville Community Council.
A tri-city meet was held in Savannah with a number of the club members
attending as well as one of the sponsors and Mrs. Rudy Moss, the head of the
Young Judean Council for the city. A pleasant time was had by all. Plans to
have the tri -city meet in Jacksonville this coming vear were discussed.
The number of social affairs held during the year was smaller because of
war conditions, but two of the larger affairs were a very successful hay ride held
in January for members and their dates, and a partv held at the home of one
of the members as a closing treat of the season.
