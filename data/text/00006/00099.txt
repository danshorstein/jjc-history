JUNIOR CONGREGATION
I he Junior Congregation has maintained a continuous existence of 10 years,
since it was organized by Rabbi Margolis in 1932. It conducted services every
Sabbath morning, on all festivals and High Holy Days. Through its activities
many pupils of the Hebrew School received excellent training in the conduct of
religious worship and gained acquaintance with the ceremonies and melodies
connected with each holiday and festival. Regular attendance of the Junior Con¬
gregation service is the best preparation for Synogogue worship in adult years.
It is to be deplored that of late not enough children attend these services.
I he Springfield district» in which the Center is located, has not lost much of
its Jewish population. The Junior Congregation was largely attended by children
in the neighborhood of the Center.
Last year the Junior Congregation was regularly attended by about 20
children, except on High Holy Days and Passover when the attendance was
about 75.
The J unior Congregation officers were installed at a special Friday night
service held on March 1 3 which was witnessed by a large congregation.
1 he Junior Congregation held two theatre parties for its members, wh ich
were very successful.
