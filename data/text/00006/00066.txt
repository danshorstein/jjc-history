Adolph B. Weil Auxiliary, B’ nai B rith, was ogranized officially at the con¬
vention of the Fifth District held in Jacksonville during May - June 1942. Due
to the inspiration of the fine work being done by the various auxiliaries of the
District, an enthusiastic group of women met with the officers of the District
Auxiliary and formed the local auxiliary.
A need for such an organization has been felt for sometime by the members
of the A. B.Weil Lodge, and the ladies are very enthusiastic over this new ven¬
ture.
Although the auxiliary was only formally organized during the recent con¬
vention, the group had been meeting a nd functioning as a temporary unit for
the past six months. During this time it acted as hostesses for the social hour fol¬
lowing the regular lodge meetings and was extremely active in the promotion of
the activities of the convention.
The ultimate purose of the Auxiliary is to supplement the work o( the lodge.
However it will select its own projects and activities and will retain its autonomy.
Mrs. Irving Levin was named temporary Chairman, Mrs. Adolph B. Weil was
named Honorary President. There are over sixty members, and every interested per¬
son is extended a very cordial invitation to join.
The Adolph B. Weil Auxiliary, B n ai B rith will begin its season in October
with an election of officers and a very impressive installation and charter pre¬
sentation, at which ceremony District represetation is promised.
Although there are a number of Jewish Wornep s organizations already func¬
tioning in Jacksonville, each one is doing splendid work and none overlap. There
is a definite need for the Adolph B. Weil Auxiliary, and great success is predicted
