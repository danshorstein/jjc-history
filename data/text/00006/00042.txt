I he six girls and boys who were confirmed on Sunday evening, May 24th,
constitute the first class to complete the 10 year curriculum of the Sunday School.
1 he Confirmands made a deep impression upon the large congregation that
assembled to witness the ceremony. 1 hey gave evidence of a fine Jewish back¬
ground and a mature understanding of their role as Jews and Americans. Every
one of the Confirmands had a number of years of instruction in the daily Hebrew
School in addition to their Sunday School studies, and three were Hebrew School
graduates. This fact, plus the higher age level has made it possible to produce
what was considered by many the best Confirmation Class in the history of the
Jewish Center. The manner in which the Confirmands read their prayers and
delivered their speeches so deeply impressed a prominent member of the local
reform congregation, who witnessed the ceremony, that he was moved to write a
glowing editorial of praise and commendation which was published in the South¬
ern Jewish Weekly in the issue o f May 29th.
