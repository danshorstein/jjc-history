JUMIOR HADDASSAH
With its fine group of leaders Jun¬
ior Hadassah has made great strides
during the past year toward establishing
itself as the most outstanding group of
young Jewish girls in the city.
Opening its year of activity with
a lovely membership tea> at which time
a substantial number of new members
were added to the rolls of the chapter,
Junior Hadassah carried through an elab¬
orate series of social affairs: formal and
informal dances, ,card parties, and an¬
nual Donor s Affair, all of whi ch helped
to strengthen the social bonds uniting
all Hadassah workers.
Endeavoring to improve the cult¬
ural status of its members, Junior Ha-
Jassah sponsored a series of study groups
which formed convenient units for the
discussion of Jewish problems and Zion¬
ist ideals. Many interesting lectures were
delivered to these groups by different
speakers.
The purely Zionist activities of Jun¬
ior Hadassah during the past year were
many and fruitful. A special program
was devoted to Youth Aliyah, the project
ol transferring youths from persecution-
ridden Europe to Palestine. Another pro¬
gram was devoted to initiating graduates
of Young Judea into Junior Hadassah.
By far the most inspiring event this
year was the very successful Regional
Convention whi ch was held in conjunc¬
tion with Senior Hadassah. A fine group
of girls coming from the neighboring
states as well as members of the local
Junior Hadassah participated in th e many
social and cultural events of the Con¬
vention. The highlight of this event was
the luncheon held on Sunday at the
Roosevelt Hotel. J he guest speaker, Mrs.
Moses P. Epstein brought a vital and in¬
spiring message to the assembled dele¬
gates.
With all quotas lor Junior Had¬
assah projects (Henrietta Szold School
of Nursing, Pardess Anna, Myer Shefey-
ah Chidren s Villege, an d Youth Afiyah)
satisfactorily filled, Junior Hadassah
brought to close a success ful year oi act¬
ivities.
1 he program mapped out for the
coming season promises to give Had¬
assah members a stiff better outlet for
their pioneering spirit and their idealism
for the cause o f Zion.
