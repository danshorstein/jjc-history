JUNIOR CONGREGATION
Junior Congregation is
modeled after the main con-
gregation. It is an organization
of students grades 3-7 who at-
tend our Afternoon Hebrew
School and Day School. These
services allow our youth to par-
ticipate, lead, and apply
knowledge and skills gained
through Hebrew training.
Students receive points for
attendance and participation in
the Junior Congregation ser-
vices. This year the reward was
a trip to “WET AND
WILD.”
AUTO
(Adults United To Organize)
AUTO, the Supplemental School organization, gives children and parents an opportunity to share Judaica ex-
periences together.
It has always been a Jewish philosophy to protect our
“kinder” and give to them what we never got! Those
times of protection are the links of an unbroken chain in
which our children need to experience and share the
knowledge of what survival is about. We are moving for-
ward with new and exciting programs involving the leader-
ship and intuition of our children with parents being the
bumper guards on the sides.
We are hoping to be able to guide instead of protect,
teach and share instead of lecture and brag. A school
newsletter by our students is one way we plan to open up
a new line of communication. We are very concerned with
the welfare of our children and have taken our first steps
in the marathon. Please be there to help our runners stay
in the race! We need your support!
Have a Happy and Healthy New Year.
Joni Shmunes and Stella Finck
