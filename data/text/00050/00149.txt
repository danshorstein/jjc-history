President’s Message
YEARBOOK MESSAGE
In preparing this message, I thought about who would read it. Of
course, it might be read by some of our members. But it may also be
read by some unknown person in the future, maybe a child cleaning out
a dusty bottom drawer. What can be said to that child, who might be
reading this yearbook 25 years from now, that will convey to him or her
the essence and priorities of the Jacksonville Jewish Center in the year
19914992?
“Your Synagogue, 25 years ago, chose to place its resources into
educating your parents. Your Synagogue renovated and expanded the
beautiful and adaptable building that you are still enjoying. Your
Synagogue accepted the challenge of paying off its mortgage. Your
Synagogue committed itself to meeting the religious and lifecycle needs of the congregation. Your
Synagogue, through the active involvement of hundreds of its members, attempted not only to survive,
but to thrive as a vibrant Jewish organization. That was the essence of the Jacksonville Jewish Center
as it entered the year 5752.”
Robin, Shira, Aliza, and Zachary join me in wishing you a healthy, and happy new year.
Jeffery B. Morris,
President
