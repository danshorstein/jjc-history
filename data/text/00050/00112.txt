Cub Scout Pack 14
Throughout the sixty-one years of the of-
ficial Cub Scout programs at the Jewish
Center, traditions continue that do not change
with the passing years. A Cub Scout’s oath
to “Do my duty to God and my country and
obey the Law of the Pack” is the heart of the
program. Respect for parents, peers, and
leaders and a reverence for nature remain a
constant requisite Changing technological ad-
vanees present a challenge to Scouts in the
various skills that they learn, while counter-
balanced by the camping skills that bring
them closer to nature and self-sufficiency.
Our pack had fun in weekly and monthly
meetings. A soccer game, softball game,
Pinewood Derby, Blue and Gold Banquet
(Sgt. Steve Weintraub was our guest speaker),
Scout Sabbath, campouts, and local field trips
added to the excitement. Most important, the
boys worked hard to achieve many badges
that rewarded the skills and information they
learned.
Thanks for a great year to our den leaders
and their assistants: Larry Smolley and
Karen Andres; Sharon Pineda and
Stella Finck; Bob Fleet and Sheila
Fiske. A special thank you to Lanny Zim-
merman, Cubmaster. In addition to these
volunteers were the Pack Committee
members, Bruce Glassman, Peggy
Organes, and Colleen Geeman, who
helped the pack run smoothly.
Yours in Scouting,
Carole Goldberg
