The Family Council, now heading into its third year, seeks to bring ever more families into the learning process and making it a series
of enjoyable family events.
Last year, activities ranged from a Succot party, which also addressed hunger in the community, to a Bar/Bat Mitzvah party, in which
children and their parents were required to crystalize their values and understanding of the process of maturing Jewishly. Other
highlights included making ritual objects, with an appreciation of their history, and minicourses in some of Judaism’s lesser appreciated,
but no less important, holidays.
Officers of the Family Council going into the 1990 season were Peggy Organes, chairman; Sheree Spriggs, secretary; David Schmerer,
programs; Stevie Levine, publicity; George and Ellen Rubens, chroniclers; Beverly Barnhard, art; and Henry Blumenthal, alternate board
representative. With continuing emphasis on expanding participation in the planning process, more posts may be created as needed.
