] he serious crisis experienced by the Zionist movement during the past
year, following the issuance by Great Britain of the White Paper, had an
unexpected effect upon all workers unto Zion. Instead of resulting in the
adoption of a defeatist attit ude, Zionist leaders were heartened to note a
renewed vigor and stubborn determination on the part of all Jews to continue
working for the achievement of the Zionist ideal.
I he Zionist youth ol Jacksonville, grouped in the four local Young
Judean clubs, reacted to the crisis in no different manner. I hroughout the
year, under the inspiring leadership of their sponsors, Young Judeans worked
enthusiastically; they debated on pertinent problems in Zionism, collected
funds for the purchase of land in Palestine, gave parties, organized outings,
and participated in the competitive Merit System, devised to familiarize them
with Zionist personalities, Palestinian songs and dances, and enrich their
knowledge of the Hebrew language and the geography of Pretz Israel.
Along the religious lines, Young Judea sponsored a Friday ni ght service
at the Center, at which time Rabbi Margolis spoke on “H as Zionism Failed?
Members of Rothschild Young Judea gathered at a banquet held on the
evening of May 19 in Begal s Restaurant where Philip Selber, member of the
Young Judean Council, addressed the participants on the subject of Palestine.
During the summer, several Y. J. members ol the Henrietta Szold Club,
