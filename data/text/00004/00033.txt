With the continuation of hard work, Troop 2, Center Girl Scout troop,
pleted another year of progress.
Under the leadership of Mrs. N. V. Gross with the assistance of Mrs.
H. Haber and Mrs. J. S. Gallinger, thirteen girls passed their second ci ass
requirements and will receive badges. Now they are ready to begin work
on their First Glass badges. Many of the girls have also earned stars and
other badges, for their fine work during the year.
Much credit for these girls fine achievement in obtaining these awards
is due to the Daughters of Israel, their sponsors, who willingly financed their
many needs.
The troop had a Weiner Roast at the Z,oo picnic grounds, visited the
Telephone Company and Southern Dairies. They sponsored Friday evening
services during National Girl Scout Week, with a talk by Rabbi Morris D.
Margolis and a short address by Miss Frances Gregory, Girl Scout director
of Jacksonville. Also during the wee k Rabbi Margol is presented a program
on the radio dedicated to all the Girl Scouts of America. J he entire troop
