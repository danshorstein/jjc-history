After nearly eight years of earnest
and intensive work on behalf of charity,
cultural enlightment, assistance of needy
students and the Zionist cause, the Jew¬
ish Progrssive Society, lately operating
under the name of Zionist Progressive
Society, gave up its Zionist charter in
order to facilitate the establishment of
a local Zionist district, devoted exclusive¬
ly to Zionist work.
Although the past year marked the
end of the organization as such, the
Zionist Progressive Society showed no
let down in its activities. Under the
efficient leadership of its founder and
president, Mr. I. M. Lieberman, the
Society presented an outstanding and
varied program.
o n D ecember 14, in collaboration with the local Hadassah, the Society
sponsored a talk by Adrian Schwartz, ardent Zionist worker, who was visiting
the United States on a tour of lectures.
In this same month of December, the Progressives had their installation
of officers banquet. A success ful “I atke and card party was held during the
Chanukah festival, on the evening of December 25, at which time a radio
was raffled off.
Recognition of the Society s efforts for Palestine came on February 8,
1939, when it received its charter from the National office of the Zionist
Organization of America.
In June, two delegates from the 1 ocal group attended the Zionist Organi¬
zation of America convention which was held in New York. 1 he delegates were
Mr. Harry Sachs and Mr. Philip Selber, who upon their return presented a
most colorful and enlightening report of the convention.
During the Shekel campaign, members of the society helped substantially
to assure its success. 511 Shekels were sold and a check for $263.00 was
mailed to the Central Shekel Committee. J he local chairman of the campaign
was Mr. Joseph Robin.
Mr. I. M. Lieberman and officers of the Progressive Zionist Society
wish to thank all of its members, local organizations and the Board of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center for the splendid cooperation they have given to
make the life of the society one of substantial and worthy accomplishment.
They most heartily urge all of their former members to join the new Zionist
group and to continue the work for the establishment of a political and cultural
center for the Jews in Palestine.
The organization will continue to function as a society still maintaining
their varied cultural, charitable and welfare program. Election of officers will
take place with the opening of the Fall season.
