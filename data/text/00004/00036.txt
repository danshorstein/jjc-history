J he Jewish Men’s Club of Jacksonville has just completed its second
year of fine work in the community. Under the able leadership o f Nat
Shorstein, the membership during the past year increased to over two hundred.
During the past year the Men s Club gave its first annual spring dance
at the D uval County Armory on April 11th, to raise funds to build a recreat¬
ional unit in this city. I his affair was very successful.
I he club has assumed the responsibi Iity of maintaining a diamond ball
league and for the first time in the history of local Jewish diamond ball leagues
there was a regularly scheduled split- half season, featuring professional um¬
piring. J he A.Z.A. team, winner of the first half, was declared champions
by reason of their victory over the Esquire Club, second half winners. Other
teams entered represented the Jewish Men’s Club and the Southern Jewish
Weekly. Sam Bergman acted as president of the league. On February 7th
o f this year the Men’s Club held its first installation Ball. This affair also
proved to be very successful. A number of interesting debates and cultural
programs were also presented at the regular meetings. J he organization also
sponsored Friday evening services. During the coming year the Club expects
to further its plans in securing funds for a recreational unit. It is the hope
of the organization that each and every person in Jacksonville will become
a member of the club.
