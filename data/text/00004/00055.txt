I he Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society and
Home for 1 he Aged was organized for the purpose
of giving shelter and aid to transients, and supplying
them with Kosher Food and lodging and relieving
the needs of local indigents. J he Society maintains
a Free Loan Fund, the Omiles Chesed, whereby
many families are enabled to help themselves over
a financial crisis. J he Society also gives immigrant
aid wherever possible.
I he Lad ies Aid S ociety also aims to establish
a Home for the Aged, a Moshev Zekenin, towards
th e achievement of which it is making steady pro¬
gress. Last year, the Joint Home Committee pur¬
chased two desirable lots to be used for the erection
of an Old Aged Ho me. The site of the property is
the northwest corner of 5th and Perry Streets. A
Campaign Committee was chosen with Mr. Ira Stein,
temporary chairman and Mrs. Leo Michelson, tem¬
porary secretary. Plans are in effect to attempt the
erection of the home in the very near future.
The annual reports given at the installation of
officers during the early part of the year have shown
most unusual progress, stressing the methods used by
the Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society in pro¬
viding the needy with food, clothing, fuel, medicine,
etc., without any embarrassment and loss of self-
respect.
