Each Sabbath morning, while services are being conducted in the main
Synagogue, children of the Center s religious schools gather in the small syna¬
gogue and hold separate services under the supervision of two members of
the Hebrew school staff.
I he ritual consists of selections in English and Hebrew from the prayer-
book, congregational singing and responsive reading. In this way, the Junior
Congregation has been instrumental in developing our children s knowledge
of Hebrew.
During the past year, weekly “sermonettes” dealing with highlights of the
Bible were delivered by the attending instructors, Mr. M. Nimovitz, and Mr. J.
S. Gallinger, the Rabbi and selected members of the congregation. These
talks, especially adapted for youngsters greatly increased their understanding
of Jewish history and the basic principles of Jewish Law.
By electing officers, cantor, sexton, and page announcer from their own
midst, children are being prepared to conduct their own services. Th is year a
special class lor the training of Junior cantors was formed and conducted on
Friday afternoons by Mr. Morris Nimovitz.
Every major and minor Jewish holiday is appropriately celebrated by mem¬
bers of Junior Congregation. When there is a Bar Mitzvah ceremony, the
juniors adjourn to the main synagogue for the rites and the Musaf service.
On the occasion of certain holidays, the Juniors are tendered receptions
by the mothers. Last summer its members had a much-enjoyed outing to the
