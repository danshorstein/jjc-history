JOEL GOLDMAN
In addition to achieving over a 50% increase in membership, the B’nai B’rith A. B. Weil Lodge #800 has increased its service
activities in Jacksonville area in quality and in number.
Two additional teams and new uniforms were added to the Little League Baseball program. An increase in the need for the services of
the B’nai B’rith scholarship loan fund caused the Lodge to undertake an emergency fund drive so as not to refuse any request for funds
to attend college by worthy students. The Anti Defamation League, contributed to increased fellowship and understanding in this area.
Maximum assistance and cooperation has been given to the fledgling Hillel group at Jacksonville University and B.B.Y.O. is catering to
the needs of young teen agers.
The luncheon meetings are continuing at the George Washington Hotel every second and fourth Thursday. Most meetings are high¬
lighted by guest speakers and all interested persons are invited to attend.

MRS. JACK DORMAN
B’nai B’rith Women is an organization of women interested in local and national problems. Among the local projects are anti-Defama-
tion, Philanthropy, Pinecastle School for Retarded Children, Hillel, B’nai B’rith Youth, River Garden Hebrew Home, Muscular Dystrophy
Assoc., Cancer Society, Tuberculosis and Cerebral Palsy.
National allocations go to Israel, Leo N. Levi and the National Jewish Hospital, Bellefaire Children’s Home in Cleveland, Children’s
Home is Israel, etc.
Our meetings are held monthly on the 4th Wednesday of each month. We are striving to increase membership through B’nai B’rith
indoctrination classes and interesting programs.
The opening Luncheon and Fashion Show will be held on September 30th, and will feature clothing from several leading stores.
