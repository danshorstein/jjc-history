MRS. A. L. SCHNEIDER
The Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah is composed of approximately 800 Jewish women who meet the second Tuesday of each month
to serve together in the ranks of the American Zionist movement. Hadassah’s members join in efforts to affect the national Hadassah
program of health, vocational and education services to Israel and the dedication of our local women to active participation in the life and
expression of American community affairs.
With more than 310,000 members in 1250 chapters and units in the United States and Puerto Rico, Hadassah today is the world’s
largest Zionist organization. While its pioneering efforts in medicine and social welfare have permeated every facet of Israeli life, it has
conducted significant activities on the American scene to foster creative and intelligent living through education programs stimulating
understanding of the Jewish cultural heritage. At the same time, Hadassah participates in efforts to safeguard democracy and support of
the United Nations.
Since its founding, Hadassah has raised more than $115,000,000.00 in behalf of the Jews of Palestine and Israel. This is a tremendous
contribution—one that was made possible only through the selfless devotion of Hadassah’s dedicated members. Together with Z.O.A.,
Hadassah supports the Jewish National Fund for the rehabilitation of the land of Israel and Zionist youth work through Young
Judaean Clubs.
Outstanding events of the coming year will be the opening luncheon to be held at the Center on September 8, the Membership Tea
on November 10 and a Tourish luncheon which will be the featured attraction of the month of January. The month of February will high¬
light Hadassah Hospital supplies, while March and April will be devoted to programs honoring contributors and donors to Hadassah’s
projects. The closing meeting will be held May 12th.
Hannah Senesch Group
The Hannah Senesch Evening Group of Hadassah, as part
of the Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah, has given those
women who can attend only evening meetings an opportunity
to participate fully in National Hadassah’s diversified program.
Membership is open to all Jewish women, with meetings on
the second Monday of every month.
