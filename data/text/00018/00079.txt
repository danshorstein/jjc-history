Cub Scouts Pack 14
The Cub Scouts Pack 14 of the Jacksonville Jewish Center held a very
successful year. In February 50 Cub Scouts were registered at Scout
Headquarters.
Pack meetings are held monthly at the Center. The dens meet weekly
and prepare an exhibit for their monthly project on the theme of the month.
One of the successful events of the year was a Carnival held in the
Park across the street from the Center. The entire Cub Pack were guests
of the Jacksonville Baseball Club. The annual Father and Son Banquet
was held at the Center in March.
Two boys became Webelos during the year — Stewart Hecht and
Leon Yergin.
We look forward to another enjoyable year for the boys as well
as parents. C. CLARENCE GHELERTER

A new program of the Boy Scouts of America was organized this year, Explorer Post No. 14. The Charter was pre¬
sented at the annual Boy Scout Charter Presentation Banquet.
The Explorer program is for boys 15 to 18. Among the activities last year was the Explorer Ball, a cruise on the
U.S.S. Bronson Navy Destroyer and camping trips.
Victor Sultan is the Post Advisor, assisted by George Glossman. The Committee consists of Harry Kolitz, Chairman,
Morris Leibovitz and Shelby Trager.
