BRUCE WITTEN
This past A.Z.A., the junior order of B’nai B’rith, had a very successful year, one of the most outstanding in its history.
Our chapter, along with our sister B.B.G.’s, were host this year to the North Florida Council Convention held in April.
We had two big dances and much of the credit for their success goes to our sweetheart, Marsha Leibovitz. Marsha has also helped
us plan many fine monthly get-togethers.
For community service we passed a jug every week in which boys put money to aid in the fight against Cancer. We also helped
in the Cerebral Palsy Telethon held last winter.
This past summer many of our members attended the District Convention in North Carolina, and we are proud of our own Jerry
Rothstein who was elected District Aleph Godol.
Gator Chapter 272 is looking forward to another successful year under its new administration and under the guidance of our two
advisors, Sollie Safer and Sandy Whitman.

The B’nai B’rith Girls carried out a successful program of activities for the past year. The chapter contributed to the March of Dimes
and to Cerebral Palsy drive.
Social events included a couple of beach parties, slumber parties and a “Come-as-you-are” breakfast. Several members attended the
Regional Convention in Miami, where prizes were won for the Jacksonville scrapbook and chapter song.
In April the B.B.G. were host to the North Fla. Region. Edna Steinfeld, former President, were elected Girls’ Vice-President of the
Council.
