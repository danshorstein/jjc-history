Young Judaea
Young Judaea concluded a successful year of activity with two Junior clubs and two
Senior clubs; Judaeanettes, Ruthettes, Zionettes and the Estherettes, under the direction of
the Youth Commission with Mrs. Norman Moss as Chairman and Mrs. Irving Brukner
as City Director.
City-wide events included the Annual Sabbath Service held at the Center during
December 1958 at which time the Presidents of each club participated and the parents acted
as hostesses. In March 1959 the Annual Israeli Fair for the benefit of the Scouts in Israel
was held on the grounds of the River Garden Home for the Aged. A talent show was
presented in addition to the many booths set up for display and for the sale of many
articles. The third annual event was the closing affair, the Bikkurim Festival and Banquet
held at the Center. Awards and honors were given to various members. Tel Yehudah
scholarships were given to Judy Bossen, Marilyn Halpern, Elaine Brukner and Byron Kolitz.
An outstanding event of the year was the Zionettes’ performance of Israel songs and
dances at the Stephen Foster Memorial Festival at White Springs in May. The Zionettes
also participated in Israeli dances at the Flannah Senesch Evening Group of Hadassah in
January and the Estherettes presented a medly of Israeli songs. The Winter Conclave was
held in Daytona Beach. Jacksonville Senior Judaea was host to Eddie Berman, National
President of Young Judaea, at a large rally in the Center Auditorium.
The newest group to join Young Judaea is the Ruthette Club for 11 yr. old girls and
is directed by Mrs. M. Goldstein. Nancy Moss served as Sr. Judaean leader with this group.
The Judaeanette Club for boys and girls ages 10, 11 and 12 met under the direction
of Mrs. Brukner and Mrs. Mitchell Schmer with Elaine Bruckner as Youth leader. Each of
the groups had a full year of activities and social events.
During the summer Camp Judaea was held at Camp Blue Star, Hendersonville, N. Car.
and was attended by Kay Kluglose, Judy Wilensky, Evelyn Goldstein and Phyllis Chamison.
Mrs. Irving Brukner retired as City Director of Young Judaea at the Bikkurim Festival
and Miss Sybil Horovitz was appointed new City Director. Mrs. Norman Moss, Chairman
of the Youth Commission, also retired and will be replaced by Mrs. Charles Kluglose.
