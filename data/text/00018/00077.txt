Boy Scout Troop 14
DR. HARRY YERGIN
From a small group of 14 boys Troop 14 has grown to a strong troop of ap¬
proximately 45 boys under the leadership of Dr. Harry Yergin, Scoutmaster and Morris
Leibovitz, Chairman of the Committee.
The season opened with a wiener roast and swimming party. Overnight hikes were
held at the Scout Camp Echochetee where the boys worked on their advancements, climaxed
by a 3 day hike to Silver Springs. Sabbath services were conducted under the open sky by
Senior Patrol Leader Simon Selber. Marvin Slott was inducted in the Order of the Arrow.
The Ner Tamid award was presented to Eddie Mizrahi at Scout Sabbath.
The award of Eagle Scout was presented to Steve Halpert at the Mohawk District Court
of Honor held at the Center. The Bernard Glossman Award to the outstanding Scout of the
year and Simon Rothstein Plaque as the best Scout of 1959 were presented to Bruce Yergin.
The Flying Eagle Patrol led by Patrol Leader Bruce Yergin and consisting of Scouts
Charles Moskovitz, Jay Trussell, Frank Moss, Bobbie Libby and Harry Shmunes were
awarded medals as the patrol having the best attendance of the year, most advancements in
merit badges and showing the best Scout spirit.
