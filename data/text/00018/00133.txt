IRVING KLEPPER
Recognition of the “Home” as a haven for the aged and infirm has been widely accepted by the Jewish communities of Florida.
Thirteen years of devoted services to over 200 residents have demonstrated the worth and need of a Jewish Home for the Aged in the
State of Florida.
The story of the growth of the Home could be told in two different ways. The first is its physical growth from a wooden frame
residence housing eleven residents to two modern brick buildings providing all the facilities, including air-conditioning, for 72 residents
who enjoy comfortable and gracious living. The emphasis upon individualizing services has resulted in planning each building or wing
to meet the special needs of the individuals in that section. Whether incidental personal services or intensive nursing care is required, each
area is geared to encourage the individual to live to the maximum of his capacity.
The other aspect of development is the changing concept of services in a Home for the Aged. The Moshav Zkenim of yesteryear
provided food, lodging and little else. Today we recognize the needs of the aged in the “Home” are the same as those in the community.
Added to the basic services of shelter and care is an enrichment program to round out the patterns of daily living. Whether it is the
weekly Bingo game, a social affair, Chapel services, Bible study, Hobby Shop or publishing their own periodical, the end result is a warm
and rich environment which enables the older person to continue living an active and interesting life.
Life at River Garden continues to be dynamic and meaningful because the Home has become a miniature community with all the
resources that exist in a community: This enables the residents to continue in sharing the rewards as well as the responsibilities for com¬
munal living.
