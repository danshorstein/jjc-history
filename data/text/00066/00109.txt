Troop 14 completed
yet another productive and
adventurous year. The
troop hiked, fished,
canoed, camped,
completed numerous merit
badges and followed the
leadership of five of our
troop members who
attained the rank of Eagle
Scout, the highest level of
scouting.
The 2007 Eagle
Scouts are: Brett Jackrel,
son of Debra & Ted Jackrel, Benjamin Hecht, son of
Rose & Larry Hecht, Bradley Goldring, son of Stacey
& Bruce Goldring, Cory Brenenson, son of Estelle
& Scott Brenenson and Max Rogozinski, son of Randi
& Sam Rogozinski.
Troop 14 attended summer camp here in town and
traveled to Camp Daniel Boone in North Carolina. The
boys worked hard on their merit badges. The camp in
noted for its
intense scrutiny of
merit badge
advancement. In
the qualification
for rock climbing,
Harrison Phillips
was awarded for
his excellence.
Geoffrey Berlin,
Sanford Vining,
Zach Sandler,
Charles Pett and
Jeffrey Golding
all earned merit
badges ranging from communication, leadership, archery
and craft.
Meetings and events
always run smoothly
thanks to Richard Ford
and his staff, Scout Billy
Wilensky and every parent
volunteer.
The troop’s remarkable
dedication to the path of Eagle Rank
is recognized throughout the district
and at a national level. Because of
this, Scoutmaster Louis Schwartz
was honored for his decade of
leadership to Troop 14 with two
awards. In recognition for these
achievements,
Larry Hecht, on
behalf of the National Boy Scout
Council, presented Louis with BSA’s
Shofar Award and the Presidential
Scoutmaster Award. Louis will now
move on to represent our troop on a
council-wide level.
Boys ages 11 to 18 are encouraged
become part of the proud tradition of
scouting at the Jacksonville Jewish
Center. Attend our meetings on
Thursday evenings at 7 p.m. in the
Center’s Neal F. Portnoy Youth Lounge.
— Stacey Goldring
