The National Council of Jewish Women is an organiza¬
tion which, in the spirit of Judaism is dedicated to further¬
ing human welfare in the Jewish and general communities,
locally, nationally, and internationally. Through an integrated
program of education, service and social action, it provides
essential services and stimulates and educates the individual
and the community toward their responsibility in advancing
human welfare and the democratic way of life.
It is in the spirit of Faith and Humanity that the Jackson¬
ville Section, Inc., carries out the following projects in our
community.
1. Mental Health Forum — Our annual program and sem¬
inar, jointly sponsored with the Mental Health Associa¬
tion of Duval County, increases public understanding in
this field.
2. Medical Loan Shelf — We supply hospital and sick room
equipment to the indigent of our community at no cost
to the individual. We are the only source of this equipment
in the community at the present time.
3. Pre~School Vision Screening — Our trained volunteers
test the eyes of Pre-School Children in Duval County for
Amblyopia (lazy-eye blindness). Private kindergartens,
Federal and GJEO school are included. We were the first of
several participating organizations to conduct this service.
4. Teacher s Aid — Our trained members aid classroom teach¬
ers with academic instructions of children with speech and
hearing handicaps.
Mrs. Joseph Mason
5. Senior Citizens Program — Under the direction and sup¬
ervision of Council volunteers, residents of the Garden
Home make hand-sewn layettes, which are distributed
through local welfare agencies for needy families in our
community.
7. Overseas Program — Through the Ship-a-Box Committee,
we send work and play materials to the kindergartens in
Israel. This is done by contributions of toys from our mem¬
bers and the children of the Sunday Schools of our Jewish
Community.
6. V.I.P S. — Volunteers in Public Schools.
8. Project ‘'PACT” — (Portable Articles for Children and
Teachers) Working in cooperation with the Children’s
Museum, Council volunteers will assembly portable dis¬
plays to be presented in the classrooms of the Duval Coun¬
ty schools, to augment their Social Studies program. It is
for the project, that the Jacksonville Section of National
Council of Jewish Women was given the 1970 “EVE”
Award, in the field of Education, by the Florida Publish¬
ing Company.
