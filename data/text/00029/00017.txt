Message from the President
Dear Friends:
The new decade of the seventies offers mankind
many new and exciting challenges in science, medicine,
social reform and education. Our sacred domain is that
of religion, the art of achieving the good life. Here too
we envisage new vistas to be conquered in the decade
ahead. In the words of the late Chief Rabbi of Eretz
Yisrael, Abraham Isaac Kook, “the old must be re¬
newed and the new must become sanctified.”
With our new dynamic Rabbi, Dov Peretz Elkins, our
new energetic executive director, Sidney Cohen, and
the sacred privilege and responsibility of planning and
building a new synagogue center on State Road 13, I
feel that we are on the threshold of an era unparalleled
in our Jewish community. We must all share together
the opportunity of meeting our new challenges with a
new spirit of accomplishment, of generosity, and of
dedication to the principles of our Faith.
WILBUR MARGOL, President
I know that our congregation will continue in the
year ahead to make an important contribution to Jew¬
ish life in the Jacksonville community and to the wel¬
fare of all men.
May this New Year bring you and all your loved ones good health,
contentment and peace. May mankind bring about a return to dignity for
all men and trust in God.
President
PHILIP SELBER
Chairman of the Board
On behalf of the Officers and members of the
Board of Directors and the Board of Trustees, may we
extend greetings to all of our members for a Happy
New Year, in the traditional words of
“LE SHANAH TOVAH TIKATEVU.”
