“On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to
God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to
help other people at all times; to keep myself physically
strong, mentally awake, and morally straight.”
With these words, Boy Scouts of Troop 14 dedi¬
cate themselves to the principles of the Boy Scouts of
America, an organization which has continued to grow
since its birth in 1909 to over 33 million boys today.
Troop 14, the third troop to be organized in Jack¬
sonville, celebrated its Fiftieth Anniversary on Febru¬
ary 22, 1970. At this time honors were given to all
Scoutmasters, Charter members and Eagle Scouts.
1969-70 was a very busy year for Troop 14. Led
by Scoutmaster Stephen Hackel, members of the
Troop participated in camping trips to Anastasia State
Park and the Center Southside Site where they ac¬
quired the knowledge necessary for advancements.
Scouting is available for all boys between the ages
of 11 through high school. Weekly meetings were held
at the Center and patrols met with their leaders in
various neighborhoods..
