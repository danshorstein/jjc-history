The Center Day Camp located on the Center's
Southside Site at 10149 State Road No. 13 has been
in continuous operation since 1946.
During nine weeks of the summer, children of the
Center membership and of the entire Jewish commu¬
nity have available to them the finest facilities for fun
and growth in this area.
The varied program of activities include swimming
in the Olympic pool, skiing, all active sports, arts and
crafts, singing and dancing, horseback riding, field
trips to places of interest, ice skating and roller skat¬
ing.
During the past summer names of the groups were
changed to represent the Tribes of Israel and most at¬
tractive identification, were placed in the area of each
group.
Jewish values are an integral part of the Camp pro¬
gram and campers participate in the blessings before
and after meals, enjoy a Shabbat program, complete
with Kiddush, challah, Israeli singing and dancing.
Tisha B’AB was observed during the morning with a
presentation by Rabbi Elkins which made all aware of
the importance of remembering this tragic day in the
history of the Jewish people.
We look forward to many new concepts for the
Day Camp in 1971 and hope that you will include our
Camp in the summer plans for your children.
