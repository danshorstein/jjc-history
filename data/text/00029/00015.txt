The Rabbi’s Page
Dear Friends:
I feel highly privileged to be part of the re¬
newed and reinvigorated Jacksonville Jewish Cen¬
ter. Every institution has periods of stability and
periods of rapid growth. The next few years will
be, without doubt, years of rapid growth for our
congregation, and I find this to be a great chal¬
lenge for me as spiritual leader of such a growing
congregation.
The growth I refer to is not necessarily phy¬
sical growth, growth in numbers of members, (al¬
though it usually attends such growth). The
development I refer to is in spirit, in program, in
devotion, and in form. There seems to be a spirit
of renewal in the air for our synagogue center. The
excitement of all those working on our many new
programs is becoming contagious.
As I meet with our committees, as I speak to
individuals, I sense a spirit of devotion and dedi¬
cation, and a deep sense of mission. I sense a desire
to make Judaism come alive for the generation of
the 1970 as it never has before in America. I sense
an urge for a creative, vibrant, alive and dynamic
religious community.
I see this renewed vigor not only in the
growth of our Solomon Schechter Day School from
less than 100 students to almost double its former
size; not only in the rapid progress we are making
in planning for our new religious center in South
Jacksonville; not only in the growth of our staff
RABBI DOY PERETZ ELKINS
and the creativity of our programming; not only in
the youthfulness of our professional and lay lead¬
ers, but in everything that is associated with the
Jacksonville Jewish Center.
Elaine and I and our children, in the few short
months we have been here, feel that we will be
very happy in Jacksonville, working together with
all of you in helping to bring about a Jewish com¬
munity in which the ancient spirit of Judaism will
burn strongly in the hearts of all its adherents.
Faithfully,
Rabbi
