ATID is the College Age Organization of Conser¬
vative Judaism sponsored by the United Synagogue
of America. It was formed and is led by college stu¬
dents and college-agers who want to live and learn
Judaism and continue their identification with the
Conservative Movement.
ATID invites those who are between 18 and 24 who
wish to involve themselves in Jewish life and thought.
ATID Chapters are formed on campuses or within
Conservative Congregations.
Local ATIDniks had a most successful summer
with get-togethers each Wednesday at different homes
with varied and informative topics discussed as: Black
Heritage, Archeology in Israel, Jewish Defense League,
Suicide Prevention, Soviet Anti-Semitism and the
Arab Refugee Problem.
Members also participated in the Shabbat Services
at the Center Southside Site on August 8th and ended
the season with a well attended social affair.
KAREN GARTNER,
Chairman
