The overall capacity of the Home is 185 beds,
divided into 6 nursing units averaging 30 residents per
unit. The accent is on the wellness of the person rather
than his illness.
The Home is accredited by the Joint Commission
of Hospital and Related Health Institutions and ap¬
proved for Medicare and Medicaid patients by the
Division of Health and Rehabilitation of the State of
Florida.
The Home’s emphasis is on a total Jewish way
of life to substitute for the family who is no longer
able to care for their aged. The Synagogue, the Holi¬
day observations, and the Sabbath observation creates
this climate of Jewishness.
The other components of human needs are the
social and personal relationships which develop from
sharing with another human being.
A broad range of programs, arts and crafts and
even drama, produces this sense of sharing and belong¬
ing. This year’s production of Carmen Shapiro by the
River Garden Players was as well received as last
year’s Fiddler on the Roof.

ices to the aging of Florida. There has been a rather
significant growth of the Home since March, 1946
when it first opened its doors.
With the opening of the Coleman addition, River
Garden can anticipate even greater achievement in
this our Silver Anniversary year.
