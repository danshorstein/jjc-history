LEONARD COHN
The year 1969 has been a successful one for A. B.
Weil Lodge. During the year, a further study of Hillel
on the J. U. campus was made, and as a result we
hope to offer substantially greater assistance to this
group in the coming college year.
Our local A. B. Weil scholarship fund was again
instrumental in helping to provide a college education
to some of our local youngsters, who might otherwise
not have been able to continue with their education;
and we hope to expand our activities in this field in
the coming year and thereby offer assistance to a
greater number of prospective college students.
The A.D.L. committee has substantiaUy increased
its efforts due to the rising tensions and pressures at
home and abroad. This being an election year, the
problems are readily evident.
There has been a renewed interest in the Lodge
by many of our more reliable stalwarts, ably assisted
by some of our more active newer workers; and as a
result, it appears that the new slate of officers will be
able to show a very substantial increase in member¬
ship, along with increased activity and accomplishment
in the regular endeavors of the A. B. Weil Lodge.
