The Solomon Schechter Day School PTA provides four
human components: parents, teachers, administrators, and stu-
dents, the opportunity to work together for a common cause—our
schools.
The PTA continues to sponsor KALEIDOSCOPE, a day
devoted to the arts. Our on-going BOOK-A-BRATION this past
year has provided over 600 volumes tot he Henry Kramer Library.
The SHABBAT BOX program continues to help bring learning
home from school. Through the generosity of Publix and Albertson’s,
additional computer hardware and software are in place in our
classrooms.
The PTA’s fundraising projects, which include the maga-
zine and candy sales and book fairs, enable us to provide the
financial means to obtain much needed equipment and supplies for
the Day School and Pre-school. Our newest project, the aluminum
can drive has been so successful, it will be continued.
As Kahlil Gibran states in The Prophet, “...the vision of
one man lends not its wings to another man. ” Perhaps, by bringing
together the four human components of the PTA, the vision of all
will lend itself to all.
