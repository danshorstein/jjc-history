Cub Scout Pack 14
Cub Scout Pack #14 continued its tradition of interesting and rewarding events throughout the year. Our
great group of boys, ranging in age from 2nd through the 5th grade, met in one of the four dens once a week. Pack
meetings with all the boys and their families were held once a month.
Cub Scouts aim high: at developing good character, spiritual growth, good citizenship, sportsmanship,
comaradarie with other boys, and a sense of personal achievement. Thanks to our extremely competent and caring
den leaders, the boys have earned many badges of achievement while having lots of fun. Several cubs have begun
work on their Aleph Badge which is awarded through the National Committee on Jewish Scouting.
Our Pack Meetings have included: the exciting Pinewood Derby; annual Blue and Gold Banquet with guest
speaker, Sam Kouvaris; a mock game show with parents who shared their knowledge of U.S. presidents; a repre-
sentative from the Mayor’s office in charge of the conservation project, “Keep Jacksonville Beautiful”; and a com-
munications expert demonstrating the latest innovations. We added two very successful Sunday family sporting
events—a soccer game and baseball game. Our two older Webelo dens camped out with the Boy Scouts. This event
earned the Cubs many badges and a true desire to continue in the Boy Scout program. Led by Sandy Kadish, the
Cub Scouts, Boy Scouts, and Brownies participated in a moving and rewarding Scout Sabbath. The year ended with
a favorite—our annual father-son campout, preceded by a family dinner and swim at the Center’s Recreational Park.
Along with the den leaders, our success was due to the hard work of the Pack Committee which serves as a
planning and organizing group. Our thanks to: Lanny Zimmerman who celebrated his 10th year as Cubmaster of
Pack 14. Please call the Center office if you would like to volunteer in the Cub Scout program.
Yours in Scouting,
Carole Goldberg, Pack Committee Chairman.
