MOSAIC: Jewish Life in Florida
is coming to Jacksonville
Museum of Science and History
March 17-May 31,1991
MOSAIC is the first comprehensive project to retrieve, document, and preserve the
history of the Jewish experience in Florida from 1763 to the present. The immediate
goal is a 2,000 square feet modular exhibit which opens in Miami October 19, 1990,
and travels to seven other cities in Florida through 1992. The exhibit contains nearly
400 photographs and 80 artifacts, loaned by Florida Jewish families, synagogues, and
organizations, and state historical archives.
MOSAIC, the traveling exhibit, will discuss the themes of where Jews came from, what
they did, the families and institutions they formed, how they interacted with and for their
communities, and significant contributions Jews made to the development of Florida.
Visitors will also gain knowledge about Jewish traditions and the heritage Jews strive
to pass on to future generations.
