That’s What a Synagogue is For!
All these items were donated by
Center members and friends of the
Center. Money was collected sent as
well. At least in some small way the
Center was able to help another
synagogue family celebrate the New
Year.
Children of the Center schools created
New Year cards to send with the
donations to South Carolina. The cards
were just precious and reflected a
sincere sense of caring and concern.
This was a real learning experience for
the children and another lesson in
mitzvot. The same week they also
brought food, water and stuffed animals
to the school to be sent to South
Carolina.
On Wednesday, Rabbi Kentof and his
band of merry shofar blowers visited the
local hospitals. What a sight! The
Jewish patients were teary-eyed as the
children blew the shofar to usher in the
holiday.
What I have shared with you are only a
few of the wonderful things that are
continually happening at the
Jacksonville Jewish Center.
Caring and sharing...that's what a
synagogue's for.
--Isabel Balotin, Editor
