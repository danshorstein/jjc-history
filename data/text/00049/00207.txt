Torah Lishmah
Genesis One constructed the history of life as inherently directed towards a better society. Yet there has
never been an age when mankind was not frustrated by endless conflicts and even greater means for self-
destruction. The modem world is teetering on the edge of total collapse. Perhaps the greatest challenge remains
the same: the recognition that life is a gift for which we owe thanks and effort to restore the origninal direction.
No person, no nation, no government has the right to distort God’s plan for its own benefit only, or to do as
they please. If this is true, it is a biblical lesson, not a scientific one! As we read the Torah from week to week
we find frequent examples of the basic truths. Those participants who help us read the Torah absorb these
reverential features which give us a more hopeful outlook for the future. It can also be helpful towards blunting
the impact of “heavy metal” on the personalities of our young people.
This past year saw a more intensive arrangement of class and havurah members taking responsibility for
all the readings on special occasions. We are especially grateful to the doctors and attorneys and other
professionals who take on the longer readings and perform on a professional basis as they would in their own
practices. Little did they know that when they entered the program it would become a lifetime occupation for
the highest goal in Judaism. Torah Lishmah- for its own sake, it own reward. We are thankful to those groups.
Ted Gross,
Coordinator of Torah Readers
