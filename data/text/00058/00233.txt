American Red Magen David for Israel
Founded in 1930 as a First Aid Society, Magen David
Adorn has become the State of Israel’s emergency medical,
disaster, ambulance and healthcare network. 365 days a year,
24 hours a day, every person in every corner of Israel looks to
Magen David Adorn to perform the usual tasks carried out by
Red Cross Societies throughout the world. Within minutes,
MDA's rescue units arrive on the scene for medical
emergencies, accidents and, unfortunately, terrorist bombings.
Trained paramedics administer immediate help, and then
transport the patient to the hospital if necessary.
Mitzvah Chapter’s Annual Raffle proceeds help support
the Emanuel Celler First Aid Station in Haifa.
Even though the symbol of Magen David Adorn, a red
Star of David, is recognized by the American Red Cross,
efforts are still going on to achieve international acceptance
of our symbol.
As part of our continuing efforts to raise every possible
dollar, we have arranged for anyone who can, to donate their
car, van, motorhome or boat—running or not!—to benefit
ARMDI. Its fully tax deductible. Call (800) 550-3333 and
mention ARMDI for details and/or pickup.
You can show your support for the State of Israel for
only $18 (chai - life), and do the mitzvah of pikkuach nefesh
- saving a soul, by joining today. “For us it’s only a check, for
them it could mean life itself.”

Brandéis University
National Women’s Committee
The mission of the Brandéis University National
Women’s Committee is to provide financial support and
growth for the Brandéis University Libraries, which serve a
community of students and scholars, in education and
research, united by their commitment to the pursuit of
knowledge. The National Women’s Committee offers its
members opportunity for intellectual pursuit, continuing
education, community service, social interaction, personal
enrichment, and leadership development. The members act
as emissaries to support and enhance the image of Brandéis,
a Jewish-sponsored, non-sectarian university where
excellence is the tradition.
For more information, call Norma Levy, 273-1575.
