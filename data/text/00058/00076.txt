There once was a mensch named Dov Kentof,
who came here from points north and south.
He’s taught our children with stories
that have come from his head to his mouth.
There surely is someone in heaven,
who looked down on Jax from above,
and knew that we needed a person
whose name was Dov.
With Hebrew and history and Torah
he taught us by showing the way,
to do mitzvot and care for each other
and to do something good each day.
He came to us as Noah and Moses,
carrying his rod and his staff.
After goats and donkeys and chickens,
we’re waiting for a visit from a giraffe!
He set examples for Jewish living,
whether in campsites or in his back yard.
To observe Shabbat and kashruth is easy,
and he makes us think really hard.
LTI and Kadima conventions
were always the highlight of our year.
Even if our homes were overflowing,
our children would stand up and cheer.
Our lives you have influenced so often,
our children have gained oh so much.
How can we all ever thank you,
for each one of us you’ve managed to touch.
