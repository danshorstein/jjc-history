Simhat Torah Honorees
The High Holiday season reaches its joyous climax on Simhat Torah, the last day of the festival
of Sukkot. Because of our perennial love of the Torah, it has been referred to as Israel’s “bride”. On
Simhat Torah, we bring to a close the annual cycle of the reading of the Torah and immediately
begin anew. An ancient tradition evolved of honoring worthy men by calling them to the Torah for
the last reading, Hatan Torah, and the beginning portion, Hatan Bereshit. In our synagogue tradition,
we also honor the contributions made by a “Woman of Valor”.
Sol Goldman
Hatan Torah
Sol was born in
Jacksonville, the
son of Russian
immigrants who
worked hard to
support their family
and insisted he
receive a Jewish
education. Sol has
always been a member of the Center. He
celebrated his Bar Mitzvah at the 3rd &
Silver St. shul.
Sol’s father was a kosher butcher
and taught him the meat packing business
from childhood. After graduating from
Robert E. Lee High School, Sol and his
bother Joe started a small slaughtering
house that gradually grew into the Gold
Merit Packing Company. He married his
wife Hilda in February 1958.
Sol is someone you can count on
at the Jacksonville Jewish Center. He
attends Sunday minyan regularly and is
always available to be the 10th man when
called at the last minute. On Shabbat
mornings, you can always find Sol in his
“regular” seat with his warm smile and
friendly handshake.
Over the years, Sol has given
generously of his time and resources to
the Jacksonville Jewish Center. Along
with the Goldman family, Sol and Hilda
helped dedicate the beautiful Goldman
Sukkah shortly after the Center relocated
to its current facility.
Most recently, the Sol and Hilda
Goldman Scholarship Fund was
established as a permanent endowment to
benefit those needy and worthy students
desiring admission to the Solomon
Schechter Day School. “We feel the
greatest contribution to Judaism is
through the education of the children and
we are thankful we can help those in
need,” Sol said.
We were pleased to show our
appreciation to Sol with this honor.
Evan Yegelwel
Hatan Bereshit
Evan, our immediate
past President and
current Chairman
of the Board, has
helped guide the
Jacksonville Jewish
Center through some
of its most exciting
and challenging
times. Under his stewardship, the Center
re-enforced its financial foundation and
developed a leadership team to meet the
demands of the next century. He did it
all with a sense of history, humility and
humor.
A native of New Jersey, Evan
graduated from Rutgers College in 1977
and from the University of Florida,
Holland Law Center in 1980. Upon his
arrival in Jacksonville, Evan immediately
immersed himself in the affairs of his
community and his synagogue. He has
served for more than 10 years on the
Board of the Jacksonville Jewish
Federation and was the first president of
the River Garden Hebrew Home for the
Aged Foundation. He serves on the
national executive committee of AIPAC,
the Florida Regional Board of the
AntiDefamation League and most
recently was appointed to the Board of
Directors of the United Synagogue of
Conservative Judaism, Southeast Region.
Nowhere, however, has his
commitment been more steadfast than at
the Jacksonville Jewish Center. He has
given tirelessly of his time, energy and
resources. Evan is not only active in the
administrative affairs of the synagogue,
but also serves as a Sunday minyan
captain.
Evan and his wife Arlene were
honored with the “Independence Issue
Award” at a recent Israel Bonds Dinner.
Together with their daughters Esther and
Tania, we wish them all a well deserved
mazel tov.
Kathy Osterer
Woman of Valor
Kathy exemplifies
the true spirit of a
“Woman of Valor”.
A wife, mother,
daughter, sister,
granddaughter and
friend to many, she
gives to everyone
and everything with
energy, enthusiasm and commitment.
Originally from New York, Kathy
Osterer became involved in her
synagogue and her community, almost
from the day she arrived in Jacksonville.
A past president of Women’s American
Ort and the Center Sisterhood, Kathy has
held numerous chairmanships and board
positions with Hadassah, Center
Sisterhood, River Garden, Jacksonville
Jewish Federation and her daughters’
schools. Kathy has served as Vice
President of Membership for the
Jacksonville Jewish Center and currently
serves as its Youth Vice President. A
Kadima advisor for the past five years,
Kathy is actively involved in planning
conventions, housing hundreds of
teenagers every year and supervising
monthly youth meetings.
A fundraiser extraordinaire, Kathy
chaired the 1995 River Garden Foundation
Gala and co-chaired the very successful
1998 major Center fund raiser, “Raffle
for Education.” Her special love, the
Sisterhood Judaica shop, has thrived
under her chairmanship.
Currently Kathy is employed by
River Garden Foundation as its Director
of Development
Honored for her devotion to
family, friends, and synagogue, Kathy
shared this simcha wth her beloved
husband, Morrie, four daughters Natalie,
Jessica, Melissa and Ashley, her father,
Jerry Fixel, as well as her entire family.
May she and her family be blessed with
many years of health and happiness.
