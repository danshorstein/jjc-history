Scouting
Boy Scout Troop 14
Troop 14 celebrated its 80th anniversary by continuing
the scouting tradition at the Jacksonville Jewish Center with
another year of outdoor fun, adventure and activities in 1998
-99. Bob Fleet served as Scoutmaster, aided by Assistant
Scoutmasters Louis Schwartz and Billy Wilensky. Youth
leadership was provided by Eli Fleet, Junior Assistant
Scoutmaster, Justin Jackrel, Senior Patrol Leader, and Jared
Fleet, Assistant Senior Patrol Leader.
This year, the troop participated in the River Bend
District Camporee at Echokotee. The scouts also camped at
Oleno State Park and at Kings Bay Naval Base, where they
toured a Trident submarine. On a boat trip to Nassau Sound,
they also enjoyed tubing and jet skiing. Other activities ranged
from participating in a Food Bank project to playing miniature
golf.
Thanks to the leadership efforts of Eagle Scout Sol
Proctor, funds were raised to purchase a trailer for troop gear.
The trailor was put to use right away, hauling gear to summer
camp at Camp Daniel Boone in North Carolina.
If you enjoy
camping, sports and other
outdoor activities, and are
between the ages of 11 and
18, join us at our
meetings on Thursday
nights at the Center. We
also welcome volunteer
assistance from adults.

Scouts on a white water rafting trip.
Troop 14 looks forward to continuing the tradition of
providing Jewish youth with a Boy Scout experience, as we
enter the new millenium.
Many scouts made advancements this year. Seven boys
completed the requirements for the Ner Tamid award.
The theme of this year’s Blue and Gold Banquet was
“80 Years of Scouting—80 Years of Eagles”, commemorating
this special anniversary. Eagle Scouts, who were former
members of Troop 14, were invited to participate in the Eagle
Court of Honor for Eli Fleet, who became the 56th Eagle
Scout from this troop. Brian Sexton, the voice of the Jaguars
and an Eagle Scout, himself, was the guest speaker.
