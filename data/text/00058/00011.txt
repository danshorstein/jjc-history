My Father
by Gil Kentof
I have many fond childhood memories of times spent with my father. I
have specific memories of a one week hiking, canoeing and rock climbing trip in
North Carolina and many Passover seders with my family at home. The most vivid
of these memories, however, are the trips my father and I made together to Lake
City, Florida. I was to obtain my falconry license and ultimately catch a wild hawk.
I'm certain this sounds quite strange and unusual but they are memories I will
always cherish.
I was fifteen years old at the time 1 decided to learn every aspect of this
ancient sport. After passing a difficult exam, and several 5tate equipment
Inspections, with lots of encouragement from my father, I became the youngest
falconer in the 5tate of Florida. That winter my father and I proceeded down the
highways in search of a young migrating red tail hawk. On Sundays, (not Shabbat)
we would rise at 5 a.m. in 30 degree temperatures to catch just the right hawk. I
now think to myself, "What other father would do such mishegas?"
During those years, my father taught me some extraordinary lessons
through his actions. I learned how persistence alone is omnipotent and how the
love of nature can bring you closer to God and Judaism. Through those lessons, I
also learned to pursue my dreams, full steam ahead!
I finally caught my hawk and practiced the wonderful sport of falconry, but
my fondest memories are those mornings I spent with my Dad!
