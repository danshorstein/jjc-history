Center Day Camp — The Place to Be!
The Center Day Camp programs were out of this world. In addition to the traditional,
fun activities like swimming, arts and crafts, and Judaica, our campers rode horses, went
boating, and even met a Bengal tiger!
A helicopter from Jax NAS landed on our field to celebrate the 4th of July with us.
Our Drama Camp sang patriotic songs and waved flags to welcome our guests.
Camp Yom Tov went bowling, played laser tag, roller skated and splashed around
Wet N’ Wild.
Camp K’ton had weekly themes for their
activities: “A Camping We Will Go.”, “Old
MacDonald’s Farm”, “Celebrate the Century”, “Outer
Space Place”, “Ocean Fun”, “Wacky Zoo Animals”.
“Under the Big Top Festival” and “Tropical Fantasy”.
A highlight was learning about fish at Fisherman’s Dock
and then about learning goy taku, the ancient Japanese
""art of fish painting.
Judaica Camp collected food for JFCS, visited
the sick and learned about kashrut and kosher symbols.
A variety of great Specialty Camps included: Basketball, Judaica-on-Wheels,
Computers, Drama, Baseball, Arts & Crafts, Sports, Cheerleading and Golf.
Evening ‘lock-ins”, color war and the annual Camp Carnival were a big hit. Parents
and campers enjoyed the booths, games, prizes, food and raffle at the Carnival.
CIT’s had leadership and first aid training, fun trips and wonderful bonding
experiences.
Many thanks to everyone who made this
summer’s camp such a big success, including: Lisa Wolfson, Hannah Bendit, Edith
Horovitz, Dodi Kress, Sharon Seebol, Adria Krantz, Billy Wilson, Gayle Bailys,
Frances Loncle, all the volunteers and the Center Maintenance Department. See
you next summer!
— Laura Cagan, Camp K’ton Director
Mike Schultz, Camp Yom Tov Director
Pam Maman, CIT Coordinator
