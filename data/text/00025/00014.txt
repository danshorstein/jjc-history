(Deeds of Kindness)
To translate the moral and ethical impera¬
tives of Judaism into everyday activities in
the public arena is the platform on which we
stand. In this platform is contained our feel¬
ing for social action. “Have we not all one
father? Hath not one God created us?”
The words of Malachi are the words we
say to all our fellow human beings oppressed
in all the four corners of the earth whether it
be our own American negro or our fellow
Jews in the Soviet Union for we know only
too well the consequences of bigotry and
hatred. In our time we have seen the de¬
struction and the inhumanities to our fellow
man. The Jacksonville Jewish Center con¬
fronts the major issues of the day. Whatever
they may be we see it is our task in the
light of Jewish tradition to take the stand by
means of debate or action in response to these
problems.
Also involved in theory and practice is our
awareness of the ecumenical spirit that now
prevails. We of the Jacksonville Jewish Cen¬
ter are doing our part in our community to
better understand our brethren of different de¬
nominations and to help them understand us.
Our concern for the sick, our response to
the aged, our involvement with the troubled
have portrayed to the community and our
membership the heart of the Center. “We are
involved with you. Be thou involved with us.”
“On three things the world stands, Torah,
Prayer and Deeds of Kindness, our house of
worship is truly a house dedicated to deeds
of kindness.”
