HENRY ROGOZINSKI,
President
This past year “Gators” A.Z.A. No. 272 was proud
to send its largest delegation in 30 years. There the
charter was presented by Eliot Borkson and Alan
Harris, who won first place in the debating contest.
Throughout the year our Chapter has tried to
fulfill its obligation to the national program. “Gators”
AZA has done this by working with such programs as
the road block for the M.D.A., hosting program with
speakers like Judge Lamar Weingeart and holding
unique social affairs.
A.Z.A. is a fraternal order of young Jewish men
who are dedicated to preserving our seven cardinal
principals: Conduct, Patriotism, Judaism, Fraternity,
Filial Love and Charity.
“Gators” A.Z.A. has concluded a well rounded and
beneficial year and expects to go further in future
years. “Gators” would like to thank Mr. Myron Blatt-
ner who was our past Advisor, for all that he did for
our Chapter. Also we would like to express our grati¬
tude to our sister Chapter “Gators” B.B.G , for making
this year what it was.

LINDA HILSENRAD,
President
During the summer months Gator B’nai B’rith
Girls were very active. First they participated in Proj¬
ect Headstart and then had the annual invitational
tea for prospective members. In November the girls
went out Halloween night and collected candy for the
children in Brewster Hospital, Hope Haven and Duval
Medical Center. Along with our brother A.Z.A.’s we
collected over $400 for the Muscular Distrophy drive.
At regional Convention our newspaper took second
place in competition; our alma mater placed third; and
Sharon Raskin won first place in the oratory contest.
By means of a raffle Gators collected $115 which they
donated to the International Service Fund. We con¬
cluded our official year with an Installation Luncheon
for the newly elected officers at the Holiday Inn.
Superlatives were presented and new officers installed.
