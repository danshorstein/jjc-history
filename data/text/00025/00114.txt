Girl Scouts with Mrs. Herbert Lewitt, Leader
The Center Sisterhood Girl Scout Program, spon¬
sored by the Sisterhood under the chairmanship of
Mrs. Jack Price, completed its season in May. The
Arlington Junior Girl Scouts and the Southside Brown¬
ies consisted of 29 girls.
The Juniors, Troop No. 210, led by Mrs. Morton
Benjamin, Mrs. Arthur Eisen and Mrs. Marvin Hal-
pern had several outings to places such as Camp Chow-
enwaw, Boone Park and Fort Caroline; made yarn dolls
for the children at Hope Haven Hospital; received an
honor award for cookie sales; made pencil holders, leaf
prints and learned the art of outdoor camping.
The Brownies, Troop No. 269, led by Mrs. Herbert
Lewitt, celebrated Tu B’shevat by planting a tree at
River Garden Home; visited Cohen’s Candy Kitchen;
made menorahs for their homes; planted and harvested
a string bean garden and enjoyed an active Scouting
year.
Sincere thanks are due our leaders who enthus¬
iastically led our girls through an active and fruitful
season.
