HENRY LASRIS,
Scoutmaster
Troop 14 has just completed one of its most active years
under the direction of Henry Lasris, Scoutmaster, and his
dedicated committee of fathers.
The year opened with a trip to visit the local professional
football team. This was followed by a cook-out and swim party
at the Center campsite. The Chairman for this affair was
Irwin Smith. A month later the Troop participated in the
annual Camporee at Silver Springs. Among the highlights of
this Camporee was a visit to the Snake Farm and competition
in the various Scouting events.
Two Scouts received the Ner Tamid award. The recipients
Bruce Blumberg and Mark Zimmerman were ably taught by
Dr. Harry Yergin. Through the courtesy of the Center and
the Men’s Club the Troop purchased sixteen two-man tents.
The tents were initiated on a field trip to Gold Head State
Park.
The Boy Scouts contributed to the Center Sisterhood
Bazaar with a booth and they also contributed to the Men’s
Club Picnic by building a monkey bridge which was one of the
highlights. The year was climaxed with sixteen boys attending
a week at Boy Scout Camp at Orange Park, achieving advance¬
ment in rank and merit. Earl Berman and Charles Trager
were elected to the Order of the Arrow.
There were many other field trips during the year to such
places as Crandall Point, Fort Clinch and Black Creek. Jeffrey
Goldstein was awarded the Eagle Scout rank.
