HAMISHA ASAR B'SHEVAT (Fifteenth Day of Shevat), the New Year of the Trees. Often referred to
as the Jewish Arbor Day, this holiday commemorates the ancient need for trees in the Holy Land. It
is celebrated everywhere with appropriate songs and games. Fruits long associated with the trees that
once grew in the Holy Land (and have begun to grow there again) are eaten on this day.
