Young Judaea is a unique movement which be¬
lieves in Zionism, a healthy American Jewish com¬
munity, and the powers of youth to work for an ideal.
Today leaders, seniors, directors, and Youth Commis¬
sion members all work hand in hand triyng new ideas
and new plans for fulfilling the Young Judaea goal—
“£o develop generations of American Jewish
youth rooted in their heritage, and devoted
to attaining self-fulfillment as Zionists
In addition to the separate programs and activi¬
ties each club, city-wide functions this year included
a Ruach Rally, a discussion concerning religion in
Israel, two speakers from Israel, a raffle, and the start¬
ing of Chug Chalutzi. During the year many Judaeans
attended Winter and Summer Conventions, a District
Convention, two Chug Chalutzi Cconventions, and
many inter-city conclaves. Four Judaeans went to
Camp Tel Yehudah, the national camp of Young
Judaea, while fifteen others attended Camp Judaea in
Hendersonville, North Carolina.
During Summer Convention held at Camp Judaea
Saralee Newman was elected Programing Vice Pres¬
ident of the Southern Region, and Marvin Kliman was
elected Secretary.
