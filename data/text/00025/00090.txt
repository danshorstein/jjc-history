Mr. Alan Gordon, on behalf of his mother, Mrs.
Robert J. Gordon, and his brother, Samuel Gordon,
presents the Robert Gordon Leadership Grant for 1966
to Barbara Talpalar and Jerald Morris, as Rabbi Turet¬
sky observes. This scholarship, begun in 1965, in mem¬
ory of one of the most distinguished lay leaders of the
center, is awarded to the student or students with the
most promising leadership potential, and provides for
attendance at Camp Ramah. These two students were
among the eighteen youngsters who left Jacksonville
for a summer at Camp Ramah, in Wingdale, N. Y.,
and Palmer, Mass.
The students who went to Camp Ramah were:
Earl Berman, Martin Berman, Mayer Becker, Belinda
Felhandler, Jeff Goldstein, Carol Gross, Ricki Gross,
Stanley Halpern, Jeffrey Morris, Jerald Morris, Sharon
Raskin, Margaret Rose, Cynthia Safer, Paula Saraga,
Harry Sinoff, Barbara Talpalar, Barry and Jonathan
Turetsky.
The Minyan Club pictured here is made possible through the combined
efforts of the Education Department and the Men’s Club Committee who
assist in the serving of breakfast. Rabbi Turetsky and Cantor Marton are
in the back row.
