RELIGIOUS EDUCATION
First graders with their teacher, Mrs. Nathan Sandler
A complete education within one general
framework to meet the needs of Jewish
students makes the Foundation School the
shining jewel in our educational system. No
desirable educational endeavor has been
neglected. Library trips and field trips have
been arranged. On Jewish holidays the Foun¬
dation family has planned many home-like
functions.
The Foundation School this year con¬
ducted a successful experiment in conducting
a 4th and 5th grade together. Much com¬
mendation has been bestowed on the pioneer¬
ing efforts of the teachers, and the parents
who worked wholeheartedly with them.
Mrs. Norman Moss and her students
Accomplishments were also learning ac¬
tivities in the Hebrew and General studies
department.
Reports of our progress at the First
National Conference of North American Day
Schools in December indicated that not only
is our Day School one of the least expensive,
but our pupils receive more extended induc¬
tion, such as the time for English studies. By
meeting the standards of the national office,
our school was awarded a certificate of attain¬
ment and the use of the name Solomon
Schechter Day School.
