19 ADAR 11
L. C. 6:27 p.m.
PURIM (literally, "lots") — this gayest of Jewish holidays is
nonetheless named for the lots cast before Haman to select a day
of execution for the Jews. On the fateful day, however, it was the
wicked Haman who perished — thanks to Queen Esther and her
cousin, Mordecai. The full story is told in the Megillah (Book of
Esther) which on this day, is read aloud twice in the synagogue.
During the reading, children twirl their groggers (noisemakers)
whenever Haman is mentioned, to drown out his name.
