B’nai B’rith Women is synonomous with its motto
“Service, Fidelity, Devotion.” There are many philan-
trophies which rely on B’nai B’rith for their support.
In America they are National Jewish Hospital in
Denver, Colorado, Bellefaire Children’s Home for emo¬
tionally disturbed youngsters, in Cleveland, Ohio—in
Hot Springs, Arkansas, the Leo N. Levi Hospital, B’nai
B’rith Family Center at Mayo Clinic and the Salk
Foundation.
In Israel, B’nai B’rith sponsors two Kibbutzim,
Childrens Home for emotionally disturbed youngsters,
Martyr’s Forest and Hillel Foundation.
In Duval County we have seen a tremendous re¬
sponse for just one of the aspects of B’nai B’rith. A
Hillel Counselorship is in the process of establishment
at Jacksonville University through the combined ef¬
forts of B’nai B’rith Women, the B’nai B’rith Lodges
and the Jacksonville Jewish Community Council. This
will offer to the Jewish students at Jacksonville Uni¬
versity “A Home Away From Home.” At Duval Medi¬
cal Center we have given our services in seeing to it
that the Mental Theraphy Dept, and the Pharmaceu¬
tical Dept, were supplied with pill bottles, baby food
jars, and torn clean nylon hose to be used by the
Center. Through our efforts the Florida State Associa¬
tion of B’nai B’rith Women have aided us in this
project and have undertaken the collection of same
all directed towards Jacksonville’s Duval Medical Cen¬
ter.
B’nai B’rith Women work at the River Garden
Hebrew Home for the Aged, on the United Jewish
Appeal, Israel Bond Drive, Lake City Veterans Hos¬
pital, and any and all assorted drives such as the
United Nations Day Luncheon under Citizenship, Vet¬
erans Service.
This year we are continuing to distribute 1,600
Calendar Memo Date Books to the public schools
through the AntiDefamation League in conjunction
with the Jewish Community Council.
Another successful program constantly perpetu¬
ated is the “Dolls for Democracy program.”
