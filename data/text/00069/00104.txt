JACKSONVILLE HEBREW HIGH SCHOOL
The Diaspora Jew is always tied to a place: a holy place, a place of
worship. We sometimes refer to God as Ha-Makom, meaning literally, “the
place” - not because God lives at a specific address, but because we can
find the Divine anywhere we care to look. Makom: Jacksonville Hebrew
High School will offer teens a diverse menu of places and opportunities
for creative and fun experiences, including teen celebrations of Shabbat, a
monthly “Wild on Wednesday” (a.k.a WOW) social event with a Jewish twist
co-sponsored by the Center’s United Synagogue Youth (USY) chapter, and
an overnight trip for each grade.
Hazzan Jesse Holzer
A program coordinated through the Jacksonville Jewish Center,
MAKOM is open to all Jewish teens living in Jacksonville who are in 9th
through 12th grades. Classes are slated to meet on Wednesday evenings
from 7-9 pm. Contact Hazzan Holzer for more information.
