Mr. & Mrs. Club
Brandéis University National Women’s Committee pro¬
vides financial support for the Brandéis University Li¬
braries, which serve a community of students and schol¬
ars, in education and research, united by their com¬
mitment to the pursuit of knowledge. Members act as
emissaries to support and enhance the image of Brandéis,
a Jewish sponsored, non-sectarian university where
excellence is the tradition.
The Jacksonville chapter was organized in September
1991 and has about 120 members. In addition to
fundraising projects, members participate in study groups
using college syllabi prepared by Brandéis University
professors. A visiting professor attends annually.
Membership in the National Women’s Committee is
non-sectarian and open to non-graduates of Brandéis
and to men.

For forty years, Mr. & Mrs. has been fostering fellowship
and a common bond for the promotion of religious,
cultural and social activities among fellow Jews.
The club meets at the Jacksonville Jewish Center every
third Saturday of the month for a social evening of
dancing, entertainment and dessert. There are several
seated dinners throughout the year and a gala New Year’s
Eve Party with the works!
If you’re thinking of joining the club, why not try it out?
Call any Mr. & Mrs. officer or board member to “Be Our
Guest!” Transportation can be arranged for those who
need it.

Women’s American ORT
Women’s American ORT is a fund-raising group that helps
to train teachers and provide high tech educational programs
in schools in Israel, the United States and many countries
throughout the world. ORT assists refugees who are fleeing
from persecution.

American Red Magen David
Magen David Adorn (Red Star of David) which was
founded in 1930 as a First Aid Society for the city of Tel
Aviv, now provides life-saving and health related services
for the entire population of the State of Israel, as well as,
performing the usual tasks carried out by Red Cross
Societies throughout the world. While the symbol of the
Magen David Adorn is recognized by the United States as
a member of the international life-saving societies, work
is still being done to achieve international recognition.
