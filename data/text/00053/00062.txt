JUNIOR TROOP 5
Junior Troop 5 has had a great year. Twenty-seven girls worked together
on badge and service projects. Badgework was geared toward the “Sign of
the Satellite.” Signs are difficult to earn, requiring the completion of
multiple badges and other activities. The girls completed the badge
“Skysearch” by camping out at the Museum of Science and History.
Besides viewing a planetarium show, the girls viewed the moon and stars
with members of the local Astronomy Club on the roof of the museum and
learned to use star maps.
Service projects this year benefited the Salvation Army, Hubbard House
and South Florida troops still suffering from Hurricane Andrew. A baby
shower held joiñtly with Brownie Troop 882 benefited the Emergency
Pregnancy Service and charity programs of the Family Practice Clinic at
St. Vincent’s Medical Center.
The highlight of our year was a trip to a friend’s farm. The girls fed a new
baby calf, went on a hayride, pumped fresh water and each took an egg
home from the chicken coop. A few weeks later, Deborah Meisel hatched
her egg and the little black chick “Alex” visited the troop at nine days old!
