Henry Kramer Library & Resource Center
The Tofield Collection purchased by the Center congregation in the early sixties contained many fine Judaica
volumes. Through the years the congregation has acquired new titles. There are now more than 4,000 titles in
English on many subjects; i.e., Bible, prayer, Talmud, philosophy, literature, Israel, poetry, humor and fiction.
These books are available for members to enjoy. Donors of new volumes are noted by special bookplates.
The Solomon Schechter Day School is expanding its
program through the eighth grade. New books are needed
for the new grade levels. The Solomon Schechter P.T.A.
Program chaired by Edith Horovitz is endeavoring to
meet this need. Purchase of books in honor of birth¬
days, special events and special persons will aid this
program.
Library volunteers are needed to assist the Library Com¬
mittee with the increasing needs of the Library. The
Committee performs many hours of volunteer service
to insure the accessibility of the extensive collection of
books.
The Library is open on Sunday mornings. The book
cart is available after Shabbat services for the conve¬
nience of worshippers. The Library Committee joined
with the Center Sisterhood for Shabbat and Book Re¬
views.
