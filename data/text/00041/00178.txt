Solomon Schechter Day School
The continuing growth of the Day School attests to
the fulfillment of the dreams of the founders in 1960. The
Day School has grown to a record 206 enrollment, from
Jumping Jacks (2 years old) through the 7th grade.
The Solomon Schechter Day School was founded
with the premise that our children will, as they mature,
continue the heritage of our people, support Israel,
participate in Jewish communal activities, and assume
responsibilities of leadership, thus ensuring the quality of
Jewish life and the future of our community.
In addition to a rich program of Hebrew and Judaic studies, we must provide a superior secular
studies program. Our common goal has always been an exceptional educational institution for
Judaic and Secular studies. Our dream has come true. Our school is open to all Jewish children
in the community regardless of affiliation or ability to pay.
There is overwhelming evidence that the students of Jewish day schools remain committed to
the survival of Jewish life and provide the reservoir of leadership needed for a strong Jewish
community.
