This year the Jacksonville Jewish Center as well as the entire community enjoyed the talents of a
variety of musical performing artists. The PERFORMING ARTIST SERIES was co-ordinated by very
capable Sylvia Shorstein and Sybil Ansbacher and assisted by Isabel Balotin. This series was the first of
its kind to be presented by the Center. We thank the Patrons, Sponsors and everyone who supported
this endeavor . . . you made it a success!!!
The rich, enchanting voice of Cantor Harold Orbach initiated the
Performing Artist Series with the beauty of his remarkable tenor voice.
His varied repetoire delighted the audience. We are very grateful to the
Levin Family who sponsored Cantor Orbach in memory of Harriette
Levin.
Pianist Yefim Bronfman’s playing has been described as “remarkable”.
For all those who attended his performance, it was an evening of fine
music. Mr. Bronfman combined all the exuberance and vigor of a young
virtuoso with the insight and maturity of an established master.

American Ballalika Company
Four talented entertainers, who have been called “gypsies” danced, sang and
played a variety of unusual instruments, including the Balalaika. The lavish
costumes, lively vocals and efferevescent personalities added to the evening’s
excitement.
Arthur Siegel
Broadway pianist and composer, Arthur Siegel
brought back the beauty and delightful quality of the
“old songs.” Mr. Siegel stirred memories when he
sang his own composition “Love is a Simple Thing.”
We thank Eddie Siegel and his family for their support.
