SUNDAY SCHOOL
The Sunday School of the Jacksonville Jewish Center is planned expressly for children in Kindergarten, First and Second
Grades. A two hour program provides each child the opportunity to experience the richness of their Jewish heritage through
song, dance, role playing, cooking, short stories, games arts and crafts. Emphasis is placed on the holidays and festivals. Jewish
values and mitzvot are encouraged.
The objectives of our Sunday School are as follows:
1. To provide the young child with the opportunity for wholesome Jewish experiences, especially those related to the
observance of Jewish festivals both at home and in the synagogue.
2. To make meaningful to the child the Jewish element in his environment.
3. To offer and provide opportunities for Jewish creative expression through handiwork, song poetry, prayer, rhythms and
music discussion, drama, etc.
4. To acquaint the child with Jewish folklore, particularly those stories which teach ethical principles anchor are related the
Jewish festivals.
5. To establish a foundation of knowledge of Jewish practices, history and thinking, as well as a basic Hebrew vocabulary
which can later be built upon and expanded in the Hebrew and Judaica High Schools.
Sunday School co-ordinator is Trida Bloom, Judy Geller is the music instructor.
