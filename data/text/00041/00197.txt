The Symbolism of Israel
Random Impressions of an Inspiring Journey,
Bob and Marlene Bossen
Peaceful co-existence! Here at the “Good Fence”
on the Lebanese border, and Arab leaves the
Israeli clinic after being treated by an Israeli
doctor! People who need people can and will live
in peace.
On a peaceful, productive kibbutz where people
go about their daily labor and children laugh and
play, this bunker is a constant reminder of the
ever present dangers.
These young Israeli soldiers, male and female, are
hitching rides to be home in time for Shabbat.
Even their ever-present machine guns don’t keep
people from picking them up.
Inside the 2,000 year old walled city of Jerusalem,
our emotions are stirred as we retrace the steps
and try to visualize the lives of our ancestors.
Excavations continue to reveal our glorious
heritage.
The Knesset where the Israeli Parliament meets.
It is said that there are as many differences of
opinion inside the Knesset as there are windows
on the outside.
Lest we Forget! Everywhere we went there was
the ever-present contrast of the carefree tourists
and the military vehicle with loaded guns
prepared for any eventuality.
It’s the thought that counts! In old synagogues we
visited there were arks of hand carved wood and
simple hand woven curtains. Each ark was
strikingly beautiful - because of the love and
reverence with which it was built.
This picture, to me, represents the whole idea of
Israel. The enduring strength and resolve of the
Jewish people are exemplified by the ancient and
massive stones of the Western Wall. The Jewish
ideals of living in peace and thanking God for all
blessings are demonstrated by men praying at the
wall. Life would be so simple if many would only
follow ten simple rules!
Against the backdrop of nature’s majestic beauty
stands a grim reminder of the Holocaust. A
sculpture of emaciated bodies wrapped in barbed
wire.
