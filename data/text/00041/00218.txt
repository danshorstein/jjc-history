Torah Readers
TORAH READING, THE TEST OF TIME
Among the attributes of God described in Exodus is: “slow to anger” interpreted to
mean affording opportunities for improvement. In chaotic times when traditional values
are ostentatiously breached, we need the synagogue to help direct our lives. Its
atmosphere, ideals and rituals provide a strong influence for family unity and personal
achievement. Opportunities to lead the service or read special portions provide for
personal growth and recognition. The total gamut of celebrations recognize individual
worth and dignity.
We are thankful to many people, young and old, who have devoted so much of their
time to the more arduous task of learning the varied Torah and Haftorah readings. Their
names are recorded in the first column of the weekly Bulletin. They have helped to
maintain Judaism's high standard of study as a way of life. We hope they will always enjoy
the fruits of their labors.
