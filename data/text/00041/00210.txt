Simhat Torah Honorees
The Festival of Sukkot reaches its joyous climax
on Simhat Torah, the last day of the Holiday. Simhat
Torah is the happiest holiday on the Jewish calendar
because we celebrate our joy and pleasure with the
Torah.
Because of Israel’s perennial love of the Torah,
it has been referred to in Jewish tradition as Israel’s
“bride.” A tradition evolved in ancient times to honor
the individual called for the last reading of the Torah
—the Hatan Torah or the groom of the Torah. The
person honored to begin the Torah reading from its
beginning is called Hatan Bereshit the groom of
Genesis. We also honor a woman who has .made a dis¬
tinctive contribution to our Synagogue and to Jewish
life in general—the “Woman of Valor” honor. We
will honor these individuals at Services on Simhat
Torah morning, Wednesday, October 21.

The Jacksonville Jewish Center takes pride in its Daily Minyan. Through the tireless
efforts of the captains and co-captains, we have minyans every morning and evening.
The anniversary date of the passing of a loved one presents an opportunity to observe a
Mitzvah, if not a double Mitzvah.
The Mitzvah of observing the Yahrzeit is obvious - the second Mitzvah is the
opportunity to insure a minyan so that others, besides yourself, can also observe or
attend a service for their own reasons.
The Daily Minyan needs you. For a satisfying and rewarding experience, attend once or
twice a month on a regular basis.
If you plan to attend, call the captain of the day and time.
