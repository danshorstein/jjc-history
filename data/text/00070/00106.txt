MAKOM: Jacksonville Hebrew High School
opened its doors in the Fall of 2010. The program sought
out to be a place for friendship, spiritual growth, new
skills and experiences, rich traditions, bettering the
world, and lasting memories. With a committed core in place, MAKOM begins
its second year with new ideas to enhance our Hebrew High School experience.
Through linking up with USY, week to week programming is greatly enhanced
by the leadership of our USY board. Students play a pivotal role in creating our
curriculum each semester- the electives are greatly influenced by students’ interests.
In the 12th grade, high school seniors participate in the Siyyon Program, which
serves as a laboratory for developing Jewish leadership skills as young adults
through social action and advocacy. With an eye to the importance of experiential
learning, MAKOM focuses on social action programming, teen leadership skill¬
building, and an annual “MAKOM on Wheels” experience to bring informal
Jewish education to a new level. Simply put: MAKOM is awesome. Come join!
