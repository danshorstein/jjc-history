COMMITTEE MEMBERS
The Jacksonville Jewish Center’s
theme of this past year was Kehillat
Kesher, a “connected community.” We
on the membership committee serve
as the “host and hostess committee”
for the Center, inviting prospective
members to join, and then working to
make our new members, as well as our
existing members, feel connected and
welcomed.
There are many ways one can
become “connected” (or better
connected) at the Center. Here are just
a few that happened this past year:
One can connect at the JJC House
of Assembly [Beit Knesset] for simchas,
for anniversaries, for purposes of social
action, to enjoy the Yegelwel pool, for
Sisterhood and Men’s Club functions,
for book reviews, and other social
events. We assembled for the annual
Tiyul to Shul when we welcomed our
new Center members. We assembled at Purim with festivities that included the congregational Megillah
reading, as well as a Purim parody video, coffee and dessert bar, and a fun Purim carnival.
One can connect at the JJC House of Study [Beit Midrash] and connect with others in classes as basic
as Hebrew Literacy or Foundations of Judaism, for lessons in Jewish cooking and Jewish art, at the weekly
Talmud class or at our annual Safer Shabbaton. Many came to study at Family University.
One can connect at the JJC House of Prayer [Beit Tefillah] whether it be Shabbat morning services,
holiday services, or our daily minyan that is held twice a day. New and innovative ways have been introduced
into a variety of services. Kehillat Kesher Services were offered the second day of Rosh Hashanah and the
Shabbat before Thanksgiving. A late Friday night service has been added for the first Shabbat each month.
KolTrain brings in musical instruments for an upbeat, uplifting Kabbalat Shabbat Service. Healing services
as well as Shabbat Meditation have also been attended by many.
By welcoming, greeting, hosting or even just visiting with other members, new or existing, we are all
participating in creating a connected community!
