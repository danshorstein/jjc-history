A Message From
Rabbi Dov Kentof
Youth and Education Director
Most of us seem to want continally to drag down the
image of God instead of coming up to His level. We lift
our eyes upward to the sky and think He is at the end of
the farthermost horizon, when actually that is only His
beginning. We think we have probed His secrets fully
when it comes to attendance at the University of the
Universe.
This brings to mind the marvelous story of the boy
who failed his entrance exams at Princeton University.
Before heading home he stopped off to see President
Robert Goheen and thanked him, saying, “I’ve learned a
lot here.”
President Goheen asked in amazement, “But you were never enrolled here. What could you
possibly have learned from us?”
The young man replied, “How little I know!”
Goheen answered, “Son, we will take you in. You are already two years ahead of the freshman
class.”
This is also true of the knowledge of Judaism that we have. You, the student of our
Supplemental school, have only begun to acquire your heritage.
Through the combined efforts of all of us, we will understand who we are and where we are
going. One human life is mystery beyond all comprehension. How much more must your
knowledge be of yourself and of God.
Alina, Gil, Tali and myself are extending our best wishes for a new year of happiness,
prosperity and peace.
Rabbi Kentof & wife, Alina
