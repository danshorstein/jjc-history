Simhat Torah Honorees
Sunday, Oct. 10, 1983 — 12 Tishri 5742
DR. NATHAN SCHNEIDER,
Hatan Torah
Dr. Nathan Schneider is a con¬
cerned and dedicated Jew who
takes a sincere interest in the well¬
being of his family, friends and
synagogue.
This year he assumed the reins
of the Religious Committee with
confidence and ease after being a
member of that committee for
many years. Nathan was also
Chairman of the Education Com¬
mittee and an avid worker on the
Adult Education Committee and
Board of Directors, charter mem¬
ber of the Minyanaires and pres¬
ently serves as a Minyan Captain.
He has also taught Hebrew for the
Hebrew Literacy Campaign.
Nathan recently retired from the
Department of Health where he
was manager of the Public Health
and Medical Laboratory. This posi¬
tion brought him distinguished
recognition in his field.
The Center is proud to have a
man of Nathan’s calibre on the
Board of Trustees and as a leader
of our congregation. Sharing this
Simha with Nathan are his wife
Thelma and sons, Dr. Victor Sch¬
neider and Michael Schneider and
his six grandchildren.
LAWRENCE DuBOW
Hatan Bereshit
Laurie was chosen for his sus¬
tained involvement and contribu¬
tion of time, intelligence and energy
on behalf of the Center Building
Fund. Although his executive busi¬
ness responsibilities take him all
over the country, he still finds time
to spend many hours on Center
business. He served two years as
President of the Center. During
his tenure major changes were
made in our building and large
gifts were contributed.
As a speaker he commands atten¬
tion, not by virtue of his position but
by the essence of his personality.
He is one of the most eloquent and
forceful speakers of the Center and
has been most articulate in ex¬
plaining the Center’s plans and
prospects during his annual High
Holiday Synagogue Appeal. On
several social occasions, Laurie
served as Master of Ceremonies with
his usual charm and wit, mak¬
ing each affair an outstanding suc¬
cess.
In all his efforts on behalf of the
Center, he is always supported by
his wife Linda, a fabulous fund
raiser in her own right. His chil¬
dren, Susan and Michael, share in
the honor as do Laurie’s parents,
Bill and Anne DuBow.
CLARA MANN,
Woman of Valor
Our Woman of Valor, Clara
Mann, is a woman totally devoted
to the youth of our congregation
and community. Her dedication to
the Scouting program has brought
her recognition locally and nation¬
ally, as well.
She served as Sisterhood Hospi¬
tality Chairman, member of the
Youth Commission, volunteer Li¬
brarian for the Day School Library,
and volunteer teacher’s aid. Clara
is the President of Afternoon Ort
and Parliamentarian for Sisterhood.
She describes herself as “a person
willing to help whenever help is
needed.” Because of her loyal and
dedicated nature, Clara has stead¬
fastly contributed to the ongoing
Scouting program of the Jewish
Center.
Clara and her husband. Dr. Mil¬
ion Mann, are world travelers.
Wherever they have ventured, they
have made it a priority to investi¬
gate the Jewish community and
share their experiences with us.
She is a devoted mother to her
children, Irene and Ian, who is
presently studying at the Jewish
Theological Seminary. Because of
her positive influence, her children
have been very involved in the
youth program of the Center.
Daily Minyan
The Jacksonville Jewish Center takes pride in its Daily Minyan.
More than 100 different men are involved in this mitzvah. Through
the tireless efforts of the captains and co-captains, we have
minyans every morning and evening.
The anniversary date of the passing of a loved one presents an
opportunity to observe a mitzvah, if not a double mitzvah. The
mitzvah of observing the Yahrzeit is obvious-the second mitzvah
is the opportunity to insure a minyan so that others besides
yourself can also observe or attend a service for their own reasons.
The Daily Minyan needs you. For a satisfying and rewarding
experience attend once or twice a month on a regular basis.
Weekday evenings are in need of men to make a minyan, please
make an effort to attend whenever possible.
