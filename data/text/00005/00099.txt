1) Chanukkah (Dedication Days) celebrates the re-dedication of the Temple
by the victorious Maccabees (167 B. C. E.) after their struggle against the
Syrian King Antiochus who, with the aid of the Jewish apostates, had attempted
to suppress the religion of the Jews. For eight days lights were kindled every
evening in commemoration of their victory for religious liberty.
