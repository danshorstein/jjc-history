Functioning under the direct supervision of tfie State Banking Department,
the Progressive Credit Union enables small merchants to secure short term loans
at a small rate of interest, affording maximum security to its share-buyers and
saving-depositors.
The Progressive Credit Union is an outgrowth of the Jewish Progressive
Society which is one of the oldest organizations in the city and which is still
active in its charitable and welfare work. Mr. Alfred Stein, member of the B oard
of Directors of the Credit Union is President of the Society, of which the Credit
Union is a part.
The Union was organized two years ago and has a members hip of 140 with
a paid-in capital of $28,000. I‘t makes loans at the rate of 6 per cent interest,
50 per cent of the profits being refunded to the borrowers. Every loan is issured
by the Cuna Mutual Insurance Company. J he Credit Union pays 5 per cent
interest on shares and 4 per cent on saving deposits.
During the past year, the Credit Union National Association held its con¬
vention in Jacksonville, marking the first time the association met in the S outh-
east. The convention was held on May 1, 2 and 3rd of this year, at which time
directors from all over the country attended.
There are 47 Credit Unions in Jacksonville and 12,000 throughout the country.
