Having set as its ultimate goal the
building in Jacksonville of a social and
athletic center which would serve the
entire Jewish community, the Jewish
Men’s Club has succeeded during the
past four years in keeping alive among
our people the realization of the great
need which exists for such an institu¬
tion.
Its efforts have, indeed, not been
wasted. This year, the project initia¬
ted by the club is in the process of
becoming a rea lity. The B oard of
the Jacksonville Jewish Center has
already made arrangements for the
purchase of a house adjacent to its
building, and plans are being dis¬
cuss ed for the merging of the forces
of three organizations (The old Y.
M. H. A., the Jewish Men’s Club
and the Jax Jewish Center) and the
initiation of a campaign for recruiting
members for the new organization.
In addition to this achievement, dur¬
ing the past year the Jewish Men s
Club has presented its members many
interesting activities and sponsored
several worthy projects. The Club
presented a cup to the A. Z. A. fra¬
ternity for winning the Jewish Dia¬
mond Ball League tournament after
defeating teams of the four organiza¬
tions comprising the league. It coop¬
erated with the Jewish Welfare Board
in its program of entertainment for
the Jewish soldiers stationed in the
vicinity and also with the Jackson¬
ville Jewish Community Council
which raises funds for national and
overseas relief.
With the coming of the New Year,
the Jewish Men s Club issues a call
to the Jacksonville Jewry, young and
old, to join in and lend its help to¬
ward the establishment of the finest
Jewish Athletic Center in the South.
