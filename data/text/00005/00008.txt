Under the leadership o( Mrs. H. Setzer, assisted by Mrs. Blanch Slott and
Mrs. Joe P. Safer, another successful year in the history of Troop 2, Center
Girl Scouts troop, has been brought to a close.
In January, the troop organized an outing to Camp Chowenwaw where its
members enjoyed two days of swimming, canoeing and camp life. March was
the month of the Girl Scouts Cookie Sale and eighteen cartons were sold by
the troop which established a fine record. A luncheon followed in April, when
the girls got together at a local restaurant and afterwards enjoyed a theatre show.
May was an active month for Troop 2. 1 he Girl Scouts Sabbath was held
in the Center Synagogue and was addressed by Mrs. J. H. Crawford, local
Scout executive. During the month of May the girls also visited the Danish
ship, Denmark and presented the crew with a lovely gift. In return, the girls
were offered the run of the ship.
During the year, the girls were also active in Arts and Crafts work and
sewing. Ash trays were modeled out ol pine and necklesses were made of
paper straws.
Seven girls passed their fist class requirements during the past year and
became second class Girl Scouts. All of the girls are prepared to continue their
fine work during the coming year.
