There are halls in the heavens above that open but
to the voice of song . Thus speaks the Zohar o f the im¬
portance of prayer. And, indeed, social activities which so
many use as a substitute for participation in synagogue
worship is not enough to make the life of an individual
spiritually satisfying.
Especially heart-warming to the pious Jew is the si giit
of children at prayer. For in them he beholds those who
will further and preserve the most beautiful aspect of our
religion—its ritual and ceremonialism.
The Jun ior Synagogue maintained by the Center has
been the object of admiration for many a visitor of our
institution. The reputed composer of liturgical music, Can¬
tor Kaminsky, after observing a Sabbath morning service
conducted by our children in the Prayer Room, expressed
himself in the following words: In my long career as
cantor I have travelled over the entire Ien gth and breadth
of this country and visited many places of worship; but
nowhere have I seen a service in which children pray with
so much sincerity and evidence such familiarity with the
ritual of our Synagogue and its traditional music. The
arrangement of the children s service is also highly com¬
mendable.”
During the past year, the Junior Congregation con¬
ducted services regularly every Sabbath morning and held
special services on the High Holid ays and the festivals of
Succoth, Pesach and Shovuoth. Billy Schemer officiated
as Junior Cantor and he was ably assisted by Nathan
Krestul, assistant cantor and Jay Cohen, page announcer.
An additional feature of these services was the weekly
sermonette in which the instructor in charge explained to
the children the moral lessons contained in the portion of
the Bible read on that morning. These talks were fre¬
quently made by the children themselves.
Mr. Morris Nimovitz was the instructor in charge.
