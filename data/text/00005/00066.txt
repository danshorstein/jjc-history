The cover of this year’s publication was designed
after much thought. The editors had in mind to por¬
tray something symbolic of the role the Jacksonville
Jewish Center plays in our community. The final de¬
cision, which seemed so simple, was nevertheless most
fortunate for none of the characteristics of this institu¬
tion are more outstanding than its work among the
young people.
The cover depicts a typical scene, on any afternoon
or Sunday morning, at the doors of the Center. It is
alive with the energy and zeal of the Jewish children.
Were nothing more accomplished by this institution
than the transmission to the young of the cultural and
religious heritage of our people, it would have fulfilled
its mission.
Although, comparatively speaking, the Center is
still a very young organization, the Jewish community
of Jacksonville has already received into its folds new
leaders whose background has been supplied by this
institution.
Very few organizations anywhere possess the all¬
round continuous youth program offered by the Center.
From early childhood in Sunday School and Hebrew
School to Junior Congregation, to Cub Pack, to Boy
Scouts and Girl Scouts . . . from Young Judea and A. Z.
A. to Junior Hadassah and Masada . . . from these to
active participation in the larger life of the adult com¬
munity as it expresses itself through synagogue, sister¬
hood, fraternal, charitable and Zionist organizations,
the Center has the facilities to give the Jewish child the
opportunity for a full, active and happy life and to elicit
from it the finest qualities of a good citizen and a loyal
Jew.
