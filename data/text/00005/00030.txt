In his closing address To Israel our great teacher Moses sai d: This day
thou hast declared the Lord to be thy God and to walk in His ways, to keep
His statutes, His commandments and His judgements and to hearken unto his
voice. And the Lord has declared you this day to be unto Him a chosen people
as He had spoken unto you that you may keep His commandments.
Israel was the pioneer nation in the field of morality, and was first to preach
the ideals of human brotherhood. Israel became the chosen people, because it
chose to follow in the way of God, and recognized the great value of the laws
of morality, of justice, of righteousness, which teach us to love our neighbors
and to pursue peace.
♦
The words of Moses are also addressed to me, on this day. It is not enough
that I was born a Jew, but on My Bar Mitzvah day I choose of my own free will
to take upon myself the duties of a member of the chosen people—to live accord¬
ing to the commands of the Torah, a clean life, an honest life and a moral life.
I pledge myself at all times to observe the festivals and holidays of our
religion; to help our fellow Jews whenever and wherever they are victims of
persecution and hatred; to help redeem the land of Israel for the people of Israel;
to advance the cause of brotherhood and peace among all men. I also pledge
myself, in accordance with the principals of our faith, always to be ready to do
my duty as an American citizen, and to help our country in every good cause.
