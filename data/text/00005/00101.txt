JACKSONVILLE SECTION
I he National Council of Jewish
Women is an organization whose pur¬
pose it is to unite all Jewish women in
unselfish service work and in concrete
manifestations of their unlimited pride
in Americanism. Although differing
widely in 'their backgrounds and
modes of religious worship, the se wo¬
men nevertheless possess a common
bond in their desire to aid the under¬
privileged and the needy.
Established in Jacksonville only two
years ago, the Council has already
found a place in our community. Its
primary aim was to demonstrate to
our fellow citizens who do not share
our racial and spiritual background
that we feel the same community re¬
sponsibility as they do and wish to
join hands with them in bettering the
conditions of all human beings.
This we have done very concretely.
The Toyery, the Immigration and Nat¬
uralization Bureau, our hospital visit¬
ing and our participation in the work
of the Red Cross, all these are ac¬
tivities designed to serve all classes
and creeds.
On the other hand, our social ad¬
justment program for New Americans
and our part in the program o f the
Jewish Welfare Board for the enter¬
tainment of Army-Navy men are de¬
voted to making newcomers of our
own faith feel more welcome in our
community.
