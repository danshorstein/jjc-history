A Program and a Record of Achievement
By IRA STEIN
President Army and Navy Committee of the Jewish 'Welfare Board
IRA STEIN, President
The Army and Navy Committee
of the Jewish Welfare Board concerns
itself with meeting the religious, per¬
sonal, social and recreational needs of
the Jewish soldiers and sailors and
is interested in providing the normaliz¬
ing influences that go to make a better
and happier man in uniform.
In order to serve the men o f the
military to the fullest possible extent,
the Committee, at the time of its for¬
mation, elected the following ten-point
program: l) religious services at Camp
Blanding and at the U. S. Naval
Air Station, in cooperation with local
Rabbis, 2) arrangements for attend¬
ance of soldiers and sailors at religious
services held at the Center and
Temple, 3) arrangements for special
gatherings and social affairs in the
auditorium of the Center and the ves¬
try room of the 1 emple, 4) home
hospitality for the service men at the
homes of the members of our two
congregations, 5) hospital visitation
under the direction of and with the
cooperation of the Red Cross and the
Post and Air Station chaplains, 6)
providing a calendar of events in
Jacksonville to include cultural and
recreational activities in which the
service men are interested, 7) contri¬
buting to the well-being of each man
on the basis of his personal needs
and problem^, 8) providing informa¬
tion for relatives and friends who
wish to visit them at the Camp or
Air Station, 9) contacting the parents
of the service men and keeping them
informed as to the welfare o f their
sons and the services extended to
them by the Committee and 10) pro¬
viding a list of rooming accomoda¬
tions available for them when coming

Organized in March 1941, the or¬
ganization immediately went into ac¬
tion and within a very short time
this comprehensive program had be¬
come a reality.
Dances or buffet suppers were held
each Saturday night at the Jewish
Center or in the Vestry Room of the
l emple.
Each Sunday morning, services were
held at Camp Blanding, Rabbi I. L.
Kaplan and Rabbi M. D. Margolis
alternating weekly in taking charge
of the services. During the month ol
August, the cojnmittee was fortúnate
in having assigned for this purpose
Rabbi Will iam B. Schwartz of Law¬
rence, L. I., who volunteered his ser¬
vices during his vacation and con¬
ducted services on Friday evenings
at the Camp. A traitional Kiddush
was held after the services, followed
by a social hour at which refresh¬
ments were served to approximately
200 soldiers.
Congregational Seders were held at
the Temple and at the Center during
Passover. Over 300 Jewish men in
uniform attended these Seders free of
charge, the entire expense being paid
by individual members of the two
congregations. In addition to that,
over 100 men were invited in private
homes for Passover feasts.
On Shovuoth, the Committee made
arrangements for leave from Camp in
order that the men may attend ser¬
vices at the Center and Temple. Over
225 attended and received invitations
for dinner at private homes. Approx¬
imately 73 soldiers were given meals
at a local Jewish restaurant.
Kits containing writing material,
Bibles, Prayer Books and Jewish Cal¬
endars were distributed by the com¬
mittee to about 650 Jewish soldiers
and sailors. 1 hey were supplied by
the National Office.

sonville area was assigned by the
National Office in the person o f Mr.

our city the latter part of Apri I. Mr.
J arail h as since been actively engaged

During the six months ol its exist¬
ence. the Army and Navy Committee
has conscientiously and wholehearted¬
ly performed its set duties and visibly
aided the men in uniform to adapt
themselves to the army and navy life.
The committee has thus filled a vital
need created by the national emer¬
gency and established a splendid re¬
cord of achievement of which Jack¬
sonville Jewry may justly be proud.
