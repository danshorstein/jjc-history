For a number of years, tbe Young
Judea clubs in Jacksonville have been
a strong factor in developing tbe per¬
sonalities of the young boys and girls
reaching adolescence age by charging
them with the responsibilities of office
and acquainting them with the age-
old yearning of Israel to return to
its homeland. Thanks to the training
in the routine organizational life which
Jewish youngsters are given in these
clubs, material for leadership in more
adult organizations is furnished from
the folds of Young Judea. The a bility
of many of those who today are shap¬
ing the course of A. Z. A., Junior
Hadassah and Masada can thus be
traced to their earlier life, the period of
formation in which Young Judea
played a vital part.
Directing the activities of the clubs
is the central body known as the
Young Judean Council of Jackson¬
ville which is composed of the leaders
of the various clubs, their presidents,
and representatives from the various
Zionist organizations in the commun¬
ity. Last year the Council was under
the chairmanship of Mrs. Rudy Moss.
A number of fine projects for the
coming year are already being plan¬
ned by the Council and it is hoped
that several new groups can be or¬
ganized to supplement the old groups
who are now reorganizing.
