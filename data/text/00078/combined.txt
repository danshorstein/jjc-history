RABBI SAMUEL BENJAMIN
FOREWORD I
A long cherished ideal has at last been realized. The Jacksonville
Jewish Center is now a reality surpassing in many ways the fondest dreams
of the most enthusiastic optimists. The edifice has called forth the admiration
of all those who have seen it, and its fame has already spread to other com'
munities. Lavish compliments have been paid Jacksonville Jewry for this
great achievement, and the local Jewish community may well congratulate it'
self upon the acquisition ot such a splendid institution. =
The new edifice, however, will fail in its purpose unless it has the
living spirit of Judaism within it. The goal which the Jacksonville Jewish
Center has set for itself ought to win the support not only of every Jew in
the community but of every public spirited individual in the city. The program
of the Jacksonville Jewish Center calls for the maintenance of such activities
which will satisfy the religious, social, cultural, educational, recreational, and
physical needs of the community. \
To achieve this aim the new building has a beautiful synagogue which
can accomodate almost one thousand people a spacious auditorium and ball
room which is equipped with a stage for the presentation of plays and has
all the facilities to make it suitable for all public functions such as banquets,

A Bet Hamidrash has been provided for daily prayer and study, and social =

By these means the Jacksonville Jewish Center will be enabled not only =
to conserve the noble ideals and traditions of Judaism but to play an inn e

The influence of the Jacksonville Jewish Center is already making itself \
felt in the community. Never before have such large congregations attended \
Jewish services as now crowd the Center synagogue. The enrollment in the E
Hebrew and Sunday School has markedly increased. Social affairs already held E
in the Center auditorium have proven pronounced successes. It was the in- E
spiration of the Jacksonville Jewish Center which has brought the Southern =
Interstate Young Juáaea Oratorical Contest and the coming convention of this

realisation of this program. A united community actively supporting this
institution will make possible for the Jacksonville Jewish Center to realise its E
ideal of being not only a structure of bricks and stones but a building with a =
soul portraying the beauty of Jewish life, enhancing the glory of America, safe- I
guarding the spiritual welfare of young and old, and ever functioning in the
service of God and Man. =

OUTLINE HISTORY OF
THE JACKSONVILLE JEWISH CENTER MOVEMENT
Dr Samuel Benjamin appeals for the erection of a Jewish Center at his
first appearance before a meeting of the ‘ Congregation Bfoai Israel, April
11th, 1926.
Congregation B’nai Israel extends invitation to all Jewish organisations
in Jacksonville to unite with it in a movement to build the Jewish Center
May 23rd, 1926.
Mr.-Harry Finkelstein accepts chairmanship of Jacksonville Jewish Center
Building Fund Committee May 30th, 1926.
Building ■ Fund Committee reports the purchase of the house and lot on
the N. W. corner of Silver and West Third St. June -20th, 1926.
Building Fund Campaign for $250,000 November 29—December 12, 1926
Organisation Meeting of the Jacksonville Jewish Center a corporation
not for profit January 6th, 1927.
Breaking of ground June 5 th, 1927.
Laying of Cornerstone September 18th, 1927
First Affair in Jacksonville Jewish Center, Hanuka ‘ Celebration
December 25th, 1927.
Dedication of Jewish Center January 8th, 1928.
