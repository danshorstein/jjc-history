RABBI SAMUEL BENJAMIN
FOREWORD I
A long cherished ideal has at last been realized. The Jacksonville
Jewish Center is now a reality surpassing in many ways the fondest dreams
of the most enthusiastic optimists. The edifice has called forth the admiration
of all those who have seen it, and its fame has already spread to other com'
munities. Lavish compliments have been paid Jacksonville Jewry for this
great achievement, and the local Jewish community may well congratulate it'
self upon the acquisition ot such a splendid institution. =
The new edifice, however, will fail in its purpose unless it has the
living spirit of Judaism within it. The goal which the Jacksonville Jewish
Center has set for itself ought to win the support not only of every Jew in
the community but of every public spirited individual in the city. The program
of the Jacksonville Jewish Center calls for the maintenance of such activities
which will satisfy the religious, social, cultural, educational, recreational, and
physical needs of the community. \

can accomodate almost one thousand people a spacious auditorium and ball
room which is equipped with a stage for the presentation of plays and has
