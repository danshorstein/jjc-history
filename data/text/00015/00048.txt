“Bringing the Youth Back to the Synagogue/’ These
words symbolize the aims and efforts of the United Syna¬
gogue Youth. With a wide program of cultural, social, and
religious activities, USY steps up to be one of the fastest
developing youth groups in the United States and Canada.
Beginning its eighth year of development, CYL will
again participate in the Leadership Training Institute, being
held in Hendersonville, North Carolina; the National Con¬
vention, in W ashington, D. C. and the Regional convention,
this year selected to be held in Jacksonville. These conven¬
tions combined with our own socials, study groups and Sab¬
bath service participation will help inspire the members to
acquire a better understanding of Judaism.
The Charles Palot Club, the younger division of CYL,
is now in its third year. It also strives for the same aims as
put forth by the older group. CPC gives the basic founda¬
tion and fundamental leadership for those entering CYL.
The financial asistance of the Center Sisterhood and the
Center Men's Club have given us the advantage to be able
to attend the conventions which USY offers. CYL-CPC are
indeed grateful to those organizations and to the Center
Youth Activities Committee for their wonderful cooperation
and assistance.
To Mrs. Milton Lazarus, our deepest appreciation for all
her help throughout the years as our advisor. She has given
inspiration, loyalty and warm feeling to every member.
