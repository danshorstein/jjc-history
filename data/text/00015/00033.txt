Holiness Carved in Beauty
Framing the doors of our Ark are a series of panels de¬
picting eight sacred occasions of the Jewish year. The sym¬
bols are sculptured in walnut and are embellished by Hebrew
lettering of an appropriate quotation.
1. The Sabbath. Two candlesticks and the kiddush cup;
a loaf of twisted Hallah bread in the background. The
inscription: “Sabbath holiness.”
2. Rosh Hashanah. A shofar, a set of scales symbolic
of divine judgment in the background. Inscription: “Happy
New Year” and the word “Repentance” which together with
the two Hebrew words in the next panel occur in the Une
Tañe Tokef prayer.
3. Yom Kippur. A prayer book with the verse “On this
day shall Atonement be made for you” inscribed on its open
pages and a Charity plate, with a tall taper candle alongside.
Inscription: “Prayer and Charity.” The full quotation is
“Repentance, Prayer and Charity lead to Grace and Salva¬
tion”.
4. Siikkot. Lulav (palm branch) set into a holder with
myrtle and willow branches, a etrog (citron) lies alongside.
Inscription: “Thou shalt rejoice in thy feast.”
5. Hanukah. Menorah with candles modeled after an
antique piece found in the Jewish Museum. In the back¬
ground are a small shield with the letter “mem” for Macca¬
bees and a cruse of oil. Inscription: “Rock of Ages, my
Salvation.”
6. Purim. Megillah scroll of Esther coming out of a
silver case, inscribed are the first three words of the Book of
Esther and the names Mordecai and Esther. In the back¬
ground are a crown and scepter. Inscription: “Peace to all
his seed”, the three closing words of the Book.
7. Passover. Four cups embellished with grape leaves
and the Hebrew word Pesah which represent the four-fold
promise of redemption. In the background are three mat-
zohs used at the seder. Inscription: “Let my people go that
they worship me.”
8. Shavuot. Torah scroll in decorated cover with silver
ornaments. In the background are stalks of wheat and grape¬
vines symbolic of the Bikhurim, first fruits, theme of the
festival. Inscription: “Torah of Truth”.
Note: A fuller description of the significance of the sacred
occasions symbolized here will be found in the explanation
of the Hebrew Calendar printed in another section.
