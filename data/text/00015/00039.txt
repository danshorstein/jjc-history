Center Sisterhood
Sisterhood s last year was a very full and fruitful one.
Our membership of approximately 600 supported our pro¬
gram, so that every function undertaken proved highly
successful. Because of this cooperation, Sisterhood was able
to meet all of its annual obligations, and assume extra
ones as well.
Into our General Fund comes money from (a) Dues
(b) Food show and Bazaar (c) Rummage sales (d) Lunch¬
eons (e) The sale of World Book Encyclopedias and (f)
Incidentals.
First to be taken care of were our regularly established
commitments, which include:
a. Sisterhood’s assistance to the Center Youth League,
by helping to defray expenses to National Conven¬
tions, Leadership Training Institutes, and Regional
Conventions.
b. Sisterhood’s assistance to the Center’s Education
Program, by providing funds for our Sunday Reli¬
gious School.
c. Providing prizes and awards for pupils and refresh¬
ments for special celebrations.
d. Sisterhood’s Administrative needs.
1. Get acquainted brunch for new members
2. Membership Tea
3. Hanukah Institute
4. Mailings
5. Programs
6. Girl Scout and Brownie troops, etc.
Some of the additional services that we were able to
provide were:
a. Subsidizing delegates to a Young People’s League
convention.
b. Awarding of a scholarship to one of our Flebrew
School graduates for a special summer course in
Hebrew at the Jewish Theological Seminary to train
as assistant Hebrew teacher.
c. Modern tubular steel furniture for a classroom con¬
sisting of 24 tables and 36 chairs, also provided a
teacher’s desk.
d. Menorahs for the three girl scouts who earned a
special religious award, known as the menorah
award and initiated this year by our Rabbi and scout
leaders.
(1) Committee Chairmen, identified by respective banners, who reviewed projects and activities at opening meeting
(2) Bazaar and Food show committee chairmen
(3) Participants in "Chapeaux a la Mode" skit at Donor Coffee
