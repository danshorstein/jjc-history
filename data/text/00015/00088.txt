HARRY YERGIN
This year the largest group of boys was inducted into the
Cub Scouts in the history of Jacksonville Cubbing. This
class was known as the Leo Michelson Bobcat Class. The
troop consists of approximately 75 boys and is the largest
pack in the city.
Among the activities for past year was the Fathers’ and
Sons’ Banquet which was held at the Center and a wonder¬
ful time was had by all. The outing at Talbot Island was
a huge success. Fathers and Sons played ball, enjoyed fish¬
ing and picnicing,
1 he Minstrel show, which was directed by Cubmaster
Dr. Harry Yergin was put on with all the talents from our
Cub Pack. Enough funds were raised to register the Pack
for the coming year. First prize for selling the most tickets
was won by Alan Greenspan.
A general meeting of all Cub Scouts will be held shortly
after the Holidays to plan another active and successful year.
