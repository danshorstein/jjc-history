BOY SCOUTS - TROOP 114
MANUEL GOLDSTEIN
This has been a year of intensified activity in Scouting
lore, practices and skills. At mid-summer the membership
of Troop 14 is divided into the following categories: 3 Eagle
Scouts, 2 Star Scouts, 5 First Class Scouts, 4 Second Class
Scouts and 4 Tenderfoot Scouts.
At this year’s Charter Presentation, Stephen Hackel was
presented with the Outstanding Scout award; Herbert Fish¬
ier for Advancement and Attendance; and George Glossman
for Best Service and Attendance.
This year’s sojourn at Camp Echokotee saw much ad¬
vancement along with the fun of camping and outdoor life.
The program of weekend overnight hikes has furthered the
boy’s knowledge of fire-building, cooking, practical compass
work and trail blazing, etc.
Our troop was well represented at this year’s Philmont
Expedition by Scouts Herbert Fishier and Stephen Hackel.
They reported a wonderful time along with learning ad¬
vanced skills and Scout craft.
Under the able leadership of Marshall Grauer, an ex¬
plorer Post is in the process of being formed. With the good
work of the troop committee and under the leadership of the
Scoutmaster we look to bigger and better things in this com¬
ing year and for years to come.
4 Scouts Stephen Hackel and Herbert Fishier, who attended Camp
Philmont this summer.
