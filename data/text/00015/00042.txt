The past ten years have witnessed the growth of our American
Jewish Community in population, the growth of organizational life,
and the growth of instruments for conveying knowledge of Judaism.
Our Men’s Club is part of this development; it shows a healthy
increase in number of members, it shows a desire to be of service
in aiding congregational as well as community activities, and it has
the ability to foster new techniques for imparting Jewish knowledge.
Where the Men’s Club has lived up to this, the Synagogue has
grown; where it has failed, the synagogue has lost ground.
JOSEPH LIPSON
Now, what can the Men’s Club do for the synagogue. First, the club cannot he considered as an independent fraternal or philan¬
thropic organization. The Men's Club is part of a religious institution and a religious movement. Whatever it engages in must always
express Jewish dignity and self respect as well as be in harmony with Jewish tradition and discipline. They must help by means of their
activities to contribute to the advancement of Jewish religious values.
The important thing is the deed not the word. This is what your Men’s Club does:
1. Organizes an annual Sunday School picnic
2. Award trophies for outstanding Hebrew school students
3. Serve breakfasts Sunday morning to Minyan Club members around Bar Mitzvah age to aid in attendance.
4. Conduct an annual membership campaign; many of our members through contact with the Synagogue soon join the Center.
5. Hold nine meetings a year with interesting speakers both on entertainment and cultural levels.
6. Created a fund for Visual and Audio aids for the Hebrew schol and other projects.
7. Developed a Jewish study program for disseminating Jewish knowledge to those who wish to learn.
8. Created a fund for the training of Religious School teachers when needed.
9. Conduct an annual Blood Bank drive held yearly for the use of ill people. This hank is never appreciated until needed.
10.Contributed a set of Torah furnishings for the new Ark.
