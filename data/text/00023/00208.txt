ADAR II
PURIM (literally, “lots”) — this gayest of Jewish holidays is nonetheless
named for the lots cast before Haman to select a day of execution for the
Jews. On the fateful day, however, it was the wicked Haman who
perished — thanks to Queen Esther and her cousin, Mordecai. The full
story is told in the Megillah (Book of Esther, our symbol) which, on this
day, is read aloud twice in the synagogue. During the reading, children
twirl their gragers (noisemakers) whenever Haman is mentioned, to drown
out his name.
