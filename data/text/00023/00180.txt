B'nai B’rith Women are dedicated to serving you, your family,
our country and all humanity, through our support of the following
B’nai B’rith philanthropies; National Jewish Hospital for tuberculosis
and chest diseases in Denver, Colorado; Bellefaire Children’s Home
for emotionally disturbed children in Cleveland, Ohio; Leo N. Levi
Memorial Hospital for arthritic & rheumatoid diseases in Hot Springs,
Arkansas; the B’nai B'rith Family Center for Jewish patients at the
Mayo Clinic in Rochester; and the Four Freedoms Library in Wash¬
ington, D.C.; B'nai B’rith projects in Israel include the Martyrs’
Forest, Hillel at the Hebrew University, the Veterans’ Rehabilitation
Center and the B'nai B'rith Women’s sponsored Children’s Home.
The most widely known B'nai B'rith project Anti-Defamation League
works for all Jewish people and all people of all faiths. Locally:
“The Dolls for Democracy”, distribution of memo books to the pub¬
lic schools, sponsorship of the B'nai B’rith Girls, Aid to Pine Castle
School, Hillel at the University of Florida and The Very new Spanish
Officers
Classes and English Classes at the River Garden Home for the Aged.
We also sponsor two Jewish papers daily to the River Garden Home
for the Aged.
The State Convention held in Miami was attended by Mrs.
Danglo, President and she was elected to the State Board. The Dis¬
trict Convention was attended by the State Federation of BBW, past
president Mrs. Jack Dorman and the chapters encumbent President.
This coming year we will have several changes in this chapters
activities; The “Calendar Ad-Date Book” which promises a new
vista in the financial income. The newly formed B’nai B’rith
Women’s Bowling League. To keep our youth informed and active
we the women of B'nai B’rith find that the closeness of the parent
organization with the BBG’s affords us the opportunity and awarded
our President by being made an honorary member.
All this proves that the name B'nai B’rith is synonymous with
our Motto “SERVICE: FIDELITY: DEVOTION.”
