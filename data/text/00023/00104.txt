Another banner year in the annals of the C.P.C.
group has been successfully recorded.
Among the many outstanding and interesting pro¬
grams that were held during the past year were a discus¬
sion by the Director of the Civic Council on Youth Af¬
fairs concerning the council’s teenage activities, and a
fascinating discussion and demonstration on the methods
of hypnotism.
Interesting telephone calls were placed to our former
Rabbi Goldblum in Pittsburg; Carol Silvers, Correspond¬
ing Secretary of USY in New York; and to Dr. Morton
Siegel, Director of the USY.
1964-65 C.P.C. Officers
Conventions were numerous for our local chapter this
year, as we had the honor of serving as the host chapter
for the 400 members in attendance of the Regional Con¬
vention. This was the 13th annual Regional, and a good
and profitable time was enjoyed by all. We also hosted
the first USY National Send-off, to raise money for the
project “Building Spiritual Bridges” which builds dormi¬
tories for schools in Israel. Over $240 was raised for the
fund.
The following represented us at the 1964 L.T.I.:
Saralee Newman, Mendy Marks, Leslie Edwards, and
Dolly Fleet.
