AZA can proudly boast of accomplishing one of the most sought for projects, that of
“OPERATION AMIGO,” a project which brought 30 Guatemalan students to Jacksonville
for two weeks. Through the efforts of Bruce Gibbs and Louise Hilsenrad, along with Engle¬
wood High School and the B'nai B'rith members who attended Englewood.
We held an interfaith program and a study of Judaism. Gators BBYO were host to the
North Florida Convention at which time Jeff Bootzin was elected as North Florida Council
President. The discussion groups subjects pertaining to the Jewish Youth, World Problems
and Domestic Problems were very successful.
Our members participated in several cookie sales.
Our thanks to Myron Blattner, who again this year gave of his time with endless patience.

“Gators" BBG is proud to say that it has had a very successful year. “Gators'5 started
the year with an invitational tea for prospective members. We then collected candy on
Halloween for the children in Brewster and Hope Haven Hospitals. We received many
honors at our Regional BBYO convention (all of Florida) in December. We were awarded
“Most Spirited" of all the chapters, our scrapbook won first place, our Oratory won second
place, and our Alma Mater won first place. BBG has had two successful Brownie Sales in
which we collected over one-hundred dollars. Last month we presented a program for the
residents at River Garden. We concluded our official year with an Installation Luncheon for
the newly elected officers at the Town House Restaurant. Superlatives were presented and
new officers installed.
