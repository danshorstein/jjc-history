Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society was founded forty
years ago to help the needy of the Jewish faith. It was the
dream and hope of our founders to establish a Home for the
Aged in Jacksonville. This dream became a reality when
River Garden Hebrew Home for the Aged was founded in
1946. Since that time our role became that of the auxiliary
to the Home.
We provide extra-budgetary needs such as furnishings,
redecorating, air conditioning, kitchen and infirmary equip¬
ment, as well as equipment for the Hobby Shop. Funds are
raised through Tombola, Donor and Social Printing proj¬
ects, in addition to annual dues and life memberships in
Ladies Aid. Linens are provided by an annual Linen Shower
which we sponsor.
The Grey Ladies Program of the Home is a Ladies Aid
project. Within the past year, this service was expanded to
include a corps of teenage Gardenettes who serve on week¬
ends and during vacation periods.
River Garden residents participate in projects served
by auxiliary members, among them being the residents’
magazine, ceramics and art classes, and other Hobby Shop
activities.
The auxiliary supplies a door-to-door transportation serv¬
ice for the weekly Golden Sunshine Club meetings held at
River Garden. This autonomous group emerged from our

weekly Senior Citizens meetings. Ladies Aid representatives
continue to lend support and guidance to the group which
provides fellowship and companionship to oldsters from the
general community as well as River Garden’s own residents.
With the opening of the new Wolfson Infirmary wing,
the work and responsibilities of the auxiliary has reached a
new magnitude. Ladies Aid pledges to continue its efforts
and cooperation in bringing not only the material but also
the affectional and spiritual values so necessary for our
elder citizens.
