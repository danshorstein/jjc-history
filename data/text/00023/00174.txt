Hadassah was founded in 1912 by Henrietta Szold, and from
a mere handful of dedicated American women, has grown to a
membership of over 318,000.
Hadassah projects are actively functioning on two far-flung
horizons. On the American scene, an Educational Program, a Youth
Activities program, the Young Judeans and the Junior Hadassah,
Hadassah co-sponsors with Z.O.A. Camp Judaea, a summer camp in
Hendersonville, North Carolina. In Israel, Hadassah encompasses
Hadassah Medical Organization, Medical Center, Youth Aliyah,
Vocational Education, and Jewish National Fund. By helping Israel
help itself, so that it can take its place with dignity among the
freedom-loving nations of the world, Hadassah reaffirms the Jewish
faith in the heritage of freedom.
Two groups comprise the Jacksonville Chapter: the Senior
Hadassah and the Hannah Senesch Evening Group. Almost 800
women boast membership in the local chapter, and annually seek
to raise quotas totaling nearly $20,000 in order to meet commitments
largely in Israel, as well as in America.
