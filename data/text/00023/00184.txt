The National Council of Jewish Women is an organization dedicated to
furthering human welfare in the Jewish and general communities, locally, nationally,
and internationally.
Some of our community projects are:
Teachers’ Aide — helping elementary school children with
problems with their academic work;
Juvenile Shelter — a program of recreational activity the
year-round conducted by our volunteers;
Medical Loan Shelf — supplies hospital equipment to indi¬
gents of the community through the Visiting Nurses Assn.;
Mental Health Fair — educational program for the com¬
munity that is co-sponsored with the Mental Health Assn.;
Sewing Committee—makes baby garments for needy mothers
and this year sent a package to the Alaskan Disaster;
Mrs. Sol Goldman,
hearing and speech President
Tribute Fund — purchases hearing aids for indigent
children;
Ship-a-Box — sends supplies and toys to kindergartens in
Israel.
The Jacksonville Section this year will observe its 25th.
Anniversary of community, state, national and inter¬
national service.
