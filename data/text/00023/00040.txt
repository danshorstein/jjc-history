The Torah Fund Program was an inspiration to all
making us aware of our responsibilities as Jewish women.
This year, we again had "‘Breakfast with the Rebetzin”, a
brunch held at the home of Rabbi and Mrs. Arnold Turetsky
honoring our Torah Fund Contributors. "Phis fund helps to
maintain the Seminary and its many projects.
April 1st was a very interesting Social Action program
with Mr. Bill Basford, State Representative, as our guest
speaker.
The Closing Luncheon was held on May 6th featuring
the Installation of New Officers. Mrs. Jack Becker and Mrs.
Nat Cohen were the narrators of this most inspiring candle-
lighting ceremony. Afterwards, Mrs. Norman Slaff, the
newly installed President presented Mrs. Louis Slott, out¬
going President, with a gift from the Sisterhood in apprecia¬
tion of her leadership and devotion. Rabbi Arnold Turetsky
added to the Installation with a magnificent message.

Sisterhood works very closely with our two United
Synagogue Youth groups and sponsors the Girl Scouts and
Brownie Troops. This year, we are happy to announce that
we presented four scholarships for our youth to attend Camp
Ramah.
The annual Florida Branch Conference was held in
Hollywood Beach on May 11th, 12th, and 13th, and was
attended by Mrs. Arnold Turetsky, Mrs. Mitchell Peltz, Mrs.
Norman Slaff and Mrs. Beryl Weinstein. We are very fortu¬
nate that the 1965 Conference will be here in Jacksonville
on May 10, 11, & 12.
Funds which enable us to fulfill our obligations to the
Center are obtained from our Gift Shop, sale of Barton’s
Candy, World Book Encyclopedia, Leagrams, and the
beautiful family tablecloth put together by Mrs. Harry
Slott and embroidered by many of our members.
We are looking forward to another successful year with
your continued support. We, of Sisterhood, wish you and
your dear ones a very wonderful year.
