1963-1964 was truly a “Chai” year as River Garden celebrated
its 18th year of service to the aged and infirm of Florida. The
Morris and Sarah Wolfson Pavilion opened in October of 1963 and
since then has been operating at full capacity.
The added facilities opened new vistas of activities which con¬
tinue to enrich and broaden the range of recreational and social

events. The 4th Floor solarium has become a class room, a lecture
hall, a card room, and a lounge, with a magnificent view of the
river. A full size stage and large auditorium encourages River
Garden players. In their pageantry of color, song and costume, the
players have won warm acclaim for their productions.
They have proved without doubt that old age is no barrier to
a rich and active life.
All is not fun or play at River Garden. There are the ever
prevalent problems of illness so common to the aged. The healing
arts of medicine and nursing give the support necessary to maintain
life and help the aged to live to maximum capacity. Emphasis is on
“wellness” and not on “illness”.
It is this basic principle of a healthy mind in a healthy body
which dominates the way of life at River Garden.
