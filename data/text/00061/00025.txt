Simhat Torah Honorees
The High Holiday season reaches its joyous climax on Simhat Torah, the last day of the festival
of Sukkot. Because of our perennial love of the Torah, it has been referred to as Israel’s “bride”. On
Simhat Torah, we bring to a close the annual cycle of the reading of the Torah and immediately begin
anew. An ancient tradition evolved of honoring worthy men by calling them to the Torah for the last
reading, Hatan Torah, and the beginning portion, Hatan Bereshit. In our synagogue tradition, we
also honor the contributions made by a “Woman of Valor”.

Rarely do
you hear of a
person with as
generous a nature
as Mel Gottlieb.
He has celebrated
many successes in
his professional
and personal life,
but his true gift is his philanthropic
spirit.
There isn’t a local Jewish agency
that hasn’t been touched by Mel. He
gives financial, emotional and
administrative resources to the entire
local Jewish community. A native of
Jacksonville, his reach extends beyond
his home town. Mel is co-founder of the
Dresnick-Gottlieb Institute for
Emergency Medicine in Israel; President
of the Jacksonville Jewish Foundation;
and Vice-President of the Jacksonville
Jewish Center, the Jacksonville Jewish
Federation, River Garden Foundation and
the Jewish Community Alliance. He
serves on various civic boards.
Mel is the founding sponsor of
the Jacksonville Jewish Center’s Annual
Golf Tournament, the proceeds of which
provide tuition assistance to the Center
schools. Typical of his vision was the
establishment of the community’s B’nai
Tzedek program, which encourages
philanthropy among Bar/Bat Mitzvah
students by encouraging them to
establish their own personal account for
charitable giving.
Mel has been honored numerous
times, even being named Philanthropist
of the Year 2000. We were proud to name
him Hatan Torah for 2001.
No matter
what time of day, no
matter what your
purpose, if you
walk into the
Jacksonville Jewish
Center, it is likely
you will run into
Bruce Horovitz.
Bruce’s devotion to the Center
goes way beyond his job as Executive
Director. For starters, he attends minyan,
davens, and reads Torah, greets new
members, attends programs in the Day
School, the Preschool and the Religious
School.
Bom in Savannah, Georgia, Bruce
grew up in Jacksonville where the Center
was his second home. He was not only
a student in our Religious Schools, but
also a leader, Bruce served as president
of both the Charles Palot Club and USY.
While most of us know Bruce
as an avid Gator fan, he graduated with
a degree in Journalism from Boston
University. As a reporter for the
Jacksonville Journal he won several
statewide awards. Before becoming
Executive Director, he served on the
Center Board of Directors for many
years, as Vice President, chairman of the
Recreation Committee, and an active
member of the Building Fund Committee.
During his tenure as Executive Director,
the Center has been able to pay off the
mortgage on the building.
Bruce’s enthusiasm and strong
values and commitment to the highest
level of service for our congregants,
earned him the title Hatan Bereshit
A native of
Princeton, N.J.,
Beth Shorstein
moved here 22
years ago and
immediately
became active at
the Jacksonville
Jewish Center.
A member of Center Sisterhood
for 21 years, Beth served as Vice
President 3 times and as its President.
Beth received a leadership award from
the Florida Branch of Women’s League
and has served on its board.
Beth has been active in many
areas of Center life. She sings in the
choir and created a children’s choir.
She taught in all of our schools,
concentrating on music. Beth held
positions in the Day School PTA. For
seven years she was the leader of
Brownie Troop 882, and created the first
Daisy troop at the Center. She served
on the Center Board of Directors for
over ten years and chaired the Bikkur
Cholim/Mitzvah Committee.
Beth’s volunteerism has
extended beyond the synagogue as a
member of Tikvah Hadassah, a past
president of the National Council of
Jewish Women, on the planning
committee of Grove House, a member
of the River Garden Board of Directors,
as well as the Jewish Hospice
committee. Beth was awarded the
Michael J. Wilson Social Action award
by the local Chapter of the National
Association of Social Workers.
Beth’s extensive and prestigious
education serves her well at Jewish
Family & Community Services where
she is the Coordinator of the Jewish
Healing Network.
