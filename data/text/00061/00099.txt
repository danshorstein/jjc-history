Camp Neshama
“Summer Camping at its Best!
Camp was a blast! Sports and games, swimming,
computer science, arts and crafts, boating, field
trips, hanging out with friends, sleep-overs... and
that was just the beginning! Each day at Camp
Neshama was filled with exciting activities geared
to the variety of interests of young campers.
Rabbi Dov Kentof brought camping at Neshama
to a new level. He taught outdoor skills like tent assembly,
preparing a campfire and cooking “eggs in a blanket.”
His Judaica programs were wonderful and the Shabbat
presentations were enjoyed by campers and their
families.
The field trips were awesome. Visits to the
Museum of Natural History, Stephen Foster State
Park, Keystone Heights, Bowl America, Skateland,
Adventure Landing and the Annabelle Lee were
some of the wonderful experiences. For the
youngest campers, exciting outside activities came
to them. Critter Connection was a favorite.
Specialty Camps concentrated on one area of
interest to campers. They were a big success.
Everyone had a wonderful summer, and we look
forward with great anticipation to the 2003 Camp
Nesahama season.
— Kelly Waeltz, Camp Director
