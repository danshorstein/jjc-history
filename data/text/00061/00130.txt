Scouting
Brownie Troop 882
Brownie Troop 882 had a wonderful first year. Many of the girls were members of our Daisy Troop last year
and stayed together as they moved up.
Our year was filled with exciting trips and activities,
as well as community projects. We started with a
wonderful birthday party for Juliet Gordon Low, founder
of Girl Scouting. It took place at the I.M. Sulzbacher
Center for the Homeless, where we left a variety of gifts
that the girls staying there could enjoy throughout the
year.
Our very first cookie sale was a great success. We
sold 2,100 hundred boxes of cookies for a total profit of
$945. Many boxes were donated and we took these “Gifts
of Caring” to the Park Place Retirement Community. We
enjoyed a tea and cookie party with the residents.
We also enjoyed fun field trips to the Karpeles
Manuscript Library Museum, and to the Imax Theatre
to see the movie “Beauty and the Beast.”
Thank you to all who helped make this
year special. I look forward to more great
Brownie experiences next year. For more
information on Brownie Scouts, call Sonya
Cohen at 268-5685.
