Situated on the banks of the St. Johns River, the
Home now consists of three modern buildings able to
house 175 residents in safety and comfort. To assure
privacy and dignified living, there are 75 single rooms
with private baths and 40 twin bedrooms.
In addition, the Home has designed its nursing
program upon a most positive note to encourage “well¬
ness” rather than “illness.” There are 7 nursing areas
which permits .proper classification of the total needs
of our residents besides the medical services of River
Garden.
Chairman of the Board Sheldon Gendzier
This year marks the beginning of the second quar¬
ter of River Garden’s services to the aged and infirm
of Florida. Since 1946, when its doors were first opened
in a converted mansion, the Home has admitted 588
permanent residents and 200 short-term patients.
Providing a positive program creates a climate
of hope that the infirmities of aging need not be a
depressing element in life as meaningful social and re¬
ligious activities enhances the life style of each indi¬
vidual.
Goals for the future are a continuum of providing
more and more enrichment to the lives of our aging
and infirm residents
