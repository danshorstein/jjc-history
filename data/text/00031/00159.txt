The Center Youth League is an affiliate of the
International United Synagogue Youth Program for
students in the 10th, 11th and 12th grades. An
active program is offered throughout the school year
including all phases of synagogue involvement,
study, social events and action on current issues.
The 1972-73 season was off to a great start
when all paid-up members spent the day at the
popular Ichnatuchnee Springs, picnicing and tubing
down the river.
Meetings, held on the second and fourth Sun¬
days of each month, were well attended and cover¬
ed many relevant topics with their relationship to
Jewish concepts such as ‘‘Who Shall Live and Who
Shall Die”, “Prayer in the Public Schools”, “Black
Power” and “Can We Immunize Against Pre¬
judice.”
C.Y.L. also plays an active part as a member
of the Southeast Region of U.S.Y. and delegates
have attended all regional activities, as well as
the National Convention in Washington, D.C.
The annual Sports Day was inaugurated at the
Center Southside Site with all Center U.S.Y. groups
participating. Installation of officers at the closing
Banquet and Dance rounded out a wonderful year.
The Officers and Board members of C.Y.L. wish
to extend greetings and the wish for a Happy New
Year to the entire Congregation’
