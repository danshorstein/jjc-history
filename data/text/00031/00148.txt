It is for the sake of our children that we live
and exist, and for that reason one of the most im¬
portant goals of the Jacksonville Jewish Center
is to train our children and imbue them with the
zeal and fervor of the sages, prophets and philoso¬
phers of Israel.
There is a wonderful story of the Hasidic Rabbi
who returned from a strenuous day’s labor and
said to his wife: “I must sleep or I shall faint.
No matter who comes, do not disturb me.” In a
little while he appeared at the bedroom door with
an anxious face to modify his request. “I made a
mistake”, the Rabbi said, “If a child comes, awaken
me.
Our goal at the Center is to bring to our children
the teachings of the Torah by combining MID¬
RASH and MAASEH, study and practice.
To accomplish this goal an active and multi¬
faceted program of Jewish study and practice in¬
cluding EDUCATIONAL, RECREATIONAL, SO¬
CIAL and CULTURAL ACTIVITY is offered.
Nursery School and Kindergarten, Solomon
Schechter Day School, Religious and Sunday
School, Minyan Club, Youth Choir, Kadimah,
United Synagogue Youth, Day Camp, Camp Ra-
mah and ATID are the vehicles of growth and
maturity offered under the auspices of the Jack¬
sonville Jewish Center.
The resources, manpower and creative talents
of the Jacksonville Jewish Center are dedicated to
the proposition that the torch of our ancestors
must be passed to our offspring in a way which will
enable them to carry on in a better and grander
fashion for the glory of God, the Jewish people
and all mankind.
