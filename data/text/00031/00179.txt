If any institution can be used as a barometer of rapid changes in a
society, it is the school. Nowhere is this better reflected than in the
Religious School. In the past two years, a majority of the first four levels
of our afternoon Hebrew School transferred to the Solomon Schechter Day
School. Many of the courses in the advanced section of the Sunday Religious
School have been radically changed. As teachers of the new mini-courses,
half of the staff will be young intellectual parents, teaching in this area for
the first time. This trend can also be seen in the membership of the Edu¬
cation Committe, where Dr. Saul Eisen has joined Mr. Sheldon Gendzier
as co-chairman.
Perhaps the greatest change in American Jewish education may be
noted by the gradual disappearance of the Jewish teaching profession, so
we must rely on the talents of the community for much of the staffing of
our department.
To the Chairman named above, to the new sub-committee chairman or
the Sunday Religious School, Mr. Lewis Meisel, to all our teachers and office
staff we owe much thanks for meeting the every-increasing challenges of
religious instruction.
