Troop #147 was very busy in 1971-72. In Septem¬
ber and October we spent our time getting acquainted
with one another and the Scout Program to prepare
for the Investiture of our new members. On Veteran’s
Day we went to Skateland and in Novmber we
planned and started rehearsing for a Hannukah Pro¬
gram at River Garden Home. We made yam dolls and
filled a box with Thanksgiving foods for a needy Jew¬
ish family. In December we baked the cookies which
we took to River Garden for our Program and we also
participated in the Hannukah Carnival.
In January we started preparing for our World
Day Program and Father-Daughter Banquet. When the
big day came we had embroidered handkerchiefs for
our fathers and made yarn holders for our mothers.
We planned and served a delicious meal and even
made the aprons to serve. We put on a beautiful World
Day Program and planted a tree at the Campsite. In
March we baked Hamantaschen and helped man a
booth at the Purim Carnival. We participated in Girl
Scout Sabbath with our sister Girl Scout Troop. We
also started to work on our project for the Jubilee
held at the Coliseum for all of the Gateway Council
Girl Scouts.
In April we invited our friendship troop to partici¬
pate in a fun day with us at the Campsite. We played
games and prizes were awarded. We also visited the
Humane Society to learn how to take care of our
pets. In May we made yam covered hangers for our
mothers as gifts for Mothers’ Day and on May 12
we had our Mothers’ Day Tea and Fly-up at which
time all but three of our girls became Junior Girl
Scouts. Mazel Tov!
