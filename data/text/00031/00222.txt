Cub Scouting, as an introduction to the Boy Scouts
of America continues to be one of the outstanding youth
programs offered at the Center. Boys between the ages
of 8 and 10 years find many new and challenging ex¬
periences in Cubbing.
The Cubs in each Den become a close unit as they
work together to develop new hobbies, take field trips,
explore nature and work on craft projects and skits.
The annual Rocket and Pinewood Derbies give the boys
the opportunity to design and make their own rockets
and cars and to enter them in competitive racing.
In honor of Boy Scout Week, the Cubs participate
in the late Friday Sabbath Service and the annual Blue
and Gold Banquet. To close a most successful year, all
members of Pack 14 and their families gathered at the
Center Southside Site for an evening of swimming,
sports, games and cooking over an open fire.
The Cub program would not be possible without
the interest and dedication of many. Our thanks to Cub-
master Sidney Engelhardt for his many years of service,
to Mrs. Fred Kupperman, head Den Mother, and to
the Den Mothers who served with her. Thanks also to
A1 Wolfson, Webelos Leader; Stanley Ruby, Committee
Chairman; and the members of'his committee: William
Newman, Murray Husney, Carl Miller and Ted Freed¬
man, Institutional Representative.
Individual Dens meet weekly at the homes of Den
Mothers. Monthly Pack Meetings are held on the last
Thursday of each month at the Center. New members
are always welcome.
