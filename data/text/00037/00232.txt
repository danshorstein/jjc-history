This has been a banner year for the A.B. Weil Lodge. We have been involved in many successful community projects. We participated in the
Bell Ringer campaign for the Salvation Army at Christmas, a C.P.R. mannequin was donated to the Red Cross, convalescent cards were sent
to Jewish patients in local hospitals. We gave an award for outstanding service to a local policeman. Lastly, and most important, as our main
project for the year, we are donating a Torah to the Hillel House at Florida State University.

An important step in lodge growth was achieved with the merger of the Wolfson Lodge with our lodge this year. We are anxious to affiliate
all interested Jewish men in our fine organization.

Much of the success of this year was due to our President, Milton Hecht and the fine group of officers who worked closely with him. We
expect the upcoming year to be even more successful and once again urge all Jewish men to join and become active.

 

Jewish War Veterans

Lt. Meyer Leibov

The Hebrew Union Veterans (now Jewish War
Veterans) was founded as a direct result of slander
and insinuation that Jews had not participated in the
military during the Civil War. By 1860, however, Jews
had become an accepted part of the U. S. Military.

Jewish War Veterans maintains a strong united
front in Congress, making its voice heard on subjects
such as foreign policy, civil rights, defense spending,
veterans benefits, housing and education.

Through development of national positions on
these issues, J.W.V. helps to insure an effective demo-
cracy and the protection of our rights as Jews, Amer-
icans, and veterans. Hospital programs, veterans
and dependents rehabilitation, scouting, summer
camp scholarships, community activities - J.W.V.
involvement means a better life for everyone.

Every year J.W.V. Post No. 199 participates in the
Memorial Day ceremonies and in the Armistice Day
parade. This year, Memorial Day ceremonies took
place at 3 p.m. on Monday, May 29 at Evergreen
Cemetery. Colors of all veterans groups were pre-
sented and a wreath was laid.
