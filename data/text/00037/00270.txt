As I look back on the past school years, Iam extremely grateful and pleased.
The Solomon Schechter Day School has grown from a Nursery and Kinder-
garten and First Grade to a full six grades. We have experienced many product-
ive years. The last school term was a most enjoyable and successful year.

The Solomon Schechter Day School has an innovative, bicultural curri-
culum of Hebrew learning and General Studies. The highly structured yet
individualized program in language arts, science and social studies, has been
developed by a skilled staff of instructors, resource personnel and university
consultants. Cultural field trips to the Museum of Arts and Sciences, the
Opera, Ballet, the Jacksonville Symphony and the Hayden Burns Library
enhance the child’s awareness of the world through observation, listening
and participation.

The Physical Education program is geared to the abilities of each child with
emphasis on sportsmanship and skill. Included in the program are swimming,

tennis, and small group games. The daily curriculum includes Hebrew language

instruction with a full program of Judaic-Hebrew learning experiences through
dance, song, art, role playing and prayer.

The Jacksonville Jewish Center Solomon Schechter Day School provides

all Jewish children in the community an outstanding General Studies education \

and a rich and rewarding Hebrew-Judaic education. The students of the day
school continually score high on achievement tests and have experienced much
success after leaving our school. This is all credited to a well-rounded program
and the creative atmosphere provided by the teachers, parents and the Educa-
tion Committee of the Center. Marilyn Sandler
