Cub Scouting as an introduction to Boy Scouts, plays an outstanding role for boys between the ages of 8 and 10. The boys advance
from Wolf to Bear and then on to Webelos.

In this day and age when there are so many temptations, our boys find themselves interested in completeing their achievements
toward a higher rank, and participating in the monthly pack meeting. There they put on skits and show off their crafts.

During the year, they shared in many new and different experiences. They look forward to the Annual Pinewood and Rocket
Derbys, Scout Sabbath and the Blue and Gold Banquet.

To close the year, members of Pack 14 and their families gathered at the Center Day Campsite for an afternoon of swimming and
cooking over an open fire. The highlight of the event was the presentation of the Larry Hacker Cub Scout Award. It went to the
Cub with the best attendance at Den and Pack Meetings, achievements and good will. The receipient for 1978 was Stuart Haft;
an individual plaque was presented to Stuart and his name has been added to the permanent plaque which remains at the Center.
