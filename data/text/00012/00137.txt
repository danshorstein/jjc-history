HARRY GAINES
This, our 17th year brought the Esquire Club closer to
its aims and purposes and to the community. This was
brought about in part by the introduction of a parents’
committee consisting of Mr. Arthur Gutman, Mr. Lionel
Gaines, Mr. Seymour Burns, Mr. Sam Siegel, and Mr. Sam
Cherry. Attorney Arthur Gutman gave generously of his
time in drawing up a constitution. Our sincerest and
warmest thanks go to these gentlemen and to the Jackson-
ville Jewish Center for their co-operation throughout this
year, and for the facilities that they placed at our disposal.
Success of our social functions was due to the untiring
effort and planning of our mascot, Miss Carol Wolfson.
The year’s social activities were highlighted by the Annual
Fall Dance, to which a banquet was added making the
evening a most memorable occasion.
In fulfilling our philanthropic program, we visited Hope
Haven and provided entertainment and refreshments. Do-
nations were made to the Center and Temple building
funds. The religious program included presentation of
books of Jewish interest to all the Bar Mitzvahs of the
city, and a Friday Evening service conducted at the Center
by our membership. Sports were represented by the club’s
competition in softball, basketball, and bowling leagues.
In recognition of the year’s accomplishments, the Jackson-
ville Pi Lambda Phi Alumni presented Esquire with the
achievement award given each year to the outstanding boys’
youth group.
We would like to thank Richard Hollins, our advisor,
for his time and effort in helping us carry out our program.
Esquire anticipates even greater accomplishments next
year with continued progress in the fields in which we
have come so far this year.
