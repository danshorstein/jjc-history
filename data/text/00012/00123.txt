SIDNEY ENTMAN
River Garden is now in its eighth year of serving the
aged and infirm people of Florida. The Home makes
every effort to meet all of the many needs of the aged.
As the life span is extended due to the miracle of modern
medicine, it is accompanied by many social, mental and
emotional problems which many elderly people find diffi-
to cope with.
Many look to the Home for a solution to their prob-
lems and those who make their home with us generally
resolve their difficulties. Unfortunately, the lack of bed-
rooms necessitate others to wait many months before they
are admitted. In order to help those on our waiting
list, the Home has inaugurated an Extension Program.
The Home takes the same responsibility of the resident
on the Extension Program as they do for the residents
of the Home. Sleeping accommodations are provided in
a nearby furnished rooming house, and the resident utilizes
all the services and facilities of the Home during the day.
This program is supervised jointly with the Jewish Wei-
fare Society. Each room is carefully selected to meet the
highest standard. The owner of the lodging house is
made aware of our program and cooperates with the
Home in making our residents comfortable.
As a result of the Extension Program, River Garden
is now serving 50 residents, as compared to its rated bed
capacity of 43. The demand for the services of River
Garden is increasing, and the Home is now planning a
new Building Program. The most urgent need is in the
infirmary area, which houses the chronically ill and
mentally confused. Our present facilities are inadequate
and insufficient to give proper care to the ailing and
helpless aged. A modern infirmary wing will enable the
Home to give excellent service to the most helpless res-
idents, who must have individual care and attention.
To extend life without providing a good life is no
blessing. River Garden is providing a good life for its
many residents and is looking forward to open new doors
for those eager and waiting to enter.
