FRANKIE SCHEMER
The B’nai B’rith Girls of Jacksonville in carrying out
the Seven Fold Program of BBG have made great strides
in the past year. In the first year of our existence, it was
enough that we were organized, but in this, our second
year, we have endeavored to prove that we are really a
part of B’nai B’rith -— a true sister organization of AZA.
The chapter celebrated National Jewish Book Month
with two very good book reviews, and by making posters
which were displayed in the Public Libraries as well as
the Center and Temple.
National Brotherhood Week was observed with a meet-
ing to which each member brought a girl of a different
faith and a discussion on “The Meaning of Brotherhood”
was featured.
Combining Community Service with Social Activities
proved to be sheer pleasure when the girls turned out to
serve as hostesses at the newly instituted USO Dances.
A good part of the chapter attended the regional con-
vention in December at which Patsy Yales was elected
regional recording secretary. Two delegates attended the
district convention held in Hendersonville, N. C. in June.
At a luncheon our outgoing president, Sandra Rosen-
field, was presented with a past president’s pin and our
two Advisors, Mrs. Dave Eber and Miss Florence Price
with Advisor’s pins.
