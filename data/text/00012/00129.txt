MRS. DAVID ARMEL
The Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah is an integral
part of the 300,000 women dedicated to the ideals of
health, education and social welfare for our people in
Israel and to the principles of freedom and democracy
toward all peoples of the world.
Our chapter with its nearly 800 members actively
engages in, not only all phases of Hadassah life for Israel,
but takes an active part in civic and community life in
Jacksonville.
Beginning with a modest Child Welfare Station in
Jerusalem in 1912, today Hadassah conducts a network
of teaching, healing and research projects in Israel.
In May 1953, one hundred doctors were graduated
from our Medical School and as many students were en-
rolled for the coming year.
Todays newest challenge is the Vocational Education
program for rural youth — which brings technical train-
ing courses, which will best enable them to assume their
role as productive builders on many economic fronts.
Our Chapter 219, is made up of 3 day groups and
one evening group. This year each group will meet in
its respective meeting place on the second Tuesday of
each month. There will be at least 4 chapter meetings
during the year at which time all groups will meet
together. The evening group will meet every second Mon-
day of the month at 8:30 p.m.
