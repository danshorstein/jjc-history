BERNARD GLOSSMAN
The Boy Scouts of America Movement had its begin-
ning on February 8, 1910. The Jacksonville Council was
organized on February 5, 1920. Troop 14 obtained its
initial charter in April, 1920, and since that date the fol-
lowing achieved the rank of Eagle Scout: Alazer Spievak,
1927; David Rothstein, 1928; Louis Wilensky, 1932; Joe
Mizrahi, 1934; Simon Rothstein, 1934; William Rose,
1942; Sol Proctor, 1945; Edwin Safer, 1945; Irving
Spevak, 1948 and Stanley Kantor, 1951.
Under the present administration we have had our usual
individual and patrol projects, training and advancements
and overnight hikes.
At the annual Scout Sabbath held on February 13th,
the Ner Tamid Award for Reverence was presented to
Martin Rothstein, who fulfilled the requirements as set
forth by the National Boy Scout Headquarters.
Two patrols were entered in the District Camporee at
Crandall, Fla., on the St. Mary’s River and received an
A and B rating. We also obtained third place honors at
the Scout-O-Rama with a display on Air Defense.
In March the troop held its annual Charter Presenta-
tion Banquet and awards were presented to the following:
