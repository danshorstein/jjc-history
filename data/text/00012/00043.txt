THE CANTOR AND CHOIR
The Jewish Center Choral Society has completed what
has been the outstanding year of its history, not only ex-
panding its program of presenting concerts in neighboring
communities, but by presenting locally one of its finest
concerts.
The local concert, which was held March 8th under the
sponsorship of the local B’nai B’rith lodge, featured as
soloist the renowned American Cantor, David Kusevitsky,
in a concert which will be long remembered as the finest
evening of Jewish music in the history of Jacksonville.
In addition to their annual concert, the Choral Group
also appeared in radio and television programs, as well as
in many other local programs. The group also appeared
in concert in Savannah on March 16th and in Augusta on
April 12, being very well received in both communities.
Cantor Abraham Marton not only conducted the Society
in both communities, but appeared as guest soloist.
Cantor Marton personally appeared as soloist in St.
Petersburg on February 1st in a memorial ceremony for
the late Chaim Weizman, and in Tampa on April 29th in
a fifth anniversary celebration of Israel’s independence.
An unusual distinction came to the Choral Group when
they were invited from the state at large to be the repre-
sentative of typical Jewish music at the “All Florida Folk
Festival” sponsored by the Stephen Foster Memorial Com-
mission and the Florida Federation of Music Clubs on
Sunday afternoon, May 10th at the Stephen Foster Me-
morial Grounds at White Springs, Florida.
CANTOR ABRAHAM MARTON
The Choral Group presents a varied repertoire of liturgi-
cal and Jewish folk music. The work of this group has
contributed in large measure to the meaningful and in-
spirational development of the synagogue music now a
part of the Center’s religious services. From the larger
group of the Choral Society are selected the volunteer
choir for Friday evening services and augmented choir
for the High Holy Day Services.
Cantor Marton not only acts in the official role of Can-
tor at all Center services, but also directs the Choral Group
and the program of training students of the Hebrew School
in the traditional Hazanut chants.
