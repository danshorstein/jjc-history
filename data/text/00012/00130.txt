RITA NANCY ESKIN
Jr. Hadassah is the young women’s Zionist organization
of America. We believe in the national existence of our
people, in the right of our people to its homeland in
Israel and we strengthen that belief by concrete contribu-
tions.
From the very beginning it has been Jr. Hadassah’s pur-
pose to participate actively and concretely in the building
of the Jewish homeland through specific projects. Since
1920 when it was organized, Jr. Hadassah has taken part
in colonization through Meier Shfeyah and Youth Aliyah;
in land redemption through Jewish National Fund and
Pardess Anna and in national service through the Henrietta
Szold School of Nursing, and more recently through Cha-
lutzim pioneering.
“Am Habonim Smacha—a joyful Mother of Children*’.
In 1925 Jr. Hadassah first undertook the support of
Meier Shfeyah. From a children’s home accommodating 100
girls, we developed a village of more than 400 youthful
citizens. To us in Jr. Hadassah it represents our contribu-
tion to the historic and exciting task of Jewish—Nation—
building—one of the most dynamic and constructive move-
ments of our times.
Then, too, we remember our duty as Americans, as
American citizens we think of the development, the ex-
tension, the preservation of the democratic system here
in our own country. Our task as Jr. Hadassah members,
in addition to helping meet the immediate needs of Israel
—is to try to inspire the life of the Jewish community in
America.
As Jr. Hadassah has served in the past, with the utmost
devotion, so it must serve in the future.
