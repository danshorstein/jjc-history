BYRON SELBER
During its fourth year of organization the Center Youth
League has continued to hold its position as one of the
most dynamic organizations of the Jacksonville Jewish
Center. It has made great strides towards fulfilling its
aim; to offer opportunities to Jewish Youth to continue
and strengthen their identification with Judaism and the
Synagogue.
The program during this past year was broad in scope,
based on self-development, interests, and needs of the
Jewish teen-ager. The bi-weekly business meetings on
Sunday evenings, which have become a must on the
calendar of Jacksonville youth, are followed by an inter-
esting cultural program and social.
One of the high points during the year was the organ-
ization of a Post-Confirmation study group which held
one series on Sunday nights and one on Friday evenings
after services. The participants held very thought-provok-
ing discussions on some of the basic philosophies of our
religion.
Other features of this year’s program included par-
ticipation in Sabbath services, athletics, projects, and
Saturday evening canteens in the Youth Activities Build-
ing, climaxed by the annual Spring Dance.
With the financial assistance of the Sisterhood and the
Men’s Club, C.Y.L. participated in several conventions—
the Leadership Training Institute for Regional United Syn-
agogue Youth, the Second Annual Convention of the
United Synagogue Youth, and the Southeastern Regional
Conventions. These conventions are of great assistance
in invigorating local membership and leadership, and also
in strengthening the Regional and the National organ-
izations.
The Center Youth League looks with pride on the
achievements of the previous administration and considers
them a challenge for the coming year. With the wonder-
ful inspiration and guidance of Rabbi Tofield, the con-
tinued support of the Center family, and with the assist-
anee of our advisor, Miss Myra Thier, C. Y. L. eagerly
looks forward to a year of successful development.
