P. N. COLEMAN
The Jewish Community Council represents and acts as
the spokesman for Jacksonville Jewry. As the most repre-
sentative Jewish organization in the city, it has on its
Governing Boards, which make policies and set the pro-
gram of the Council, the representatives of some 25 dif-
ferent organizations.
The Council was formally organized about 20 years ago
and received its charter from the State of Florida. Among
its presidents have been such outstanding community
workers as Alexander Brest, L. A. Stein, Morton Hirsch-
berg, David Harris, David Lasarow, Sam Wolfson and
Leonard Moss. The Council has developed from an organi-
zation which raised some $3,000 in one year to a high of
over $346,000. Within the past five years, it has raised
about one and a half million dollars, distributed annually
to over 40 different beneficiaries. Its actual fund-raising
costs have approximated about 1%.
The Council operates through committees, selected by
the President and the Governing Boards. Its major standing
committees are: Campaign, Budget, Refugee, Public Re-
lations, Armed Services and Nomination. Other committees
function for specific purposes, such as the present Census
Committee, responsible for the on-going census study of
our Jewish community.
The “Commentator,” published by the Community
Council, is the official voice of the Jewish Community.
It is mailed, at no cost to all Jewish families in Jackson-
ville. Its columns contain news and articles of local and
national importance. This community newspaper has been
twice awarded national recognition as an outstanding
publication by the National Council of Jewish Federations
and Welfare Funds.
The Community Calendar, listing all organizational
events taking place each month in the city, is maintained
by the Council for the benefit of all. These and many
other functions make the Jewish Community Council the
representative of the entire community. It is, as once
described—the Voice, Arm and Heart of the Jewish Com-
munity of Jacksonville.
