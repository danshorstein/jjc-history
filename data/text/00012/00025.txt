MRS. SAMUEL J. THIER
What does the Sisterhood accomplish? The answer may
be obvious to those of us who have been close to the work
and participate in the many activities and worthwhile pro-
jects. Large numbers within our membership take the
organization for granted. This one time a year when we
take a quick glance at the past year, it is not amiss to
point out a number of highlights in our program.
The range of Sisterhood interests is wide. Sisterhood’s
influence within the community is profound.
This very Year Book, a project of long standing, and for
a number of years directed by that indefatigible worker,
Mr. Hyman Selber, was in recent years made a Sisterhood
project and benefited from the zealous cooperation of Sister-
hood members whose names are given on the Year Book
Committee page. Through their solicitation of advertising
they have raised the larger portion of the funds which the
Sisterhood has been able to put to the disposal of the
Building Fund Committee. From the present effort the re-
building of the Synagogue will be advanced substantially.

League of America we promulgate a variety of activities
