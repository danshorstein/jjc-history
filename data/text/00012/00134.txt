FRANKLIN RESNICK
A.Z.A., the junior order of B’nai B’rith, had a very
eventful and successful year and fulfilled its five fold and
full program.
Under the Community Service part of our program, we
formed lines at the Red Cross Blood Donor Center. We
also donated to various charitable organizations, sent cigar-
ettes to the Armed Forces in Korea and contributed to the
Children’s Home in Israel, a national A.Z.A. project. One
of our new projects is to collect drugs for Israel.
In the cultural field, we had various debates, discussions
and an oratorical contest. A speaker from the Urban Lea-
gue also gave a very interesting talk on cooperation be-
tween negroes and whites at a combined meeting of B’nai
B’rith and A.Z.A.
The social aspect of our organization, a beach party,
hay ride and various other events throughout the year
were all very successful. In the athletic field our luck was
unusually good this year. We played various schools and
youth groups and came out with flying colors.
We had 15 Alephs at the past Florida Regional Con-
vention in Miami. Solly Safer was elected Regional Aleph
Godol; Neil Chonin, Aleph Sopher (Editor of the State
Paper) and Franklin Resnick was appointed Regional
Community Service Chairman.
Attending the District 5 Convention at Hendersonville,
N.C. were Stephen Feinstein and Franklin Resnick as dele-
gates, with Raymond Blasenstein as alternate.
Eighty per cent of the membership took part in the
annual A.Z.A. Sabbath.
The closing event of the year was our Annual Alumni
Banquet and Dance, which was attended by 203 paying
guests. The speaker was Dr. Sam Proctor.
Our success throughout the year would not have been
possible without the guidance and leadership of Mr. David
Weinstein and Mr. Edwin Safer our Advisors. We do want
to take this opportunity to thank them and also to ex-
press our gratitude to the Jacksonville Jewish Center for
all the help and cooperation they have given us.
