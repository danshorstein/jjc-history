MRS. MORTON TURBOW
The National Council of Jewish Women, organized in
1893, is one of the oldest women’s organizations in
America. It now has 250 sections throughout the country
with an enrollment of over 100,000 members. Council’s
purpose is to bring about a closer fellowship among
Jewish women, to foster a program devoted to social
welfare, community affairs, education, and international
understanding for peace. Its program is one of service and
education.
Council’s overseas projects are threefold: the support
of the School of Education of Hebrew University in
Jerusalem; maintenance of graduate scholarships awarded
to students from Israel and some European countries for
study in American universities; and shipments of clothing,
play and study material to orphanages in the Middle East.
Local services include Council’s Medical Loan Shelf,
Golden Age Club, sewing for the less fortunate, Service
to Foreign Born, and parties for hospitalized servicemen
at the Naval Air Station. Through education, Council
gives an opportunity to all its members to work toward
the betterment of the individual, the community, the
nation, and the world.
