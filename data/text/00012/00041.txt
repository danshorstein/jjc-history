GIRL SCOUTS AND BROWNIES
Another successful year was completed by Brownie
Troop 197, Girl Scout Troops 29 and 222. These troops
are part of the Center’s program of character and citizen-
ship building and are under the direct sponsorship of the
Center Sisterhood.
Members of the troops have participated in all phases
of the Girl Scout program as established by national head-
quarters; International Thinking Day, Cookie Sale, Court
of Awards and Girl Scout Sabbath.
As part of their outdoor activities the girls participated
in cook-outs, hikes and a train trip to St. Augustine. The
individual troops sponsored Hanukah parties, wiener roasts
and other events as part of their social program.
Arts and crafts included the making of handkerchiefs,
sewing kits, pot holders, corsages for Mother’s Day and
scrap books.
The girls participated in the following civic projects:
folding papers for the Visiting Nurses Association, col-
lecting jelly glasses for the Duval Medical Center, making
napkins and favors for the patient’s trays at the Medical
Center for various holidays, and assisting in the Heart
Campaign. Other projects undertaken were sending school
supplies to the Israeli Scout Association and clothing to
an Israeli family. Troop 29 devoted a great deal of their
time to converting a garage into a beautiful club house
and meeting room.
The year’s activities were concluded with a beautiful
annual luncheon under the supervision of mothers from
each troop. Among the guests present were the Misses
Bonnie Wimpie, Mary Lou Garrett, Mrs. J. E. Ferrell and
Mrs. Donald Rowe.
