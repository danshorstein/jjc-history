ATHLETICS (Continued)
The third year of an enlarged athletic program, made
possible by the completion of the Activities Building,
witnessed an up-surge of interest and vastly expanded
schedule of activities. William Goldman took over the chair-
manship from Frank Diamond and Sam Silver became act-
ing Athletic Director upon the resignation of Sidney X.
Cohen. Under their leadership and with the assistance
of an active committee, many new features were added.
The department was especially activated during the sum-
mer months.
The outstanding addition in the total field of athletics
was “Ladies Night” on Wednesdays. Under the super-
vision of Patricia Stevens Studio the ladies enjoyed a
most beneficial program of supervised exercises, ballet,
free activity and healthful, relaxing steam baths.
For the most part, this department carried on with well
established features of previous years for men, women and
youth of all ages.
During basketball and baseball seasons the Center
entered teams in the respective Church Leagues. Sunday
afternoon will again be devoted, whenever the gym is
available, to basketball games and practice.
An active season is anticipated for 1953-54. Detailed
schedules will be announced for all programs through the
Center Bulletin and through direct mailings to those who
are signed up in this department. They will be centered
around the Equipment that the Center possesses with
Activities listed and others that may be inaugurated as
the demand arises and in keeping with the space avail-
able.
