KNOWING IS GROWING...
Fostering a love of learning for the sake of learning while instilling knowledge and a strong ethical foundation
grounded in Judaism is an important goal at the Martin J. Gottlieb Day School. Acting with Kavod (respect) and
Kindness is fundamental to daily interactions. Students develop important leadership skills as they assume roles in
the Knesset, the student government. In addition to learning Hebrew as the language of our people, students benefit
from participating in our dual language program by becoming more sophisticated thinkers, and by developing better
listening and memory skills. An added benefit is the fact that students can graduate from the Middle School with 3
high school foreign language credits. Students participate in the Regional Science Fair and in highly individualized
math classes allowing students to move as quickly or as slowly as needed to master the skills. While our annual
retreat allows for important bonding experiences, our 8th grade trip to New York highlights American and Jewish
history, cultural activities and awesome Kosher dining. After guiding our students to achieve their personal best in
the classroom, students then take their academic skills beyond the formal classroom putting lessons into action. As
part of our award winning Mitzvah program, hands-on projects in the community allow students to take knowledge
to a new level and see their role in the world around them.
