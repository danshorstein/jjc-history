SOCIALIZE, BOND AND PRAY
Since 1901, members of our congregation have been gathering together each morning and evening to socialize,
to bond and to pray. Each one serving as a member of that day’s minyan, ensures that the service can be
conducted and those who need to say kaddish can do so.
We are the only conservative congregation to hold minyan twice a day, every day, between Atlanta and Miami.
And we do so, not only because of the dedication of our members but through the leadership of our minyan
captains. These individuals are volunteers, and work to create small networks with those that attend on their day
to ensure we have a minyan.
