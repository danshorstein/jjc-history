BOY SCOUT TROOP 14
Troop 14 continued to carry on the tradition of Jewish Scouting in a very successful 99th year for the Troop. Service
projects for the year included participation in the North Florida Council Special Needs Camporee helping children
and their families camp and River Keeper Cleanup Day. Camping highlights included a family campout at the
Center’s Beth Shalom Park. Corey Kriesel became the 85th Eagle Scout from Troop 14. His Eagle Scout project was
to design and build a butterfly garden for Mandarin Community Club including spectacular metal flower sculptures.
Much appreciation goes to the Fleet Family Fund for continued support of Scouting programming throughout the
year. Scouting is open to all boys from kindergarten until 18 years of age. For more information, contact Scoutmaster
Allan Andrew at aandrew@alum.mit.edu
