SISTERHOOD
Making a difference.
It is truly an honor to
be President of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center
Sisterhood. We have made
such a difference in so
many places.
We have made a
difference in the general
community by our social
action project—Support
the Girls. Women donated
new and gently used
bras and other personal
products, which were then
distributed to local shelters.
People tend to stay away from donating ‘unmentionables’, so
our contribution of over 150 items filled a big gap for the local
needy population.
We have made a difference in the Conservative Jewish
community by reaching our goal of $18,000 for Torah
Fund, which supports the training of our rabbis, cantors and
professional leaders. Our Torah Fund program, Oh, the Places
You will Go, honored several women who have matriculated
through the Jacksonville Jewish Center’s school system and
have gone on to succeed in making a difference of their own.
We have made a difference in the Jacksonville Jewish
Center community. I am proud to say that we raised over
$18,000 for programs at the Center. We continued our $5,000
pledge to the L’Dor vaDor campaign. The money is targeted to
help fund day school, preschool and professional development
in the area of special needs. Our Judaica Shop is open every
day and staffed by our members. It is a showcase for other
shops nationwide, and we should be proud. Sisterhood is
the backbone of the Center, whether it is donating money or
volunteering countless hours to make our programs successful.

We have made a difference by giving seed money to Zahava,
the group that targets the needs of our younger women. They
held several well-attended programs this spring and had a very
positive Sisterhood experience. We look forward to seeing great
things from them!
And we have made a difference in the personal connections
that our women have made to the synagogue, to Jewish life and to
each other. Baking hamantashen, doing Shalom Yoga, delivering
holiday flowers and sending Leagrams are but a few. Exploring
Kosher Trader Joe’s and having a panel of power-house Jewish
women telling us about their experiences coming up through the
ranks as Jewish women are others. In any case, Sisterhood is
unique in that it puts a Jewish perspective on important issues.
We were privileged to hear from the remarkable Margie Miller,
International President of Women’s League for Conservative
Judaism, during our Sisterhood Shabbat.
I want to give a shout out to all of the members of the Board
of Directors and the cadre of volunteers that make our programs
so successful. Without them, we would be just a shell of what
we are today.
I also want to thank Marjie Rogozinski for four years of
dedicated service as President of Sisterhood. Her energy, ideas,
hard work and perseverance have made a huge difference in
molding our Sisterhood into the relevant, multi-generational and
active entity that it is today. I know she will continue to help us
to grow and to flourish.
One of my goals for this year is to continue the legacy
of those who have laid the groundwork and to build upon
that foundation. Communicating the great things we do is so
important. Please visit our website, www.jjcsisterhood.com and
our Facebook page, JJC Sisterhood, to follow us and keep up to
date on our latest adventures.
Creating meaningful and relevant programs that highlight
the Jewish connection that we have to our community, our
synagogue, to each other and to ourselves is my most important
goal. I know we will go from strength to strength and continue
to make a difference!
