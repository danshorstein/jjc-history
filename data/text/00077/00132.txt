SIYYUM
THE TWELFTH GRADE YEAR OF SETZER YOUTH EDUCATION
Siyyum, a program for 12th graders leading to Confirmation, reflects the link between Jewish learning and
social action. Through involvement in Siyyum, our teens gain a deeper appreciation of the wisdom and beauty of our
tradition while making a real difference in the quality of life for those who live at the margins of the Jacksonville
community. The gatherings are filled with fun, friendship and spiritual growth as the students learn and celebrate
Jewish identity through Tikkun Olam. Thanks to the Zimmerman Family Foundation, those who enroll and regularly
attend Siyyum qualify for a full merit scholarship to participate at the annual Policy Conference of the American
Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) in Washington D.C., at which thousands of supporters of Israel gather to
learn, network, and lobby on behalf of Israel. Cognizant of the challenges many Jewish students face on college
campuses, Policy Conference helps prepare undergraduates to respond to anti-Israel sentiment and serve as more
effective and informed ambassadors on behalf of Israel. Students meet with leaders of national prominence and
participate in the process of lobbying congressional representatives. At year’s end we look forward to the students’
participation in Confirmation, a community celebration of our 12th graders’ completion of their Jewish secondary
education. If you would like more information about the Siyyum program as your child approaches 12th grade,
please contact the clergy office at dberke@jaxjewishcenter.org.
