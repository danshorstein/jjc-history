Setzer Youth Education
HEBREW HIGH SCHOOL
This year our Hebrew High program focused on preparing Jewish teens for life
in the 21st Century. Together with Rabbi Rosenblum and guest speakers, teens
in grades 8-11 participated in informal, experiential programs, community
service opportunities and positive peer interactions. We engaged with Jewish
values and concepts while learning how to handle a variety of real-life issues
teens may face in high school, college and beyond. Some highlights of our
year included a trip to an escape room, Shabbat dinner at Rabbi Rosenblum’s
apartment and decorating our very own mezuzot. We learned about the Jewish
communal organizations in Jacksonville that are supported by Federation and
discussed our top priorities for allocating funds to each organization. We also
took part in the first-of-its-kind High School Global Screening Day for Yom
HaZikaron, Israel’s Memorial Day, along with Jewish teens from over 70
Rabbi Shira M.T. Rosenblum high schools, in six countries, across four continents. The teens explored the
Chair intersection between Jewish and American values as well as some of the ways
Christina Levine in which Judaism relates to our lives today.
Setzer Youth Education at the Jacksonville Jewish Center is excited to
announce a new Hebrew High School program for the 2018-2019 school year! Building upon the successes of
this past year, the Jacksonville Jewish Teen Leadership Initiative will offer our teens even more opportunities
to give back to the greater community. The program will have an increased emphasis on social justice and
the cultivation of leadership skills as our teens continue to mature into Jewish young adults. We look forward
to learning and growing together in the coming year!
