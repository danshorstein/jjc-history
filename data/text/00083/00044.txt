During the past year the girls of
Troop 2, sponsored by the Daugh¬
ters of Israel and led by Mrs. Louis
Slott and Mrs. Sinclair Afchan
have made much progress.
The majority of girls have com¬
pleted their second class require¬
ments and are well on their way
toward first class.
They have contributed gen¬
erously to the Girl Scout Victory
Fund and have collected maga¬
zines and books for use of service
men.
Because of their outstanding
achievements, Sylvia Flauman and
Eileen Grossman were chosen to
represent Troop 2 at a city-wide
week end at Camp Chowenwah.
While there the girls learned the
democratic way of scouting in
work as well as play.
Girl Scouting offers numerous
outlets for the enthusiasm of voung
girls from ten to fourteen. With
ten program fields to choose from,
including first aid, arts and crafts,
music and dancing, out-of-door
sports and games, nature, com¬
munity life, homemaking, etc.,
every girl should be able to find
that for which she is best suited.
The members of the troop have
a warm welcome for all interested
in scouting.
