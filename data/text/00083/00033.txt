At the beginning of this past year the Hebrew School presented a serious
problem. Due to a decrease in the teaching staff during the previous year,
the total number of registrants was only 55. The age groupings in the classes
covered 2-, 3- and 4-year spans, and the discipline in one class needed im¬
provement. The first step toward the correction of these problems was the
reorganization of the classes. This was not a difficult task, because the
students did not differ greatly as to what they had studied and covered.
The children were classified according to their ages into six divisions: 7 to 9
years, 9 to 10, 10 to 11, 11 to 12, 12 to 13, and a special beginners group from
11 to 13. The second step was the decision to use the Hasheiur Series, which
was introduced the previous year as the basis of curriculum. Since the
ultimate objective was to have a 7-year Hebrew course, a post Bar Mitzvah
group was also organized. Due to certain difficulties, however, this group
met together with the 12 to 13-year-old groups. In addition to the Hebrew
texts, the curriculum also included Sidur and prayers, customs and ceremonies,
assemblies and singing. The final step in this process of reorganization
involved a closer check on attendance, discipline, etc.
These measures were successful. At the end of June, the registration
of the school was 77, discipline was good, the classes were covering their
assigned work, and it was possible to divide the first age groups, the 7 to 9
year group, into two classes: 6 to 8 and 8 to 9. The children attending the
Bar Mitzvah preparation class were also required to attend regular Hebrew
classes and cover the prescribed work.

In June we were faced with the problem of a summer school, or rather
the extension of the Hebrew school to July 29. There were many reasons
why this was inadvisable. One of the important reasons was that in the
past about 35 children attended Hebrew School after the closing of the public
schools. However, it was decided to try the summer sessions again this year.
And contrary to all pessimistic expectations and despite the handicap of
working with two teachers in the place of three, at the time of the writing
of this report, July 20th, the school had a registration of more than 50
children and a very excellent attendance. The attendance tapered off to
about 25 at the end of July.

In conclusion, due to the excellent cooperation of our teachers, Mrs.
H. Abrams, Mr. J. Robin, Mr. S. H. Rosenberg and Rabbi M. D. Margolis,
the Talmud Torah progressed this past year. Mr. Rosenberg was the prin¬
cipal of the school as well as the teacher. To continue this progress it is
essential that the Center keep up the high requirements for the teaching
staff and that it continue with one carefully planned program for a number
of years. Thus will the Talmud Torah of the Center grow from strength
to strength.
