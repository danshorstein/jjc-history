Jacksonville Jewish
Center Sunday School
The Sunday School during the past
year was under the direction of Mr.
Samuel H. Rosenberg. The curriculum
as in the past, provided for a kinder¬
garten and a ten year course in History
and Customs and Ceremonies. The So-
loff series of history books, which were
hitherto used in grades five, six and
seven were retained but each book
spread over a two-year period, begin¬
ning with the fourth grade. The third
grade used Hillel’s Happy Holidays in¬
stead of Ish Kishor’s History of Israel.
The registration in all classes num¬
bered about 225. Attendance of both
teachers and pupils was good. Regular
assemblies twice a month were held in
the auditorium, with members of the
different classes preparing the pro¬
grams. Efforts were also made during
the year to build up in the children
interest in home ceremonies, such as
lighting candles and reciting the Kid-
dush as well as attendance at Syna¬
gogue services.
The staff of the Sunday School con¬
sisted of the following: Mrs. M. J. Rei¬
ser, Mrs. Samuel H. Rosenberg, Miss
Victoria Levy, Mrs. Philip Selber, Mrs.
Harry Abrams, Miss Raela Fink, Mias
Eunice Davis, Miss Mollye Mizrahi, Mr.
Morris Ansbacher, Mr. M. J. Reiser
and Mr. Philip Selber. Rabbi M. D.
Margolis met with the Confirmation
Class on Wednesday afternoons for a
course in Bible studies and the prepara¬
tion for the Confirmation ceremony.
The Jacksonville Jewish Center owes
a debt of gratitude to the fine staff of
teachers whose loyalty and devotion
to Jewish education has made possible
the development and upbuilding of our
fine Sunday School.
