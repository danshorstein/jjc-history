CENTER NOW SERVES ITS
SOUTH SIDE MEMBERSHIP
MORE ADEQUATELY
Parents of children from the South
Side who plan to atttend the Hebrew
School maintained by the Center will
be pleased to learn that the Center has
purchased a new bus and increased its
facilities for transporting the children
from that area to and from the Center
for instruction.
In view of this added convenience,
the officers of the Center anticipate a
sharp rise in enrollment from the
South Side.
The institution also is continuing the
nursery and kindergarten which was
such a successful innovation of the
1942-43 season.
Morris Bar-Am of New Haven,
Conn, has taken up his new duties as
principal of the Sunday School and in¬
structor of Hebrew. He has had 18
years of experience in teaching and
should prove a valuable addition to
the Center’s educational program.
