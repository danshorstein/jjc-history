THE
JUNIOR
CONGREGATION
During the past year a new experi¬
ment was tried with the Junior Con¬
gregation. Instead of meeting sepa¬
rately in the Prayer Room on Sabbath
mornings, the Junior Congregation met
in the main Synagogue, joining the
older worshippers at 9:45 for the read¬
ing of the Torah and staying through
the end of Musaf. The attendance va¬
ried from about 15 to 35 children. While
the pupils of the Hebrew School who
attended gained a first hand experience
with the procedure and ritual of the
adult Synagogue they were not able
to participate with understanding in
the prayers themselves and they lost
their identity as a Junior Congrega¬
tion. No officers are therefore listed
for the year. It is hoped that at the
beginning of the coming year a new
plan may be adopted which may re¬
tain the advantages of both the new
and the old arrangements.
