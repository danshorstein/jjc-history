To uphold and continue the name
the Henrietta Szold Young Judean
Club has made foritself in this city,
this organization, through its many
American and Zionist activities has
made remarkable progress during the
past year.
Starting the winter season off a
“pot-luck” supper was held at the
home of Miss Raela Fink, co-sponsor
with Miss Eunice Davis.
The membership of the organization
has increased constantly, taking girls
from the ages of 13 to 16, and
especially those new to the Jewish
Community. The total number of
members has reached 30.

In helping to contribute to the
Keren Hanoar Fund in Palestine,
which Young Judaea supports the club
participated in several activities, such
as dances, rafffes and candy sales. The
majority of the members cooperated
in the J. N. F. blue box collections,
flower day sales, and Mother’s Day cer¬
tificates sales.
Through the cultural programs di¬
rected by Sarah Dwoskin and her com¬
mittee, the members were able to gain
an appreciation of Jewish customs and
ceremonies, current events and to en¬
joy Palestine folk songs and dances.
The music was directed by Marion
Freed. At the Young Judean Sabbath
held in the Center Synagogue, a paper
was read by Rita Gendzier on what
“Young Judaea Means to Me,” a co¬
operative project of the club. A num¬
ber of the members attended the
Leadership Training Course given by
Dr. Aharon Kessler of National Young
Judaea under the auspices of the local
Zionist Youth Commission. Twenty-
nine girls enjoyed a four-day week¬
end house party at the Jacksonville
Beach home of Mrs. Joe Becker, which
was the culminating event of the sea¬
son. A “rush” party was also held on
July 29 at the home of Sarah Dwoskin
for prospéctive members, featuring the
playlet, “Thumbs Up for Palestine.”
