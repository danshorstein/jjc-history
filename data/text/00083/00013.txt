Mr. Simon Selber — Died Nisan 13, 5703
5
The membership of the Jacksonville Jewish Center announced the
loss of one of its devoted members and friends, Mr. Simon Selber.
Mr. Selber was a simple and God-fearing man and lived at peace
with his fellow human beings. He was a good father and a good husband.
He was a zealous worker for the Center and was vitally interested
in the welfare of his people, his religion and its spiritual heritage.
With loving care he supervised the Center building and painstak¬
ingly worked for its physical and aesthetic improvement. The Center will
be forever grateful for the years he has devoted to the improvement and
beautification of the Center Cemetery.
Mr. Selber’s devotion to the synagogue has inspired the loyalty of
his children who became indefatiguable workers for the Center and prom¬
inent leaders in the community.
His memory will live eternally in the hearts of all who knew him
and his spirit will never depart from the Center where he worked so hard
and faithfully.
We thank God for the life of Simon Selber. May his soul be bound
up in the bond of life eternal.
