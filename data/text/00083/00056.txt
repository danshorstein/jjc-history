Under the auspices of the Daughters of Israel the Center has
made a fine contribution to the war effort. During the winter months
members of the organization met regularly once or twice a week,
as the need arose to sew for the Red Cross. The women completed
702 garments, including pajamas, kit bags, boys flannel shirts, slips,
men s shirts, petticoats and blouses. This work was under the chair¬
manship of Mrs. Charles Fink and Mrs. Cele Schemer.
Mrs. Abe Shapiro led an enthusiastic group of women through-
r?'
out the summer twice a week in Red Cross bandage work, on Mon¬
days from 7 P. M. to 10 P. M. and on Wednesdays from 9 A. M. to 4
P. M. The auditorium of the Jacksonville Jewish Center was utilized
as the work room. Under the supervision of Mrs. Shapiro the wom¬
en, among whom were also Gentile neighbors, accomplished a great
deal of indispensable work. Several times during the season the Red
Cross commended the Jewish Center group for their fine accom¬
plishments, having exceeded several times their quota and having
produced more than any other group in the city.
Mrs. Shapiro was ably assisted by Mrs. David Moscovitz, chief
Instructor; Mrs. Sam Bryan, chairman of supplies; and Mrs. E. L.
Henry, chairman of packing. Richard Talisman, boy scout of Troop
14, assisted the group on various occasions.
