Outstanding among local Jewish groups
is the Junior Order of B’nai B’rith. Led by
their energetic and untiring advisor, Melvin
Margol, together with the advisory board
and a group of capable officers, the local
chapter has once more risen to attain great
heights in carrying out the ideals of the
national “Five Fold and Full” plan. To fill
the gaps in membership caused by the loss
of a large number of older fellows to the
armed services, the Supreme Advisory Coun¬
cil lowered the age limit to 14. The local
chapter took full advantage of this op¬
portunity by immediately bringing in these
younger boys to fill in the ranks of those
boys who are now serving in the armed
services of our country.
The outstanding event of the year was
the annual Valentine dance given February
13. The dance was held in the beautifully
decorated auditorium of the Jax Jewish
Center. It was attended by a large group
of young people, among which were many
college boys. The feature of the evening
was the presentation of A. Z. A. sweetheart,
Miss Rita Gendzier, with a large bouquet
of beautiful American roses. The retiring-
sweetheart was given an official sweetheart
pin and guard. A beautiful wallet was
presented to Melvin Margol in appreciation
for his services for the past year. The Sec¬
retary, Leonard Silver, was awarded a check
for his untiring efforts in making the dance
a financial as well as social success. Music
was furnished by the popular band of Miss
Addie Berry.
The chapter also handled all arrange¬
ments for the' Georgia-Florida dance, which
was sponsored by the A. Z. A. and the
Hillel Foundation of the Florida University
jointly.
The chapter also sponsored a Friday
Night Service during the1 month of March.
The chapter assisted in the annual J. N. F.
drive under the supervision of Mr. J. Robin.
The chapter received national recognition
for its efforts in the Russian War Relief
drive. The picture of the advisors and the
chairman of the drive appeared in the
national magazine, The Shofar.
The past year also found the local A. Z.
A. very successful in atheletics, under the
coaching of its junior advisor, Melvin
Margol. The A. Z. A. captured the “Y”
basketball league. It also defeated the
Esquire Club 5 to 3 and 4 to 3 in soft ball
while dropping a 13 to 7 decision in foot¬
ball.
To Leonard Silver and Beryl Weinstein
much credit is due for the successful affairs
of the chapter, especially the Valentine
dance and minstrel. At the present time
the boys are working on the fourth annual
minstrel and fashion show, scheduled to be
held the end of August and which will
culminate a most successful year of A. Z.
A. activities.
The chapter is also preparing to send a
basketball team, a debating team and an
orator to the forthcoming Florida State
A. Z. A. convention to be held over the
Labor Day week-end.
