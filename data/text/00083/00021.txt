Heirs of a Great Spiritual Heritage
By MORRIS D. MARGOLIS
Rabbi of Jacksonville Jewish Center
As the year 5703 is drawing to a close, the democratic
| world and the Jewish people in particular, have good rea-
3 son to look more hopefully into the future.
| During the past three years a monstrous and brutal
§ war machine has rolled across many countries destroying
| everything in its path. It registered victory after victory
| and it seemed that its power was inexhaustable. An era
| of darkness and slavery faced the world, which according
1 to one boast, was to last a thousand years.
| But as the might of the United Nations has made it-
| self felt with increasing strength, Hitler’s dream of world
| conquest now is crumbling in the dust.
In recent months the triumphs in North Africa and
§ Sicily, and the growing revolts in the occupied countries,
1 all point to the ultimate destruction of Nazism and
| Fascism, the twin evils of modern civilization.
With the day of liberation in sight the remaining Jews
i of Europe take courage in the hope that it may yet come
I in time to save them. The dark clouds are brightening.
s
At the same time the issue o£ the White Paper re-
| mains. The next few months will decide the course of
3 history, so far as the Zionist movement is concerned.
I Either we will be permitted by the Mandatory Power—
3 Great Britain—to continue rebuilding our ancient home-
I land or we will be cast off as an old shoe.
What has happened is familiar history to our people. 3
Over-confidence in its own strength, an insatiable desire |
for more and more conquests, unwillingness to tolerate the |
existence of any other power—these have brought ruin |
to military nations of the past. These blind passions are |
also sure to bring the downfall of the despotic conquerers 3
of our day. The road to disaster is clearly marked for If
those who would dominate the world by the power of the g
sword. g
The Jew, whose memory is long, and who has wit- g
nessed the rise and fall of many tyrants, cannot but have =
complete confidence in the defeat of the enemies of God =
and man. With the Psalmist, he reaffirms his faith in the §
supremacy of the spirit. “Some trust in war chariots, 1
some in horses but we shall put our trust in the name of i
the Lord.” The conviction is growing in the hearts of man 1
that righteousness and justice, kindnesss and decency shall 1
prevail; that evil shall be destroyed, that a just and peace- J
ful society shall ultimately be established, based on the 3
ethical and moral principles of the Law of God. =
On Rosh Hashonah, we Jews, heirs to a great spiritual |
heritage, gather in our synagogues to give expression |
through our prayers and songs to this hope which still 1
lives in the hearts of millions of men already over- |
whelmed by a brutal power. May our petitions be ac- 1
ceptable to God. May He send redemption to our people |
and to all mankind during the coming year. Amen.
