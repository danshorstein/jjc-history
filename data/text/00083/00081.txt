THE NATIONAL COUNCIL OF IEWISH WOMEN
The first principle of the National Council of Jewish Women, the
organization for United Jewish womanhood, is Unity. It is the only National
organization in this country which includes every type of Jewish woman—
Orthodox, Conservative, Reformed, Zionist, Non-Zionist and unaffiliated,
working together for the American Way of Life.
Our principal aims are the welfare and advancement of American women,
the betterment of conditions in our cities, states, our nation and the world.
Council exerts every effort to make its members conscious of their duties in
a democratic community.
The Jacksonville Section, in the three short years of its existence has
achieved city wide prestige and the good will and appreciation of city and
national organizations for our non-sectarian welfare work, such as Toyery,
Nursery Schools, War Service Volunteers, Hospital Visiting, our very im¬
portant work for the Foreign born, besides our legislative, religious and edu¬
cational projects.
In these times of world crisis, the Council faces an increasing demand for
service, plus an ever growing burden on its budget. Therefore, it is our aim,
for the coming year, to secure the loyal cooperation of every Jewish woman
in the community, to work untiringly for the betterment of self and fellow
man. , ,
Mrs. Oscar L. Silverman
