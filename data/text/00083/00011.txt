Mr. Abe Newman
This Yearbrook is dedicated to Mr. Abe Newman, our esteemed
President, in grateful appreciation for his sincere devotion to and tireless
work for the Jacksonville Jewish Center and for the Jewish community
at large. His high ethical standards, his simplicity and good sense, his
Jewish zeal and his fine leadership have endeared him to all.
It is indeed a rare blessing for a community to have a leader of his fine
qualities. Mr. Newman’s personality kept in balance the various conflict¬
ing tendencies that are sure to develop in an institution that serves both
Conservative and Orthodox elements.
His personal integrity and honesty of purpose won for him the
respect and admiration of all Center members. He was, therefore, able
almost single handed to conduct a drive for the retirement of the large
Center mortgage which after months of hard and persistent effort was
crowned with complete success.
The heavy burden under which the institution had been laboring all
these years was suddenly lifted enabling the Center to scan new horizons
of achievements in serving the religious, educational and social needs of
the Jewish community.
With almost equal zeal Mr. Newman entered into the annual drive
of the Jacksonville Jewish Community Council shouldering with its presi¬
dent a great deal of the responsibility for the most successful campaign
in the history of Jacksonville Jewry.
Mr. Newman, therefore, well deserves the recognition given him when
he was overwhelmingly elected Jacksonville’s representative to the Ameri¬
can Jewish Conference.
This dedication is only a small token of the affection that fills the >
hearts of the members of the Center for Mr. Abe Newman. On the occasion
of the New Year we pray for his well-being and wish him and his dear
ones the blessing of good health, prosperity and happiness.
