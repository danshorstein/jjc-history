B’NAI B’RITH AUXILIARY
After several years of preliminary
organizational efforts, the Women’s
Auxiliary of the Adolph B. Weil Lodge
of B’Nai B’Rith finally came into ex¬
istence on November 18, 1942, when
its charter was formally presented to it
by Mrs. Louis Ossinsky, vice-president
of District Five Women’s Auxiliary.
Mrs. Irving Levin acted as temporary
president during its organizational ef¬
forts, after which she was succeeded
by Mrs. Max L. Friedberg, the present
president.
Although the organization is only
several months of age, it has already
achieved outstanding success in sev¬
eral of its projects. Its most marked
success came with its designation of
having led all organizations in the
City of Jacksonville in the project of
furnishing Company Day Rooms at
Camp Blanding, by having completely
furnished four company day rooms.
This project was under the joint
chairmanship of Mrs. Irving Levin and
Mrs. Philip Selber. Most of the fur¬
nishings were secured through the ac¬
tive efforts of the ladies in collecting
used articles of furniture and reclaim¬
ing them for use in the Day Rooms.
In addition to the Company Day
Room project, the auxiliary also aided
in the furnishing of the new Hillel
Home at Gainesville, Florida, and has
aided the Lodge i'n some of its own
projects.
A very bright future is predicted
for this newest of all Jewish organiza¬
tions in Jacksonville. Plans for an even
greater achievement are now being
made.
