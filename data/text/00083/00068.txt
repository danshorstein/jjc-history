For two decades the Ladies’ Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society and Home
for the Aged has been of much service to needy people of Jacksonville,
supplying them with food, fuel, clothing, medical care and housing. The
society, through its non-interest loan fund has enabled many families to
overcome financial difficulties and re-establish themselves. During the win¬
ter transients pass through our city, and these are given food and lodging.
Each year at Passover, contributions are sent to the General Orphan
Home f or Girls in Palestine, and special food baskets are sent to state
and city institutions. In addition to the regular program of aid work, on
all major Jewish holidays, extra funds and food baskets are distributed to
indigent families.

The important work of building a Home for the Aged has been retarded
due to the war, nevertheless many contributions have been received, and the
Society continues its efforts towards the fulfillment of this much needed
project. A substantial portion of the Home Funds has been invested in
War Savings Bonds, in this manner contributing to the war effort. Any¬
one interested in buying a bond now, to be used as a gift later when the
home can be built is urged to do so.
Following the needs of the times, we have contributed to the American
Red Cross. The membership has cooperated wholeheartedly with the USO
in sponsoring monthly dances at the Center for the men and women in the
armed services.
During the past year, funds for the continuation of our work were raised
from dues, individual contributions, luncheons, card parties, summer fund,
turkey project and chain card parties.
A special sick fund, “The Rose Nabin Memorial Fund,” was started in
loving memory of Rose Nabin, who dedicated her life to charity.
