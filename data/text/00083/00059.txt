THE CENTER AND THE
COMMUNITY
The following pages are dedicated
to a description of the various Jewish
organizations in the community. These
organizations are autonimous inde¬
pendent bodies. Yet the Center has
directly and indirectly had a pro¬
found influence upon their work and
has contributed greatly to their pro¬
grams.
The great majority of the organiza¬
tions use the Center as a meeting place.
The Zionist groups receive idealogical
and moral support from the Center
pulpit, Hebrew School and Sunday
School. The Rabbi and the Teachers
of the Center are often called upon
to lead study groups and help plan the
cultural programs of these organiza¬
tions. The Jewish Community Council
summons its leaders and workers from
among the ranks of the members of the
Jewish Center. The Rabbi and the
Vice-President served as Executive
Secretaries of the Community Council
for the past six years. The Center
President and members of its Board
are among the most active leaders of
the Community Council. The same
may be said about many of the other
organizations, the Y. M. H. A., the
B’Nai B’Rith, the Jewish Welfare
Board, etc.
The Center Synagogue serves the
religious needs of nearly 400 member
families and many unaffiliated fami¬
lies. It maintains the only “Minyan”,
morning and evening, in the City.
Both the orthodox and the conserva¬
tive rituals are adhered to on the High
Holy Days so that every Jew who
wishes to worship in the Center may
find complete satisfaction.
The Center maintains a Talmud
Torah in the Community which is open
to all, members and non-members.
This year two buses will provide
transportation to Talmud Torah pupils
from many sections of the City.
The Center sponsors a Boy Scout and
Girl Scout troop and a Cub Pack. It
assists in every possible way the Young
Judean Clubs and the A. Z. A. fraterni¬
ty.
The Center conducts a weekly radio
program over station WJAX which
features brief talks on Judaism and re¬
lated subjects and which enjoys a wide
audience both among Jews and Gen¬
tiles.
Thus the Center is carrying out the
purpose for which it was originally
brought into being . It is the hub of the
wheel of Community life. It radiates
its inspiration for Jewish living and
provides the majority of our Jewish
men, women and children with re¬
ligious, educational and social activities
throughout the year.
