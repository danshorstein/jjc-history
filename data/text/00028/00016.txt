The above prayer is from our High Ploliday Prayer Book and is known
as the “Hineni”. I remember reading this prayer aloud, to those assembled
at my election as your President, nearly two years ago. Its lines stirred me
very deeply then. They are even more meaningful and relevant to me today.
Standing on the threshold of a new year, each and every one of us
should read this prayer as if each of us was its author. For each of us is in¬
deed praying for “Kol Yisrael.” Each of us is not only our “brother’s
keeper,” we are also our brother’s brother. Therefore, let us all work to¬
gether to build a better congregation, a better community, and a better
world.
May the Holy One, blessed be He, inscribe and seal for each of us in
the book of life a year of health of body, a year of enlightenment of the
mind, a year of hope and joy and above all a year of peace.
