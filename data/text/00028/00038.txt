Sponsored by the American Zionist Council and
arranged by the Executive of the World Zionist Or¬
ganization and the Jewish Agency. It included thirty
selected individuals from various sections of the
United States.
The idea was executed in depth and with under¬
standing, to provide American “opinion-makers” with
information on the realities of Israel life, attitudes
and objectives. Conversely, it helped to interpret
American points of view vis-a-vis the relationship of
the two communities. This was done with meticulous
care, with facts and details from all points of view,
with plenty of opportunity for questions and answers.
Among the prominent government figures and per¬
sonalities who participated in the discussions, using
the give and take of questions and answers, were
Jerusalem’s Mayor Ted Kollek; Yaakov Herzog of the
Prime Minister’s Office. The cool, leisurely, open-
minded meeting which took place with Prime Minis¬
ter Golda Meier, in her office prior to her departure
for London, will long be remembered. An informal
“conversation” with Foreign Minister Abba Eban at
the Ministry, whose wit sparked his presentation,
“Israel’s Foreign Policy,” was another important high¬
light.
Cantor Marton was privileged to have been asked
to join this group of leaders, journalists and policy
makers throughout the country.
