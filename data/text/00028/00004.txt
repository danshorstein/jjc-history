Whatever was our yesterday, today, as every day, we start anew.
Will we -- as individuals, as families, as nations -- meet problems today we have not yet
solved? Of course we will.
We can, therefore, without despair, without discouragement, set ourselves to any problem we
face today and tomorrow.
We need only realize that ours is not to complete the task, yet neither can we neglect it.
To know this gives each man more power than the atom, more fire than the stars.
And we can be sure of its daily truth, not only from life around us but from the Book of 0od,
wherein He made us a promise - - a covenant with the perpetual generations of Man - - that He
will never destroy the world or permit Man utterly to destroy himself.
We have this promise. Yet the decision to choose the good remains for each of us to make
anew each day. Indeed, each man is the earth's greatest promise.
You have the opportunity to fulfill this promise every day in each of the six different relation¬
ships given to Man:
1. In your relationship to yourself: Respect yourself for being made
in the image of Sod; and do what you can to fulfill the talents
you have been given.
2. In your relationship to your family: Realize that the greatest gift
you can give your family - far more than any other possession -
is yourself.
3. In your relationship to your community: Love your neighbor as
yourself.
4. In your relationship to the nation: Give your country your passion
for freedom and justice.
5. In your relationship to the world: Seek to unite the nations of the world
in brotherhood and peace.
6. In your relationship to God: "Do justly, and love mercy, and walk
humbly with thy God."
