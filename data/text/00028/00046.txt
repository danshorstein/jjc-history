BARRY SINOFF
The Men’s Club pledges a 1969-70 season as suc¬
cessful as the season we have just completed. Once
again we plan to have a full well rounded schedule of
programs.
This past year our members were treated with an
election program which featured a speaker for each of
the three major candidates debating the election from
the standpoint of Jewish ethics and morality; Rabbi
Yaacov Hilsenrath, speaking on the disturbing issue
of modern American youth and a delightful program
for sports fans featuring two of the A.F.L. players
here for the All Star Game in January. In addition we
played host to Etz Chaim’s Men’s Club and the Tem¬
ple Brotherhood, a program which was highlighted by
Mr. Kivie Kaplan, the National President of the Na¬
tional Association for the Advancement of Colored
People.
As always, the major concern of the Men’s Club
has been the youth of the Center. The Club once
again sponsored the Sunday School Picnic which not
only gave a wonderful time to the parents and chil¬
dren who attended but earned extra funds for the
Committee headed by Dr. Ronald Levin. This Com¬
mittee, an integral part of the Men’s Club, provided
funds for and supervised Air Conditioning and Heat¬
ing, enlarged sanitary facilities, etc., in the White
House at the Campsite. In addition the interior had
a complete paint job. These improvements will make
the House more useable for the Campers and the vari¬
ous youth organizations who may desire to use it.
Our plans for the coming season call for an even
better selection of programs and an enlarged group of
projects to help make the Jewish Center a more dy¬
namic force.
