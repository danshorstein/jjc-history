The 1968-69 season was filled with many new
sights and sounds for Troop 14. Scoutmaster Stephen
Hackel embarked on a program that took the troop to
camp grounds unexplored by Troop 14.
Despite rain and bad weather Troop 14 braved
the elements at Anastasia State Park and the camp¬
ground near St. Augustine proved a real favorite with
the boys. Five mile hikes to Fort San Marcos and
fishing were enjoyed by everyone.
Nature lore and “finding your way” were at the
top of the agenda for the year and the boys learned
both while “hunting wild turtles” at Little Talbot Is¬
land. Gary Gendzier, our chief veterinarian, led the
expedition and gave every boy a lesson in “turtle sur¬
gery.”
Once a month the Scoutmaster brought in a film¬
strip on a subject that would interest the boys. Films
on undersea geology, first aid, nature, and Walt Dis¬
ney were shown. Movie night was a great favorite and
was enjoyed by all.
