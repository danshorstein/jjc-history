ROSH HASHANAH (the Jewish New Year), a solemn two-day
holiday when the Shofar (ram's horn) sounds forth in the
synagogue to call the people to repentance and to reawaken
responsibility to the Lord.
^All days of the Jewish year begin at sundown. Therefore, each
holiday really begins at sundown of the day preceding.
