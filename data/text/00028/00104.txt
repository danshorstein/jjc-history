Officers of C. Y. L.
The past year of C.Y.L. started out with a Beach
Party at Talbot Island and ended with our annual
Installation Banquet.
The highlight of our study groups ranged from
open discussion with a Catholic Priest to a better un¬
derstanding of Orthodox Judaism with Rabbi Wachs-
mann of the Etz Chaim Synagogue. We also held two
overnight study weekends, one at the Center Camp¬
site and the other at the Jewish Center. The study
weekend at the Camp was led by our advisor Maury
Weinberg on the topic “Morality”. The weekend held
at the Center was led by a controversial rabbinical
student, Danny Seigel, using the text “Hammer on a
Rock” as a guide.
C.Y.L. also planned various programs such as
“How Much Government is Too Much” with guest
speaker Joe Carlucci, a city councilman, and “Unrest
on College Campuses” which was led by an American
History teacher from Wolf son High School, Steve
Whitfield.
Our social programs consisted of the annua]
Thanksgiving Dance and numerous conventions and
home parties. On the sports scene C.Y.L. basketball
players trounced AZA in a game 40-30.
The last meeting was elections at which time
Sammy Slom was elected President. The annual In¬
stallation Banquet and Dance rounded out the year.
We would like to thank our wonderful advisors
Miss Rita Weinstein and Maury Weinberg, along with
the past officers, for all their help.
