TED GROSS, Principal
Our standards have remained high as shown by the many real
accomplishments the school has been able to achieve during the past
year. Three of our graduates are in colleges of Jewish studies. The
maximum Bar Mitzah goals of commitment to Hebrew High School,
Torah reading, and service leadership were attained by a rising per¬
centage of students. Our synagogue-affiliated clubs have repeatedly
demonstrated their national leadership. Fourteen pupils increased their
sensitivity to complete Jewish living in the atmosphere of Ramah
camps.
Giant steps have been taken for the advancement of our primary
educational system. Leading authorities were brought in to analyze
and systematize the elementary departments, at great expense.
With educational needs as the primary goal for fund raising ac¬
tivities of our Sisterhood, the school was provided with the latest de¬
vices in audio-visual equipment which will help us to pay greater
attention to individual problems. We can learn more from the theory
of general education than from its application, and the steps taken
during the past year will help us to attain even higher standards.
Our special thanks are due to the many laymen who gave so de¬
votedly of their free time in every aspect needed to maintain such a
wide variety of activities. We thank the chairmen of each of the com¬
mittees as well as the people who replaced the professional teachers
we could not always obtain.
JACK SHORSTEIN,
Chairman
