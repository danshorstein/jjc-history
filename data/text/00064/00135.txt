About the Cover:
We are proud to feature a mixed-media collage by
longtime congregant and talented artist, Kim Margol
Robbins.
About her work, the artists explains: “It is my
hope that this work of art will provide a thought provoking
moment, as we ponder upon what this past year has
brought to our lives, and what this new year will bring.
As you look in the lower right hand corner of this
piece, you will see Jerusalem, a symbol for all of our
people and the historic miracle of our Jewish existence
and survival throughout the ages. Looking even deeper,
you might observe what appears to be an ominous sense
of change threatening our world of “Light.” Through the
darkening shadows, chilling waters, and piercing
protective eyes of “The Almighty,” you sense that now
is the time for us to be grateful and show appreciation
for all that we’ve been given.
As you enter this New Year 5766,1 pray you find
a “Better World,” peace, prosperity and guidance from
Hashem.
This work is dedicated to my precious father. He
has, and will continue to be for all of my tomorrows, a
symbol of what is best in God’s creation of man:
unbridled love in devotion to family, friends, and those
in need, wisdom, strength, and humor, and always a deep
sense of pride in Judaism.” —Kim Margol Robbins
