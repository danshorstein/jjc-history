As the direct service arm of the Jewish community,
we are seeing the strain that the economy,
downsizing and anxiety has put on families. Our
emergency food pantry has seen more than a 200%
increase in demand, including an increase in the
number of families seeking food for Passover.
Our programs have grown in response to demand
for services. Staff has responded to requests for
ways to talk to children about war and how to care
for an aging parent. Services to senior adults
continue to be a priority lor the agency as the
population (and some of us!) ages.
To date, the Jewish Healing Network has trained 100 volunteers. We are currently
serving 40 clients, as well as hundreds of individuals in the hospital. Additionally,
35 clients have received services from the Jewish Hospice Connection.
The Board of JFCS is looking forward to another year of service to our local
community, and wishing everyone a healthy, happy and safe new year.

JFCS Does What?
Adoption
Through our adoption process, adoptive
parents and birth parents are matched to
create a loving placement that fits the best
interest of the child.
Career Directions
JFCS provides workshops and career
counseling services for individuals
entering the work force or seeking to
make a career change.
Children’s Programs
The children’s programs at JFCS, include
school-based programs, counseling,
suicide prevention and play therapy.
Counseling
JFCS is best known for its quality mental
health services. A staff of qualified
professionals works with individuals,
couples, families and children.
Child Safety Programs
JFCS is a provider of safe and nurturing
homes for children in the State of Florida’s
custody.
Emergency Financial Assistance
JFCS distributes funds to individuals who
are having difficulty paying rent, utility
bills or deposits.
Feed A Needy Neighbor (FANN)
JFCS provides groceries to families and
individuals experiencing hunger.
Life Skills Training
Helps people lead productive, self-
sufficient lives by teaching skills such
as budgeting, tenant/landlord rights, job
search techniques and more.
Older Adult Services
• Geriatric Care Management
• The Kosher Nutrition Program
• Transportation
Jewish Healing Network
Trained volunteers serve the needs of
homebound and frail Jewish individuals
through friendly visits, meals, phone
calls.
Jewish Hospice Connection
This program offers expertise by
professional staff who are sensitive to
Jewish needs at end of life.
Ryan White Title II
JFCS is the lead agency for the
distribution of Ryan White Title II funds.
These funds are used to assist people
living with HIV/AIDS.
Support Groups
JFCS offers a variety of support groups
such as grief, life transitions,
intermarriage and more.

Home Is Where The Heart Is
Mount Carmel Gardens apartments
was designed, even to the smallest
detail, to provide comfortable and
pleasant surroundings. From the
initial concept, Mt. Carmel Gardens
has strived to create respectable
living, providing more than housing.
Mt. Carmel Gardens offers a 17-story
building on a beautifully landscaped
site, 24-hour staffing, community
facilities, and planned programs, all
of which were developed as part of an
overall concept ...a concept of
responding to the desires of retirees
for security, comfort, safety,
independence and the enrichment of
stimulating social-recreational
activities shared with others.
Mount Carmel Gardens is
conveniently located, with
transportation, shopping, hospital and
doctor’s offices nearby.
