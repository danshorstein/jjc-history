We grieve for the thousands who lost their lives or loved ones in Hurricane
Katrina’s tragic wake. May God, who ever remains close to the broken-hearted,
enfold, protect and comfort those who survived the maelstrom only to face an
uncertain future.
In the spirit of these Days of Awe, as we contemplate the frailty of human
existence, let us also find strength in the imperishable strength of the Divine
Image implanted in each of us. In the face of our nation’s great suffering, the
words of the Haftorah for the Day of Atonement challenge us to heed the moral
imperative of helping God’s despairing children:
Share your bread with the hungry, take the homeless into your home.
Clothe the naked when you see him, do not turn away from those in need.
Then cleansing light shall break forth like the dawn, and your wounds shall
soon be healed.
And you shall rebuild ancient ruins, restoring old foundations laid long ago.
You shall be known as the rebuilder of broken walls, the restorer of dwelling
places . —Isaiah, 58:7,10,12
