Jacksonville Jewish Center
ENDOWMENT CAMPAIGN
On Yom Kippur 5765, the Jacksonville Jewish Center unveiled an
exciting and far-reaching campaign that will give our synagogue the
financial security to plan, expand, repair and renew.
In a moving speech from the pulpit, Michael DuBow, Vice
President, asked the congregation, “Do we want to maintain the
status quo or do we want to grow our educational programs; enhance
professional staff; and build replacement and maintenance reserves
to maintain this wonderful facility?”
“Do we want to go ‘ above and beyond ’ where we are today and build
on our success that has brought us to this point — of course we do.”
Michael’s speech reflected on how far we have come. Today, we
have no mortgage. We are debt free, but the building fund is virtually
depleted. The synagogue strives, through dues, fundraisers,
contributions and individual endowments, to create enough revenue
to cover expenses. Still, the budget struggles to remain balanced
each year. We are not desperate, but we are not totally secure.
When complete, this historic campaign will have created a
$10 million endowment from which annual resources can be drawn.
It is an ambitious vision to be sure, but it can be done.
A key component of the Jacksonville Jewish Center Endowment is
the commitment of congregants to go “above and beyond” their
annual dues level in support of this effort. To date more than
$1 million has been pledged by members at this level.
Opportunities are still available for every member to participate with
a minimum contribution of one year’s dues, payable over five years.
