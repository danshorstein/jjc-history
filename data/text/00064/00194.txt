Parent-Teacher Association
The Parent Teacher Association, of the Martin J. Gottlieb Day School,
first and foremost, promotes the welfare of the children in the school, the
home, the synagogue and in the community. The PTA is designed to
create a relationship between the parents and the teachers to enhance the
educational process for all of the students. The PTA provides high quality
programs and equipment through fundraising and service programs.
Each year the PTA supervises fundraising efforts such as the magazine
sale, giftwrap sale and the book fair. Other programs include Red Ribbon
Week for drug awareness, Kaleidoscope Day and vision screening. The PTA works to
enrich our children’s Jewish lives through the efforts of our Holiday Observance volunteers.
The students have been enjoying our newly renovated library. The library includes a new
puppet theater and story area, made possible by the funds raised by the PTA.
The past year was a momentous one with the dedication of our school as the Martin J.
Gottlieb Day School, a Solomon Schechter School. A beautiful ceremony and reception was
held - a befitting and enduring memorial. This past year also marked our final year with our
principal, Mr. Willard Kennedy. He will be greatly missed by parents, students and
teachers alike. With Dr. Judy Stoner as principal, we look ahead to a new chapter in our
school’s history.
PTA support is essential for parents, teachers and students. Without our amazing
volunteers, none of this would be possible. 1 am honored to part of this PTA.
L’Shana Tova,
— Amy Goodman
