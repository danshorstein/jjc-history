The Jewish Center conducts five schools for the
imparting of Jewish knowledge, synagogue skills,
and the Hebraic ethical-religious heritage.
1. THE SOLOMON SCHECHTER DAY SCHOOL,
encompasses grades 1 - 6 and a Pre-School. A full-day,
superior bi-cultural curriculum is offered.
2. PRE-SCHOOL: Nursery (ages 3 - 4 years) and a
Kindergarten (5 year olders) meet five mornings per
week. The Pre-School integrates Judaic values and
observances with the general Nursery and
Kindergarten experiences.
3. SUNDAY SCHOOL is an introductory, primary
program for children attending public or private
schools. Kindergarten, first and second grades are
conducted.
EAGER KINDGERGARTNERS anxiously learn how to
read English. And after they’ve mastered their native
tongue, on to Hebrew.
4. HEBREW SCHOOL is a 6 hour per week
experience for children in grades 3 - 7 and leading both
to Bar/Bat Mitzvah at age 13 and to continuation in the
high school in grade 8. Sessions meet on Monday and
Wednesday, 4:00 - 6:00 p.m. and onShabbat mornings.
5. HIGH SCHOOL is designed for teenagers in
the 8th - 12th grades and offers a wide variety of Judaic
learning opportunities on Tuesday evening and/or
Sunday morning.
