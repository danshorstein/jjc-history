TORAH READERS
“This Book of the Law Shall Not
Depart Out of Thy Mouth”
This commandment to Joshua became the basis for
the continuing centrality of the Torah in Jewish life. The
institution of the regular readings of the Torah, which has
continued in force to the present day, is one of our oldest
forms of worship and public instruction. Readers not only
render an important service to the congregation, they also
place emphasis on the fact that in Judaism education is
paramount. It is no wonder then that this skill has been
highlighted in our educational procedures. The most
dedicated and gifted of our students are capable of chanting
the weekly and holiday portions during all of their teen
years.
Included in these pictures are the current group of
readers. However, many have continued to participate well
beyond their Confirmation year. Not included is the
sizeable number of parents, grandparents and relatives who
have been trained to chant special portions at Bar Mitzvah
occasions.
