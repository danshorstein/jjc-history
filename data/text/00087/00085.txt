A. B. Weil Lodge #800, of Bnai Brith, continues to
take its place among the prominent organizations of this
City.
Serving the Jewish community through the Anti-De¬
famation League, Hillel Foundation, Youth Organizations
(AZA and BBG), among other philanthropic and cultural
activities the local lodge expects the coming year to be its
greatest year of acheivement.
The Lodge will conduct its regular meetings at the
LOUIS SAFER
Luncheon Club which meets on the first and third Thursdays
of each month, and will hold a bi-monthly evening meeting.
With a membership of over 300, the Lodge hopes to in¬
crease its membership to 450 during the coming year.

The B’nai B’rith Women extend their heartiest greetings
to the Jacksonville Jewish Community and invite the ladies
to take part in its program.
The program of B’nai B’rith Women is varied and in¬
cludes service to those in need in this country and abroad.
Our hospitals in this country and the Children’s Hospital
in Israel are a part of the B’nai B’rith program. The Anti-
Defamation League, B’nai B’rith Hillel Foundations, B’nai
B’rith Youth Organizations, and various philanthropic and
cultural projects are part of our activities.
B’nai B’rith Women hold fund raising affairs for the sup¬
port of these projects. Plans are underway for sponsoring
a Barn Dance on Oct. 18th and the annual Mad Hatters
event in the Spring.
It is imperative that all women of Jewish faith join
with us in B’nai B’rith to help carry on this vital and
interesting work.
