Organized in 1896, the Jewish War Veterans of the United
States is the oldest active veterans organization in the coun¬
try. Here in Jacksonville the Lt. Meyer Leibovitz Post No. 199
continues to be the patriotic voice of American Jewry. Its
members are dedicated to the service of our country and to
uphold the fair name of the Jew. The Jewish veteran, a
living symbol of American patriotism, can justly combat the
vicious attacks from subversive sources.
Members of the J. W. V. participate actively in other
veterans organizations, such as, the American Legion, Veter¬
ans of Foreign Wars, Disabled American Veterans, etc. Rep¬
resentatives of J. W. V. are members of local, state, and
national Veterans Councils and committees. Because of its
relationship with leadership in veterans circles, J. W. V. is
able to effectively serve Jewish interests in important areas.
Lt. Meyer Leibovitz Post No. 199 keeps up with the na¬
tional program by its regular visits to the Veterans Hospi¬
tals; by conducting military services for those who died for
our country; in working with the Public Relations Committee
of the Jewish Community Council; and in various other pro¬
grams which include social and athletic activities.
During the past year the Ladies Auxiliary was reacti¬
vated, and is once again taking an active part in veterans
and community work.
