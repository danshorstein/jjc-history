With pleasure we of Esquire present to you our club of
'52, beginning with thoughts of acheivement and a desire
for improvement, and ending with the fulfillment of these
goals, and the satisfaction of a job well done.
During this 16th year of Esquire, our membership in¬
creased to thirty, the largest since the club’s beginning.
This increased membership enabled us to further our social,
„ cultural and athletic program.
Our primary project in religious activities consisted of
the presentation of books depicting the life of a Jewish youth
to every Bar Mitzvah in Jacksonville. The athletic program
was climaxed by the winning of the Center Baseball League
championship.
Each month Esquire looks forward to a social function
which presents new ideas in the way of party themes and
provides the highlight of the social activities of much of
the Jewish youth. The success of these affairs was in great
measure due to the enthusiastic support and planning of our
mascot, Miss Ina Jean Yoffee.
With the improved facilities for meeting provided this
year by the Youth Section of the Jacksonville Jewish Center
and the cooperation of our entire membership, Esquire looks
forward to even greater success in the future.
