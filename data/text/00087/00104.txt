One of the most important functions of the Jack¬
sonville Jewish Center is its service to the Jewish
community in providing appropriate and dignified
burial facilities for the members of the congregation.
Under the chairmanship of Max Rubin, the Center
Cemetery Committee during the past year continued
its efforts to appropriately care for the burial re¬
quirements of Center membership as well as to pro¬
vide adequately for future needs. The new Memorial
Park, developed during the past few years, has be¬
gun to relieve the overtaxed facilities of the old Ever¬
green Cemetery, the remaining burial plots of which
became almost completely exhausted during the past
year.
During the past year a complete audit and analysis
of the financial structure of the Cemetery’s fund re¬
vealed the necessity for an adjustment in the schedule
of perpetual upkeep as well as in the schedules for
annual maintenance. Maintenance costs due to in¬
creased costs of labor and other necessities, necessita¬
ted the adjustment which was the first increase in
maintenance charges in over twenty years. Rates
have been adjusted in keeping with this analysis and
the splendid cooperation of the membership in accept¬
ing these new schedules has been gratifying.
The committee has continued its unselfish devotion
to the needs of the community and is on call at all
times.
