The Jewish Welfare Society has served the needy in
Jacksonville for many years. It is a member agency of the
Jacksonville Community Chest and the Jewish Community
Council. The agency’s functions are divided into various cate¬
gories.
The Society works with the National Council of Jewish
Women and the United Service for New Americans in helping
New Americans adjust themselves in our community. It ad¬
ministers the funds furnished by the Jewish Community
Council which, make their resettlement possible.
Local families receive financial assistance and receive
casework help to enable them to strengthen themselves and
find a more satisfying way of life.
Transients are counseled and helped wherever possible,
either with food, lodging, employment or contact with their
families.
The Welfare Society works in close relationship with
River Garden in processing applications for the Home and
furnishing a casework service to the residents of the Home
to make their initial adjustment to group living easier,
and their continuous residence richer.
The Society is the only Jewish licensed child placing
agency in the State of Florida and maintains a Foster Care
Program as well as an Adoption Program.
The Society offers a limited Social Service Program with
Jewish inmates at the Florida State Prison at Raiford.
With the financial help of the Levy Fund, the Jewish
Welfare Society has inaugurated, to a limited extent, the
operation of the Family Consultation Center. It serves
families where there are problems due to parent-child rela¬
tionships, as well as families experiencing martial discord.
Under the auspices of the Jewish Welfare Society, the
Family Consultation Center is non-sectarian. The Center has
completed 2 years of highly successful operation gaining
wide community and professional recognition.
Mr. Clifford J. Bodarky is Executive Director of the
Jewish Welfare Society and Director of its Family Consul¬
tation Center. Mr. A. H. Rothstein is President of the Jew¬
ish Welfare Society, and Dr. S. Halpern serves as Chairman of
the Administrative Committee of the Family Consultation
Center.
The Society is a member of the Florida Conference of
Social Welfare and the National Conference of Jewish Social
Welfare.
