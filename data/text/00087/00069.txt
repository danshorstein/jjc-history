This has been a banner year for Cub Scout Pack 14,
sponsored by the Jewish Center. All the Cubs did outstanding
work in completing their achievements. The quota of 32
boys has been reached and will be exceeded with the induc¬
tion of a number of boys during the opening months of our
new session. Due to the very high rating of the Pack, it is
possible Scout Headquarters will allow the Pack to extend
its quota to take in several more boys of the ages from
eight years to eleven years inclusive.
The Pack has engaged in a very interesting program
of activities. Among the many was a trip to the Green
Cove Springs Naval Base; a tour through the Jacksonville
Glass Factory; a bowling tournament; a night baseball game
at the local ball park. Highlighting the year of activity was
a combined banquet with the Boy Scouts of Troop 14, where
the Pack received its annual charter.
Since the success of cubbing depends largely upon ac¬
tive parent participation, much credit is due to the Den
Mothers who so willingly and ably gave of their time and
talents to make the weekly Den meetings successful. The
untiring efforts of Mrs. Nathan Robin in organizing the
Mothers into a cooperative team is greatly appreciated.
Further credit for the outstanding achievement of the
Pack this year goes to the enthusiastic committeemen and
officers who devote much time in developing interesting and
educational monthly programs for the boys. They deserve
much credit and recognition. Dr. Carl Hackel who became
Cubmaster this year has done an outstanding job in coordina¬
ting all the activities of the Pack. Under his guidance the
Pack hopes to make even greater progress in the coming
year.
The Pack is now composed of two Dens in the South-
side, one in Riverside, and one in Springfield, where the
weekly Den meetings are held. Once a month the Dens meet
at the Center for a combined Pack meeting. The following
are members of the Pack: Stephen Banks, Joseph Borkson,
Sam Chepenik, Stephen Entman, Michael Falis, Herbert
Fishier, Stephen Freedman, Stanley Gelman, George Gloss-
man, Stewart Goldberg, Wesley Green, Michael Greenberg,
Stephen Hackel, Stephen Halpert, Norman Jaffe, Byron
Kolitz, Terry Lasris, George Levin, Lewis Manilove, Ben
Mic.helson, Edward Robin, Larry Rosenblum, Howard Safer,
Michael Sharf, Michael Schwartz, Ronnie Spiwak, Stanley
Stone, Jerry Tobias, Dennis Wood, Bruce Witten, Edward
Witten, and Henry Davis. Applicants for membership in the
Cub Scouts should contact the Center office or a member of
the committee.
With the cooperation of the parents, and with our
able officers and committeemen, we will enter our new season
with hopes for even greater acheivement from our Cubs and
their Pack.
