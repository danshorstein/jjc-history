Junior Hadassah, through its fully organized social,
educational and cultural activities, provides a place for
every young woman between the ages of 18 and 25. The
social program, often coordinated with Masada, is not
limited to formal socials, but also consists of informal
and impromptu gatherings. The constant educational study
groups and cultural programs make each member aware of
her position as an American Zionist, to the needs of her
community at home; simultaneously, establishes a feeling
of understanding toward Israel—what accomplishments have
been acheived and what she can do to maintain and secure
Israel and strengthen the bond between American and Is¬
raeli Youth.
Meir Shfeyah, the underprivileged children’s village is
a source of pride to our group. Working with Senior Hadas¬
sah on this project, we also assist in the support of the
Henrietta Szold School of Nursing, Youth Aliyah, and J. N. F.
Many young women become members of Junior Hadas¬
sah with no understanding of Zionism and its purposes, but
once they are integrated into our unit, they see the reasons
for the extensive fund raising. Young women find a confi¬
dent feeling of belonging; of being wanted; of being needed.
They find a place for themselves as part of the Zionist
movement. Jr. Hadassa not only enlightens its members, but
develops leadership for the greater task ahead in Senior
Hadassah.
