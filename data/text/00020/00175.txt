In Jacksonville roughly 830 women make up the ranks of
Senior Hadassah, which meets on the second Tuesday of every
month. These women are joined together by a common bond of
helping Hadassah in her program of health, vocational and edu¬
cational services to Israel, and of a desire to be an integral part
of American community life.
Hadassah is the world's largest Zionist organization, number¬
ing 1250 chapters containing a total of more than 318,000 mem¬
bers. Besides being active in Israeli social and medical welfare,
Hadassah members have also been instrumental in stimulating,
on the American scene, a greater interest and understanding of
the Jewish cultural heritage.

Newly returned from Israel, Hadassah’s president, Mrs. Joel
Fleet, was most impressed with all facets of Israeli life. She
visited the Alice Seligsburg vocational and educational school,
which specializes in fashion and design; and of course the
Hadassah Medical Center which is considered to be a citadel of
science in the East, serving everyone regardless of race, color, or
creed.
Outstanding events of the coming year will include the open¬
ing luncheon, the Israeli Showcase, and the Membership Tea.
In the spring Hadassah’s donors will be honored and in May
will be the closing luncheon.
