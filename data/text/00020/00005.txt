Year Book Theme
The theme of our cover is in tribute to the activities of our lay leadership this past year. We have used the Torah which indicates
the heritage which was assumed by all of Israel.
The question is often asked, "Who are they who must fulfill the responsibilities of the Torah?"
The answer that is often given places the responsibility upon the Rabbis and the scholars for the preservation of Jewish life. The
Jacksonville Jewish Center during the past year gave another answer to this question. They adhered to Jewish tradition by showing
that in the absence of rabbinic leadership the obligation of upholdi ng the Torah rests upon the shoulders of each and every layman.
In the other illustrations we have depicted the various types of activities in which our laymen have participated.
With this issue of the Year Book we take note of the importance of lay leadership and lay participation in our congregation. In
the synagogue, in the classroom, in the choir or in committees, our laymen have proven how vital they are to the success of our program.
