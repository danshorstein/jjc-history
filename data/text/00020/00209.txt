fTAANIT ESTHER (Fast of Esther)—This holiday commemorates
the heroism of Esther, beautiful Jewish Queen of Persia.
Joined by her people, she fasted for three days before risking
her life by appearing unsummoned before her King, Ahasuerus. Her
pleas led to the salvation of the Jewish people and the downfall of
Haman (see Purim, below).
■ i» PURIM (literally, “lots”) — this gayest of Jewish holidays is
P ■ nonetheless named for the lots cast before Haman to select
I a day of execution for the Jews. On the fateful day, how¬
ever, it was the wicked Haman who perished—thanks to Queen Esther
and her cousin, Mordecai. The full story is told in the Megillah (Book
of Esther, our symbol) which, on this day, is read aloud twice in the
synagogue. During the readings, children twirl their gragefs (noise-
makers) whenever Haman is mentioned, to drown out his name.
