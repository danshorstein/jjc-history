|| HAMISHA ASAR B’SHEVAT (Fifteenth Day of Shevat), the
New Year of the Trees. Often referred to as the Jewish
«L» Arbor Day, this holiday commemorates the ancient need for
trees in the Holy Land. It is celebrated everywhere with appropriate songs
and games. Fruits long associated with the trees that once grew in the
Holy Land (and have begun to grow there again) are eaten on this day,
as part of the celebration. In Israel, the day is also observed in a very
practical way. There, where the land had been denuded by centuries of
neglect, and the need for trees has re-arisen (and reforestation has be¬
come a national goal), children and adults plant trees throughout the
land.
