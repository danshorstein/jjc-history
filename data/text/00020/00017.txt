Nine Rabbis do not constitute a minyan, but ten laymen do.
This lesson was taught to us very tragically during this past year.
With the passing of Rabbi Tofield, of blessed memory, our con¬
gregation was left without Rabbinic leadership. With the lay
leadership which the Jacksonville Jewish Center possesses, the
program continued in all of its fullness and significance. Few
congregations can take pride in such devotion, self sacrifice of
time and effort for the cause of Jewish life.
The moral of this past year’s experience is not that we now
have a Rabbi and it is our turn to relax. The moral lies rather in
our appreciating how important we all are to the success of our
synagogue program. A Rabbi without the full cooperation of the
membership is indeed without success, a congregation without
Rabbinic guidance can still accomplish much. It is necessary that
we realize our own personal obligation to the Center program
and recognize the important role that we can play in making that
program vital and meaningful.

Your Officers and Board have performed superhuman tasks

health and happiness be yours for the coming year as you bless
