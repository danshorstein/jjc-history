Dedication of To field Memorial Library
On Sunday, May 28, as the highlight of the United Synagogue
Southeast Region Conference, the Jacksonville Jewish Center dedi¬
cated its new Judaica library in memory of our late departed and
beloved Rabbi, Dr. Sanders A. Tofield.
The solemn and inspiring memorial service, chaired by Mr.
Harry Gendzier, was addressed by Dr. Isaac Klein of Buffalo’s
Temple Emanuel, a lifelong friend of Rabbi Tofield of sainted
memory. Also participating in the services were Rabbis Morris B.
Chapman, Moshe Goldblum, and Allen Rutchik.
President Louis Safer presented a facsimile of the dedication
and memorial plaque to Mrs. Sanders A. Tofield. Mr. Joe Zel,
chairman of the Library Committee, presented the library to the
Center.
The library, made possible by contributions of members and
friends of the Center, has as its nucleus, the personal library of our
late Rabbi. Over 1500 volumes in Hebrew and English covering a
wide variety of subjects of Jewish content for adults and school
children are available for reference, study, and pleasure reading.
Rare old additions as well as the latest best sellers are in circulation
and new books are constantly being added. Our library is reputed
to be one of the largest of its kind in this part of the country. In
charge of organizing the library was our Library Committee, aug¬
mented by voluntary help from numerous members as well as our
young people, and spearheaded by Mrs. Philip Selber, Mrs. Bernard
Harry Gendzier, Lionel Games, Mrs. Philip Selber and
