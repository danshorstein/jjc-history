Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society was founded thirty-eight
years ago as an aid society to help the needy of the Jewish faith.
It was always the dream and hope of our founders to have a Home
for the Aged in Jacksonville.
This dream became a reality when River Garden Hebrew Home
for the Aged was founded. Since that time, our role became that
of the auxiliary to the Home, helping to nurture its growth and
development.
Another successful year has been completed by Ladies Aid. Our
eight hundred members play a special part in serving River Garden
wherever we are needed and in many capacities. We provide extra-
budgetary needs such as furnishings, redecorating, air conditioning
and kitchen and infirmary equipment. Funds for these needs were
raised through Tombola, Donor and Social Printing projects as well
as through annual dues and Life Memberships in Ladies Aid. Linens
were provided through our annual Linen Shower.
In the realm of service, Ladies Aid sponsors activity and volun¬
teer programs in the Home. Since its inception four years ago, over
twenty-one thousand hours of service have been given to the resi¬
dents through our Gray Ladies program. We sponsor regularly
scheduled entertainment and parties through our Special Holidays
and Friendly Visitors program. River Garden residents participate
in projects served by volunteers of the auxiliary such as the resi¬
dents magazine, Ceramic Classes and the Hobby Shop.
This year from our Senior Citizens program has emerged a club
with its own officers. It is called "The Golden Sunshine Club."
Their goals are to create a new fellowship of senior citizens for the
purposes of companionship and to participate in civic and philan¬
thropic activities.
