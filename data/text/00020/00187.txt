Brandéis University, the first Jewish-sponsored non-sectarian
school of higher learning, opened its doors in 1948. This year
marked the graduation of the 10th class at the Waltham, Massa¬
chusetts campus.
The Brandéis University National Women’s Committee was
founded with the sole purpose of the support of the University
Library, the heart of its cultural life. No fund-raising activities
are held, as all money is derived from Annual and Life Member¬
ships, and from contributions to the Book Fund. There are over
71,000 members in 113 Chapters throughout the nation.
The National Women’s Committee notes the development of
Brandéis University with pride. The growth of the Library must
keep pace with the growth of the University, and the addition of
Graduate Schools places new demands on the Library. Women
throughout the country have made a definite contribution not
only to the Library but also to the University by fostering good
will for Brandéis in their respective communities.
