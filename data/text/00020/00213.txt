LAG BA’OMER (33rd Day of the Omer)—this holiday com¬
memorates the lifting of a plague from the students of
Rabbi Akiba, an event v/hich occurred on the 33rd day of
Counting the Omer (the counting of the “omer” or “measure” of new
grain begins on the Second Day of Passover, and continues for 49 days).
In observing Lag Ba’omer, Jews honor the memories of Rabbi Akiba and
his copatriots, Bar Kochba and Rabbi Bar Yachai. These leaders de¬
voted their lives to an heroic effort to end the Roman occupation and re¬
establish the Jewish nation. (Rabbi Akiba ultimately died by torture at
the hands of the Romans.) The holiday is celebrated with athletic games
(symbolized here by bow and arrow) because Rabbi Akiba and his
students carried bows and arrows to disguise their true purpose when
they went into the woods to pursue the forbidden study of the Torah.
