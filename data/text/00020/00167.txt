River Garden having celebrated its 15th anniversary looks to
the future with high hopes and anticipation for future expansion of
its facilities. Increasing needs for the specialized services of the
Home makes it imperative to look ahead.
The Home is now overcrowded as it serves 78 people in a plant
designed for 70 residents. As many more people reach late muturity,
the need for the Home will increase proportionately.
The master plan is to replace the original wooden structure with
a modern 3 story fireproof building to accommodate 50 more resi¬
dents. It will be specifically designed for the ailing and infirm aged
who require nursing care. Another phase of the program is to pro¬
vide for short term convalescent care on a community wide basis
where the individual could come to the Home for post hospital
care and then return to his own home.
In addition, River Garden is considering a housing program ad¬
jacent to the Home to provide an independent living arrangement
for elderly people who could participate in various aspects of the
Home’s program. It would provide the aged with an easy access to
companionship, enriching experiences, as well as a sense of security.
Although River Garden’s plans for the future are ambitious,
they are the only solution to long range planning for anticipated
growth of the elderly segment of the population. River Garden has
proven its worth to the Community by its services to aged and infirm.
The Home again looks to all its friends to share and benefit in the
future growth of River Garden.
