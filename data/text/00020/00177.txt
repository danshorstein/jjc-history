Five Young Judaean Clubs totaling 85 members were active
in Jacksonville during 1960-61. The Zionette Club and Hashome¬
rim Club are senior clubs and Tova, Bas Yisroel, Blue and White
Stars are junior clubs.
The clubs follow the Young Judaean program dedicated to
Jewish cultural stimulation such as holiday celebrations, discus¬
sions, speakers, debates, love of Israel and Zionism, Israeli singing
and dancing, American affairs and participation in community
endeavors. Sociability of the members together with the clubs’
aims and purposes make for enjoyable and meaningful club ex¬
periences.
The clubs had outings, hikes, campfires, speakers, song fests,
parties and the combined affair of all the clubs, "The Israeli Fair"
held annually at River Garden. The funds raised at this affair are
sent to the Israeli Scouts to build and maintain the "Hava," a
meeting center for American Young Judaeans and the Tsofim in
Israel.
This year Young Judaea purchased their own camp in Hen¬
dersonville, N. C. with 18 attending from Jacksonville. This camp
is a non-profit undertaking of the Southern Zionist Youth Com¬
mission, of which Mrs. Harold Rabhan is local Chairman.
