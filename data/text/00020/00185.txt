The National Council of Jewish Women is a volunteer service
organization which through a program of service, education and
social action helps to meet human needs.
Council’s local activities include recreational activities at the
Juvenile Shelter and at River Garden Home for the Aged. In the
past two years it has tested the hearing of almost 20,000 Duval
County children, has continued to provide wheelchairs, crutches
and hospital beds through our Medical Loan Shelf and has sewed
layettes for needy families.
Council continues its support to Israel by training leaders for
of Jewish Women
welfare and educational work through scholarships and by sending
toys and clothing to children in Israel, and has built a new Hebrew
Univ. High School.
Through its programs and projects Council provides a strong,
unifying cord of mutual interests and aims for all Jewish women,
regardless of a diversity of thinking and background, so that they
may work together in the spirit of Judaism for the good of all hu¬
manity.
The Jacksonville Section has both a daytime and evening group,
meeting every third Wed. and fourth Thurs., respectively.
