HANUKAH (Dedication)—Anniversary of the day when the
Jews, led by Judah Maccabee and his brothers, freed the
Temple from Syrian invaders. At the Temple’s rededication,
only enough undefiled oil was found to light the Holy Lamp for one day.
Miraculously, it burned eight days. Thus, on the first day of Hanukah,
Jews light one candle of the Menorah (eight-branched candlestick, our
symbol), adding another flame each day until all eight are lit.
ASARAH B’TEVET (Tenth Day of Tevet)—a day of fasting
(\ and mourning. It was on the 10th day of Tebeth that the
Babylonians began their siege of Jerusalem. Thereafter, the
walls were breached (hence our symbol: the wall-breaching catapult), the
city was taken, the Temple destroyed, and the first Hebrew Common¬
wealth brought to an end.
