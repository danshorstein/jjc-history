PESAH (Passover), the Feast of Unleavened Bread—an eight-day celebra¬
tion of the deliverance of the Jews from Egyptian slavery. During the tra¬
ditional Seder meal, on the first two nights of Passover, Jewish families
read the Haggadah (a ritual recitation of the story of the Exodus).
Throughout the Passover periods, foods are selected according to special
dietary laws. Instead of bread, Jews eat matzoh (unleavened bread) to
recall the hasty flight from Egypt, when there was no time to allow the
bread to rise.
