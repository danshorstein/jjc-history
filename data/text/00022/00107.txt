TWEEN-AGERS
Our youngest group affiliated with our Youth Activities
Program is composed of youngsters in the fifth and sixth
grades. Each month the group enjoys a social activity such as
bowling, ice skating, a picnic or a Hanukah Dance Party.
The Club’s success is due mainly to their advisor, Mrs.
Bernard Glossman, and to the mothers’ committee consist¬
ing of Mesdames Seymore Hoffenberg, Manuel Katz, Byron
Morris and Ben Zimmerman. Special thanks go to our CPC
sponsors Lauren Silverberg, Gail Price and Howard Rosen¬
blatt.
The co-ordinator for 1963-64 will be Mrs. Bernard
Glossman with a mothers’ committee of Mesdames Seymore
Hoffenberg, Byron Morris and Ben Zimmerman.
MAJOR’S PRE-TEENS
Following a major revision this past year, the Major’s
Pre-Teens will now consist of 7th and 8th grade boys and
girls to make this organization a two year grade span as all
our other clubs at the Center. This practice has been found
the most suitable for this age level, with their programs
geared to the pre-teen level in monthly social, religious and
athletic affairs.
Many thanks are due Mrs. M. J. Reiser, advisor to the
group, for her tireless help and to the mothers’ committee,
Mrs. Byron Morris, Mrs. Seymour Morse, Mrs. Irwin Gend-
zier. Special thanks are also due the sponsors, Melvin
Gottlieb and Susan Krivan.
Mothers’ committee for 1963-64 will be under the chair¬
manship of Mrs. M. J. Reiser with Mesdames Irwin
Gendzier, Manuel Katz, David Paikin and Isidore Weiss.
