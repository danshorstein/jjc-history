Home For The Aged
Arthur Gutman, President
Home to 120 residents. Increasing infirmities and disabilities among
the aged made it imperative to design the new building as an in¬
firmary with all the complexities of a hospital. It contains a clinic
wing with examining, dental, podiatry, physiotherapy, laboratory,
and x-ray rooms for specific services. The nursing divisions on the
second and third floors are equipped with an audio visual call
system connected from each bedroom to the nurse’s station. The
furnishings and decor of the bedrooms are homelike and attractive.
To further the concept of the Home as a community activity
center for the aged, a large auditorium, solarium and chapel will
encourage public participation in events at River Garden.
Climaxing the story of the new building is the intangible value
of the beautiful scenic view of the St. Johns river from the 4th
story solarium and sun deck. The setting, the facilities and services
create for the aged a setting where they can find security and com¬
fort in the later years.
