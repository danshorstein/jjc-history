HANUKAH (Dedication) — Anniversary of the day when the Jews, led by
Judah Maccabee and his brothers, freed the Temple from Syrian invaders.
At the Temple’s rededication, only enough undefiled oil was found to light
the Holy Lamp for one day. Miraculously, it burned eight days. Thus, on
the first day of Hanukah, Jews light one candle of the Menorah (eight-
branched candlestick, our symbol), adding another flame each day until
all eight are lit.
