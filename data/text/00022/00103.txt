Another banner year in the annals of our oldest USY
group sponsored by the Center has been recorded. The bi¬
weekly meetings starting at 7:30 p.m. consist of a half hour
business meeting, a varied and diversified program for 45
minutes, followed by social or Israeli dancing until 9:30
p.m. One can see these youngsters on the 2nd and 4th
Sundays of each month in the Center’s Youth Activities
Building participating in various workshops, getting a news¬
paper out on time or participating in one of their many
Judo Exhibition following one of the USY Meetings by
the Bill Beach Judo Academy.

Several items of interest were accomplished this year,
mainly:
• A new set of by laws was ratified by the membership
and put into use.
• One of our members was elected to President of the
Southeast Region at the Regional Convention in
Montgomery, Ala. during May of 1963, with 31 of our
members attending.
• 19 delegates represented the group at the annual
L.T.I.
The annual Best Boy and Best Girl Awards were presented
to Mitchell Perin and Linda Halpern at our annual Banquet
held in May. Our Building Spiritual Bridges program was
supported by the sale of snowballs, waiting on tables, car
wash, sale of name labels, etc. which made it possible for us
to contribute $109.00 to the National USY fund raising
project.
The delegates to L.T.I. this year from the C.Y.L. were
as follows: Karen Caslar, Dotty Garten, Herbert Gartner,
Phyllis Glossman, Richard Kidder, Ann Klein, Sheila
Kramer, Susan Krivan, Madelyn Levin, Anita Mésnick,
Cheryl Morse, Jerry Portnoy, Joe Saportas, Jerry Saferj
Lauren Silverberg, Harold Sutton, Francine Trager, Bob
Wolf son and Bruce Yergin, with Mitchell Perin a Counselor-
in-Training.
Our appreciation to Mrs. Ann Lewis for her guidance
throughout the year. Our advisors for 1963-64 will be Miss
Evelyn Brauner and S. Robert Shorstein.
