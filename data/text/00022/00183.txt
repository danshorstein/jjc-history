B’nai B’rith Girls, the future B’nai B’rith Women, enjoyed a marvelous and ex¬
citing year.
Many of our functions of the past year include a Car Mazuzah Sale, a Heaven
and Hell Dance, discussions on the A.D.L., People to People Plan, and the Amigo
Plan. Also, our College Convention here, in which we entertained 100 BBYO’ers
from North Florida, was a fantastic success.
We are also very proud of our Past-President, Louise Hilsenrad, on being appointed
head of the “Amigo Plan” in Fla., at District Convention in Hendersonville, N.G.
We ended the year with our Annual End-of-the-year Party and then our Installa¬
tion Luncheon, at which the Best Girl Award was presented to Louise Gutman.
We can truthfully say that our Order is taking its share of the responsibility to
secure peace for ourselves and all the peoples of the world. At present we are
working on a Latin American student exchange program, Operation Amigo, Inc.
In the coming year, thirty students from Latin America will, for a period of two
weeks, attend Englewood High School and live with American families. This pro¬
gram is now being carried out by BBYO’s all over the nation.
Other programs that we are working with such as People-To-People, Domestic
Peace Corps, and more, all have the same goal—everlasting peace for all mankind.
We would like to thank our advisor, Myron Blattner, for without his guidance,
we could not have had all the success we did. We would also like to thank all the
parents who have worked with us this year.
