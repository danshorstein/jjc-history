With sincere gratitude we humbly dedicate this Year Book to the late

RESOLUTION
Adopted November 8, 1962, at Board Meeting
WHEREAS, The Jacksonville Jewish Center stands bowed in grief along with the
family of our late beloved and revered synagogue leader,
Robert J. Gordon, Alav ha-Shalom
WHEREAS: The Jacksonville Jewish Center feels its own inability to record in
detail and to adequately evaluate the great accomplishments, fine contribution
and abiding influence of Robert J. Gordon, and
WHEREAS: The congregation feels spiritually compelled to express its sorrow for
this irreplaceable loss and whereas Robert J. Gordon served for nearly two
decades in almost every responsibility in our congregation, serving as Treasurer,
Chairman of the Board, Chairman of the Building Fund and serving closely with
the standing committee who needed his services, and
WHEREAS: It was his personality that served as an inspiration to all who worked
with him, being a source of harmony and good will when differences endangered
our unity, and
WHEREAS: It was the bigness of his outlook that motivated him to support even
those projects that he did not favor, for in his own eyes his own desires were not
as important as the general welfare of the congregation, and
WHEREAS: In the light of his tremendous achievements and in the light of his
profound influence, his own genuine humility motivated him to shun all honor and
refuse all credit for great accomplishment, and
WHEREAS: It was his unique status in the eyes of his fellow members and his own
generous contribution of time, effort and finances that permitted him to influence
others to make ever greater sacrifices on behalf of the congregation, and
WHEREAS: The efforts of Robert J. Gordon are reflected in the progress and
success of almost every Jewish, fraternal, philanthropic and cultural organization
in this community, be it therefore
RESOLVED: We, the Officers, Board of Directors and Trustees of the Jacksonville
Jewish Center in meeting assembled, deplore his passing and extend our deepest
heartfelt sympathy to his wife, Lillian Gordon, his sons, Samuel and Alan, his
grandchildren, and other members of his family.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED: That this Resolution be spread on the minutes of this
meeting and a copy be forwarded to members of the family.
