The revitalized Center Men’s Club under the direction of
Jack F. Shorstein this past year had the youth and drive to
make it one of the best years the Men’s Club has enjoyed.
Primary emphasis was placed on the monthly dinner meet¬
ings with topnotch programs throughout the year under the
capable chairmanship of Stanley Kantor.
The annual Membership Affair was the highlight of the
year with Michael Donziger making it one of the top social
affairs of the year. Another annual affair which has become
one of the long awaited days each year is the Picnic held
jointly this past year by the Men’s Club and Sisterhood
under the capable direction of Milton Felder and Robert
Shmunes of the Men’s Club.
In addition to providing a fine opportunity for fellow¬
ship, the Men’s Club provides scholarships for Camp
Ramah, a Blood Bank, adult education program, sponsors
Sunday Religious School picnic, supports the Sunday morn¬
ing Minyan breakfasts, and provides awards for the Scout¬
ing program along with scholarships to Camp Philmont.
With the second year under the able administration of
these veteran officers, the Club looks forward to an even
more dynamic program and a large membership drive to
increase their current roll of 180 strong. The membership
drive is now under way and all interested men are en¬
couraged to contact one of the officers or the Center
Office.
