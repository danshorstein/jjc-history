From Strength to Strength
Rabbi Moshe V. Goldblum, our Rabbi for the past two
years, has recently left our Synagogue for the pulpit of Con¬
gregation Beth Shalom in Pittsburgh, which is the largest Con¬
servative Congregation in that city. While the congregation
was sorry to hear of this decision, we were happy for Rabbi
Goldblum’s personal advancement to the large metropolitan
synagogue.
His two years of service at the Jacksonville Jewish Center
were marked with much devotion and dedication to the needs
of all our congregants. His warm personality and friendly smile
quickly won the admiration and friendship of our members. The
Rabbi and his personable wife Evelyn will long be remembered
for the devoted way in which they instilled Judaism among our
members. We know that Congregation Beth Shalom will also
receive great inspiration from their association with Rabbi
Goldblum.
Almost completely through the tireless efforts of Rabbi
Goldblum and his wife, the Foundation School was re-organized
and begun after a lapse of several years. The Rabbi’s continued
devotion also played a significant part in our weekly Shalos
Seudos services and study sessions.
We wish them much Mazal and success in their new
endeavors. RABBI MOSHE V. GOLDBLUM
