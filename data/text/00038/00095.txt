As an added bonus a two story , 13 room house was situated

"The Land Acquisition Committee was
formed under the co-chairmanship of Sam
Spevak, Dr. Neal Roth and Alex Freund.
Sam Spevak had been very active in the
real estate business and synagogue leaders
were depending on his expertise to pull
them through. He did not let them down.”
"My recollection is that I met with
Louis Safer who said the Center was looking
for a new site”, Spevak said. "It just so
happens that a few years previous to this I
had been in the house building business
and we ran down every piece of property
in Duval County that was five acres or larger.
At Spevak’s disposal were maps of the
entire Duval County area he had used in
his own business. The search for land
began.
Several pieces of property were consid-
ered. Among them were an 11 acre tract
owned by Ed Ball of Old St. Augustine Road
and a 33 acre parcel right on Goodby’s Lake,
in the vicinity of where the Beauclerc Bay
Apartments now stand. The latter was ruled
out, Spevak said, because the land was too
low.
"I looked at a lot of property, but there
was one piece that looked like the best
thing for the Jacksonville Jewish Center”,
Spevak said.
The land was a 35.8 acre parcel just off
State Road 13. As an added bonus, a two
story, 13 room house was situated on the
front of the property. "The purchase price
was about $75,000”, Spevak recalls, "But
the house was worth between $20 - 25,000.
You knock off the value of the house and we
were paying about $50,000 for the land,
or a little over $1,000 an acre.”
An added consideration at the time of the
land search was the Center Day Camp,
thriving under the leadership of activities
director Robert Fischer. The camp had
everything going for it with one exception -
a permanent site. Whether renting a pool at
a local motel or renting campgrounds in
Switzerland, the day camp was faced with
the problem of* available resources. Given
these considerations, the Mandarin property
site became irresistible.
