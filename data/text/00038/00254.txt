"Everything came our way in time” — Sam Spevak
by Bruce Horovitz and Isabel Balotin
R arely does a synagogue in America have an opportunity
to be "ahead of the times”. For the Jacksonville Jewish
Center, the opportunity came 16 years ago.
It was on August 9,1963 that the Center purchased 35.8 acres
of land in an undeveloped part of town known as Mandarin, or
the "boondocks” as it might have been referred to back then.
Today, the synagogue sits on that property, right in the
middle of perhaps the most rapidly developing section of
Jacksonville.
Whether it was insight, luck, or more likely a combination
of both, the Jacksonville Jewish Center can proudly boast of
having been ahead of the game, ready to provide its program of
the future in a location that has become synonymous with
growth and progress in our community.
The selection of the State Road 13 property was no accident.
To those who were involved, the process meant long hours of
hard work.
There are few written records available to recount events
leading up to the purchase, but certain facts stand out in the
memory of those involved.
"We had been meeting at the Center on Third and Silver
and a lot of people in the community were saying that we ought
to move," recalls Wilbur Margol, a member of the Board who
was later to become Center President. "I remember one meeting
in particular that was very vocal, and as a result of that meeting,
we appointed a committee that was best qualified to find us
a good piece of property.”
