Of course, you’re a hero to your children!! They love
and admire you, respect you, usually obey you - and above
all, imitate you.
Which, you see, is the rub. They imitate the wrong things
you do as well as the right things. If you aren’t careful about
right and wrong, in your own behavior, your children will
get confused. They’ll either think the wrong things are all
right, or they’ll lose their hero-worship for you.
Do you, in effect, tell your child, “you go get a Jewish education - but include me out!” or,
“you have to find time from play and public school for a Jewish Education - but I’m too busy
to get one!” or, “we’ll enroll you in a Jewish school, but we won’t give a darn about your atten-
dance, progress, cooperation, continuation!” or, “the teacher knows best, he’s always right -
you do what he says, but don’t expect me to!”
What a hero! You want your child to become smarter, more intelligent. But he imitates you -
remember? Learned anything new lately, yourself, in Hebrew, Jewish history, literature, art,
Yiddish, current events? Taken any new courses, read any new books? You want your child
to be a good Jew, or a better Jew, or a happier Jew. Setting him an example, are you? Making
you home and your daily life more Jewish? Taking more part in Jewish affairs? You want your
child to respect Jewish life and Jewish studies. All right, try showing some respect yourself!
Start his Jewish education early, keep him at it indefinitely. Visit the school, get to know his
teachers and his books, get active in the parents group. Try to give your child the kind of Jewish
environment his school is giving him.
When it comes to Jewish education, what kind of a hero are you?
