LT. MEYER LIEBOVITZ POST 199
Jacksonville, Florida
Meyer Leibovitz Post 199, Jewish War Veterans honored former
W. A. V.E. Frances Wilensky (Deceased) by erecting a flag pole and
memorial plaque at the Mount Carmel Gardens, Mt. Carmel Ter-
race, just off University Blvd., on June 3, at 11:00 a.m. The first flag
to be flown is a flag that has flown over the U. S. Capitol, Washing-
ton, D.C.
Mrs. Wilensky, one of the early enlistees into the W.A.V.E.’s
attained the rank of Chief Petty Officer, U. S. Navy, before her
discharge. She became active in civic and veterans affairs becoming
a member of Post 199 immediately after her discharge. She was
instrumental in the construction and operation of the Mt. Carmel
development and saw it through to completion. One of her many
dreams was to see a flag of the United States fly over this complex,
however, her dream was never fulfilled due to other committments
of money. The Jewish War Veterans have now completed her
dream for her with this dedicatory ceremony. She, through the help
of Congressman Charles Bennett, procured the flag that will be
flown at the ceremony on June 3rd.
Accepting the honor were her sons Daniel and Billy Wilensky, who
then transferred the responsibility of the flag pole to the Board of
Directors of Mt. Carmel.
Mrs. Wilensky is the first woman to be honored and Post 199 Jewish
War Veterans is grateful to all those who have contributed their
money and time to make all this possible.

Commander A1 Sinoff and Billy and Danny Wilensky.

This has been a banner year for the A.B. Weil Lodge. We have been involved in many successful community projects. We participated in the
Bell Ringer campaign for the Salvation Army at Christmas, a C.P.R. mannequin was donated to the Red Cross, convalescent cards were sent
to Jewish patients in local hospitals. We gave an award for outstanding service to a local policeman. Lastly, and most important, as our main
project for the year, we are donating a Torah to the Hillel House at Florida State University.
An important step in lodge growth was achieved with the merger of the Wolfson Lodge with our lodge this year. We are anxious to affiliate
all interested Jewish men in our fine organization.
Much of the success of this year was due to our President, Milton Hecht and the fine group of officers who worked closely with him. We
expect the upcoming year to be even more successful and once again urge all Jewish men to join and become active.
