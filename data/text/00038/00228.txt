Message From The President
Laurie DuBow
As we close the book on the year 5739, it would seem to be an appropriate time to crystallize our
thoughts on where we will, or could possibly be, going in 5740.
There is no question about things being uncertain. They are uncertain, but I submit to you they have
been, and are always uncertain to some extent. The mood of the economy is up or it is down depending
on what leadership is saying and doing... And so it is in the synagogue; we react to optimism in a favorable
manner ... we believe in those who are leading us.
We are pleased with fellow congregants who have the desire and capability to continue the reputation
of our Synagogue Center as a leader of the community. The names are too numerous to mention here, but
they have given us the support and involvement needed to maintain our programs and facility. These
people have been a great asset to me ... I could not function or continue to serve you without their help.
I am enthused about what happens at our Synagogue, and most importantly I am confident your
Officers and Board of Directors know who and what we are ... where we are ... and where we are going.
Don’t weep for “the good old days”. Speaking for myself, “the good old days” were not all that good
anyway, whereas today isn’t all that bad . . . and tomorrow will be only as good as you and I make it.
Linda, Susan, Michael and I send our best wishes to you for a Happy and Healthy New Year.
Laurie DuBow
The DuBows, Susan, Laurie, Linda and Michael.
