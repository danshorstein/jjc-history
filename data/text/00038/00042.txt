U. S. Savings Bonds,

STATE OF ISRAEL BONDS!
You can’t argue with success, as the saying goes. And Israel Bonds
have had a lot of success since their inception in 1951. Over 350
of the largest banks and financial institutions in the United States own
Israel Bonds. Almost $4 billion worth have been sold, of which $2 billion
has been repaid as they came due.
State of Israel bond money never leaves the United States. The
proceeds remain here in the form of bank credits, permitting Israeli
industrialists and agricultural experts to purchase equipment and
machinery to create jobs for newcomers to Israel, who are arriving
by the hundreds each week.
There are dozens of ways you can use Israel bonds other
than in your investment portfolio . . .
Gifts to Universities: every major college in the United States has
accepted Israel Bonds. If you choose Harvard, they’ll match your gift
with an equal Israel Bonds purchase.
Use Israel bonds instead of cash for travel in Israel.
Pension Plans: Many pension fund and Keogh Plan retirement funds
use Israel Bonds as investments.
