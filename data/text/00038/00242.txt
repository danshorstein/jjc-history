Why does God in his
infinite wisdom require
Ten Men to hold a
Minyan?
If there are only five
books of the Torah
why can’t five
compose a Minyan?
If seven is a lucky
number, why can’t
seven meet to pray?
If the individual is
most important why
can’t one pray for
all?
Doesn’t God know that
Ten is hard —
It’s too early — too late
too far to go.
MINYAN AIRE PROGRAM
The Jacksonville Jewish Center Minyanaires are dedicated to a program
of prayer services, comraderie als well as social and intellectual stimulation.
A year of forums on contemporary social and religious issues which affect
the Jewish community and the community at large are being planned.
As part of its service to the health needs of the congregation, the Minyan-
aires in co-ordination with the American Heart Association, sponsor C.P.R.
classes (Cardiac-Pulmonary Resuscitation), and Blood Bank Drives.
This Sunday morning program begins with minyan at 9:00 a.m. in the
Witten Chapel, followed by breakfast and the speaker of the day. For infor-
mation call Chairman, Gene Kornblum — 737-6127.
Doesn’t God know
how difficult it is
to raise a group
often?
Wouldn’t it be easier
to obey the commandments
with less?
But then of course God
knows that anything easy
loses its meaning —
And without the struggle
of getting ten —
The Daily Minyan would
come to its end.
