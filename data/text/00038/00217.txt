The Holocaust Museum was prepared by the
9th graders of the Judaica High School of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center. Under the guidance of
Rabbi Dov Kentof, the museum depicted the catas
trophic period in our history.
Why, you ask, must we have a Holocaust
Museum? Can’t we just “forgive and forget?”
If we do “forgive and forget” then we, as today’s Jews, are sinning
against ourselves. Only from history can we begin to understand
and control our present and future. History cannot be repeated; so, we
must not ever “forgive and forget. ” We must inspire the present
generation of youth to help build a world where genocide could not,
once again, occur. We must always remember the physical and moral
fiber of a people that resisted the machinery of destruction against
overwhelming odds. And, finally, we must realize the weapon of
Anti-Semitism, and how the Nazis wielded this too to create a
Holocaust.
