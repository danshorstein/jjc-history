A great asset to the Solomon Schechter Day School is the collection of library books. The PTA sponsored a project in honor of
birthdays - the students and families added many fine titles to the collection. In this unit interested users will find reference books,
many delightful fiction and many interesting non-fiction books. They have both Judaica and non-Judaica themes.
The Sisterhood - Library Shabbat was a pleasurable experience with Gene Kornblum as the reviewer. Members were
encouraged to enjoy the attractive Reading Room with the available magazines and periodicals. Two new periodicals “Sh’ma” and
Reconstructionist” have been added to the list of available magazines: Moment, Midstream, Commentary, Near East Report,
Ba’Lam, Jerusalem Week, Jerusalem Post, Jewish Post and Opinion, Yiddish newspapers.
The Library and Adult Education Committees are sponsoring a Sunday evening “Reading Circle.” Books on Judaica are read
and discussed.
The Library Cart is available at the Kiddush on several Saturdays through the year. It gives members an opportunity to read
books and magazines and periodicals available in the Library. A visit to the Library would reveal many more enjoyable titles.
Members of the Center should avail themselves of this splendid Library. The surroundings are conducive to browsing and
reading. A unique collection of the Library are the Hebrew and Yiddish Books capably catalogued and collated by Jack Becker.
The Library Committee members devote many service hours to maintain this facility. Their assistance is greatly appreciated. The
Library is open on Sunday mornings 9 a.m. to 12 noon.
The generous support of the Center membership makes possible the purchase of new books and library supplies.
— Minnie Schreiber
