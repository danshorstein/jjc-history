Outstanding Center Events
September 16, 1945—Annual Cemetery Memorial Services.
September 17, 1945—Junior Hadassah Annual Yom
Kippur Dance.
October 1, 1945—B’Nai B’Rith Youth Commission Meet¬
ing.
October 3, 1945—Daughters of Israel Opening Luncheon.
October 9, 1946—Men’s Club Supper Meeting.
October 10, 1946—Senior Hadassah Thrift Luncheon.
October 11, 1945—Special Center Board Meeting at home
of Harry Gendzier, for purpose of discussing Ex¬
pansion Drive.
November 1, 1945—Buffet Supper and open meeting of
Center membership to discuss Expansion Drive.
November 2, 1945—Special Balfour Day Services. Guest
speaker, Mr. Michael Adilman, Vice President Sa¬
vannah Zionist District.
November 4, 1946—Athletic meeting with Lt. Ben Becker
as speaker.
November 9, 1945—Sgt. Carl Alpert speaks at services.
His topic, “At the Crossroads.”
November 13, 1945—Celia Adler, Yiddish actress, is
sponsored by Jewish Community Council.
November 23, 1946—A. Z. A. Thanksgiving services. Sol
Proctor, president, speaks on, “Youth Thinks Out
Loud.”
November 26, 1946—First meeting of Daughters of Israel
Study Group at home of Mrs. C, Fink. Subject:
“Modern Jewish History.”
November 27, 1945—Center Men’s Club meeting. Debate
between Chaim Weizmann and Meyer Levin Young
Judean groups on subject: “Resolved that at the
present time Zionism should concentrate on im¬
migration rather than on political demands.”
November 28, 1945—Daughters of Israel Thanksgiving
Dance outstanding success.
November 30, 1945—Special Chanukah services. Rabbi
Panitz speaks on “The Flickering Lights.”
December 5, 1945—Boy Scout Chanukah meeting. Guests,
Mr. J. Richard, District Neighborhood Counsellor
and Mr. Pete McMullen, Field Executive. Mr. and
Mrs. M. J. Safer hosts at Latka Party in honor of
their son, Edwin, upon his attainment of the rank
of Eagle Scout.
December 6, 1945—Joint Temple-Center Services at
Center. Rabbi I. L. Kaplan spoke on “The Story of
Chanukah.”
December 12, 1945—Joint meeting of all Zionist groups.
December 14, 1945:—Jewish Theological Seminary Sab¬
bath. Sermon topic: “The Eternal Light.”
December 16, 1945—B’Nai B’Rith Girls and A. Z. A.
Bazaar.
December 28, 1945—Hillel Sabbath. Miss Sonya Heyman,
president of Hillel Foundation at the Florida State
College for Women, spoke on “Youth Calls Amer¬
ican Jewry.”
December 31, 1945—Daughters of Israel New Year’s Eve
Dinner Dance.
January 7, 1946—Harry Gendzier elected for third term
as President of Jacksonville Jewish Center at an¬
nual meeting.
January 11, 1946—Esquire Club Sabbath.
January 13, 1946—Installation of Rabbi David H. Panitz.
Rabbi Jacob Kaplan of Miami, guest speaker.
January 15, 1946—Irving Davidson, Jewish humorist,
sponsored by the Jewish Community Council.
January 17, 1946—Progressive Credit Union Banquet.
January 21, 1946—First meeting of new Center Officers
and Board.
January 22, 1946—Meeting of Center Men’s Club.
January 25, 1946—Z. O. A. Conference Sabbath with
Rabbi Sylvan D. Schwartzman of Augusta, Geor¬
gia, as guest speaker.
January 25, 1946—Senior Hadassah Oneg Shabbat, in
honor of visiting Hadassah members.
February 1, 1946—Installation services for new officers
and Board of Directors of Center, with Rabbi
Panitz as Installing Officer.
February 5, 1946—Cantor David Putterman, of Park
Avenue Synagogue, New York, sponsored by Jew¬
ish Community Council.
February 8, 1946—Boy Scout Anniversary week services.
Herbert Sohn spoke on “Be Prepared.”
February 13, 1946—Reorganization meeting of Scouts.
Announcement made of inner patrol contest to
begin on February 20th.
February 15, 1946—B’Nai B’Rith Young Organizations
Sabbath.
February 19, 1946—Conert sponsored by Farband.
February 22, 1946—National Brotherhood Week Sabbath.
Dr. Eugene C. Few, pastor of the Riverside Park
Methodist Church speaks on “In Peace as In War—
Teamwork.”
February 26, 1946—Men’s Club Supper in honor of re¬
turned service men. Symposium on: “Is The
American Occupation of Germany Succeeding.”
February 27, 1946—Senior Hadassah Membership Tea.
February 28, 1946—B & P W Division of Hadassah spon¬
sors Card party.
March 3, 1946—Y. M. H. A. sponsors Dance at Center.
March 7, 1946—Jewish War Veterans elect Isadore Green¬
berg as Commander.
March 10, 1946—Young Judean Palestine affair and
Puppet Show.
March 15, 1946—Joint Center-Temple Purim Service at
Temple. Rabbi Panitz spoke on the topic, “More
Than Once.” i
March 17, 1946—Center Purim Seudah. Speaker Rabbi
Irving H. Lehrman, of the Miami Beach Jewish
Center.
March 26, 1946—Men’s Club Supper meeting. Sympos¬
ium on “Can American Jewry devise an effective
education program for survival.” Participants:
Rabbi Panitz, Rabbi Lefkovitz and Mrs. Carl
Alpert, with Sgt. Carl Alpert as moderator.
March 26, 1946—Daughters of Israel Card Party.
March 29, 1946—Hadassah Sabbath. Greetings by Mrs.
Maurice Cherry, president.
March 31, 1946—Junior Hadassah sponsors April Fools
Dance.
April 5, 1946—B’Nai B’Rith Girls Sabbath. Bernice Mar-
gol spoke on “To Be Useful and Helpful.”
April 12, 1946—Men’s Club Sabbath. Greetings by Her¬
bert Sandler, President.
April 14, 1946—Children’s model Seder.
April 14, 1946—Jewish Movies sponsored by Cub Pack.
April 17, 1946—Joint meeting of Scouts and Cubs.
April 23, 1946—Men’s Club supper meeting featuring
Ladies Night.
April 29, 1946—Hadassah-Masada play, “Out of the
Frying Pan.”
May 1, 1946—Daughters of Israel Closing Luncheon and
installation of officers. Mrs. David H. Panitz,
installing officer.
May 5, 1946—Boy Scout and Cub Scout banquet, and
presentation of awards.
May 12, 1946—First annual Jewish Center Men’s Club
Monte Carlo Night.
May 20, 1946—J. N. F. Banquet sponsored by “Nachlat
Jacksonville” with Judge Morris Rothenberg, pres¬
ident of the Jewish National Fund as speaker.
June 5, 1946—Confirmation of eight students of the
Center Religious School.
June 9, 1946—Closing of Center Religious Schools.
June 10, 1946—Beginning of Center Day Camp, under
direction of Harry H. Kolitz.
June 21, 1946—Rabbi B. Leon Hurwitz of Butler, Penn¬
sylvania occupies Center pulpit as guest Rabbi.
July 5, 1946—Rabbi Samuel Geffen of Teaneck, New
Jersey, speaks at Sabbath service. Cantor Abra¬
ham Marton conducts services as guest Cantor.
July 26, 1946—Rabbi Carl Manello occupies pulpit for
Sabbath weekend services.
August 2, 1946—Day Camp Ends.
August 9, 1946—Rabbi Hyman Cohen of Meriden, Con¬
necticut speaks at late Sabbath services.
August 20, 1946—Rabbi Harry Zwelling, former Army
Chaplain of New Britain, Connecticut, occupies
Center pulpit for Sabbath services.
