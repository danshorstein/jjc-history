The Jacksonville Section is a part of the National
Council of Jewish Women, which for fifty-two years
has been the voice of country-wide cross section of
thinking American Jewish womanhood. Mrs. Joseph
Glickstein, President of the local section made the
following New Year’s message:
Surely, our recent records give ample evidence
of the fact that today, as in the past, dozens of
governmental agencies have sought Council aid in
interpreting their programs to the American people,
people.
Through our service to Foreign Born program,
we offer a complete immigration service to all
American Jewish communities. Through our Social
Welfare projects, our Sections maintain local phil¬
anthropic institutions and cooperate with govern¬
mental and other agencies in the furtherance of
worthy activities. Through our other three depart¬
ments, we conduct a program of education for
action in the fields of Social Legislation. Internat¬
ional Relations and Peace, and Contemporary
Jewish Affairs.
’’With the recent merger of Council and National
Refugee Service we will be confronted with all
problems pertaining to those who will be fortunate
to come to these shores. The local committee is

— By Mrs. Hannah Soloman, that great social
leader who ranks in stature with Jane Adams as a
welfare pioneer, Council has consistently pursued
a path of civic service and progressive thinking
which has made it an integral part of Jewish Comm¬
unity life.

As leader of this most worthy organization I wish
to solicit all women of the Jewish Faith to become
