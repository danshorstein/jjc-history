A, B. Weil Lodge, B’Nai B‘Rith
Under the leadership of President’ Felson, the
A. B. Weil Lodge of B’Nai B’Rith has continued its
fine work as the leading Jewish service organization
in the community.
A membership drive in the Spring and early
Summer culminated with the numbering five hundr¬
ed brothers, the largest' membership in its history.
During the past year, the lodge sponsored a
dinner dance for returning servicemen and its War
Service Committee continued its work for those
men and women still in the armed forces.
Many activities are planned for the coming year
with emphasis being placed on those connected
with the youth of the community.
The local lodge sponsors a chapter of A. Z. A,
the leading Jewish boys organization in the country
and a chapter of B’Nai B’Rit'h Young Women. It
also lends its support to the Hillel group at the
University of Florida and is one of the guiding fac¬
tors behind present plans for the construction of a
Plillel house in Gainesville.
