Club
The Esquire Club completed its first post-war season this
September. The greatly expanded program provided relig¬
ious, social, cultural and athletic activities for the teen age
members of this local Jewish club.
For the past year Eli Becker served as president of the
club. Miss Elsie Baker, mascot of the club, assisted the club
in its work. The membership was augmented this past year
by several new pledgee.
The religious activities of the past year included. Sabbath
services held at both the Center and the Temple. The club
was also active in the J. N. F. and Community Council drives.
The club’s athletic program v/as highly successful with ihe
members participating in a number of basketball and foot¬
ball games.
The highlight of the social season, climaxing many informal
affairs, was the Autumn formal dance held in the Center
auditorium. The activities of the year rounded out with a
banquet and dance given at the Windsor Hotel.
The Esquire Club, as a local Jewish organization of Jack¬
sonville, looks forward to a successful program of brotherhood
and community endeavor for the coming year.
