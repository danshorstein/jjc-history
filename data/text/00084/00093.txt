Troop 14, Boy Scouts of America, sponsored by the Jack¬
sonville Jewish Center, made considerable progress this year.
A very busy season was spent with each month seeing an
overnight hike and two weeks at summer camp in Orange
Park. The Troop’s membership of boys from 12 to 18 years
of age is almost at full capacity.
An inter-patrol contest, one of the many activities of the
fun and work filled year was won by the Bat Patrol, with
Jack Eff as Patrol Leader, winning for each of its members
a one week expense-free attendance at Scout Camp, in addi¬
tion to several individual prizes to the high scorers of the
contest.
Jack Eff, Patrol Lead er of the Panther Patrol, received
honors of the year, winning the annual A. Z. A. award and
being elected to the Order of the Arrow, a National Scouting
Honorary organization.
Under the advisory leadership of Scoutmaster, Irvin Roth¬
stein and his Assistant Scoutmasters, Albert Bressler and
Herbert Feinberg, the Junior Assistant Scoutmasters, Sol
Proctor and Edwin Safer and Senior Patrol Leader, Leon
Robins, the Trocp looks back at its past successes and for¬
ward to its new season with confidence in its continued
success.
