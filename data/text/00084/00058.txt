Sabuot (the Feast of Weeks) is so called because it completes exactly
seven weeks from the second day of Passover on which the Omer (a measure) of
the new barley was brought to the Temple. The holiday celebrates the early
wheat harvest in Palestine. It also commemorates the, covenant which was
established at Sinai between God and Israel.
