TROOP COMMITTEE
DR. HARRY YERGIN
This past year has seen Pack 14 grow to a membership of 57 boys
divided into 8 Dens serving the entire community in all areas of
town. Each Den is led by a Den Mother who serves for a 4 month
period. The Den Mothers and Committeemen have developed a
most successful program that has proved rewarding and fruitful.
Arts and crafts, nature study, games, story telling, community service
and various other Cub activities involved the entire Pack in an edu¬
cational and informative program.
Seven Cub Scouts have graduated from cubbing into Scouting
this past year.
Many field trips were held during the past year. A highly
successful Minstrel Show, directed by Cubmaster Yergin, was held
and enough funds raised to register the entire Pack. The season
was closed with an all day outing at Camp Echokotee with all Dens
participating in the Knights of the Round Table program. Sports
and swimming were enjoyed for the balance of the day.
The fall program is now in the final planning stage and the first
meeting of the Pack is scheduled immediately following the Holi¬
days.
