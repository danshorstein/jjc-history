MISS INA SELBER
During the 6th year of organized activity in Jackson¬
ville, the Center Youth League has continued along impor¬
tant lines of culutral, social and religious development. We
have instilled in our members the definite aims and objec¬
tives of National U.S.Y. (United Synagogue Youth).
During the past year, C.Y.L. divided its membership
into two groups, Senior and Junior. The younger club func¬
tions under the name U.S.J., (United Synagogue Juniors)
and has thus far proved most successful.
Other projects included, distribution of the Center Year
Book, a Halloween Party, a Mid-Winter Dance in Decem¬
ber, and the Third Annual Spring Dance.
The financial assistance of the Center Sisterhood, Center
Men’s Club and the Palot Memorial Fund, enabled us to
send a number of delegates to the Leadership Training In¬
stitute in Hendersonville, N. C., held during the summer
of 1955; and to the National Convention in Boston, Mass.,
last December. We of the Center Youth League and United
Synagogue Juniors are indeed grateful to these organizations
and to the Center Youth Activities Committee for their
wonderful cooperation.
