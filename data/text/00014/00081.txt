Our Post was organized in 1939 and was renamed
in memory of Lt. Meyer Leibovitz in 1946.
The Post has always given service to our fellow-
men, regardless of race, creed, or color, service to our
country and all its citizens and service to the cause
of peace, of brotherhood and of humanity. It has
demonstrated its capacity for useful service to the
Country and to Jewry since its formation by Jewish
Veterans of the Civil War, fifty-eight years ago.
Jews have fought, bled and died in every war of
our republic. We seek no special pleading for our¬
selves as veterans: However, when needed by our
fellow Jews, we are available, for we have earned
the right to fight on their behalf to preserve our con¬
stitutional privileges as Americans.
The alertness of J.W.V. was responsible for
stopping the distribution of anti-Semitic literature in
Jacksonville on numerous occasions. Acts of bigotry
were nipped in the bud. It has answered the call
for blood donations. Veterans themselves in our com¬
munity away from home, find in J.W.V. family and
closest of kin. When the last battle of life is fought,
we deem it our duty to pay respects to our departed
brothers to whom the entire world is indebted.
