MURRAY RUBENS
This past year has been a banner one for our Chapter. At the Regional Convention
of A.Z.A. three of our members were elected to major offices in the Region; Stephen Fein-
stein, Aleph S’gan; Herman Paul, Aleph Mazkir and Morris Rosenzweig, Aleph Shotare
Godol. We also won the Regional Basketball Championship.
Our big social affair was our annual banquet which proved an outstanding success.
Much of our success in carrying out our program of religious, social, cultural, com¬
munity service and athletic activities, is due to the untiring efforts of our advisor, David
Weinstein.
As the Junior order of B’nai B’rith, by building a strong chapter of A.Z.A. we will
help strengthen our parent organization in the future.

MARGO PILTON
B.B.G. is sponsored by B’nai B’rith Women. It is an organization for girls between
the ages of 13 to 18. B.B.G.’s program is seven-fold; cultural, religious, inter-faith, com¬
munity service, social activities, athletics and B’nai B’rith cooperation. Our chapter carried
out all phases of the program during the past year. A special event was a visit to hospitalized
veterans at the Naval Air Station. Our chapter also won second place in a Regional Story
Telling Contest.
B.B.G. is the sister organization of A.Z.A. and we work closely together in carrying
out our respective programs.
B.B.G. strives to mold its members into fine Jewish women and to foster friendship
and fellowship on local, regional and national levels.
