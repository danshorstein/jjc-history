The Jacksonville Zionist District began its activi¬
ties last fall with the Convention of the Southeastern
Zionist Region held in Jacksonville September third
through fifth.
A large delegation, representing seven states, at¬
tended, with Mortimer May, President of the Zionist
Organization of America, as guest speaker. Cantor
Marton served as chairman of the Convention.
The local District carried on an extensive educa¬
tional, cultural and public relations program. The
last public meeting highlighted a testimonial dinner
given in honor of Cantor Marton for his distinguished
CANTOR ABRAHAM MARTON
service as president of the local Z.O.A. for the past
three years. Rabbi Irving Lehrman of Temple
Emanuel, Miami Beach, was guest speaker for the
occasion. Jacob J. Becker was chairman.
In the field of membership we note with pride a
gradual increase, brought about chiefly through the
efforts of David Moss and his committee.
The year ahead will be a great challenge and a
historic opportunity. The participation and support
of Jacksonville Jewry in the many vital activities of
Z.O.A. will enable us to carry through to comple¬
tion of our noble endeavor.
