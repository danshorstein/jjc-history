MRS. IKE WITTEN,
It is indeed a pleasure to bring greetings from our organization
to this edition of the Center Year Book.
River Garden Home this year celebrated its ninth anniversary.
This accomplishment is a tribute to the untiring efforts of its mem¬
bers and friends in Jacksonville and throughout the state.
Ladies Aid, in the spirit of Judaism, is dedicated to furthering
a haven for our aged. To provide loving care, amidst dignified sur¬
roundings, physical maintenance and the stimulation of active par¬
ticipation in programs of cooperative planning and playing.
We have always accepted the responsibility of providing the
home with all special needs which cannot be met through its op¬
erating budget. Our fund raising committees and their chairmen
are now concentrating all their efforts toward the purchase of
furnishings for the newly proposed building and to supply the new
infirmary with all items necessary for the proper functioning of
that wing.
We extend our deepest appreciation and heartfelt thanks to the
many friends of River Garden and Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid,
for their support and fine cooperation in our program and dedicated
cause.
