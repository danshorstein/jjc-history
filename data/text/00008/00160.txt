The Bnai Brith Women of Jacksonville are
striving to keep up with every phase of war activity
as designated by the principles of Bnai Brith.
It has been a great satisfaction to us to find that
the name of Bnai Brith is known in our community.
Our local Red Cross Chapter has come to recognize
us as a significant group, one that can be depended
upon to work in any capacity. We respond to all
the Red Cross activities such as sewing groups, surg¬
ical dressings, knitting, blood donors, nutrition and
canteen aids, civilian defense work, salvage collec¬
tions, etc.
We are credited with furnishing eight day
rooms, servicing of four ships with portable organs
and game kits, two regular monthly visitations to
the Jacksonville Naval Air Station Hospital, U. S. O.
dances at our Center and Temple on Saturdays and
Sundays, and parties for service men in our day
rooms, in addition to affairs sponsored with our
youth groups.
We are geared to the war effort, in addition
to maintaining our normal activities, the further¬
ance of youth welfare, Americanism, defense of
Jewish rights, social and community service and
inter-faith understanding; all of which are inherent
in the American way.
