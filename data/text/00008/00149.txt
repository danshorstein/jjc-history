The past year marked the twenty-sixth year of
service of the Jewish Welfare Society to the Jewish
community. Although the war has altered some¬
what the activities of social agencies, certain funda¬
mental problems still remain. Juvenile delinquency
has increased and countless problems arise because
of the enforced separation of families. In war as in
peace, there also are the aged and the ill, the un¬
employable and the family deprived of its bread¬
winner, all of which are the eternal problems of
the welfare organization. The Jewish Welfare So¬
ciety is a member of the Community War Chest.
