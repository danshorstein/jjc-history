The Esquire Club completed a successful season despite wartime limitations and restrictions.
Affairs given by the club provided clean, social recreation for many Jewish teen-age boys and girls.
At the first meeting of the year officers were elected. Samuel Witten succeeded Alvin Leit-
man as president. Miss Vivian Rosenthal was elected mascot. Early in the year a “smoker” was held,
at which time prospective pledges were introduced to the members. A hay ride was given followed
by other affairs at the homes of various members. At each a good time was had by all.
As always the climax of the season for the entire Jewish teen-age community was the annual
Esquire Spring Dance. This year it was held in the beautiful Cadet Room of the Hotel Windsor.
Music for the evening was furnished by a band under the direction of Bobby Walker, a senior at
Landon High School. Esquires, guests, and dates danced until twelve, when the curfew cut festivi¬
ties short.
During the year an Esquire Jr. Club was formed. It consists of boys of sub-Esquire age. It has
its own constitution and operates as an independent unit.
Esquire looks forward to another successful season in the coming year.
