CEMETERY ACTIVITIES
An outstanding achievement of the Jewish Center
taking place during the past year was the purchase
of a new cemetery tract, adjoining the Evergreen
Cemetery Association’s land on its western bound¬
ary. The plot consists of approximately eleven acres
and represents one of the finest locations available
for such purposes. No plans have been made for its
improvement, but a special committee has been ap¬
pointed to study plans fo rits future development.
The membership of the Cemtery Committee was
changed during the past year as the result of an
election held by the Board of Directors in February.
The new Committee consists of Max Rubin, chair¬
man; David Moscovitz, Max Mirkis, Joseph Hackel,
Oscar Mageris, I. M. Lieberman, Charles Kroser,
Hyman Selber, and Morris Wolf son. Oscar Magezis
has been placed in charge of the actual supervision
of the cemetery plot, while Rev. J. Robin continued
to act as chairman of the Shamoshim committee, in
charge of performing auxiliary duties to carrying
out burials according to orthodox ritual.
