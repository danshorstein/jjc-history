Troop Fourteen, Boy Scouts of America, which
is sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Center, is
at the present time undergoing a complete reorgani¬
zation in contemplation of the fall season.
Mr. Ben Chepenik, who has served for the past
three years as Scoutmaster of the Troop, is assum¬
ing the overall supervision of the program as Chair¬
man of the Troop Committee. Harry Eff, former
neighborhood Boy Scout Commissioner in St. Aug¬
ustine, has been appointed Assistant Chairman. The
committee was fortunate in securing the services
of Mr. Irvin Rothstein as Scoutmaster and Mr.
Albert Bressler as Assistant Scoutmaster. Mordy
Rosenkranz and Sol Proctor, who have been active
for a number of years in the troop, will assist in
the rolls of Junior Assistant Scoutmasters.
Scoutmaster Rothstein announced that appoint¬
ment of Troop leaders and officers will not be made
until some time in September, after the new roster
has been completed.
During the past year the troop took part in the
usual hikes, camping trips and other projects cons¬
tituting the Scout program. Eleven of the boys went
to camp as a unit during the last week of July.
Much is expected of the new re-organization
and members of the Center are looking forward
to the re-establishment of the troop as one of the
outstanding units in the city.
