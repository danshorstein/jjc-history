These Are the Facts!
Studies by the Bureau of War Records indicate that upwards of
500,000 men of Jewish faith are serving in the armed forces of the
United States; or, in military terms, more than 33 divisions.
More than 8,200 Jewish men in uniform have received citations
for valor and merit, many of them posthumously. Among the 8,200
are 78 Jewish men who each have to their cred t ten or mere awards
for valor, holding an aggregate of 881 such decorations.
There are 266 rabbis serving as chaplains, representing one-fourth
of the total American rabbinate. Fully 50 percent of all rabbis in
America have volunteered for duty as chaplains. Three Jewish chap¬
lains have lost their lives serving overseas.
Thirty-four percent of the Jewish male refugee population of the
United States between the ages of 18-45 are bearing arms for their
adopted country.
At least one-third of the Jewish physicians of the nation are serv¬
ing with the armed forces.
House-to-house canvasses in a number of representative cities show
that the percentage of Jewish men in service is slightly higher than the
ratio of Jews to the general population.
Thousands of Jewish families have given 3 or more sons to the
service; 1,059 of these families have contributed a total of 3,594 mem¬
bers to the armed forces; 81 of these families have given 448.
The Distinguished Service Cross, the second highest army award
for heroism, has been earned by more than 40 men of Jewish faith.
Of the 500.000 Jewish men in uniform,, the Bureau of War Records
has a comparative Hgure of more than 30,000 Jewish casualties in all
branches of the armed services.
