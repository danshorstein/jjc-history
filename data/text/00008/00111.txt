The Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society has
served the community in welfare work both among
local people and transients for more than two dec¬
ades. Its non-interest loan fund has enabled many
families to overcome financial difficulties. Each
Passover money is sent to orphan homes and food is
bought and prepared for inmates of the state prison.
Our organization has cooperated with the U.S.O.
in sponsoring dances and receptions for the men
and women in the armed forces, under the chair-
f
manship of Mrs. M. Michelson. . .

This has been a year of great progress for the
expansion of the Old Age Home division of our or¬
ganization. The approval and hearty cooperation of
a number of leading business men has been given
for the building fund campaign, on the basis of
their knowledge of the necessity of such a project.
Changes in our constitution have been made to en¬
large the board of directors and admit men mem¬
bers, in order to avail ourselves of their assistance.
In April our organization purchased a colonial home
on a spacious and picturesque river front estate,
and plans are now being made to prepare samé
for occupancy. . \ V
