Jacksonville Jewish Center
DAY CAMP
Perhaps the greatest progress made during the
entire year was achieved in the organization of the
Center Day Camp. Over 60 children were among
the campers, who were able to take part in a varied
program conducted in a traditional Jewish environ¬
ment.
The daily schedule included a prayer service,
Hebrew instruction, athletics, arts and crafts, swim¬
ming, dramatics, and other similar camp activities.
Each week the children visited a place of interest
in the environment of the City and also spent a day
at the Beach every Tuesday. Every Friday a genu¬
ine traditional Shabbas spirit prevailed with the chil¬
dren dressed in blue and white while they conducted
a special Shabbas service. A Sabbath procession
headed by the Sabbath Queen inaugurated the cere¬
mony, and plays, created by the children themselves,
were presented.
During the course of its eight weeks’ existence,
the Day Camp instilled in the children a love of Jew¬
ish cultural values through their participation in
Palestinian singing and dancing and in their activi¬
ties in the arts and crafts course, the objects of
which were centered around Jewish motifs. Special
attention was devoted to the study of Hebrew.
