Jacksonville Jewish Center
NURSERY—KINDERGARTEN
This branch of the Center system offers one of
the greatest opportunities to lay the foundation for
intensive Jewish education. Here the child spends
half of the day in an environment permeated with
Judaism, at the same time being prepared for en¬
trance to the public school. The daily kindergarten
had an average enrollment of thirty children, al¬
though at times the number ran over 40. The school
was supervised by Mrs. M. J.'Reiser, assisted by Miss
Gertrude Meyerhoff. This school trains the child
in the observance of Jewish customs and ceremonies,
teaches simple Hebrew words and utilizes handicraft
for the creation of Jewish religious objects.
