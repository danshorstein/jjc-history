JUNIOR CONGREGATION
In addition to the regular Saturday morning
services, a special Junior Congregation is held
throughout the school year, for the children. These
junior services were greatly improved during the
past year, both as to the type of service and as to
attendance. Mrs. Carl Alpert was in charge of this
activity, assisted by Miss Gertrude Meyerhoff, Ber¬
nard Heyman and Nathan Krestul.
AIN YAACOB CLASS
Rev. B. Safer, who has conducted the Ain Yaacob
Class on Saturday afternoons for a number of years,
continued this activity throughout all of the past
year. This class consists of a group of old men who
gather each Saturday afternoon to study the Ha-
gadic portions of the Talmud contained in the book
known as Ain Yaacob. Rev. Safer also conducted
all of the religious services held during the year, and
performed the duties usually performed by the
Rabbi.
Rev. J. Robin, who has held the position of Sex¬
ton for the past fourteen years, continued to dis¬
charge his usual duties during the past year, which
included the preparation of all Bar Mitzvahs taking
place during the year.
The religious program of the Center should ex¬
perience a considerable improvement with the ad¬
vent of the New Year. The election of Rabbi David
H. Panitz to the spiritual leadership of the institu¬
tion and the selection of Rev. Harry Beckman for the
position of Cantor should provide the institution with
an excellent staff for the rendition of religious serv¬
ices for all groups. Rev. B. Safer and Rev. J. Robin
will continue in the same capacities in which they
have served during the past year.
