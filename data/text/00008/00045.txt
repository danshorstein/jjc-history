OUTSTANDING EVENTS
September 20, 1944—Opening of Center Hebrew school
with large enrollment.
September 24, 1944—Registration for opening of Sunday
School.
October 7, 1944 — Junior Hadassah holds annual fall
dance.
October 11, 1944—Child’s Welfare Luncheon sponsored
by Sr. Hadassah.
October 17, 1944—Daughters of Israel hold annual Desert
Bridge.
October 24, 1944—Rabbi Morris D. Margolis resigns as
spiritual leader of the Center after 12 years of
service.
October 27, 1944—Dr. H. O. H. Levine, Executive Direc¬
tor of the Center, speaks on the topic, “On Coming
Events.”
November 1, 1944—Opening meeting of Daughters of
Israel addressed by Harry Gendzier and Harry Levine.
November 3, 1944—Chaplain Isaiah Rackovsky of Camp
Blanding guest speaker at Friday night services.
November 10, 1944—Young Judaean Sabbath adressed by
Lillian Schwartz, Vice President of National Junior
Hadassah.
November 19, 1944—Farewell and Testimonial reception
tendered Rabbi and Mrs. Margolis by the Center. All
groups affiliated wit hthe Center participated in pay¬
ing tribute to the Rabbi.
November 21, 1944—First meeting of parents and teach¬
ers of Hebrew School.
November 29, 1944—Daughters of Israel annual Thanks¬
giving Dance proves to be a very successful affair.
December 1, 1944—Rabbi David H. Panitz of Syracuse,
N. Y. occupies pulpit of Center.
December 13, 1944—Joint meeting of Sr., Jr., and B. &
P. W. Hadassah features report of convention held in
Cleveland and a movie, “Out of the Darkness.”
December 17, 1944—A. Z. A. and B’Nai B’Rith Girls
sponsor Chanukah Carnival.
December 31, 1944—Daughters of Israel New Year’s Eve
Dinner Dance.
January 5, 1945—Rabbi Maurice Solomon of Kansas City
occupies pulpit of Center.
January 8, 1945—Annual open meeting of Center mem¬
bership addressed by Rabbi Solomon. Center also
holds election of officers. Harry Gendzier re-elected
as President.
January 17, 1945—Harry Katz installed as president of
B’Nai B’Rith at meeting held in the Center.
January 19, 1945—Rabbi Uri Miller of New Orleans oc¬
cupies pulpit of Center.
January 22, 1945—Dr. Salo Baron speaks at Center,
sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Community
Council.
January 26, 1945—Rabbi Aaron Wise, of Schenectady,
N. Y. is guest speaker at Friday night services.
February 9, 1945—Chaplain Earl S. Stone, recently re¬
turned from overseas duty, addresses congregation at
Friday night services.
February 21, 1945—Boy Scout, Troop 14, Silver Anniver¬
sary, celebrated at Banquet held in the Center. Cub
Pack and Girl Scouts invited to participate in celebra¬
tion.
February 23, 1945—Boy Scouts conduct Friday night
services. Paul Canova guest speaker.
February 25, 1945—Six Young Judaean groups hold Pu-
rim Carnival. Proceeds go to Karen Hanoar Fund.
February 27, 1945—Center holds election of Executive
and Cemetery Committees.
February 28, 1945—Senior Hadassah holds successful
membership tea.
March 2, 1945—Jacksonville Zionist District conducts Fri¬
day night services. Mortimer May guest speaker.
March 6, 1945—Jacksonville Jewish Center holds Purim
Soodah. Dr. Max Arzt of the Jewish Theological
Seminary guest speaker. Cantor Samuel Vigoda
renders several cantorial selections.
March 9, 1945—A. Z. A. holds anniversary Sabbath which
is addressed by Beryl Weinstein and Melvin Margol.
March 9-10, 1945—Cantor Samuel Vigoda officiates at
Friday night and Saturday morning services.
March 11, 1945—Irving Davidson, noted humorist, speaks
at Center, sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Com¬
munity Council.
March 14, 1945—Concert given by Cantor Samuel Vigoda.
March 16, 1945—Chaplain Leo Stillpass of Naval Air
Station addresses congregation.
March 25, 1945—Children’s demonstration Seder held
Sunday morning.
March 25, 1945—Pierre Van Passen speaks at the Center,
sponsored by the local Zionist District.
March 28-29, 1945—Passover Sedorim held in the Center
with Student Rabbi Benjamin Sincoff officiating.
March 30, 1945—Rabbi Sincoff conducts Friday evening
services.
April 15, 1945—Roosevelt Memorial Services held, with
Rabbi Abraham I. Rosenberg of Congregation B’Nai
B’Rith Jacob of Savannah conducting the service and
giving the principal address.
April 17, 1945—Center Men’s Club organized at supper
meeting. Rabbi Sanders A. Tofield of Houston,
Texas, addresses gathering. Nat Shorstein elected
temporary chairman.
April 20, 1945—Rabbi Tofield addresses congregation at
services.
April 27. 1945—Young Judaean Sabbath conducted by
members of various clubs, with Rabbi Henry Wernick
of Suffolk, Va., as speaker.
April 30, 1945—The Farband and other Zionist groups
honor Rev. J. Robin with a banquet on the occasion
of his 70th birthday, with Rabbi Lehrman of Miami
Beach as guest speaker.
May 1, 1945—Rabbi David Panitz elected spiritual leader
of Center.
May 2, 1945—Daughters of Israel hold closing luncheon
to mark the end of a successful season.
May 2, 1945—Rabbi Arthur J. Lilywand, Exceutive Di¬
rector of the Unity Committee for Palestine, ad¬
dresses meeting of the Zionist District.
May 4, 1945—Daughters of Israel Sabbath with Chaplain
Sidney Greenberg of Orlando as guest speaker.
May 11, 1945—All youth groups affiliated with the Cen¬
ter participate in Mother’s Day services.
May 13, 1945—V-Day services held in the Center with
Chaplain Richard Singer of Camp Blanding conduct¬
ing the service.
May 20, 1945—Twenty-seven students of Sunday School
confirmed at beautiful ceremony with Rabbi Solo¬
mon Goldfarb officiating.
May 29, 1945—“Outlook for Minorities in Post-War
Europe,” topic of panel discussion at May meeting
of Jewish Men’s Club, featuring as speaker, A. H^
Hurwitz, Carl Alpert and Andre Bialolenki.
May 31, 1945—Closing exercises of Hebrew School.
June 3, 1945—Closing exercises of Sunday School. Prizes
presented to outstanding pupils of each class.
June 4, 1945—Opening of Center Day Camp with a reg¬
istration of 50 children.
June 9, 1945—Cantor H. Bettman conducts Sabbath serv¬
ices.
June 12, 1945—Cantor Bettman elected Cantor of Jack¬
sonville Jewish Center.
