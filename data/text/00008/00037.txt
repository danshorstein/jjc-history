Jacksonville Jewish Community Council
1945 Beneficiaries (Continued)
Overseas Causes
AMERICAN FRIENDS OF HEBREW UNIVERSITY—Supports the Hebrew University in Jeru¬
salem, a permanent home for the traditions of science and learning among Jews.
AMERICAN FUND FOR PALESTINIAN INSTITUTIONS—Supports 52 cultural, educational
and social service institutions which do not receive funds from any other American source.
AMERICAN ORT FEDERATION—Aids vocational retraining of Jews through educational and
technical schools, agricultural colonies, operatives, etc.
EZRAS TORAH—Grants assistance to orthodox rabbis and religious scholars in need.
EUROPEAN AND PALESTINIAN INSTITUTIONS—24 traditional charitable and educational
institutions receive allocations from an annual miscellaneous fund.
HEBREW SHELTERING AND IMMIGRANT AID SOCIETY — Conducts immigration activi¬
ties on a world-wide scale in collaboration with numerous local committees.
JEWISH TELEGRAPHIC AGENCY—Collects and distributes news about Jews all over the
world.
NATIONAL LABOR COMMITTEE FOR PALESTINE—Provides funds for cooperative indus¬
tries and labor settlements in Palestine.
UNITED JEWISH APPEAL—Comprises the following three agencies:
(1) JOINT DISTRIBUTION COMMITTEE—Major American Jewish agency for world¬
wide relief.
(2) NATIONAL REFUGEE SERVICE—Assists newcomers to this country.
(3) UNITED PALESTINE APPEAL—Central instrument for building the Jewish Na¬
tional Home in Palestine through the Jewish National Fund and Palestine Founda¬
tion Fund.
HADASSAH YOUTH ALIYAH—Brings children to Palestine from countries of Jewish perse¬
cution, and places them in agricultural and vocational schools.
AMERICAN JEWISH CONGRESS—Fights against anti-Semitism and economic discrimina¬
tion; endeavors to safeguard the rights of Jews everywhere.
NATIONAL COUNCIL OF JEWISH WOMEN—Promotes a program of war service, service to
foreign born, social legislation and civic cooperation.
National Causes
BNAI BRITH WIDER SCOPE—Maintains Hillel Foundations on college and university cam¬
puses, Vocational Service Bureau and AZA youth activities.
COUNCIL OF JEWISH FEDERATIONS AND WELFARE FUNDS — Coordinates activities of
welfare funds and offers services in research and planning.
HEBREW THEOLOGICAL COLLEGE OF CHICAGO—Trains religious leaders for orthodox
synagogues and instructors for religious schools.
HISTADRUTH IVRITH—Promotes knowledge of Hebrew language and literature in the Unit¬
ed States.
JEWISH LABOR COMMITTEE—Through labor and fraternal organizations fights to protect
religious, political and civil rights of Jews.
JEWISH CONSUMPTIVES' RELIEF SOCIETY—Maintains non-sectarian sanatorium for free
treatment and care of tuberculous patients.
JEWISH CONSUMPTIVE AND EX-PATIENTS' RELIEF ASSOCIATION—Treats tuberculous
patients who come to Southern California; non-sectarian.
JEWISH PUBLICATION SOCIETY—Promotes, prints and circulates books of general interest
on Jewish subjects.
JEWISH TEACHERS' SEMINARY—Trains men and women for Jewish teaching, research and
community service.
JEWISH THEOLOGICAL SEMINARY—Prepares men for the conservative rabbinate and teach¬
ers for religious schools.
JOINT DEFENSE APPEAL OF AMERICAN JEWISH COMMITTEE AND ANTI - DEFAMA¬
TION LEAGUE—Raises funds for combating anti-Semitic activities.
LEO N. LEVI MEMORIAL HOSPITAL—Gives care and treatment to indigent persons suffer¬
ing from rheumaitsm, sciatica and related ailments; non-sectarian.
NATIONAL FARM SCHOOL—Prepares city boys for careers as practicing farmers.
NATIONAL JEWISH HOSPITAL—Treats people suffering from pulmonary tuberculosis. Non¬
sectarian.
NATIONAL JEWISH WELFARE BOARD—Conducts special activities for Jewish men in the
armed forces; serves YMHA's and community centers.
RABBI ISAAC ELCHANAN THEOLOGICAL SEMINARY—Promotes study of Talmud; pre¬
pares students for orthodox rabbinate and trains teachers.
