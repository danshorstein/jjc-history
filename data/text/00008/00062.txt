LETTERS FROM NOTABLES
; CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES
House of Representatives
Washington, D. C.
August 10, 1945
To My Jewish Friends:
I wish to extend to you my greetings and best
wishes at this time for a happy and prosperous
new year.
I also wish to confirm my support in establish¬
ing a national home for the Jewish people in Pal¬
estine. In my estimation, the Jewish people cer¬
tainly deserve to have a national homeland in Pal¬
estine, for the wonderful part they have played in
helping to bring this war to a successful conclusion.
Secondly, they need a national home to rehabilitate
their war weary, presecuted brethren in a land that
they can call their own, and certainly they have a
legitimate claim on this land and I hope that we
are successful in prevailing upon the British Gov¬
ernment to recognize this claim. You may rest as¬
sured that I will cooperate with you in this matter
to the fullest extent.
Emory Price,
Member of Congress,
Second Congressional
District of Florida
3934 Park St., Jacksonville, Fla.
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
City of Jacksonville
Jacksonville, Fla.
August 6, 1945
To The Jewish Community of Jacksonville:
As your Mayor, it is a pleasure indeed to extend
to you my sincere greetings.
As Americans we have a heritage rich beyond
comparison—a heritage which, working together,
we shall continue to protect and foster. As citizens
of Jacksonville we have a great future ahead of us.
Your group has contributed much to our city’s
steady growth and advancement.
To all of you go my heartfelt wishes for con¬
tinued prosperity and happiness.
Cordially,
C. Frank Whitehead,
Mayor.
