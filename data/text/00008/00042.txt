Jacksonville Jewish Center
CUB SCOUTS
Cub Pack 14 (pictured above) is sponsored by
the Jacksonville Jewish Center. It is enjoying a full
year of activities, under the leadership of Cub Mas¬
ter Nathan Friedlin, assisted by Sam Cherry and a
committee of which Robert J. Gordon is chairman.
Serving with them are Harry Katz, secretary of the
committee, and Den Dads Hyman Safer, Max Mir-
kis, Louis Goldberg and Perry Safer. Chief Den
Mother is Mrs. Nathan Dwoskin.
Included in Den No. 1, of which Mrs. Nathan
Dwoskin is Den Mother, are Cubs Melvin Friedlin,
Sam Gordon, Donald Lovitz, Benjamin Dwoskin,
Harvey Joel Selber and Stanley Ruby. Den Chief
Boy Scout is Morton Harris. Mrs. Harry Katz is
Den Mother of Den No. 2. Cubs are Paul Becker,
Stanley Kantor, Harry Katz, Jr., Matthew Robins,
Bernard Nachman, Sol Safer and Norman Spitzer.
Den Chief Boy Scout is Irving Bono. Den No. 3 has
Mrs. A. Rosenkrantz as Den Mother. Cubs are My¬
ron Blattner, Joel Bressler, Bernard Fraden, Norman
Greenbaum, Harold Horovitz, William Lassk, Morris
Mirkis and Stanley Rosenkrantz. Gerald Sohn is
Den Chief Boy Scout. Den No. 4 has Mrs. L. Gold¬
berg as Den Mother. Cubs are Gary Bloom, Larry
Goldberg, Neil Presser, Charles Mizrahi, Charles
Cherry and William Isaacs. Jerry Schemer is Den
Chief Boy Scout.
The Cub Pack already has enjoyed three out¬
ings this summer. On July 11 members attended a
picnic at Jacksonville Beach, during which a pro¬
gram of games, races and a scrap book contest were
the main items of attraction. Cub Master Friedlin
took color movies of the event, to be shown at a fu¬
ture meeting. A third outing for the Cubs was
staged during the month of August.
