PESAH (Passover), the Feast of Unleavened Bread — an eight-day
celebration of the deliverance of the Jews from Egyptian slavery.
During the traditional Seder meal, on the first two nights of Pass-
over, Jewish families read the Haggadah (a ritual recitation of the
story of the Exodus. Throughout the Passover periods, foods are
selected according to special dietary laws. Intead of bread, Jews
eat matzos (unleavened bread) to recall the hasty flight from
Ecfypt, when there was no time to allow the bread to rise.
