"The intellectual tension at the Seminary
is in keeping with the passionate Jewish commit¬
ment to learning. In the Talmud, whose sixty-
three books contain the legal, historical, and
ethical writings of the ancient rabbis, it is writ¬
ten, 'An ignorant man cannot be a pious one.'
Out of the merger of learning and piety and
the focus on study for study's sake comes the
orientation of the Seminary."
