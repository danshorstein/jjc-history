Hadassah was founded in 1912 by Henrietta Szold, and
from a mere handful of dedicated American women, has grown
to a membership of over 318,000. The local chapter was formed
in 1925.
Hadassah projects are actively functioning on two far-
flung horizons. On the American scene, an educational pro¬
gram, a youth activities program, the Young Judeans and the
Junior Hadassah, Hadassah co-sponsors with Z.O.A. Camp
Judaea in Hendersonville, North Carolina. In Israel, Hadassah
encompasses Hadassah Medical Organization, Medical Cen¬
ter, Youth Aliyah, Vocational education and the Jewish Na¬
tional Fund. By helping Israel to help itself, so that it can
take its place with dignity among the freedom-loving nations
of the world, Hadassah reaffirms the Jewish faith in the
heritage of freedom.
Two groups comprise the Jacksonville Chapter: The
Senior Hadassah and the Hannah Senesch Evening group.

OFFICERS OF SENIOR HADASSAH
Almost 750 women boast membership in the local chapter,
and annually seek to raise quotas totaling nearly $17,500 in
order to meet commitments in Israel, as well as in America.
Senior Hadassah meets every Tuesday of each month
from September through May. This coming year’s calendar
offers a variety of activities with a well-rounded educational
and social program to interest every member.

This group is designed for Business and Professional women
as well as non-working women for whom evening meetings
and functions are more convenient.
Successful fund raising efforts for Hadassah’s vast medical
and humanitarian activities in many parts of the world,
coupled with congeniality at meetings, makes this an ideal
group to join.
Meetings in the Jacksonville Jewish Center are held at
8 p.m. on the second Monday of each month.

Young Judaea is a movement dedicated to developing generations of American
Jewish youth rooted by their heritage and devoted to attaining self fulfillment as
Zionists. It is sponsored by Hadassah and Z. O .A.
Some of this year’s activities were an Israeli Fair at River Garden, two regional
Conclaves, two Chug Chalutzi Conventions, two Ruach Rallies, dancing at Z. O. A.
Convention and many more things of interest. Eight Jacksonville Judaeans attended

others went to Camp Judaea in Hendersonville, N. C.
