Founded in 1917, the Jewish Family and Children’s Serv¬
ices has been assisting its clients solve family and personal
problems for the past 48 years. Last year some 300 different
families or individuals were served in one or more programs
offered hy the agency. The placement of five infants for adop¬
tion in 1964 set an all time high in this area for the agency
and spurred the search for prospective adoption applicants.
We also look for and were successful in our search for short¬
term foster homes, “big brothers” and “big sisters”. The largest
portion of our “caseload”, some 40%, came from the oldest
age groups.
Although the problems dealt with do not neatly fit
themselves into categories and often come in bunches with
ramifications in many directions, the agency’s work might be
better understood by a listing of the services offered.
1. Personal Counseling
2. Adoption Program
3. Foster Home Program
4. Assistance to the Aged

The understanding and cooperation of the Jewish com¬
munity is vital to insure the best services. The response in
terms of donations of time, clothing, and money continues to
be excellent. We need more volunteers for our Friendly Visit¬
ing Committee and quicker alerts about employment oppor-
tuities. Remember, too, that your whole-hearted support of the
LTnited Community Services and the Jewish Welfare Fund
drives make this work possible.
