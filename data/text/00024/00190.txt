B’nai B’rith today is a world-wide organization of many facets, re¬
spected and revered by all, and joined with dignity by Jews everywhere.
JVo. 800)
EDWARD MASS, President
The following are some of the many and varied
facets of B’nai B’rith:
1. Contribution to citizenship by training youth, coopera¬
tion with other groups.
2. Hillel Foundation — the means of moving the young
people on campus back to Jewish life.
3. Vocational service — assisting Jews toward economic
development.
4. B’nai B’rith Youth Organization — Founded in 1924
because of a concern over the vast number of young
Jews who were drifting away from Judaism. The leaders
of tomorrow are trained in this organization.
4. Patriotism — B’nai B’rith went into action from the
Civil War through each of the conflicts. Volunteer work¬
ers served in bringing hope and rehabilitation to the
armed forces and veterans.
6. The Anti-Defamation League, Defender of Rights, was
founded in 1913 by a brilliant lawyer. His theory of
understanding through enlightenment is the foundation
for the League’s approach to current problems. Must
men hate?
“The cycle of Brotherly Love, Benevolence and Harmony
will be never ending.”
The membership of the local A. B. Weil Lodge increased
leaps and bounds last year under the able chairmanship of
Hyman Selber with over 200 members on our rolls.
Leonard Barnett, A. D. L. Chairman, and his committee
worked to such a degree that our lodge has been recognized
by the entire district.
Victor Hubsch, the loyal and tireless financial secretary
made possible much of the success this year.
Myron Blattner, V. P. in charge of A. Z. A., counselled his
group of young men very successfully. Lionel Gaines, Emanuel
Ehrlich, Bud Goldberg, Arthur Hurwitz, Alfred Rosenzweig
and the rest of the Board all helped to make this an out¬
standing administration.
No greater reward can be received than the joy and satis¬
faction resulting from service to our brothers and sisters. By
such efforts our own B’nai B’rith and the entire community
will benefit.
ED MASS, President

This past year, under the able leadership of Aleph Godol’s Mark Hipler and
Howard Rosenblatt, Gators A.Z.A. reached new heights. Our chapter reached highest
limits and sent delegates to four conventions. Succeeding Jeff Bootzin on the North
Florida Board was the new Council Pledgemaster Elliot Borkson, newly elected Gator
Aleph Godol.
Jeff Bootzin received AZA’s highest award, Silver Shield of David. Howard Rosen¬
blatt again won the regional oratory contest and teamed with Jeff Bootzin won the
regional debate. This is the third year in a row that a Gator AZA has won the oratory
contest and have never been defeated in debate.
The chapter activities were varied and lived up to the five-fold program. Under
the religious fold, we hosted three Sabbaths, one at the Temple and two at the Jewish
Center; under cultural, we showed films of various health organizations; under com¬
munity service, we collected contributions for Muscular Dystrophy and aided in the
ALSÁC drive; in athletics, we participated in the Jewish Community Council basket¬
ball league and played Esquire Club in football and last but not least the social end,
we held at least one social a month with the girls in Gator’s BBG, the sister organi¬
zation, and conducted our first annual Stag Party May 1st at the Jewish Center
Campsite.
