Shown above are children playing tether ball at one of
four tether ball courts. In the background is the playground
equipment used by the younger set.
Shown on this page are views of the physical im¬
provements made to date at our two-year old site which
the Building Fund Committee acquired for future
expansion. The property is now primarily used for the
Center’s Day Camp and for a Family Recreation Pro¬
gram for its members which is available throughout
the year.
The operation of the Campsite and the Family
Recreation Program is under the Co-Chairmanship
of Lewis Ansbacher and Irwin Gendzier. Under their
guidance the Campsite Committee solicits family mem¬
berships and rentals for private parties, organizational
gatherings and generally strives to promote the use of
our beautiful 36 acre Campsite.
Shown to the right is a scene from a
typical Sunday when the facilities are avail¬
able to the community and their guests. The
picture shows the entire Olympic Pool with
its two diving boards and two lifeguard stands.
In the rear can be seen the circular Kiddie
Pool.
Shown at left is the shallow end of the
Olympic AAU Pool and the bathhouses in the
rear. Each bathhouse has over 200 lockers and
cubicles.
