Our Center’s Girl Scout and Brownie Troops under the
Chairmanship of Mrs. Howard Hecht, consisting of 85 girls,
had an extremely active year. Special thanks are due to the
Troop Leaders and Co-Leaders shown with their Troops below.
The Brownies were busy making tray favors for Hope
Haven Hospital, and working in a Booth for the Sisterhood’s
Hanukah Fair. The Jr. Girl Scouts collected canned goods and
foods for needy Jewish families. For World Thinking Day our
scouts presented “Dolls for Democracy”, a program designed
to create interest in Girl Scouts throughout the world. The
Cadette troop was busy with community projects such as
volunteer work for the Nalpo Children’s Home, stuffing
envelopes for the T. B. program, visiting Tallahassee while
working on their government badge, donating tray favors for
the Duval Medical Center, and sewing a patch quilt for pre-
centation at the Civic Auditorium Home Making Fair.
Our Girl Scout Banquet this past year was enthusiasti¬
cally received and brought to light the many accomplishments
of our Scouts.
