This past year was a landmark year in our Jewish
community. Through a constitutional change, the
Jacksonville Jewish Federation supplanted the
Jacksonville Jewish Community Council. The change
was made to provide a more representative format for
communal decision-making. In addition, the Federation
expanded its staff by adding a Dept, of Social Services
and hiring Susana Rothstein as its Program professional
The Federation efforts this year have significantly
changed the quality of Jewish life in our community.
The Federation will continue to provide services to
the community as in the past, and will embark in new
directions that will increase the quality of Jewish life in
Jacksonville.
The Federation will continue to conduct the Annual
Combined Jewish Appeal which raises monies for our
local agencies as well as the United Jewish Appeal and
national organizations. Our next Campaign will mark
our first ever one million dollar plus Campaign, a true
milestone in our community. The Federation has made
major increases in its commitment to both our local
Jewish Family & Children’s Services and the River
Garden Hebrew Home for the Aged. Our Russian
Resettlement program in Jacksonville has become a
model program throughout the southeast part of the
United States. The Jewish community, through the
Federation programs, has made a major effort toward
providing services in those areas where little has existed
in the past -areas such as Senior Citizen Programming,
Single Parent Family Programming, College Age
Programs, Jewish Singles in their 30’s and 40’s, etc.
The Federation provides a vehicle for service to our
community and it is the hope of the Officers and Board
that we unify our Jewish community for the betterment
of all individuals and organizations.
