What do wine and cheese parties, picnics, softball,
backjack and friendship have to do with Ha’Rayim?
Everything!
This year is going to be our most exciting ever. Several of our new members
have brought fresh ideas and enthusiasm to our group. For instance, this
year we have already had a picnic, a roller skating party, and a Luau
at the Center pool. Among the future events planned are tubing down the Ichitucknee, a camp-out,
a wine and cheese party, and to top off a fabulous year, a gala New Year’s Eve
Party.
This is just a sample of what Ra’Rayim did last year. We are a group of
Jewish couples who enjoy each other’s company. When we all get
together, we always have fun regardless of what we do.
We are not a fund raising organization. Our
goal is to provide a pleasant environment
where Jewish couples can get together and
have an enjoyable time for a reasonable
amount. Please join us for our next function.
There will be one soon!
Ha’Rayim Presidents
Bonnie and Neil Sandler

Havurat Ha-Mishpacha, a group of young Center families who have organized to plan
religious, educational and cultural experiences, have planned a very exciting year. The
year began with a Shabbat experience. The group met for afternoon Minha services and
then assembled for a tasteful Shalosh Seudot filled with singing of old and new Israeli and
Hassidic songs. The program concluded with a very beautiful and creative Havdalah and
a pleasant exchange of Shavua-tov, a good week. Any young families interested in inten¬
sifying their Jewish commitments and personal growtK through education, religious ob¬
servances and Jewish experiences please feel free to participate.
An open invitation is being extended to all young families. You may call the Center office,
Rabbi David Gaffney or Dr. Neil Abramson for more information.
