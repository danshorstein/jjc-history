ORT of Jacksonville
ORT, the Organization for Rehabilitation through Training, is an international organization which for a
century has brought freedom to world Jewry. ORT operates in 30 countries with 76,500 students learning
90 trades in 800 training units. They are people gaining an education and a future career in architectural
drawing, telecommunications, avionics, hotel management, machine design, computer technology, and
more, special programs are available for the physically handicapped, the aged, and language-training
programs for immigrants bound for English-speaking countries. Different courses for different people with
different needs and qualifications are offered.
Locally, we raise funds for the ORT program and participate in civic and Jewish community activities.
We strive to upgrade the image of vocational education in our community and to promote career education
in our school system.
Jacksonville Area Council helps support the ORT program and consists of five chapters that meet at
different times to serve the needs of all the women in the community. The Area Council is the body which
services the five ORT chapters. For membership information, contact Anita Warschoff, 737-7634.
The following are the officers of the Area Council and the five chapters and their meeting days:
Afternoon Chapter Meets 2nd Monday Afternoon Each Month
Beach Chapter Meets 1st Tuesday Evening Each Month
Beauclerc Chapter Meets 1st Friday Morning Each Month
Evening Chapter Meets 1st Tuesday Evening Each Month
Riverfront Chapter Meets 1st Tuesday Evening Each Month
