TORAH READERS
Synagogue worship is a very democratic procedure. It enables
many with sufficient knowledge to lead the service and thus magnify
the glory of God in public. For those with the greater skill to read the
Torah which varies at each reading, it adds the dimension of public
education.
A new aspect of this program is the celebration of Bar, Bat Mitzvah
anniversaries, which reinforces the skills acquired during a year of
intensive training. A rewarding scene at these occasions is the
presence of members of the family who make a special effort to
attend this meaningful celebration.
We are, therefore, most happy that a number of talented pupils
have been added to the ranks of this select group, beginning with
their Bar Mitzvahs this year. They are:

The program run at the Center may be the largest of its kind in
America. We have over three dozen people of all ages, who partic¬
ipate in the reading the year round, on festivals as well as Shabbat.
