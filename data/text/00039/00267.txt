SIMHAT TORAH CELEBRATION — SUNDAY, OCTOBER 14, 1979
CONGREGATION TO HONOR THREE LEADERS FOR OUTSTANDING SERVICE
HATAN TORAH
Judge Louis Safer
With a great deal of pride, the
Board of the Jacksonville Jewish
Center will honor Judge Louis
Safer as our Hatan Torah, the
groom of the Torah — the honor of
standing at the Torah as the last
chapter is read on Simha Torah. It
is an honor that Lou Safer truly
deserves.
Lou Safer presently serves as
Circúit Judge for the State-of Flori¬
da. As a practicing attorney for up¬
wards of 25 years Lou Safer al¬
ways had sufficient time (o share
his expertise, discernment and good
judgement for the welfare of the
Jewish Center. When the mortgage
was drawn for acquisition of our
present property, it was Lou who
served as our attorney carefully
watching out for the Center s best
interests. He also served the Jew¬
ish Center as the youngest Presi¬
dent in its history hut his dedica¬
tion and wisdom always set a fine
example of leadership for our con¬
gregation. When the Jewish Center
met on Third and Silver Strets and
two High Holiday services were
conducted at the same time our
honoree helped to conduct those
services. For many years Lou has
been serving as Chairman of the
Nominating Committee. Lou has
even filled in as a lay rabbi
when the Center was without pro¬
fessional rabbinic leadership.
Equal to the dedication that Lou
has shown at the Jewish Center
and community, he possesses per¬
sonal qualities of virtue and char¬
acter worthy of emulation. Always
a gentleman, thoughtful, considerate
and soft spoken, his easy going na¬
ture has made him a person worthy
of admiration.
Lou has set a fine example for his
family. His wife Marilyn, always
one of Lou’s greatest fans, shares
his commitments and dedication to
Jewish life.
His children Eliot, Cynthia Ba¬
ron, Deena and Shana, all look up
to their father and appreciate what
he has done for them and for his
community. Eliot Safer, now a
member of the Board of Directors
of the Jewish Center, is active in a
number of areas of congregational
concern.
We are pleased to honor Lou
Safer, a “man of conscience” whose
high ideals have truly made him a
leader of the community, and a
most worthy man to read the last
reading of the Torah. His quiet
personality and subtle humor have
earned him the love and respect of
his fellow congregants.

HATAN BERESHIT
Sheldon Gendzier
The Center Board is proud to
announce that the honor of Hatan
Bereshit — the honor of being at
the Torah at the time when the
congregation begins the reading of
Genesis, will be extended to Shel¬
don Gendzier. A tireless and com¬
mitted leader at the Jewish Center
and in the community at large,
Sheldon together with his wife
Horty and family have become so
closely identified with everything
good at the Jewish Center that this
honor is natural and appropriate.
Our Hatan Bereshit has served
most especially as immediate past
President of the Jewish Center. He
has however been intimately invol¬
ved with so many facets of our con¬
gregational life as their efforts on
our behalf are almost legendary.
An active member of the Educa¬
tion Committee for many years, an
important participant in the devel¬
opment of the Solomon Schechter
Day School, he still serves as Chair¬
man of the high school sub-com¬
mittee of the Education Committee.
Our Hatan Bereshit is not only
adept with gavel in hand, he is as
competent with a pencil and chalk
in hand sitting in front of a c’ass-
room, of bringing his own inimi¬
table style to a modern under¬
standing of Jewish history, Shel¬
don’s favorite passion. His willing¬
ness to volunteer is above and be¬
yond the call of duty and he has
brought the subject of Jewish his¬
tory to the attention of his stu¬
dents, young and old alike. Al¬
though it is true that a large family
always makes one sensitive to the
quality of Jewish education, Shel¬
don still continues to serve even
when many of his family members
are achieving their own new suc¬
cesses in business and at the uni¬
versity.
The concerns of our Hatan Bere¬
shit go beyond local concerns. Shel¬
don is a committed Conservative
Jew and, taking the example from
his father, our beloved Harry Gend¬
zier who was a vice-president of
the United Synagogue, Sheldon is
equally concerned with the affairs
of Conservative Judaism. A long
standing member of the Board of
what is now the Jewish Federation,
his interests are community wide.

A WOMAN OF VALOR
Mrs. Jack Shorstein
In a fitting ceremony on the
morning of Simhat Torah the Jack-
sonvile Jewish Center’s “Woman
of Valor” award will be bestowed
upon Sylvia Shorstein. The religi¬
ous ceremony which will bring
Sylvia and her husband Jack to
our pulpit will give expression to
the Center’s recognition of Sylvia’s
many talents and activities on be¬
half of our congregation. For over
20 years Sylvia has been actively
involved in many areas of our
synagogue programs and activities.
Her first commitment began with
Sisterhood. Through the years she
has served as fund raising Vice-
President, Program Chairman and
Torah Fund Chairman. For many
years Sylvia has been an integral
part of the Center Board and ac¬
tive in its concerns. She has al¬
ways been generous with her time
and talents. Her warm and friend¬
ly disposition has been a great asset
to the Center where she served on
the synagogue’s Membership Com¬
mittee. She recently co-chaired the
Raffle Roast with Linda DuBow
and is presently actively involved
in the Interior Design Committee
where her excellent taste and aes¬
thetic sense are used to greatest
advantage. The many beautiful
Judaic items offered in our Sister¬
hood Gift Shop are examples of
Sylvia’s “sense of the beauty.”
By initiative and temperment
Sylvia’s concerns are broad and
general. She serves on the Gover¬
nor’s Commisison on the Status of
Women and is on the Board of
Directors of the Urban Library
Council. Her varied concerns and
interests also make her indispens¬
able to the life of Hadassah and
National Council of Jewish Wo¬
men.
As a wife and mother she is an
exemplar of a “Woman of Valor.”
She was of great help to her hus¬
band Jack over the many years of
his involvement in congregational
leadership and was most helpful
during the time that he served as
President of the Jewish Center.
Notwithstanding her many activi¬
ties, Sylvia created a fitting and
Jewishly warm home environment
in which she raised three fine sons,
Mark, Michael and Neal, who have
made their own impact inthe life of
our congregation, who are a source
of Nahas for her and who are in
turn very proud of their mother’s
accomplishments.
The synagogue’s “Woman of
Valor” award is our way of paying
tribute to a central figure in the
life of the Jewish Center.
