Let me share with you some encouraging and noteworthy facts
concerning the current school year. First and foremost, we have been
blessed with a major increase in pupil enrollment, which includes two year
olds through the sixth grade. We are equally fortunate to have on our staff
sixteen very fine teachers, each of whom is contributing in an outstanding
manner to the development and education of our children.
At this point in time, we can all be very optimistic concerning the viability
and future prospects of the Day School.
As we continue to grow, however, it is important to keep in sharp focus
the reasons that led to the establishment of the Day School, for those are the
very same factors that sustain it today. Only in a day school can in-depth
Jewish education be imparted in an integrated and natural manner. In this
setting, identity and commitment to Jewish life can be instilled.
A fine general education can be assured through the efforts of talented
and dedicated and typically small class sizes which allow for individual needs.
However, it is the substantial Jewish content — basic texts, traditions,
observances, values, and Hebrew language that makes the day school as an
institution unique.
During the past year, an Extended Care Program (12:00-3:00 care for 3
year olds - 5 year olds) was started and proved very successful. It gave work¬
ing parents aid as well as it gave many other mothers a “Mother’s Afternoon
Out.”
We are very proud of the many accomplishments that are taking place
within the walls of our Solomon Schechter Day School. Marilyn Sandler
