RAFT TRIP
Troop 14, in its 59th year of existence explored new frontiers this
year. The Troop, one of the oldest in North Florida, completed the rugged,
six day Suwannee River Raft trip for the first time in history. Appreciation
goes to the Berman Brothers, Inc. for their assistance in the design and
construction of the raft.
Boys from Troop 14 also completed the lengthy, historic St. Augustine
Trail Hike for the national award.
Highlights of this past year included: the annual Ross Allen Fall
Encampment at which Troop 14 captured one of four blue ribbons awarded
all North Florida troops; Scout Sabbath, featuring guest speaker, Harold
Clark, Circuit Judge; the annual Blue and Gold Banquet; the District
Camporee and various campouts.
Scouts learned what it is like to be behind bars when they toured
the Duval County Jail as guests of the Jacksonville Sheriff’s Department.
Service projects included presentatiion of a magic show at River
Garden Hebrew Home for the Aged, and maintenance and repairs for
the residents of Hubbard House for Battered and Abused Women. Both
projects were conducted under the direction of Irving Sucholeiki.
