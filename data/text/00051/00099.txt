Keeper of the “Fifth Commandment”
“Honor thy father and mother
that thy days may be long... ”
It was this commandment which inspired the foun¬
ding of River Garden Hebrew Home in 1946. It was
this commandment which nourished the love and
care that over the years has made River Garden a
nationally recognized model of excellence in its field.
It is the commandment which underlies the tradi¬
tion of meeting 100% of local Jewish community
needs and of serving rich and poor alike with ex¬
cellence and caring.
River Garden Hebrew Home/Wolf son Health and
Aging Center is cm a 33 acre campus in Mandarin
near the intersection of 1-295 and Old St. Augustine
Road. The design of the Traditional Care, Sheltered
Care and Short Term Rehabilitation pavilions
recognizes that people with medical needs do not
have to live in medical settings. River Garden also
offers an Adult Day Program enabling members of
the community to access the unique facilities and ser¬
vices the Home offers. The River Garden Home/
Wolfson Health and Aging Center will be a part of
a larger community which will include: apartments,
a town center, a general store, a fitness center and
a campus club.
Overall, River Garden is today and tomorrow a
community where people live, love, and care; a com¬
munity of many life styles and of rich Jewish con¬
tent, and a community which every day strives to
live up to the mandate of the Fifth Commandment.

The handing down of information, beliefs, prac¬
tices, and attitudes from generation to generation by
mouth or example. .. this is tradition! This had tru¬
ly been the Auxiliary’s association with River Garden
Hebrew Home/Wolfson Health and Aging Center
since its earliest beginnings. It is an understanding
that we all need to give of ourselves in order to bring
pleasure to others.
River Garden Auxiliary is an organization whose
purpose it is to provide physical, emotional, and
financial support to the Home by working together
with the staff and residents. It is our desire to
enhance and enrich the daily life of each and every
individual who has come to live in the River Garden
community — a place where people live and love,
care and share. We do this by fundraising and
funraising!
When River Garden Auxiliary — formerly known
as the Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society — was
formed in 1922, this group took “the aged to their
hearts ... and made a home for them.” In 1992 the
same goal is at hand: To bring to the residents those
precious extras that add warmth and delight to their
lives.
“Honor thy father and mother that thy days may
be long ...” As the Keeper of the 5th Command¬
ment, this is our ongoing commitment!
