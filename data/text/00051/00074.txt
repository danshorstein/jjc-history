Isabel J. Balotin
I’m a showoff, I admit it.
Anytime the opportunity arises I give someone a tour
of our building so I can brag about our wonderful facili¬
ty. Even members who are planning a simcha are thrill¬
ed when I show them the many rooms they can use for
their joyous events. Some of them had no idea that we
have so much room.
We’re so lucky.
When talking to a prospective family, I’m quick to tell
them that our school, youth group and camp
enrollments are at record numbers. Thanks to our
dedicated volunteers and professional staffs for mak¬
ing these programs so successful, and to our members
for supporting the programs.
We’re so lucky.
All you have to do is give me an opening and I will
tell you about the many programs, religious services and
celebrations we offer for adults and children. Just enter
the sanctuary on Purim or Simhat Torah Eve and you’ll
see hundreds of happy children and their parents en¬
joying the holidays.
We’re so lucky.
When showing the sanctuary, the newcomers marvel
at how the room can be extended to seat 1800 people.
How lucky we are that we can seat all our members and
guests without compromising the beauty and sense of
the holidays. And what a wonderful place to celebrate
a lifecycle event!
We’re so lucky.
Yes, were lucky for many things. Mostly, we’re lucky
because our lay leadership had foresight to design a
building that would meet the needs of this generation
and the generations to follow. I’ll bet there are places
you have never been in this building. Come by one day
and let me give you a tour of our synagogue.
Have a happy, healthy and lucky New Year,
