“Good Shabbos,” “Shabbat Shalom,” “Good to See
You.” Whatever the greeting may be our Shabbat
Greeters have added another dimension to our “personal
touch” program at the Center.
Every Saturday morning, two volunteers greet Shab¬
bat worshippers in the Cohen/DuBow lobby. Their
friendly smiles and greetings have truly enhanced the
warm spirit associated with Shabbat. Our thanks to
these very special people.

Ted Gross, Coordinator
“No more teachers, no more books,” was the Times-
Union front page heading for the last day of school. Com¬
pare with the second prayer of the evening service. How
were we shown God’s love? — by being instilled with a love
of enduring learning. For our goals, the most difficult task
is to change behavior patterns, like coming to school on
Shabbat, davening with seriousness (Kavanah), challeng¬
ing what is new.
This year we initiated a special program on November
23, 1991. The entire service, from Preliminary to closing,
included Torah portions, was conducted by young post Bar
Mitzvahs. This arrangement is very promising for the future.
We were also able to display special talents of Bar Mitz-
vah candidates, like Mathew Safer, who could chant all the
portions, and Bat Mitzvahs who could chant from the Navi
Scroll. Pupils who maintain these skills learn basic concepts
of Judaism and enhance their association with our love for
learning. I have also found it an easy transfer to develop
new leaders of other parts of the services.
We are thankful to the many members of our team listed
in the previous Yearbook and new recruits who make
Torah Reading part of the joy of learning.
