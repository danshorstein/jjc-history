The Ladies’ Hebrew Sheltering Aid & Home for the Aged was Organized in
1923 for the purpose of conducting a HACHNOSAS ORCHIM, giving shelter and
aid to transients, supplying them with Kosher food and keeping them in a Jewish
environment; giving TZDOKOH, relief to the needy and unfortunate locally; op¬
erating a GEMILES' CHESED, a Free Loan Fund, enabling many families to con¬

Rabbi Morris D. Margolis of the Jack- -

chairman of the Educational Committee,
at a conference of orthodox and

January in Tampa.
The main purpose of this committee
was to promote cultural and religious

Rabbi Margolis, accordingly, made reg¬

succeeded in arousing considerable in¬

March 19, a very successful Purim cele¬
bration was held in the G'ainsville syna¬
gogue with aboutlOO students attending.
A committee of the Center officials in¬
cluding Mr. Max Rubin, Mr. J. Hackel,

The whole Jewish community of Gainesf-
ville and vicinity was present, and the
local women were hostesses to the stu¬
dents and guests at a reception following
the Purim Service.
Another outstanding event was the
Maimonides celebration held on the uni¬
versity campus, April 29. The program
opened with a piano rendition of He¬
brew melodies, and a number of Yiddish
folk songs were given by Madame Berte
Long, contralto. The speakers were Pro¬
fessor James D. Glunt, of the History
Department, who gave an excellent ac¬
count of Maimonides and his historical
background, and Rabbi Margolis, who
pointed out the spiritual implications of
Maimonides’ teachings to the youth of
today. A large number of students atten¬
ded.
Rabbi Margolis received complete co¬
operation in his work from the presi¬
dents of the two Jewish fraternities, Mr.
Philip S'elber of the T. E. P., and Mr.
Al Kassel of the Ph. B. D.

made through the formation of a HiUel
