1) Hamisha Asar B’Shebat (fifteenth of Shebat) is the Jewish Arbor Day, also
called the New? Year of the trees. In Palestine school children use the day
for planting thousands of trees each year. In the Diaspora it is used as an
ocasion for stressing the significance of the upbuilding of Palestine.
2) Shabbat Shekalim. During Temple times the call for the payment of the poll
tax of half a shekel by every full-grown Israelite was announced on the first
day of the eleventh month. (Adar). As a reminder of this duty the passage from
Exodus xxx, 11-16, was read on the Sabbath preceding.
