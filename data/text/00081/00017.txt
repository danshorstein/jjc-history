TROOP 14, BOY SCOUTS OF AMERICA
Troop 14 is sponsored by the Jacksonville Jewish Center, under the capable
leadership of Abe Diamond and the troop committee. The purpose of the troop
is to develop in its members a sense of loyalty to the high ideals expressed by the
S'cout laws, to build good citizenship, and to instill in the boys reverence for their
religion and the traditions of their people. , . _ x _
During the year, the troop offered its services to organizations affiliated with
the Center at various social functions. The highlight of the season was the enter¬
tainment presented by the troop at the April meeting of the scoutmasters of the
Greater Jacksonville Council, held in the Jacksonville Jewish Center Auditorium.
The troop committee consists of Mr. Abe Roth stein, Mr. David Moscovitz, Dr.
S’am Witten, Alfred Stein, Isadore Moscovitz, Rabbi Morris D. Margolis, and Abe
Diamond, scoutmaster.

GIRL SCOUTS—TROOP 2
Girl Scouts, Troop 2, which has suspended activities for a number of years, was
recently re-organized under the leadership of Miss Edith Wolf son and, Miss Ruth
Moed, assisted by Roslyn Magezis, with a membership of eighteen girls, all of them
having passed the tenderfoot test.
Many girls have expressed a desire to join the troop, and it is hoped that after
the Holidays, Troop 2 will resume its acivities with a memberhip of about thirty-
two scouts.
A troop committee will be formed early in the fall representing the Jacksonville
Jewish Center under whose auspices the troop will operate. A mother’s committee
will also be formed to assist in carrying out many of the ambitious plans for the
coming season,
