High on the list of prescribed duties is the
injunction to make provision for the education of the
young. We will fully discharge this obligation with a
complex of classrooms that will encompass facilities to
handle all facets of education from “early education”,
day school and religious school, to adult studies. Staffed
by a faculty of carefully selected and competent teach¬
ers will be able to offer the best in religious and

secular education.
The physical arrangement of the classrooms em¬
bodies the newest trend. It will consist of a series of
pods. Each grade group will contain three separate
classrooms which can be combined to form one “open
classroom” in which different educational activities can
be carried on simultaneously.

The character development of youth has always
been a prime concern of the Jewish parent. The unrest
among young people today and their search for real
meaning and significance makes the problem more
acute and the solution even more challenging. What
is needed is the possibility of participating in creative
activities of social significance ... of meeting in fellow¬
ship with other Jewish youth for social, emotional and
intellectual experiences.
The Youth Wing, containing a Youth Lounge,
Game Room, Meeting Rooms and Arts and Crafts
facilities will provide the necessary physical accommo¬
dations. Here, under the guidance of trained and
understanding group leaders, our United Synagogue
Youth, Leaders Training Fellowship, Boy Scouts and
our many other youth groups will be able to achieve
new heights in the effectiveness of their programs.
