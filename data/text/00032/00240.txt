Cub Scouting, as an introduction to the Boy
Scouts, continues to be one of the outstanding youth
programs offered at the Center. Boys between the
ages of 8 and 10 find many new and challenging
experiences in Cubbing.
The Cubs in each Den become a close unit as
they work together to develop new hobbies, take field
trips, explore nature and work on craft projects and
skits. The annual Rocket and Pinewood Derbies give
Gene Crystal, Clara Mann, Susie Cherry,
Carol Schneider, Marlene Bossen, Toby Ringel,
Judy Mizrahi and Sidney Engelhardt
the boys the opportunity to design their own rockets
and cars and enter them in competitive racing.
In honor of Boy Scout Week, the Cubs partici¬
pate in the late Friday Sabbath Service and the
annual Blue and Gold Banquet. To close a most
successful year, all members of Pack 14 and their
families gathered at the Center Southside Site for
an evening of swimming, sports, games and cooking
over an open fire. The highlight of the event was
the presentation of the Larry Hacker Cub Scout
Award to the Cub scoring the highest in attendance at
Den and Pack Meetings, achievement and good will.
The recipient for 1973 was Gary Kupfer. An individual
plaque was presented to Gary and two perpetual
plaques will be on display at the Center.
The Shofar Award to recognize outstanding ser¬
vice by adults in the promotion of Scouting among
Jewish boys was presented to Commander Sidney
Engelhardt, Cubmaster of Pack 14, in recognition of
sixteen years devoted service to the Cub scout program.
