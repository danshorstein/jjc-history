Like a single letter in an alphabet
human life can be meaningless —
or it can be part of a greater meaning.
And we do not want to be meaningless.
How then do we find meaning?
Is it found in things . . .
power, money, the talent of an artist or scientist ?
If so, we will always find someone with
more power, more money, more talent.
Shall we therefore say:
"His life has more meaning than mine."
If we do, we have forgotten the profound
democracy of creation
in which the deepest delights, the most enduring meaning of life
comes from those things all of us are given.
WE HAVE BEEN GIVEN LIFE ITSELF
mind, body, our senses and spirit
the image of God within us.
WE HAVE BEEN GIVEN THE TALENT TO LOVE
the gift of sharing our souls with others
of belonging to them, living on in them.
WE HAVE BEEN GIVEN THE TALENT TO SERVE
the delight which comes in an act of Service itself
without seeking reward.
Consider:
The gift of life: Have you ever discovered
the wonder of the sky?
the rythm of your heart.
Have you seen night pass into day
or a smile change the contours of a face?
The gift of love: Has a child ever slipped
a hand into yours because he wanted to walk with you?
The gift of service: Have you ever found tears in your eyes because
someone clutched your hand and said "thank you"
for an act of consideration?
Why do these things delight us?
It is because they enlarge our life,
They unite us with others
They are endless.
These delights are the birthright and
inheritance of each of us.
They are so close to us we often fail to see them
yet they are given to us freely and we are always free
to reacn for them.
When we do
we find the way to the fullest meaning
in our life.
And to a oneness with God
who endows all with these gifts
which endure forever.
"WE THANK YOU GOD ... FOR THE GIFT OF LIFE"
(From the Prayer Book)
