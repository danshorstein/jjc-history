We welcome Sandy Kuttler, Youth Studies and
Activities Coordinator.
The Jewish Center Judaic Studies Program for
5734 promises to be a most different one with many
ingredients for an exciting year. A new position of
Youth Studies and Activities Coordinator has been
created to meet this need. Mr. Sanford Kuttler, from
Miami, has been engaged to restructure the nature of
our educational programming.
As Scholar in Residence for the Southeastern
Region of United Synagogue Youth, and in his work
for the National Ramah Commission, Mr. Kuttler
has been involved with programming “Living Judaism”.
Sandy believes strongly that concepts are best acquired
when they are experienced.
The Melton Institute of the Jewish Theological
Seminary stresses the importance of challenging the
basic tenets of Judaism and Mr. Kuttler’s training in
this methodology will lend a new dimension to our
school. “Judaism has remained strong and enduring
due to the questions confronting it; for out of this
confrontation comes better understanding”, he states.
The Teachers’ Institute of the Seminary provided
the additional background that did not come to Mr.
Kuttler after ten years of Hebrew Day Schools. He is
a Baal Korai, Torah Reader, and has studied in depth
the areas of Bible and Tefilah.
Sandy Kuttler places great importance on the
belief that all of life is based on values. The values
accepted by the individual will determine most greatly
the results of his life. He resides at the Gloucester
Apartments and his hobbies include boating, cycling
and Israeli dancing. His duties at the Center wifi
involve directing the U.S.Y. and Kadima activities
as well as the afternoon and Sunday studies program.
