The Center Day Camp, founded in 1946 by the
Jacksonville Jewish Center, has continued its growth
each year and broadened its scope of activities to in¬
clude the finest programming and facilities of any
Day Camp in this area.
With emphasis on a qualified professional staff,
many diversified activities are made available to camp¬
ers between the ages of 3 to 15 years. The camp sea¬
son included three three-week sessions. Each session
incorporated Judaic awareness and Judaic living
through nature, play and sports. Every session em¬
phasized a particular phase of our excellent water
sports and athletic program, plus interesting trips away
from camp.
Arts and crafts projects were designed to help
each child develop his own creative ability. Some of
the most popular crafts were fruit baskets and candy
dishes made from phonograph records, Mexican wall
hangings, wind chimes and vases cut from glass bot¬
tles. Emphasis was placed on natural materials found
on the site such as bamboo, pine cones, branches,
leaves, rocks, etc.
The Camp continues to offer an excellent swim¬
ming program where a camper may advance from be¬
ginners through Junior Life Saving. The program also
included free swim periods each afternoon.

Facilities include many acres of wooded area for
primitive camping and nature trails, an Olympic swim¬
ming pool, a well-equipped playground, softball dia¬
mond, basketball court and areas for all active sports.
