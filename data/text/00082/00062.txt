MIKETZ Rosh Chodesh Tebeth
1) Hanukkah (Dedication Days) celebrates the re-dedication of the Temple
by the victorious Maccabees (167 B. C. E.) after their struggles against the Syrian
King Antiochus who, with the aid of the Jewish apostates, had attempted to
suppress the religion of the Jews. For eight days lights were kindled every evening
in commemoration of their victory for religious liberty.

RAYER cBOOK FUND
The Prayer Book Fund, established
two years ago for the purpose of
securing funds with which to replace
the old prayer books used in the syn¬
agogue service, has continued to meet
with much success. During the past
year a total of 102 prayer books were
donated, consisting of fifty-two to be
used in the daily minyon and the
fifty Sabbath prayer books.
The donors and those in whose
memory they were given follow:
Mr. Sam Smith, in memory of his
son, Helman.
Mr. B. Setzer, in memory of his
father, Zussman.
Mrs. Marx Baker, in memory of
her father and mother, Mr. and Mrs.
Elias H. Pilton.
Mrs. Joe Becker, in memory of her
father and mother, Mr. and Mrs.
Elias H. Pelton.
The Center invites further contri¬
butions to this useful fund. The
names of the donors and of those in
whose memory the books are given
are inscribed on the inside cover.
