As the local chapter of the international order of B’Nai Brith takes
stock of its activities of the past year, it is with a feeling of justification that
it looks forward to the future with sincere expectations for a continuation of its
splendid accomplishments of the preceding year,
B’Nai Brith, which was founded in 1843, has grown in nine decades to a
network of more than 400 local lodges throughout the United States and Canada.
In serving both Jewry and humanity in general, B’Nai Brith has long been
known for its work in such fields as philanthropy, defense of Jewish rights,
civic cooperation, social service, the spreading of good will, Americanization,
emergency relief, Hillel Foundations at Universities, and the formation of the
Anti-Defamation League.
The highlight of the year’s activities was the banquet tendered in the
Roosevelt Hotel on January 24th in honor of the Hon. Alfred M. Cohen, president
of the Order. Mr. Cohen addressed the gathering on the history of the organiza¬
tion, stressing the importance of the work that B’Nai Brith is today doing,
A number of B’Nai Brith members from St. Augustine, Daytona, Gainesville, and
Orlando attended the meeting.
Other highlights of the year were the visit of Mr. Hyman S. Jacobs and
Mr. Maier Triest, president and secretary respectively of District Five; the
monthly luncheons which were addressed by Mr. Joseph Wilensky, Nathan
Roberts, and Harold Cohn; and the installation of a local A. Z. A. chapter.
Three members of the local group, Mr. Abe Rothstein, Mr. Max Moss,
and the late A. B. Weil, attended a state-wide conference in Orlando, at which
time plans were formulated for the founding of a Hillel Foundation at the
University of Florida.
The chapter was very unfortunate during the past year in losing two of
its most active members through death: Adolph B. Weil, its secretary at the
time, and Joe Jacobson. In recognition of the invaluable services rendered by
Mr. Weil to the order and as a lasting tribute to his memory, the name of the
local chapter was changed to the Adolph B. Weil Lodge No. 800.
