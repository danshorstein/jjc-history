Although just organized during the past year, the Young Judean Council
has already demonstrated its value as a clearing house for all of the Young
Judean activities of the city. The local council was organized in November, 1936.
Various problems confronting the clubs, concerning individual club activities,
or the planning of general cultural programs, are all discussed by the Council
in an attempt to improve the work of the Young Judean groups. The Council
is also interested in the formation of a number of new clubs in Jacksonville.
The group is already laying plans to secure for Jacksonville the 1939
Southern Regional Convention, and with the cooperation of the local community,
the undertaking should meet with success. Also on the program for the coming
year is a musical, which will draw its talent from the individual groups.
The council is composed of the leader and two representatives from each
of the three Young Judean clubs.
