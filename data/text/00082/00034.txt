The Rothschild Young Judean Club, a group of boys and girls between
the ages of 15 and 18 years of age, under the leadership of Miss Edith Weiss,
has completed another year of outstanding work.
Its program for the past year consisted of the study of current Jewish
problems with special emphasis on the events taking place in Palestine with
their reference to the Jewish Youth; and the staging of plays and cultural
programs. During the month of April, which was designated as Young Judea
Week, the club was honored at a special Friday night service at the Center,
with its members participating in the services.
From time to time, the organization has contributed to the Boy Scout
Movement in Palestine, and to the Jewish National Fund. Late in September
the Club will present a dramatic program consisting of scenes from current
well-known Broadway plays, to be given at the Center. The society plans to
enter the National Judea Play Contest, by presenting a series of short plays
in November. The group issues a newspaper, the “R. Y. J. C. Journal”, which
contains club news, special features, editorials, and comments on club members.
The chief goal for the R. Y. J. Club during the coming year is to obtain
the 1939 Southern Regional Convention in Jacksonville.

This club, which is the youngest in
age of the three local Young Judean
Clubs, is composed of girls between the
ages of eleven and thirteen. During
the past year the club sponsored several
social affairs; and to secure funds with
which to register with the National or¬
ganization, sponsored a raffle which was
highly successful.
The club meets every two weeks in
the homes of various members, at which
times cultural programs, consisting of
the reading of Judean books and papers,
discussion of current events, and similar
topics, take place.
A reorganization of the club took
place in November, at which time Miss
Ruth Davis became the sponsor. A full
program of activity has been planned
for the coming year.

The Ivriah (“Little Women”) Young
Judean Club is quite appropriately
named, being composed, as it is, of young
girls between the ages of thirteen and
fifteen. The club is under the leader¬
ship of Miss May Goldie Setzer, who
has been its sponsor since the organiza¬
tion of the club in November of the
past year.
Since the time of its founding, the
club has been completing its organiza¬
tion set-up, and is fully prepared for a
year of well-rounded activity.
During the past year, the club has
enjoyed a number of excellently prepared
cultural programs, dealing with such
topics as controversial questions of the
day and current events.
