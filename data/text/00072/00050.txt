CANTOR’S TRIP TO GERMANY
In the summer of 2012, nine members of our congregation joined Hazzan Holzer and 70 other cantors and
some 200 congregants from around the globe in a special Cantors Assembly mission to Germany. The itinerary
blended sightseeing of two main locations, Berlin and Munich, with daily lectures by Professor Stephen Berk
and nightly concerts by members of the Cantors Assembly. The group visited places of Jewish interest and
interacted with political figures and local leaders. Highlights of the trip included a Cantorial and Interfaith
concert at the famed Berliner Cathedral Dome, the largest protestant church in Europe, and a moving ceremony
at Olympic Park, honoring the memory of the Israeli athletes that were assassinated during
the 1972 Olympic Games. There was also a chance on Shabbat morning to have a service co-led by 40+ cantors,
including our very own Hazzan Holzer. Judaism is alive and well in Germany. Center participants included:
Mike and Marilyn Mass, Dan and Cindy Wohl, Ed and Judy Mizrahi, Lenny, Lynn and Elana Maiman.
