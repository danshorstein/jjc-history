MAKOM, the place to be for Jewish teens,
had another fantastic year. We began our year with
a great kickoff event by seeing the Jewish reggae
star Matisyahu live in concert. It was an energized
beginning to a very successful year. Another
highlight was the work of our Siyyum class. At the
Jacksonville Jewish Center, Siyyum is the name
of our twelfth grade program, which serves as a
capstone to enrollment in the MAKOM Hebrew
High School of the Galinsky Academy. The high school seniors who participate
in the program research and design a project of social action to address needs
within the larger Jacksonville community. Through informal study and dialogue,
Siyyum fosters leadership skills among young adults and strengthens their Jewish
identity as well as their connection with the ethical imperatives of our tradition.
This year’s Siyyum class voted to learn more about the challenges faced by
refugees granted political asylum in the United States. To that end we partnered
with the Jacksonville affiliate of World Relief. Here’s a little information about
our local World Relief chapter and the work they do for our community.
World Relief Jacksonville: World Relief Jacksonville’s mission is to
empower local houses of worship to serve the most vulnerable. Jacksonville received 440 refugees from
Burma, Bhutan, Cuba, Iraq and other parts of the world in 2011, and 430 in 2012. World Relief helps refugees
by providing for their initial housing needs, offering English classes, arranging initial medical care and
immunizations, partnering with community volunteers and houses of worship in assisting them with orientation
to American culture.
