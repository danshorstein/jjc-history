GIRL SCOUT TROOPS
Sponsored by Center Sisterhood
A very successful year with renewed interest in Girl
Scouting came to a close at the mother-daughter luncheon
on June 7th. Rabbi J ofield opened with prayer and
Girl Scouts served under the direction of Mrs. R. L. Bloch
and her committee, assisted in the kitchen and decorated
tables.
Years Activities: Picnics, cook-outs, trips to the zoo,
parties, singing, games and folk dances; December Han-
ukah party for New American children and their parents ;
February “Thinking Day’ programs; Girl Scout cookie
sale in March.
Projects for Mother’s Day Troop 197 made aprons
for their mothers. Troop 199 made and painted flower
pots and planted cuttings. Spatter-painted greeting
cards were made by Troop 29 and Troop 35 entertained
their mothers with a tea party. All troops helped the
Visiting Nurses’ Association.
Luncheon Guests: Mrs. Albert S. Lawton, commis¬
sioner of the Duval County Girl Scout Council, Miss
Bonnie Beth Wimpee, executive secretary, Mrs. Harry
Jaffe, president of the Sisterhood.
