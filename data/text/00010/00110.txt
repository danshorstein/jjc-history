Another year for our history book has passed and
we are very proud of it as it was one of our most success¬
ful years. Under the capable leadership of our advisor,
David Weinstein, the chapter participated fully in its
Five fold and full” program consisting of Community Ser¬
vice, Religious, Cultural, Athletics and Social.
The community service program was one of the most
outstanding in years. We undertook the mailing out of
the Center Bulletins; we participated in the United Jew¬
ish Campaign; we undertook the upkeep of the grounds
of the Community Council. The annual A. Z. A. ser¬
vice was held in February at which time Stanley Rosen¬
kranz delivered the main speech entitled Jacksonville A.
Z. A., Its Past, Present and Future.” As our cultural
program Joel Selber delivered talks on the Jewish holi¬
days and festivals as they occur. Leigh Culley spoke to
the chapter on Civic Improvement in the Community.
Ping pong, softball, and basketball provided the basis of
our athletic activity for the year. “A social a month
Stanley Rosenkranz
was the motto of the social committee during the year.
Und er the chairmanship of our sweetheart, Miss Barbara
Witten, the committee achieved this goal.
At the first annual Parents Day Dinner the new
officers were presented to the chapter. Mickey Block,
outgoing president, received a token of appreciation for
his hard and loyal work to the chapter from the incoming
president, Stanley Rosenkranz.
At the annual District Five convention held in Hen-
dersonville, N. C., Stanley Rosenkranz was elected Aleph
Maskir.
In closing, we wish to extend our sincerest gratitude
to everyone who has helped to make this a memorable
year for us.
