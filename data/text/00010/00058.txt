MEMORIAL PARKS
Several years ago the Center realized that additional
cemetery facilities were needed and a plot of ground was
purchased. During the past year the soundness of that
decision became apparent. On a number of occasions
interments took place in the new Memorial Park acquired
on 44th Street East of Main Street. Our original Me¬
morial Park located on Winona Drive and forming part
of the Evergreen Cemeteries, has only a few scattered
spaces left. No more family plots can be assigned there.
M ax Rub in
We are indebted to our Cemetery Committee for its
diligence in supervising road construction and extensive
landscaping of our new Memorial Park. This they did
in addition to their supervision of the original Memorial
Park and being on call for all emergencies arising within
the Center family. In the past year as in former years,
the Committee has taken scrupulous care of indigent
cases in time of need. All Center members must feel
grateful to the Committee for its indispensible service.
