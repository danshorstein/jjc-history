emphasizing the significance of the month of Nisan in which

Shabbat Hagadol. The Sabbath which precedes the great
Festival of Passover derives its name from the Haftorah which
contains a reference to the “Great Day of the Lord."
Pesab (Passover) celebrates Israel's deliverance from Egyp¬
tian bondage. The festival lasts eight days during which
Matzot are eaten in the place of leavened bread. The cele¬
bration of the Passover with the beautiful ceremony of the
Seder on the first and second evenings has fostered by Jewish
people the ideals of liberty and freedom.
