BOY SCOUT TROOP 14
Troop 14 continued to carry on the tradition of Jewish scouting in a very successful 98th year for the Troop. Service
projects for the year included participation in the North Florida Council Special Needs Camporee helping children
and their families camp, River Keeper Cleanup Day, and making a butterfly garden for Mandarin Community Club.
Camping highlights included a backpacking weekend at Bayard Conservation Area and a visit to the perennial favorite
St. Marys River Fish Camp. Michael Ackerman became the 84th Eagle Scout from Troop 14. His Eagle Scout project
was to design and build a chicken coop for the Catty Shack Ranch Wildlife Sanctuary. Much appreciation goes to
the Fleet Family Fund for continued support of scouting programming throughout the year. Scouting is open to all
boys from 1st grade until 18 years of age. For more information, contact Scoutmaster Allan Andrew at aandrew@
alum.mit.edu or Cubmaster Brian Bell at b.e.belll978@gmail.com
