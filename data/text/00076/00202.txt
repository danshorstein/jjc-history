HEBREW HIGH SCHOOL
Rabbi Howard Tilman
Chair
Lisa Ross
Hebrew High continued its momentum from last year, bringing it to new and
exciting places, encouraging learning and growth to its students. Switching to
Sunday mornings opened up many new opportunities including field trips to
places like the Museum of Science and History (MOSH), the Olive My Pickle
plant, and the University of Central Florida. In different units throughout the
year we challenged our teens to think about Jewish identity and practice, and its
influence on their daily lives. Together with Rabbi Tilman and guest teachers,
we learned about Israeli history and music, visited with Jewish communities
around the world, discussed how Jewish culture is expressed in TV and music,
and experienced ways in which Judaism informs our political beliefs and
outlooks. Over the course of the year, students learned at a variety of events,
occasions and fun activities. Together, students saw that our Jewish texts and
values have much to teach us even today, and that learning about them can
be interesting and fun. We look forward to continued momentum and another
exciting year to come!
