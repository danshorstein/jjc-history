The Jacksonville Jewish Center
The Jacksonville Jewish Center looks with considerable satisfaction
at the year 19 33, although it was the most difficult year of the depression.
For -the Center this was a year of greater cultural and social activities
and of financial stability. This was largely due to an increased sense of
loyalty, among the jews of this community, a revival of interest in things
Jew sh and the emergence of new leadership from among the younger
people.
The following is a list of the outstanding activities of the Jackson¬
ville Jewish Center, during the past and current year:
The Hebrew school conducted five classes daily, including a class
in Chumosh and a Bar Mitzvah class, with Miss Noami Becker, Mr. J.
Robin and Rabbi Morris D. Margolis as teachers. The Sunday school
*nad an increased registration of 168 children divided into ten classes
and conducted by the following teachers: Miss Reba Wilensky, Miss
Gertrude Wilensky, Miss Dora Selber, Miss Minnie Selber, Miss Rebecca
Smith, Miss Bertha Mehlman, Miss Mollie Mizrahi, Miss Eva Greenstein,
Mrs. Maxwell Sobel and Mr. George Mehlman, in charge of the high
school department organized this year.
An adult class in history and current events is held every Sunday
morning in the Prayer Room, by Rabbi D. Kasachkoff, and a class in Ain
Yaakov is conducted every Sabbath afternoon by Mr. G. Finn. A class
m customs and ceremonies was conducted the first Wednesday in every
month for the members of the Daughters of Israel by Rabbi Margolis.
Several successful entertainments were given by the Hebrew school
and Sunday school pupils on Chanukah, Purim and Passover, and on
minor holidays. The Simchas Torah celebration was a notable success.
A very impressive Confirmation service took place on Friday, June
2, 1933, and fifteen children were Bar Mitzvah during the year.
Services were held during the High Holidays and the festivals.. Late
Friday night services were conducted regularly with members of the
Daughters of Israel acting as hostesses at a social hour at the close of
service. The daily Minyon was continued throughout the year mornings
and evenings, and Sholosh Sudos was conducted every Sabbath afternoon.
Among the special activities of the Jacksonville Jewish Center during
the year was a Prayer and Protest meeting against the persecution of
of Jews in Germany, at which time a considerable sum of money was
•raised for the Joint Distribution Committee in behalf of the German
refugees.
Boy Scouts troop 14 has made considerable progress during the
year under the leadership of Mr. A. Stein.
The following organizations took advantage of the facilities offered
by the Jacksonville Jewish Center; The Daughters of Israel, the Ladies
Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society, the Senior Hadassah, the Junior Hadassah,
the Jewish Cultural League, the Jewish Progressive Society, the Jack¬
sonville Z.onists District, the Young Judaes, the Boy Scouts, troop 14, and
the No-A-Man Club. Several out of town speakers and arcists gave
lecture%^fcd concerts at the Center.
The Jacksonville Jewish Center during the past year has made every
effort to live up to its purpose—to function in the capacity of a com¬
munity home for the Jews of Jacksonville, and to foster and sponsor
every educational, religious, social and charitable undertaking of Jack¬
sonville Jemry.
We are particularly happy to note the increased interest of the
younger people in this organization and this fact should give us greater
courage and a brighter vision of the future.
The Jacksonville Jewish Center is extermely grateful to all the
citizens and merchants of this community who have so generously sup¬
ported this year’s Purim Ball held March 1st. We particularly want to
thank those who advertised in this Reference Book and Directory. We
sincerely urge all our friends and members to patronize our Advertisers,
and to show them every other form of appreciation.

The Jewish Cultural League
The Jewish Cultural League was organized by Rabbi Morris D. Mar-
gob’s of the Jacksonville Jewish Center in November of 19 3 2 for the
purpose of fostering organized cultural and social activities among the
Jevish young men and women of this city.
During its first year of activity, it presented many interesting cul¬
tural programs on a wide variety of subjects, mainly of Jewish interest.
These programs aimed to extend the knowledge of Judaism among the
members of the League and to increase their appreciation of Jewish life
and its problems. Beside outside speakers, members of the League also
presented papers on Jewish topics. Several plays were given by the
dramatic group headed by Mr. Jack Berman. A very successful social
affair was sponsored by the League in February under the chairmanship
of Mr. Abe Rothstein.
Membership in the League is open to any man or woman of Jewish
faith above the age of eighteen.

The Daughters of Iseral
The Daughters of Israel serve in the capacity of a Sisterhood to the
Jacksonville Jewish Center, aiding the Congregation financially, and as¬
sisting in all Center activities. In addition the Daughters of Israel main¬
tains theHebrew School and Sunday School of the Center.
Meetings—First Wednesday of the month at the Center.
The outstanding affairs sponsored by the Daughters of Israel this year
were the Thangsgiving Dance and the annual Cabaret held January 31st,
which enjoyed the largest attendance in many years.
The organization sponsored the Chamisho Osor, Lag B’Omer,
Chanukah and Purim entertainments as well as the childrens’ Seder, of
the Sunday 'school and Hebrew School. The Daughters of Israel were of
great assistance to the Purim ball committee, and have especially helped
to produce this Reference Book and Directory.

WHY HADASSAH SHOULD APAEAL TO EVERY
JEWISH WOMAN
Through the devotion and determination of a group of American
Jewish women, Hadassah has developed a health program in Palestine
that could well serve as a model to the entire world. Through Hadassah’s
infant welfare stations, infant mortality has been reduced to a minium;
the maternal death rate is the lowest in the world. Trachoma has been
practically wiped out and campaigns against tuberculosis and malaria
are being carried on relentlessly.
Now that the need for curative medicine is not as great as formerly,
Hadassah is assuming responsibility for even greater work—the erection
of a Medical Center in connection with the Hebrew University. When
completed this will serve Palestine and the Near East in a similar capa¬
city proportionately to that of the Rockfeller Institute here and it is
hoped that the outstanding German Jewish scientists, whom Hadassah is
now placing in the various departments of its existing institutions, will
eventually be appointed as heads of departments in the Medical Center.
A recent important appointment is that of Professor Ludwig Halber-
stadter, former head of the Cancer Institute of the University of Berlin,
as director of the new Hadassah Radiology Institute in Jerusalem. This
is the only Radiology Institute in the entire Near East and will be a cen¬
ter of cancer treatment and research for that whole section.
With the human material available, through the upheaval in Ger¬
many, the Hadassah-University Hospital promises to become one of the
greatest medical centers in the world.
In the face of growing Anti-Simitism and discrimination, there is
little doubt but that every thinking Jewish woman will not only endorse
the work done by Pladassah but will join the ranks and give her staunch¬
est support.
JUNIOR HADASSAH - The Growth of an Idea
Junior Hadassah was organized in 19 2 0 by Hadassah, the Women's
Zionist Organization, to support war orphans in Palestine. It began its
work by financially adopting 18 6 children. Each Unit pledged itself
to support one or more of these children, and established personal rela¬
tions with its foster child through correspondence and gifts. Most of
these children are now self-supporting. Since 1925, instead of caring
for individual children, the organization has established a children’s com¬
munity at MEIER SHFEYAH, where it now supports 110 Palestine child¬
ren, many of them orphans, in a modern home and school.
In 19 28. Junior Hadassah took over from Senior Hadassah the sup¬
port of the NURSES’ TRAINING SCHOOL connected with the RothschLd
Hospital in Jerusalem. It thus supplies Palestine and the neighboring
countries with trained nurses and opens a profession to the women of
the country.
It further helps to reclaim the land of Palestine for the entire Jewish
people, by raising a specific sum for the JEWISH NATIONAL FUND
hrough the distribution of J, N. F. boxes and collections on Flower Day
and Maccabean Day (Flag Day.)
In America, Junior Hadassah gives its members an organized means
of participating in Jewish life and provides a cultural program wlrch
strives to give thm a real understanding and appreciation of the Jewish
past and present.

Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid Society
and Home for the Aged
The Ladies Hebrew Sheltering Aid and Home for the Aged was or¬
ganized eleven years ago for the purpose of giving relief and assistance to
the needy and unfortunate locally and to assist those unfortunate Jewish
travellers who came to our city by giving them Kosher food and shelter
in a Jewish environment.
With the extensive aid and relief work being done came the realization
of the pressing need for a Home for the Aged of the entire State of Flor¬
ida and for South Georgia.
A property had been purchased and is now free of all liens and
mortgages. Our goal now is to rebuild, redecorate and furnish our Home
so that it may be a haven of comfort and convenience for those old folks
who might be fortunate enough to dwell within its walls.
It is our hope that the establishment of such a Home in Jackson¬
ville will greatly benefit all Jewish people and that it will be a just
cause for pride in the heart of every Jew of our city.
