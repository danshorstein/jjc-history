JUNIOR HADASSAH Continued
The organization now consists of 182 Units, representing a total
membership of 10,000 throughout the country. The local Unit, organized
in 1928, is one of 14 comprising the Southern Region. It subscribes to
the Hadassah program and attempts to attract all Jewish young women
in the community 18 years of age or oyer, who are strongly urged to
join.
To carry out its essential fund-raising requirements, attractive af¬
fairs are offered, in which the whole-hearted support of the community
is enlisted. The Jacksonville Unit has always followed the policy of co¬
operating with other local Jewish organizations and avoiding conflict as
inch as possible. It is a vital factor in community life for the mainten¬
ance of Jewish traditions and ideals. To further its cultural aims, in¬
teresting programs are prepared for general meetings, and informal
“Oneg Shabbat” groups are sponsored jointly with Senior Hadassah, meet¬
ing on the second and fourth Saturdays in the month at the homes of
members.
Regular business meetings of the Jacksonville Unit are held at the
Jewish Center on the first Thursday of each month. The executive board
meets regularly on the third Thursday of the month at the home of the
respective members. All meetings are open to the public at large, and
eligible Jewish girls are especially invited to attend. The local organiza¬
tion is headed by the following officers and committee chairmen:
