About the Cover

The beautiful cover of the
yearbook is an illuminated page
from the Darmstadt Haggadah

from Heidelberg, Germany,
dated 1430.

The text is from the Ha
Lachmah Anyah which intro-
duces the Maggid section of the
Seder which tells the story of
Pesach.

 

This is the bread of affliction a i c
which our ancestors ate in the Elaine Metlin
land of Egypt. Data Entry

Let all who are hungry enter and eat.

Let all who are needy, come and celebrate Pesach.

Now we are here. Next year may we be in the land of Israel.
Now we are enslaved. Next year may we be free.

This overture to the poor and needy is so important that it was
written in the language of the times, Aramaic, so that it would be
understood by all.

The theme of hospitality is threaded through the Seder, through
Pesach, through all our festivals. We open our homes to those who
are homeless and we open our door to Elijah, symbol of the weary
wayfarer. To allow any Jew to suffer deprivation on this day would
be a mockery to Pesach, for it would mean that we had forgotten
that we were once slaves in Egypt. We read this passage to under-
score that many of our fellow Jews are still afflicted, that there is
still hunger in the world but that we are confident that the redemp-
tion will come and will come soon.
