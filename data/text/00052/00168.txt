A Messaae from the Executive Director

 

In last year's yearbook, Rabbi Gaffney’s message related the impor-
tance of the synagogue throughout history.

As Executive Director I get to see, on a daily basis, just how impor-
tant our synagogue is to our members. To many, it is a place filled
with warmth and love. A place to rejoice and a place to heal. A place
to be happy or sad, to fondly recall those meaningful moments in
each of our lives.

Members want to come back day in and day out because the syna-
gogue is a second home. It may be a memorial plaque of a family
member, the memory of a bar/bat mitzvah, bris, babynaming or wed-
ding. It may be a graduation, confirmation, a special event of a Centyer organization, or per-
haps a room dedication. Any one of these connects us to our synagogue.

And we keep coming back because it is a place for all of us at any time in our lives. A place
where “they'll always take us in.”

The Administrative Staff and I are proud to be part of the Jacksonville Jewish Center. My
family and I wish you a very Happy New Year. I look forward to your continued use of our
synagogue.

 

igabel J. Balotin

L’Shanah Tovah

Isabel J. Balotin
