SHABBAT GREETERS
Rhoda Goldstein and
Carolyn Krestful, coordinators

The Shabbat Greeters program has
become so successful that in just two
short years the list of participants has
doubled. Many husbands now join
their wives as a team to greet wor-
shippers as they enter the lobby. On
many Shabbat mornings, the
greeters are three generations of
Center women. The friendly smiles
and warm greetings of these volun-
teers enhance the spirit of peace and
happiness associated with Shabbat.

CENTER CEMETERY
Charlotte Fialkow and
Blanche Slott, chairpeople

The Center owns and operates two
cemeteries, both adjacent to the Ever-
green Cemetery in North Jacksonville,
Both are beautiful pieces of property
with large shade trees and mature
landscaping. The original cemetery is
referred to as the “Old Cemetery.”
The new cemetery is known as the
Center Memorial Park. The Center
provides complete maintenance of
these grounds. Both cemeteries are
open daylight hours every day except
Shabbat and Jewish holidays. A
Memorial Service is conducted each
year on the Sunday between Rosh
Hashana and Yom Kippur at each of
the cemeteries.

Just as with other life cycle events at
the Center,burial and mourning rites
are reserved for Center members.
Therefore, only full members may pur-
chase a plot or be buried in one of the
Center cemeteries. For more informa-
tion regarding the purchase of ceme-
tery plots, please call the Center
administrative office.

HEVRAH KADDISHA

Nathan Sandler, President

For almost 2000 years, Judaism has
cared for the deceased with as much
dignity as one would have in life. Bur-
ial societies exist in communities to
ensure that standards of loving-kind-
ness and dignity are maintained for all.

While Jacksonville has a Hevrah
Kaddisha, its membership is smaller
than desired. Additional members
woukd enable us to enhance the per-
formance of our tasks which include:
(1) Tahara, the traditioan washing,
shrouding and placing of the body in
the coffin; (2) making arrangememnts
for the Tahara; (3) being a Shomer,
one who watches the body until the
funeral; (4) transportation for the
Shomer.

Being a part of the Hevrah Kaddisha
means performing a mitzvah for
another Jew who can never thank
you. This is the greatest mitzvah of all.
Please calll your Rabbi or Nathan San-
dler, if you wish to take part.
