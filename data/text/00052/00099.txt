Committees
ADULT EDUCATION

Learning is a lifelong process. In
answer to the diverse needs of our
congregation, the Adult Education
committee offers a variety of courses,
seminars and educational encounters
geared to the interest of adults.
Hebrew Literacy classes were
expanded this year to incude a Shab-
bat morning beginner’s reading class
taught by Etta Drashin and Wendy
Brezin. Bruce and Kathy Starke
coordinated another highly successful
One-Day Crash Course in Hebrew
reading.

The Sisterhood Bat Mitzvah Class
concluded a two year course of study
with Rabbi Gaffney and celebrated by
participating fully in Shabbat services
April 30 and May 1, 1993. Jane
Grann arranged several book reviews
sponsored by Sisterhood. A Mini-
course on “Women in the Bible” pre-
sented by Alina Kentof provided
thought provoking Shabbat afternoon
discussions.

On Sunday mornings, Rabbi
Gaffney discussed Ethical Issues and
Dilemmas citing Jewish Classical litera-
ture for origins and perspectives on
many topics. The “Firing Line ” maga-
zine discussion group met on Shaab-
bat afternoons for a light luncheon and
lively discussions coordinated by Judy
Mizrahi and Harry Verstandig
Robin Morris conducted fun and
informative seminars on Jewish Holi-
day observances in the home.

The annual Shabbaton coordinated
by Lillie Ray Levy, hosted Rabbi Joel
Roth, Dean of the Rabbinical School
of the Jewish Theological Seminary.
Rabbi Roth spoke at three sessions
throughout the weekend on the cen-
tral theme, “Conservative Judaism, An
Authentic Approach to Jewish Life.”

More information on the 1993-94
Adult Education Program is available
through Rabbi Gaffney’s office, 292-
