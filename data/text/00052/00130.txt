TORAH READING
1992-93

Ted Gross, coordinator

The emphasis on fair and equal
treatment for all people is rooted in
the basic Torah laws concerning the
stranger (alien), the orphan and the
widow. Comment on Exodus 22:20 -
in the alien, men discovered the idea
of humanity. It is not strange, there-
fore for the newest member of the
Supreme Court to have a record of
jurisprudence reflecting these values.
From year to year, from generation
to generation, Torah principles have
fostered an innate respect for social
consciousness This is true even of
those who only pay attention to the
public reading of the Torah Scroll.
How much more is the potential for
commitment of those who do the
actual reading!

We are thankful to those who
devote time and energy to becoming
regular Torah readers, to those who
return again and again when their
particular portions come up for
repetitive reading and to those who
have volunteered the skills learned in
the Center program to assist in other
congregations. We are also thankful
to the Religious committee and its
chairman, Edward Tannen, for spe-
cial assistance.

LIBRARY COMMITTEE

Minnie Schreiber, chairman

The Henry Kramer Library and
Resource Center continues to be
first in Juadica collections, according
to the Jacksonville Business Journal.
New and timely Judaica books are
purchased each year by contribu-
tions from Center members. A spe-
cial bookplate recognizes the donor.

The Solomon Schechter Day
School PTA Book-A-Bration pro-
gram furnishes new books for the
children’s section. Family and friends
buy books in honor of the birthday
of Day School students as well as for
other occasions. So many new books

were added to this collection this
year that a new and larger card cata-
log holder had to be purchased from
various library contributions.

A paperback book holder was
donated by the Sachs Family in
memory of Debby Sachs.

The demand for new books by
young readers created a need for
new shelves. The Center Men’s Club
donated the materials and talent to
build these shelves.

Progress is being made in organz-
ing the Yiddish and Hebrew Collec-
tions on the third level of the
Library. Members of the congrega-
tion who read Yiddish and Hebrew
would render invaluable assistance
in this area.

The Henry Kramer Library has an
extensive collection of books on a
wide variety of topics. It is accessible
to all members on Sunday morn-
ings. In addition, the book cart is
made available on special Shabbat
and Holiday mornings after services
for the convenience of worshippers.
Leisure reading of Judaica is a won-
derful way to become better
informed.

The committee performs many
hours of volunteer service. This
insures the Library collections are in
order and easily available to the con-
gregation. Members are invited to
join this enjoyable endeavor.

YOUTH COMMISSION

Robert Cantor, chairman
Horty Gendzier, Youth V .P.

The purpose of the Jacksonville
Jewish Center Youth Commission is
to over see and set appropriate goals
for all of the Center Youth Groups.
Chalutzim, Kadima, USY, Boy Scouts
and Giirl Scouts are all vital to the
future of our people. The Youth Com-
mission sets proper standards for the
programming of these groups.

The Scout programs help to instill
proper values, to teach survival and
develop community skills. The Youth
Commission helps these programs
become meaningful within the frame-
work of Jewish life.

With guidelines from this committee,
our young people have an opportunity
to socialize with other Jewish youth in
a wholesome, value promoting atmos-
phere.
