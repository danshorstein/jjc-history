After five years, FAMILY COUNCIL is still providing innovative Family Education programs. This yout ‘fooken
bold next step - the development of large scale projects that span a preparation and presentation time of a month
or more. All facets of the congregation are involved: the classroom teachers in all Center schools (Center Preschool,
Solomon Schechter Day School, Afternoon Hebrew School and Sunday School), synagogue professionals, volunteer
experts, parents and children.

 

 

Under the creative direction of Rabbi Dov Kentof with the inspirational guidance of Rabbi David Gaffney, administrative
efforts of committee chair, Peggy Organes, and classroom coordination of Edith Horovitz and Robin Morris, FAMILY
COUNCIL offered three new programs.

Noah’s Ark - A Valuable Voyage -
Again! Rabbi Dov Kentof, as the biblical
Noah, took a group of students and parents
on an open touring boat for an educational
recreation of Noah’s famous voyage. The
group cruised up the St. John’s River to the
Jacksonville Zoo, to reinforce the sidra of
Noah that had been studied in the Center’s
schools that week. The highlight of the
experience was a tour of the zoo complete
with biblical references and Hebrew names
of all the animals.

 

A Fantasy Flight to Israel took off
from the Jacksonville Jewish Center’s
“airport.” Every detail was addressed to
make this adventure as real as possible.
Students of levels kindergarten through
second grade in all the Center’s schools
had studied Israel as a month long unit
which explored all aspects of Israeli life
and culture.

 

Time Travelers brought the lessons of
the patriarchs and matriarchs to life after
a month of study both at school and at
home. Lessons included hospitality,
visiting the sick, origins and recitations of
blessings and caring for animals.
