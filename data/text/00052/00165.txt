RELIGIOUS COMMITTEE

Edward Tannen, Chairman

Under the leadership of Edward
Tannen and with the expertise of
Rabbi David Gaffney, this dedicated
group of men and women meets reg-
ularly to discuss religious issues
effecting the many facets of syna-
gogue programming. A milestone
decision was made earlier this year
that enabled a woman to be called to
the Torah for an aliyah prior to her
reciting the Haftorah. A subcommit-
tee of the Religious committee was
formed to conduct the search for a
new cantor and it presented their
candidate, Joel Fox, to the Board of
Directors for approval. Barry Schus-
ter works hard throughout the year
providing Haftorah readers. Each
year before the High Holidays, this
committee spends many hours
determing honors to be given to con-
gregants during the services.
Throughout their efforts, our syna-
gogue remains a dynamic and mean-
ingful institution.

MITZVAH COMMITTEE

Bikkur Holim literally means “visiting
the sick” and is a religious imperative
for us as Jews. The ancient tradition
finds its roots in a number of biblical
and rabbinic texts.

In Bereshit , the first book of the
Torah, we learn that human beings are
created b’tzelem Elohim, in God’s
image. Judaism understands that to
mean that we are required to emulate
the ethical attributes of God, mercy,
kindness, forgiveness, graciousness.

Modern society with its wonderous
advances in medical technology, intim-
idating procedures and sterile hospital
environments, has, if anything,
increased the need for Bikkur Holim.
The needs of the sick and aged
require, not only the best of scientific
knowledge, but the loving care of fam-
ily, friends, neighbors and the syna-
gogue community.

“To minister to the sick, is to minister
to God.”
- Abraham Joshua Heschel
