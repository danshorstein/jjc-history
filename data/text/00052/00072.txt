BRANDEIS WOMEN

Brandeis University National Women’s
Committee provides financial support for
the Brandeis University Libraries, which
serve a community of students and
scholars, in education and research, united
by their commitment to the pursuit of
knowledge. Members act as emissaries to
support and enhance the image of
Brandeis, a Jewish sponsored, non-
sectarian university where excellence is the
tradition.

The Jacksonville Chapter was organized
in September 1991 and has about 120
members. In addition to fund-raising
projects, members participate in study
groups using college syllabi prepared by
Brandeis University professors. A visiting
professor attends annually.

Membership in the National Women’s
Committee is non-sectarian and open to
non-graduates of Brandeis and to men.

ZIONIST ORGANIZATION
of AMERICA

We in ZOA are proud that we have
positions based on ideological principals.
Today, ZOA intends to be a more viable
organization, stronger, more potent and a
force to be reckoned with. Events in Israel
and the diaspora show that ZOA is an
organization which Israel needs, more than
ever before. Our efforts are dedicated to
these vital areas:

1. Zionist and Hebrew education;

2. Public action in defense of Israel;

3. Youth programs in the United States
and in Israel;

4. Leadership development on. all
levels;

5. Increased support for our Israel proj-
ects in Tel Aviv and Ashkelon;

6. Publications and better communica-
tion with members in the community;

7. A more effective projection of the
organization, its purposes and _ its
accomplishments;

8. Encouragement of Aliya - Going
beyond housing and comfort, beyond
bureaucracy, overcoming attitude and
receptivity of Israelis - the realistic fact of
life is Jews need encouragement.

Let us join together, as Jews, Zionists
and Americans, devoted to the principals
of freedom and democracy and to the suc-
cessful quest to achieve peace for all
mankind.

B’NAI B’RITH YOUTH

The B’nai Brith Youth Organization
(BBYO) is the largest youth organization
in the world. It consists of two unique
Chapters, Aleph Zadik Aleph (AZA) for
young men and B'nai Brith Girls (BBG)
for young women.

AZA, which stands for fraternity and har-
mony focuses on building Jewish fraternal
bonds while taking part in projects that
benefit the community.

BBG focuses on sisterhood and social ac-
tion and encourages members to take ac-
tive roles in the community, as Jews and
as concerned citizens.

Each group is actively involved in the
Jewish community, volunteering at local
synagogues and attending regularly
scheduled programs on Jewish ethics and
modern values. BBG and AZA also work
together to make positive change in their
communities.

BBYO also promotes a feeling of unity,
togetherness and belonging through
chapter programs, conventions, retreats
and summer programs. It is a feeling that
is incomparable.

If you are a Jewish teenager in grades 9 -

12, we invite you to join BBYO. For in-
formation call: Wes Benwick (AZA)
737-9262 or Natasha Yoiron (BBG)
448-5578.
