A MESSAGE FROM THE DIRECTOR
Bernard & Alice
SELEVAN
RELIGIOUS SCHOOL
I know this year will be an amazing year for the
Bernard & Alice Selevan Religious School! Our positive,
family-style atmosphere keeps all of our students smiling,
and parents have been eager to become involved in our
school while furthering their own education with adult
classes on Sundays.
This past spring, a special Task Force for Re-
Imagination, made up of a cross section of parents from
our program as well as our professional and lay leaders,
was formed to explore some new possibilities as our
school continues to progress and improve. After much study, a new mission
statement was written.
The 2014-2015 school year will include the addition of several school
shabbatot, class mitzvah projects, a 24/7 Online Hebrew Center providing blended
learning for Hebrew practice and fun games, and an enticing “Snack & Learn”
session at drop off on Wednesdays for parents and students together. We are all
very excited about introducing our new experiential Judaism through the Arts
initiative for Judaic studies in grades 2-5 on Sundays, and a more “campy” feel
as we continue to build our madrachim (teen helpers) program and infuse lots of
conversational Hebrew throughout the school day.
We are looking forward to the addition of Rabbi Tilman as our daily t’fillah leader, as well as our Youth
Professional Steven Resnick and the continued involvement of Rabbi Lubliner
and Hazzan Holzer. We welcome Rabbi Jim Rogozen and are eager to learn
from him as he leads us into 5775.
From our Religious School family to yours, we wish you a L’Shanah
Tovah and the best year ever!
Lois B. Tompkins
