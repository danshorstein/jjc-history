Dedication
Witten Chapel
December 2, 1983
The Witten Chapel is in the “hub of the daily life of the
congregation.” Morning and afternoon services as well as early
Shabbat services on Friday evening, are held there. Its warm and
intimate atmosphere make it a perfect place for the minyan, Torah
Study classes, daily services for the Solomon Schechter Day
School, and the Junior Congregation services which are held on
Shabbat and holidays. Since it can be expanded to seat 100 people,
smaller weddings and the Friday evening services of the Bar and
Bat Mitzvah are held there.
To memorialize the love and respect for their
parents and grandparents, the families of Dr.
Joseph, Ike, Dr. Samuel and the late Louis
Witten dedicated the Witten Chapel in memory
of Ida and Max Witten.
Sisterhood Kitchen Dedication
January 28, 1984
A Pledge of Allegiance — During the Sisterhood administration of Susan Kantor, a $100,000 pledge was made to the
Center’s Building Fund to be used toward the furnishing of the comprehensive kitchen facility. Monies were to be raised
annually by a “Donor Affair,” each time counting on the generosity of the Sisterhood membership, and every year our ladies
came through because of careful planning and solicitations made by the donor chairwomen. As a result of the time, effort and
success of their leadership, %’s of the pledge has been paid.
The Donor Chairwomen Honored Were: Isabel Balotin, Barbara Cantor, Ellen Freedman, Maxine Funk, Marilyn
Garber, Rhoda Goldstein, Renee Gordon, Joan Levin, Helaine Marón, Ginette Mizrahi, Evelyn Moskovitz, Rhonda Passink,
Lois Peltz, Sue Rosenberg, Barbara Safer, Merle Selevan, Debra Setzer and Joni Shmunes.
The highlight of the day was the dedication of the Kitchen. The Center, Building Fund Committee, the Youth and Education
departments all praise the good works of the Sisterhood.
