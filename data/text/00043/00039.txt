Setzer Social Hall
October 15, 1983
The Center’s “Year of Dedication” was initiated with the official naming of the
Setzer Social Hall.
The Setzer Social Hall is named for Benjamin Setzer, of blessed memory, and
memorializes his leadership and generosity. His willingness in offering the initial
gifts; his maintenance of a continuous flow of substantial financial contributions
over a period of more than 25 years; and above all, his counsel and determination
have made it possible for the synagogue, to have had available in the 50s and 60s,
the building facilities so desperately needed then for the religious education of the
increasing number of congregants and children.
Benjamin Setzer also encouraged the acquisition of the property on State Road
13 and his enthusiasm helped concretize the final plans for the development of
our present facility.
The Setzer Social Hall dedication is not only a congregation’s collective
remembrance of an heroic personality of the past as it is also our
acknowledgment of the gratitude we feel for the nurturing of a family legacy of
generosity today.
