Seventh Annual Kadima Convention
The Jacksonville Jewish Center hosted its largest convention ever! Thirty chapters from our Southeast Region sent their delegates
to Jacksonville to participate.
The overall theme — “To Repair the Universe” — dealt with subjects of hunger in the world and the Jewish approach toward the
poor, nuclear disarmament, and the relationship between man and his fellow man.
Our delegates conducted services, arranged a special Havdalah Service and created the entire program for the weekend. On
Saturday night, a social and carnival were held for young people to enjoy.
This was by far the largest convention of its kind in the country and was regarded as the focal point of Kadima activities in the
Southeast Region. Our regional director, Mr. Bruce Klasner, addressed the young people during the convention. Activities were
directed by our convention coordinators Beth Greenburg, Jenny Verstandig, Katherine Echt, David Silverman; supervised by
advisors Fran Manas, Kim Lewis and Rabbi Dov Kentof.
Our thanks to all the families of the Center who graciously hosted our guests.
