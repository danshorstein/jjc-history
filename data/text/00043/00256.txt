Women’s American Ort
Women’s American Ort is the Organization for Rehabilitation
through Training. Ort is the vocational and technical education of
the Jewish people. For 102 years, since Ort’s inception in Russia,
we have been striving to give man and woman a greater
opportunity in life — given a trade and skill, an individual can
maintain dignity and pride.
Woman’s American Ort is the most complex and one of the
fastest growing Jewish Women’s organizations in the United
States. We are an activist group, not only taking stands, but
positively working to upgrade the quality life for ourselves, our
families, and our communities. We are women who are aware of
the changes that are occuring within our society and are
concerned by the resurgence of global anti-semitism and other
anti-democratic forces which threaten Jewish life, Israel’s survival,
as well as pluralism, tolerance and freedom for all people,
especially Soviet Jewry, and support for Israel and American
Jews.

B’nai B’rith Youth Organization
B’nai B’rith Youth Organization, the largest Jewish youth
organization in the world, is active and growing in the Jacksonville
area. BBYO is comprised of teenagers in grades eight through
twelve and, as an umbrella group, accepts teenagers from all
Jewish sects.
Programs focus upon five major areas: Jewish heritage,
recreation, social, community service and leadership. BBYO’ers
plan their own activities, with advisors acting as chaperones.
The group meets Sunday evenings during the school year, and
plans programs during the summer to stay in touch with members.
If you are interested in joining BBYO, contact Mike Armel or Jami
Goldfarb at 731-3890.
Amity II
The original Amity Club was organized in December, 1951, and
for many years was a great opportunity for the young Jewish
couples in our community to become acquainted and to enjoy a
sociable evening together once a month. It continued for many
years before disbanding.
Feeling the need for an Amity Club again, a group of couples
headed by Sylvia and Izzy Weiss sent fliers out and an open
organizational meeting was held on Februay 17, 1983. At this
meeting, it was agreed to call ourselves Amity II, to have three
events a year including New Years Eve, and that membership be
limited to 135 couples. For information, call Idlene Slaff at 733-
3190.
