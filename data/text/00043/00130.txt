Torah Readers
We at the Jacksonville Jewish Center consider ourselves indeed fortunate to possess the kind of human resources that we have;
and nowhere is this more true than in the area of Torah Readers.
During the year, our congregants had the pleasure of listening to 62 skilled and fluent Ba’aley K’riah (Torah Readers). Some are
recent B’nai Mitzvah; others are young men who are graduates from the religious schools of the Center and were trained by Ted
Gross. Some are transplants from other communities who have read Torah using their own special Torah trope notes.
Each man, young and old, volunteered his time to enhance our Shabbat and holiday services. We are grateful to them and express
our sincere YASHER KOACH!

Ted Gross Retires
After thirty years of dedicated service to the congregation, Ted Gross retired. A special dinner was held in his honor on
October 6, 1983, headed by Jeffery Morris and Jack Shorstein. Many of his former students and friends expressed their
warm wishes of appreciation and shared their humorous and meaningful experiences.
Ted served as Director of Education for twenty-seven years and is responsible for teaching Torah and Torah trope to
many of the men who are now reading from the Torah. The knowledge and influence he has had on the youth of this
congregation will be everlasting.
