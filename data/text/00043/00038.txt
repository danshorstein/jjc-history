Simhat Torah Honorees
Friday, September 30, 1983
Lewis Ansbacher
Hatan Torah
Charles Mizrahi
Hatan Bereshit
Blanche Slott
Woman of Valor
This year Lewis Ansbacher
received the honor of “Hatan
Torah” (Bridegroom of the
Torah). His diligent and tireless
efforts on behalf of the Center’s
Education, Transportation,
House and Campsite committees,
over the past 25 years, have
earned him this distinguished
honor. Over the years, “Lukie”
has maintained an abiding interest
in the Center, working for the
most part, behind the scenes, to
strengthen the Center’s programs
and financial base.
As a respected member of the
Board of Trustees and the
Executive committee, he is often
sought after for his advice “When
Lukie Talks Everyone Listens.”
The community has also
benefitted from his brilliant legal
mind as well. He was president of
the J.F.C.S. and the Duval
County Legal Aid Society. When
ever called upon he is quick to
respond to the needs of the
congregation... and because of
this dedication we honor Lewis
Ansbacher as our Hatan Torah.
Our “Hatan Bereshit,”
Charles Mizrahi, was chosen
“Bridegroom of the Beginning,” a
very fitting honor as he has been
a man of many beginnings.
At 37, he was one of the
youngest presidents of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center-a
young man with many visions. It
was during his administration
that the young couples’ club,
Ha’Rayim, was formed and the
Center Car Raffle was initiated.
Charles was responsible for
coordinating Building Fund rallies
which preceded our move from
Third and Silver - the highlight of
his presidency and another new
beginning.
The Center is indebted to him
for the part he played in
supporting Jewish Education and
the Center’s youth program. His
children have been officers of
their youth groups demon¬
strating the same leadership
qualities as their father.
A third generation Mizrahi,
Charles comes from a lineage of
dedicated and philanthropic
people. His melodious voice and
Torah reading have become
favorites of many Shabbat
worshippers. This is a man who
truly deserves our admiration.
Blanche Slott was chosen
“Woman of Valor” for her
devotion and sensitivity to the
needs of the youth of the Center.
The distinguished leadership she
provided the Sisterhood during
her three year term as president,
is also reflected by her service to
scouting and other youth
activities.
She is credited for organizing 3
Girl Scout troops and 5 Brownie
troops and served as a leader as
well.
Recognizing her dedication,
she was elected to serve on the
Center board as Youth
Commission chairman, which
gave her the distinction of being
the first woman board member.
A hard worker and talented
artist, Blanche is always willing to
share her creativity. Presently,
Blanche is serving as Administra¬
tive Director and brings to her
job the knowledge and
experience she has acquired
through her years as a volunteer.
Our Woman of Valor is
extremely worthy of the honor.
We congratulate the families of
our honorees.
Daily Minyan
Minyan captains, Barney Margolin and
Jack Silverman are calling for Minyan
while Sid Berkowitz suggests names.
The Jacksonville Jewish Center takes
pride in its Daily Minyan. More than 100
different men are involved in this mitzvah.
Through the tireless efforts of the captains
and co-captains, we have minyans every
morning and evening.
The Daily Minyan needs you. For a
satisfying and rewarding experience attend
once or twice a month on a regular basis.
Weekday evenings are in need of men to
make a minyan, please make an effort to
attend whenever possible.
