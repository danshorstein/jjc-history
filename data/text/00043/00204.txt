Brownies and Girl Scouts
For many years, the Center Sisterhood has actively sponsored the Brownie and Girl Scout
Troops of the Jacksonville Jewish Center. Scouting is a very important phase of the youth
program in the Center. It helps develop character, usefulness at home, appreciation of nature
and the out-of-doors, creation of objects of beauty and usefulness from simple things, and it
helps these young people to find their places in the synagogue and community through
service.
Under the able guidance of Mrs. Clara Mann, the entire Brownie and Girl Scout program
provides all of these opportunities for our young women of tomorrow. For further
information, please call Mrs. Mann.
LTI — A Wonderful Experience
About 40 young people from the Jacksonville Jewish Center Youth Department spent one week at LTI in the
mountains of North Carolina - studying, singing, interacting, and making new friends.
The USY overall theme, “Jerusalem,” was enhanced by a dramatic presentation, evening programs, and many
other exciting events. The Kadima overall theme was “The Many Faces of Judaism,” which dealt with the various
streams of Jewish life.
The young people participated in a mass game of experience/discussion groups; they met with Rabbis and
educators; and had many experiences in leadership development.
All of the young people are looking forward to another year of exciting programs.
