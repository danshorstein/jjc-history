Message from the Youth and Education Director
Rabbi Dov Kentof
You and I have been sending our children to Jewish schools. We have
invested in their Jewish education our own money, community funds, the
children’s precious time and efforts. Has it all been worthwhile? Have the
returns justified the investments?
But what returns should we expect? How can we measure and evaluate
them? Educational returns are so intangible.
Indeed they are, but not as intangible as we may think. We should and we do
have some ways of measuring them.
It is of course very simple to measure objectively the achievements of our children in terms of subject
matter, such as Bible, Hebrew, History, etc. We do possess the technical equipment and devices of
measuring accurately what our child has learned at any given time in any of the school subjects.
But, you may argue, knowledge is not enough; it is attitudes that count; it is the cultivation of character, of
the religious personality, and the like, that is significant.
Quite true. We all realize that mere cold-blooded knowledge is not enough. Knowledge has no value unless
it glows with the impulse to action. Knowledge must be dynamic, it must serve both as a motor and as a
rudder. It should galvanize its possessor into action; it should also guide and direct such action.
We, here, at the Jacksonville Jewish Center are committed to provide your child with the skills and the
attitude for Jewish life. Through the many programs that we offer to your child, we strengthen your child’s
commitment to Judaism and to the synagogue. We do hope that you will take advantage of our multi-program
and that this year will bring fulfillment, understanding and commitment to all of you!
Alina, Gil and Tali are joining me in extending to you and your family a happy New Year.
Rabbi Dov Kentof
