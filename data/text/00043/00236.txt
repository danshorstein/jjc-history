The Jacksonville Jewish Community Council was founded in
1935 and became the Jacksonville Jewish Federation in 1979. We
are almost 50 years old and are continuing to serve the Jewish
community in a variety of ways.
On June 5, 1983, we moved into our new home at 10829 Old St.
Augustine Rd. We have been able to make our meeting rooms
available to the different Jewish organizations and to hold
community-wide functions.
This past year the Federation has sponsored various community
programs. In December we hosted the Israeli Chassidic Festival,
which was held downtown at the Civic Auditorium. On May 2nd we
co-sponsored, with the synagogues, Yom Hashoah — a beautiful
and meaningful Holocaust Remembrance Program. In January, the
Womens Division held Education Day, which is a program to
educate the community and is put together by the presidents of the
various women’s organizations.
In order to bring the Jewish community closer together, the
Federation publishes The Commentator, our monthly newspaper.
We have been able to expand local coverage and also add national
and international articles of Jewish interest.
We will continue to conduct the Annual Combined Jewish
Appeal, which raises monies for the United Jewish Appeal, as well
as other national agencies. Our local beneficiaries are River
Garden, Jewish Family and Community Services and the
Community Kosher Nutrition Program.
As we continue to grow and enter our 50th year, we invite
everyone to visit our new building and participate in all community
events.
