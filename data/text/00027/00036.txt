These past twenty years Jews have returned to
the land to build and to create. Others have re¬
turned as pilgrims to gain inspiration. Among them
many from our own community . . . from this Syna¬
gogue . . . our children. This past year our President
Nathan Freidlin and Mrs. Freídlin, our past President
Jack Becker and Mrs. Becker, were among those who
returned.
We return to the Old City. To Its sh riñes, to
Its holy places, bringing Arab and Jew together.
May this be a harbinger of the future that the Arabs
will dwell with us as brothers and that Jerusalem,
the whole of It, be secure. To this goal may we de¬
vote the next twenty years.
The Wall is the symbol of the hopes and the
dreams of a people to rebuild the sanctuary of man,
that freedom and justice and peace may be the
heritage of Israel and of all mankind. The Old City
was reunited with the New City in June 1967, and
Jews were free to worship at the Western Wall
again. "Silence. I hug the stones! I pray. O rock of
Israel make our faith strong and your words luminous
in our hearts and minds. Pour holiness into our
moments."
And so we commemorate the first twenty years
of the third Jewish State. These twenty years are
part of the 4,000 years of the dream and the hope
of Israel, for life and fulfillment for us an d for all
men.
