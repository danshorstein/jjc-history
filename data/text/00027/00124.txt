(“The Future’’ — College Age Youth)
This past summer marked the completion of ATID’s
fourth summer discussion series for our congregation’s
college students.
The summer’s activities began with an Open
House in June and ended with a Summing Party at
the Campsite at the end of August. Each week the
students met at the home of a different member to
discuss topics of contemporary interest. The discus¬
sions were led by the students themselves, each one
volunteering to prepare a topic for a different gather¬
ing. The Rabbi, who attended several of the meetings,
provided added insight into many of the aspects of
the problems being discussed.
A planning group
ATID group taken at one of the sessions
The topics ranged from the problems of Jewish
survival to the presence of drugs on college campuses.
Among those topics discussed were The New Left,
Vietnam, Sex and the Family in the Jewish Tradition,
and Black Power.
Many of the members of ATID also participated
in Sunday afternoon programs with Negro youth at
the East Side Center of the G.J.E.O. Among these pro¬
grams were baseball, games, arts and crafts, football,
and wrestling.
ATID looks forward to another series of summer
gatherings next year with the continued participation
in social action programs.
