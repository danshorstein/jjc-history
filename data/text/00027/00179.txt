HYMAN SELBER
The year 1967 was an eventful one for A. B. Weil
Lodge. For the first time in many years a sincere
effort was made to create an effective relationship be¬
tween the Lodge and the local B’nai B’rith Youth
Organizations. I am happy to report that as a result
of the work done in this field the Lodge has instituted
a program of awards which will be given annually to
the two members of A. Z. A. and the two members
of B’nai B’rith Girls who are to be selected by their
members as representing the outstanding qualities of
young Jewish leadership. The awards will be given at
the end of the school year.
The Lodge has continued to maintain its member¬
ship at approximately the same level of the last few
years and is hopeful that the new slate of officers
whose names appear above will be able to attract new
members by the use of effective programming.

MICHAEL LISSNER
The Samuel W. Wolfson B’nai B’rith Lodge No.
2588 is now in its third year of existence and has
added a vital link to the strong chain of B’nai B’rith
solidarity and service within our community.
Our Lodge’s annual fund raising affair “A Night
in Las Vegas” and the vital and so well accepted
“Career Day” program for High School students have
become regular events and are looked forward to by
many.
In our short but most active and rewarding few
years, the Wolfson Lodge has added leadership to the
ranks of the already strong Florida State Association
of B’nai B’rith Lodges. Robert I. Roth, our charter
Two of our members have been honored with
State awards this past year. Chuck Oberdorfer for his
work in the field of Community Service and Mike
Lissner for work with the Employ the Handicapped
program. On the state wide level Robert Roth serves
as State Membership Committee Chairman, Mike
Lissner, Chairman, State Employ the Handicapped
Committee, Dr. Henry Wengrow, Co-Chairman State
BBYO Committee and Mort Kesler, serves as Vice-
Chairman of the State A.D.L. Committee.
At the time our lodge was chartered, one of our
main objectives was to foster and encourage a harmon¬
ious relationship among all members of our Jewish
community. We still consider this as our aim and are
constantly striving toward this goal.
