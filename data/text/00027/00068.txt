Our Religious School program emphasizes traditional observance.
We have introduced the latest texts that emphasize these values in our
religious studies. As a consequence, the school places maximum em¬
phasis on Sabbath attendance and worship. This is the last rampart of
Jewish survival. Jewish boys and girls who feel like strangers in the
synagogue will lose most of their identification. Our formal ceremonies
for graduation, Bar and Bat Mitzvah, Confirmation, stress a natural
association with the prayer book and synagogue procedure.
The United Synagogue recognizes the advantages of social edu¬
cation and is developing many synagogue-centered groups that make
study programs an essential feature of the yearly round-of-activities.
Jewish education never ceases. We are happy when our students re¬
main with us until the closing high school years, in the Sunday Reli¬
gious School, as well as in the Hebrew High School.
It is significant that our pupils are attending almost every Ramah
in the country, including counselor training in the Poconoes, and the
programs in Israel.
This year we were able to develop more meaningful projects for
the Prayer courses taught in the Sunday Religious School. Our pupils
are able to take leading parts in the services throughout the year, on
all holiday occasions, and all readings from the various scrolls.
Our main goal is a happily adjusted Jewish-American personality,
proud of our heritage and looking hopefully in the future.
Special thanks are due to our Education Committee, its chairman,
Mr. Jack Shorstein and co-chairman, Mr. Sheldon Gendzier, to the
Sisterhood, Men’s Club and teachers.
