This past year was a banner year for Troop 14.
It saw an increase in membership from 13 boys at the
beginning of the year to 21 boys at the end. A total
of seven new Tenderfoot Scouts and one transfer from
another troop.
We had our first camp-out at Baden-Powell, the
new Boy Scout Camp near Melrose. There the new
Scouts were initiated and two of them had a rough
time the afternoon before the initiation when they got
caught in a storm out in a boat and couldn’t get back
to shore. The shock on their faces was something to
behold when they discovered that they were in shallow
water and could have gotten out of their boat and
walked back in.
The troop also camped out at Crandall Point, a
favorite campsite because of its natural beauty; Fort
Clinch, Camp Echocotee and the Center Day Camp.
At the ZOA Banquet the troop participated in
the dedication of a beautiful plaque to the late Dr.
Harry Yergin. The plaque was in recognition for his
years of faithful service to Scouting. We are grateful
to Mr. Raymond Cohen for his beautiful artwork in
producing the plaque.
The final seven weeks of the year found the troop
taking a course in First Aid under the instruction of
Mr. Ray Smith, a member of the First Aid Squad of
the Jacksonville Fire Dept. Several of the boys quali¬
fied for the First Aid Merit Badge as a result of this
class.
The canoe which we started on is still not finished
but it is starting to take shape. If there is a parent
who is handy with this kind of a project we would
appreciate his help.
Mr. Guy Chambers, Assistant District Commissioner for Cub Scouts, presenting the
