This past year was a good year for Gators BBG. Gators
showed their concern for their community by participating in
the Teen Age March against Leukemia. A special stress was
put on the helping of the Muscular Dystrophy Association.
For two months we helped the MDA office by addressing
envelopes and we had two road blocks in which we collected
$550.00.
At BBYO Florida Regional convention in Camp Ocala,
Gators received much recognition. Carolyn Snider got first
place in Originality Plus, our scrap book received second
place, and our newspaper received first place. In addition to
these achievements, Linda Aronowitz received the Gold Star
of Deborah. Life membership was given to Linda Hilsenrad,
and Linda Aronowitz. Sharon Raskin was elected Florida
Region B’nai B’rith Girls President.
With this fulfilling year behind us, Gators is looking
for new goals to reach.

year for “Gators” A.Z.A. No. 272. As the year progressed,
so did the Gators with the Muscular Dystrophy Drive and
their renowned parties which brought fraternalism to an un¬
precedented peak. All this time the seven cardinal princi¬
pals: Conduct, Patriotism, Judaism, Fraternity, Filial Love
and Charity were kept in mind and applied not only to the
Muscular Dystrophy Drive, but also to many other worth¬
while activities.
