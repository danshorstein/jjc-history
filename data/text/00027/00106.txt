This year the Charles Palot Club has had a hard
but successful year. Our membership has grown smaller
since the Club has changed its age level from 8th, 9th
and 10th grade to just 8th and 9th grade. However,
the number of members is not as important as how
they work, and our members certainly worked hard
this past year.
The programs have not only been fun, but help¬
ful to the members of our Club. Programs such as
a talk on Driving and a tour of the Juvenile Shelter
was more than just interesting. We enjoyed working
on the Hanukah and Purim Carnivals. It was really
more fun than work for the members of C. P. C.
The highlight of the year was Regional Conven¬
tion in which everyone had a hand. Following Regional
Convention we introduced the upcoming 8th graders
Officers and Advisors Charles Palot Club
George Glossman, Skippey Miller, Bruce Hor-
ovitz, and Mrs. Madelyn Glossman.

The last meeting was an election at which time
Bruce Horovitz was elected President. The annual
Installation Banquet and Dance concluded the winter
season.
The summer for C. P. C. was not a lonely one, but
there were many visitors. The Wheelniks were here for
a weekend of fun and friendship for everyone. At the
end of a pleasant summer will come the wonderful
days of L. T. I.
We would like to thank our wonderful advisors,
George and Madelyn Glossman, for their help and
advice and also the past President Larry Browdy and
all the officers for a job well done. Good luck to the
new officers for next year!
