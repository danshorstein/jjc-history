Camp Ramah
Some of our Ramah campers: Leonard Selber, Howard Rosenblatt, Linda Halpern,
Simeon Goldblum, Richard Browdy, Michael Schneider ,and Mayer Gil Becker.
One of the greatest contributions that the Conservative Move¬
ment has made to American Jewish life is the program of the
Ramah camps. There are now five such camps in the United
States affecting the lives of more than 1500 of our Jewish
youth. It is indeed unfortunate when we realize that approxi¬
mately this same number is turned away each year because of
lack of space.
The program is unique in that it blends ideally the necessary
elements for ,a wholesome Jewish life. In a beautiful Jewish at¬
mosphere the Ramah camper is permitted to learn culturally,
develop spiritually, mature socially and progress athletically.
The campers and counselors are hand picked, and in this whole¬
some atmosphere a most profound impression is made upon
the youthful mind.
More campers are needed to meet the growing demand. Our
Center has benefited greatly in the opportunity granted our
young people to be a part of this program. Our Men’s Club
deserves tremendous credit for its fine Ramah scholarship
program.
