Songs for the Home
KINDLING OF THE SABBATH LIGHTS
The Sabbath is welcomed into the Jewish
home by the mother kindling the Sabbath
candles. She shields her eyes from the
light so that with the completion of the
blessing she sees the light for the first
time in all its Sabbath warmth and
splendor.
KIDDUSH (Santificaron)
The father of the household makes the
family deeply conscious of the sanctity of
the Sabbath through the recitation of the
Kiddush over the cup of wine.
SHOLOM ALEICHEM (Peace unto You)
The Sabbath meal should be spent in the
singing of Sabbath melodies and we in¬
clude the Sholom Aleichem, for it is the
beautiful hymn by which we welcome the
angels of peace who bring us the Sab¬
bath. Only the first two verses are chant¬
ed by the Cantor.
BIRHAT HAMAZON (Grace After Meals)
At the conclusion of the meal the family
is asked to thank God for all of the
blessings of food, home, family and its
love. The prayer of indebtedness is called
the Birchat Hamazon or Grace After
Meals. The Cantor chants the first para¬
graph of the Birchat Hamazon.
BIRHOT HATORAH
(Blessing over the Torah)
On Sabbath morning in the synagogue
we are often called to the Torah. The
democracy of Judaism is clearly expressed
for at the most solemn moment in the
service, the reading of the Torah, all are
considered worthy of coming forward to
offer their personal blessing.
YOM ZE L’YISROEL
N’RAN’NA
Two Hebrew Melodies emphasizing the
uniqueness of the Sabbath and our unique
obligation of reacting with both reverence
and happiness.
