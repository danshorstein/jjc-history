8 SIVAN
LAG BA’OMER (33rd Day of the Omer)—this holiday com¬
memorates the lifting of a plague from the students of Rabbi
Akiba, an event which occurred on the 33rd day of Count¬
ing the Omer (the counting of the “omer” or “measure” of new grain
begins on the Second Day of Passover, and continues for 49 days). In
observing Lag Ba’omer, Jews honor the memories of Rabbi Akiba and
his copatriots, Bar Kochba and Rabbi Bar Yachai. These leaders devoted
their lives to an heroic effort to end the Roman occupation and re-estab¬
lish the Jewish nation. (Rabbi Akiba ultimately died by torture at the
hands of the Romans.) The holiday is celebrated with athletic games
(symbolized here by bow and arrow) because Rabbi Akiba and his stu¬
dents carried bows and arrows to disguise their true purpose when they
went into the woods to pursue the forbidden study of the Torah.
SHAVUOT—On this day, the Ten Commandments are read in
the synagogue to commemorate the giving of the Law (Torah)
to Moses on Mount Sinai. Shavuot also marks the early wheat
harvest in the Holy Land. On this holiday Jews decorate their homes and
synagogues with greens and flowers.
