The River Garden story is a continuing one since 1946. It
is a story of elderly people who find comfort and security when
their way of life is disrupted in the community. There are many
reasons for coming to the Home, but all too often physical
changes and infirmities associated with the later years makes it
difficult for the aged to live alone or with their families.
The broad gamut of medical and nursing service provides
basic security for the residents of the Home. However, enrich¬
ing and adding meaning to the lives of elderly people is equally
as important as the basic services required for daily living. To¬
wards these goals, the Home has organized a wide range of so¬
cial, religious, educational, crafts, and work activities to broaden
the horizon of most of the Home-bound residents. With the
help of the corps of Gray Ladies, personal relationships are
maintained and group programs are planned to provide the
motivation to find living worth while.
To meet the increasing demands for its services, River Gar¬
den is planning 'to open the door for 50 more.” This slogan
implies a large building program to increase the capacity from
70 to 120 residents. The near future will see the fruition of
these plans translated into brick and mortar.
It is with pride that River Garden views its past 16 years of
service to the Jewish Communities of Florida and looks forward
to continuing to provide a dignified ,and secure way of life for
many more of our aged.
