The Priceless is Difficult to Replace
Throughout the past years Robert J. Gordon, Chairman of our Building Fund, has
given of himself with little or no consideration for his personal health or time. No sac¬
rifice was too great and no task was too difficult when it came to the Center and its
needs. Time was given by the day and not by the hour, and we all were inspired to give
and to serve through his service and devotion.
Because of ill health, Bob has not been able to continue this personal strenuous pro¬
gram of service. His spirit and guidance is difficult to equal and his outstanding price¬
less leadership is extremely difficult to replace.
May we all do our part for the Building Fund to prove to Bob that the great project
which he has nurtured in the past will continue from strength to strength in the future.
To Boh we say Hazak Vi-amatz—May you find renewed strength and vigor to con¬
tinue to inspire us for many more years.
Robert J. Gordon
