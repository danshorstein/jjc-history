Highlighting senior activities was the District Conclave held
in March. Mrs. William Goldman was adult chairman and Miss
Elaine Brukner was conclave chairman.
The culminating affair of the year for junior and senior clubs
was the Israeli Fair held on the grounds of the River Garden
Home in May with the proceeds going to the Israeli Scouts.
Eleven Judeans attended the Southern Region Summer Con¬
vention at Camp Judaea in Hendersonville, N. Car. in June.
The following were elected to serve as officers: Jerry Linsey,
District II Pres.; Michael Pousman, Regional Treasurer; Mal¬
colm Fages, Regional Programming Committee.
Camp Judaea in Hendersonville is a major project of Young
Judaea. Hadassah and Z.O.A. sponsor this 4 or 8 week camp
period with a full camping and Zionist program. Camp Judaea
serves to develop in youth the ideals of American freedom, an
appreciation of the Jewish people, a bond with the land of
Israel and an understanding of the religious and ethical con¬
cepts of Judaism.
