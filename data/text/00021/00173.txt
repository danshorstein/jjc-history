The Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah was chartered by
Henrietta Szold in December 1925 and is now composed of
Senior Hadassah and the Hannah Senesch Evening Group. Its
membership is now approximately 800 and its annual quotas run
into tens of thousands.
This past season the local chapter is proud to report the ful¬
filling of all quotas. This year Hadassah sponsored, jointly with
the Jacksonville Art Association, an outstanding exhibit of
Marc Chagall’s biblical prints. On January 12 Hadassah joined
with ZOA to participate in religious services at the Center in
observance of Hadassah’s 50th anniversary. The dramatic pres¬
entation of "Woman of Valor," the biography of Henrietta
Szold, featured an Oneg Shabbat; an Education Day highlight¬
Officers of Senior Hadassah
ed Rabbi Moshe Goldblum’s review of the book "Goats of Ein
Gedi’’ and color films of Jack and Sue Becker’s recent trip to
Israel; and the Membership Tea was an outstanding event with
the announcement of the Healarama winner, Jack Becker, whose
name will be inscribed on a place on the Wall of Healing at
the new Medical Center in Israel.
The social climax of the season was reached with a spectacu¬
lar Golden Jubilee Ball in celebration of Hadassah’s half cen¬
tury of service.
Hadassah is the world’s largest Zionist organization and
has a program of health, vocational and educational service to
Israel, as well as stimulating an interest in America of the Jew¬
ish cultural heritage.
