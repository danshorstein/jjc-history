PESAH (Passover), the Feast of Unleavened Bread—an eight-
day celebration of the deliveranc of the Jews from Egyptian
slavery. During the traditional Seder meal, on the first two
nights of Passover, Jewish families read the Haggadah (a ritual recita¬
tion of the story of the Exodus). Throughout the Passover periods, foods
are selected according to special dietary laws. Instead of bread, Jews
eat matzoh (unleavened bread) to recall the hasty flight from Egypt,
when there was no time to allow the bread to rise. (Hence, our matzoh
symbol.)
