LEWIS MEISEL
To reflect the work of the Agency and to avoid the term
"welfare,” we changed our name, the fourth name change
since our organization as the United Jewish Charities in 1917.
Some find our initials JFCS easier to use. We have become
used to many variations and will answer to which form you
prefer, the long or short.
We expect by the end of the year to be in our new build¬
ing, probably in southside. We sold our building and will
have to be out by Feb. 1963. Added to our new look are our
revised by-laws which spell out specifically our current services:
Case work or counseling by a trained stafif for family groups
or individuals; child welfare to maintain Jewish children in
their own home with their own family, and if not possible to
provide foster home, institutional or residential care of Jewish
children; assistance to clients where such assistance is not other¬
wise available; .adoption; casework and admission for the River
Garden; contact with Jewish prisoners at Raiford Prison; assist¬
ance for Jewish transients; resettling Jewish immigrants in the
Jacksonville area; social welfare action, family life education
and public relations programs, training and education for social
work; research; strengthening of family functioning or stability.
Let us remind you that we are beneficiaries of the Com¬
munity Chest-United Fund and the United Jewish Welfare
Fund. Your support makes our services possible. If you can
use our services, please feel free to call us.
