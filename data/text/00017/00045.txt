The Charles Palot Chapter has accomplished many things this past year. A successful
Membership Drive Party started off the year. . . . On October 25 th we held our Friday night
Sabbath Service. Members of the Club helped Sisterhood at the Bazaar and participated in
the program and the selling of refreshments at the Hanukah celebration. . . . Last year’s
President, Judy Safer, represented the local chapter at the National Convention of United
Synagogue Youth in Chicago, Ill. Eight members attended ,the Southeastern Regional Con¬
vention in Charleston, S. C. . . . A Certificate of Merit was presented for our outstanding
work in the Muscular Dystrophy Drive. . . . The Charles Palot Club owes much of its
success to the able assistance of our Advisor, Mrs. M. J. Reiser.

Major's Pre-Teens and Tween-Agers
Two new clubs were organized this year for future members of the United
Synagogue Youth Clubs. . . . The Majors’ Pre-Teens was organized by Mrs. M. J.
Reiser and Mrs. Esther Fishier for 7th Graders who will be eligible next year
for the Charles Palot Club. Several social events were held. . . . The 5th and
6th Graders formed a club, the Tween-Agers, under the direction of Mrs.
Bernard Glossman, assisted by Mrs. I. Gartner. Social affairs included a Get-
together Party, Purim ball, Hobo party, picnic and installation of Officers.
Meetings are held every month under the sponsorship of the Charles Palot Club.
