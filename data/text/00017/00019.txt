Chairman of the Board
This space in the Year Book is made available to me as Chair¬
man of the Board. I appreciate the honor and the opportunity. In
this particular capacity, however, I have little to add to what the
President of the Congregation placed before you in his well
chosen words on the previous page.
Let me have a few heart-to-heart words with you as Treasurer
of the Building Fund. At the present moment this function con¬
cerns me more than any other in the Center.
As you come to the building for services, take a look along the
Silver Street side. You will see how far we have progressed with
the construction of our new Classroom Building. If you look
around downstairs in the Synagogue Building, you will also ob¬
serve extensive remodeling.
This construction work was made necessary by the growth of
the Congregation and the increase of activities. Our children need
the extra space and accommodations.
Your share in this work goes without saying. We need your
contribution. Bills are mounting up. If you have not made your
pledge to the Building Fund, let the High Holiday season awaken
a sense of responsibility. The Torah Light Fund honoring names
of children and grandchildren is a worthwhile cause to support.
I want to wish each and every one of you a most happy New
Year. Help make the New Year happy for your children and
grandchildren.
ROBERT J. GORDON
