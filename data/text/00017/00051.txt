GIRL SCOUTS AND BROWNIES
Girl Scout Troop 212 and Brownie Troop 346 are sponsored by the
Center Sisterhood. Their leaders and co-leaders are happy to announce
that they had an interesting and profitable year.
The Girl Scouts met every Thursday after school on the grounds
of the J. C. A. in Arlington. They participated in the Scout Thinking
Day. It was an international program and they wore costumes of different
lands, presented a dance, and made an Israel flag. They participated in
the drive for funds for the Humane Society, painted tiles for Mother’s
Day presents, went horseback riding and took part in many outdoor
sports.
The Brownies were active this year also. They collected toys for
sick children at one of the hospitals, made special gifts for their parents,
held several cook-outs at their leader’s home, enjoyed a hay ride and
outing at a local dairy.
Both Brownie and Girl Scout troops had cook-outs at Boone Park.
They participated in the Ground Breaking Ceremony at the Center
for the new School Building. They took part in Friday Night Services
and later served as hostesses after the services. Arrangements were made
by Mrs. Irving Kramer, former Girl Scout Chairman, Mrs. Sidney Still¬
man who serves on the Girl Scout Board, and Mrs. Louis Slott, former
Girl Scout Leader.
