Dr. Sam Witten
The twelve years of service that the River Garden Hebrew Home for the Aged has given to the aged and infirm Jewish people of
Florida has given all of us a sense of security that those in need o f care can find a haven at the Home.
With the opening of the new addition last year the capacity of the Home has been increased to 75 residents. Five different wings
have been established in order to give individualized and personal care to the residents. Three of these wings provide nursing services
24 hours a day in order to meet their physical, mental and emotional disabilities. The other two wings have a homelike setting which
does not primarily feature the atmosphere of a nursing home. Recovery rooms have been provided in the nursing area to take care
of temporary illness for them.
In order to meet other temporary and varying needs of the aged the Home has inaugurated a program of Short Term Admissions.
This includes service to those who need limited convalescent care or who are recuperating from a serious illness or surgery; vacation
program for the aged whose families plan to be away from the city; and observance of the religious holidays for those whose religious
scruples prohibit traveling on the Holidays.
These added programs have broadened the scope of service of a modern Home for the Aged whose primary purpose is to serve
its community in as many ways as possible.
