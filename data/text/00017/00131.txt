HARRY GENDZIER
For the Jacksonville Jewish Community Council, 1958 has indeed been a year of decision. Faced with the bombing of the Jack¬
sonville Jewish Center, the Council gave the community the type of leadership and solidarity needed in such a crisis. The manner in
which every Jewish organization in Jacksonville supported the Council has set a pattern for the nation and has resulted in widespread
respect everywhere for Jacksonville Jewry.
During this year our Executive Secretary, Ben Stark, resigned his position and we can now announce that the Executive Committee
has filled this post with Mr. Harold E. Katz, whose picture appears on this page with that of our President. Mr. Katz comes to Jackson¬
ville with an enviable record at Nashville and Los Angeles. Mr. and Mrs. Katz and their son and daughter will be with us sometime
during August.
Under President Gendzier’s dynamic leadership, Jacksonville Jewry will see a greatly widened horizon of community activities
which the Council will inaugurate. Studies are being continued on all phases of Council work and should result in a widened scope of
Community activity with more people participating in Council afiairs and a real upsurge in community and individual responsibility
in fund-raising for our local, national and overseas causes.
