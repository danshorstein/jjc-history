The Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah is composed of approximately 800 Jewish women who meet monthly to serve together in
the ranks of the American movement. Meeting in the afternoon on the second Tuesday of each month, or in the evening with the Han¬
nah Senesch Group on the second Monday, Hadassah’s members join in efforts to affect the National Hadassah program of health and vo¬
cational education services to Israel; and as the second largest Womens group in the city, the local chapter participates actively in the
community life of Jacksonville.
With more than 315,000 members in 12 50 chapters and units in the United States, Alaska, and Puerto Rico, Hadassah is the world’s
largest Zionist organization. In Israel, Hadassah maintains seven hospitals, a school of nursing, the Hebrew University-Hadassah Medi¬
cal School, the New Hadassah-Hebrew University Medical Center, Youth Aliyah training farms, and the general youth medical services
emanating from the Jerusalem child health centers, together with a network of vocational education facilities which range from printing
to hotel management and fashion design. In cooperation with Z.O.A. Hadassah supports the Jewish National Fund for the rehabilitation
of the land of Israel and Zionist youth work through Young Judaea Clubs.
Highlights of the coming year will be the opening luncheon to be held September 9, the Membership Tea, an event of October 27
which will feature the new Israeli Fashion Show. The year’s activities will also include a "Dollars for Linen” shower for Hadassah hospital
supplies, a contributors party and the annual Donor luncheon in April.
HANNAH SENESCH GROUP
The Hannah Senesch Evening Group of Hadassah as part
of Jacksonville Chapter of Hadassah has given those women
who can attend only evening meetings an opportunity to
participate fully in National Hadassah’s diversified program.
Membership is open to all Jewish women.
