NORMAN H. FREEDMAN
This year in conjunction with National B’nai B’rith, the A. B. Weil Lodge No. 800 will strive to increase its membership by 100
or more new members.
Our program for the year consists of several very vital community functions: the Little League Baseball Teams sponsored by
B’nai B’rith have been very active with members acting as coaches and managers and providing prizes for the winning teams; in con¬
junction with Brotherhood Week this fall, B’nai B’rith will sponsor an Essay contest in the High Schools; the Scholarship Loan Fund
is presently sponsoring several worthy recipients and we hope to be in a position to handle additional recipients. The B nai B rith
sponsors Anti-Defamation League, B’nai B’rith youth organizations such as A.Z.A., B.B. Girls, and Hillel.
Luncheon Meetings are held at the George Washington Hotel every two weeks. We have had very fine guest speakers. Come and
join us.

MRS. IRVING RESNICK
B’nai B’rith Women of Jacksonville have made great strides in membership due to the varied program which includes Anti-
Defamation, Philanthropy, Israel, Hillel or B’nai B’rith Youth.
Allocations are subscribed to nationwide projects which include the Leo N. Levi and the National Jewish Hospital, Bellefaire Chil¬
dren’s Home in Cleveland, the Children’s Home in Israel, the Great Martyrs Forest and two colonies in Israel,: Local projects include
work with the Pinecastle School for Retarded Children, Muscular Dystrophy Association, the Cancer Society, Tuberculosis and Cere¬
bral Palsy Associations and River Garden Home for the Aged.
Our youth organizations, the A.Z.A. and B’nai B’rith Girls’, are learning the B’nai B’rith principles for moral, ethical and health¬
ful living.
