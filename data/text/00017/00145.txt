Israeli Dance Team at Bikkurim Festival
Jacksonville Young Judaea concluded a successful year under the direction of the Youth Commission with Mrs. Norman Moss
as Chairman and Mrs. Irving Brukner as City Director. There are three Junior Clubs, the Chaverim, Israelites and Judaeanettes, and
two Senior Clubs, the Zionettes and the Estherettes.
After a summer session at the Puldy home on Lake Brooklyn, the fall season opened with; excellent attendance. The St|te of Israel
was chosen as the theme for the year. The various clubs held an Oneg Shabbat, presented playlets, participated in fund-raising af¬
fairs and held a number of social events.
The Junior Clubs presented a Parents Day Program at the Center with a skit on Israel’s 10th Anniversary, Israeli dancing and
poems. Due to inclement weather the annual Israeli Fair at the River Garden Home was not held. The Young Judaea Sabbath took
place at the Jewish Center. Rosalind Portnoy gave the sermonette explaining Young Judaea’s activities in the city.
The 6th annual Bikkurim Festival and Banquet was held at the Center. Scholarships were given to Roxanne Mack and Byron
Kolitz /or Tel Yehudah and a scholarship to Linda Gruenberg for Machonah Avodah.
Israel dance groups were invited to perform at the Stephen Foster Memorial Festival in May.
We wish to take this opportunity to thank the following parents who served as leaders: Mrs. B. L. Friedman, Mrs. J. Parker,
Mrs. Abe Newman and Mrs. Charles Kluglose and the co-leaders, Miss Annette Gottlieb, Miss Karen Armel and Miss Linda Gruenberg.
