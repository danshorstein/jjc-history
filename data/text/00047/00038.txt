Blanche Slott Retires
It is with mixed emotions that we celebrated the retirement of
BLANCHE SLOTT. She has been a devoted employee for over forty
years, beginning as a volunteer and climbing to the position of Admini¬
strative Director. As Catering Director, Blanche has made arrangements
for life cycle celebrations for many congregants. She has been a friend
and family to us all. She distinguished herself as Sisterhood President
for three terms; organized three Girl Scout.Troops and five Brownie
troops; and served on the Center Board of Directors as Chairman of
the Youth Commission. Blanche has touched all of our lives.
We will miss Blanche very much, but are happy for her and the new
lifestyle she will enjoy in the years to come* We look forward to seeing
her at Center functions as a guest. We wish her the very best that life
has to offer, for she has given us the very best she had to offer.
Honoring Blanche were: Bob Gast, past executive director,
Lois Peltz-co-chair of the day, nephew, Ron Levin, Blanche,
Marsha Pollock, co-chair, and former executive director,
Bob Fischer.
