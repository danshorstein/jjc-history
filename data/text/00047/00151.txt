On the first day of creation, God said:
uYehi ohr — Let there be light! Vayehi
ohr — And there was light! and God saw
the light that it was good ”
Today we, too, need all the light of knowl¬
edge and understanding we can muster and
must make it ‘good’ for ourselves as Jews
and for all humanity. “With one heart
and one purpose” our National theme for
the coming year, we will strive as Synagogue
women to seek cultural enrichment and Jew¬
ish identification through the ethical teach¬
ings of our heritage.
Our goal is to continue to build a strong,
knowledgeable, vital Jewish community . .
right here for ourselves and for our people everywhere. We do this not
only by promoting our own educational endeavors but also by promoting
the welfare of our congregation and its schools. We sponsor educational
and cultural activities. We foster Jewish sentiments and observances in the
home. We encourage our youth with guidance and scholarships. We
support the education of Rabbis, Cantors and Teachers by participating in
the continuous building of the Jewish Theological Seminary of America.
Sisterhood can give meaning to life by encouraging its members to feel,
to think, to experience and to stand for values. Together we can realize
the many challenges which stand before us. Together we will try to help
ourselves and each other to understand and learn how to respond to make
our Sisterhood become alive with a positive accent on Jewish living, educa¬
tion and involvement.
May the coming year bring you health, happiness and peace.
