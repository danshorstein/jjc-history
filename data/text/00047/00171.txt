AUTO — Adults United to Organize
Sue Ann Blumenthal
President
Parents of students in the Jacksonville Jewish Center’s afternoon and Sunday
schools came together two years ago to establish Adults United to Organize —
AUTO. Its objective: to address problems and issues unique to children enrolled
in those schools; to build a closer liaison with the teachers; and to schedule
educational events and outings that involve the children, their families, and their
teachers. All three goals are being met.
Highlights of AUTO activities during the past year included Havdallah meet¬
ings, one of which featured a talk by Lili Meier, a survivor of the Auschwitz
concentration camp; a Shabbat weekend campout and a Sunday day trip, both
to Goldhead State Park; and a teachers appreciation day.
These activities have brought AUTO families closer together and enhanced a
sense of Jewish awareness. One visible result is increased parental attendance
and participation in Saturday morning Junior Congregation services.
Making up AUTO’s current leadership are Sue Ann Blumenthal, president;
Ellen Rubens, chairman of the board and past president; Harriet Kaplan,
faculty representative; Allan Cohen, Junior Congregation representative;
Mitchell Perin, treasurer; Cheryl LeBeau and Susan Frankel, hospitality;
Henry Blumenthal, reporter; Linda Schuster, field trip coordinator; Betty
Edwards, corresponding secretary; Debra Penna and Arlene Rademan,
membership; Jan Klein and George Rubens, historians; Rabbi Dov Ken-
tof, spiritual leader; and phone coordinator, George Rubens.
