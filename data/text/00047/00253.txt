The Family Council, which came into being
this past year, reaches out to mothers and fa¬
thers of present and future students in all of
the Jacksonville Jewish Center’s educational
programs. It seeks to increase parental in¬
volvement in the learning process.
Some Center parents are privileged to watch
their children build a reservoir of knowledge
about their heritage that is running deeper
than their own. But on the other side of this
coin i$ the consequent realization that rein¬
forcement at home of these Jewish values and
traditions may be missing or incomplete. The
best of intentions cannot make up for weak¬
nesses in the education level attained by the
parents.
In the course of the year the Family Council
will be sponsoring many activities designed for
parental involvement. Even when the parents
are knowledgeable and observant, their atten¬
dance at these functions will be a message to
their children that their mothers and fathers
care about what they are learning, and believe
in it.
Henry Blumenthal
