Early Childhood Department
I am honored to be serving as Director of the Jacksonville Jewish Center
Early Childhood Department at this most exciting time in the school’s history.
We are going through a period of unprecedented growth both in enrollment
and facilities.
As we celebrate this transition to a new stage in the life of our school, we
also pause to consider and acknowledge the roots from which we have grown.
While we are proud of our school at Third and Silver Streets and were enthu¬
siastic about our move to Crown Point Road twelve years ago, we are excep¬
tionally proud of our school’s new building growth and position in the com¬
munity.
We feel we have succeeded in enhancing Jewish identity and transmitting it
to many generations. We believe the Jacksonville Jewish Center Early Child¬
hood Department, with the continued support of the Jacksonville Jewish
Center congregation, will become increasingly important to these families
Marilyn Sandler who desire a quality Jewish preschool.
Throughout the school’s history, people have stepped forward to contribute their time, energy and
resources whenever they were needed. We are today blessed with a broad base of support in the Jew¬
ish community, as well as an incredibly talented and diverse group of parents, who have invested their
most precious resources — their children — in our institution.
Our survival as a Jewish people ultimately depends on how well we teach our children.
It is rewarding to me to be able to spend my time on this most worthwhile endeavor. Participating
in a school which is continually “investing in excellence” is an exciting and energizing experience.
