Mr. Alan Gordon, on behalf of his mother,
Mrs. Robert J. Gordon, and his brother, Samuel
Gordon, presents the Robert Gordon Leadership
Grant for 1967 to Harry Sinoff. This scholarship,
begun in 1965, in memory of one of the most dis¬
tinguished lay leaders of the Center, is awarded
to the student or students with the most promising
leadership potential and provides for attendance at
Camp Ramah.
This summer Mark Gendzier, Barbara Talpa-
lar, George Lipp and Larry Browdy attended Camp
Ramah in New England (Palmer) at Bridgeport,
Conn. Harry Sinoff and Ricki Gross attended
Camp Ramah in the Berkshires at Wingdale, New
York, and Jonathan Lipnick was at Camp Ramah
in Conover, Wisconsin. Two of our students, Mar¬
tin Berman and Carol Gross, were part of the
Ramah Israel Seminar.
Ramah has proved its value not only for the
educational benefits but also for the intangible
benefits of attitude and spirited participation in
religious activities.
