The 6 th and 7 th graders of Pre-US Y reorganized
this year with the intentions of making 1967-68 Tops
in membership and activity.
In April the group met at the Campsite for a day
of athletics and also on introduction to the purpose
and functions of U.S.Y. It was decided to wait until
September to vote on officers but meanwhile a steer¬
ing committee was formed.
The group presented the Model Pesah Seder for
the Sunday School with the assistance of Mrs. J. M.
Reiser. This first activity certainly showed the organ¬
ization’s leadership and enthusiasm that these Pre-
US Y’ers have to work with.
The year was closed with a cook-out and swim¬
ming party at the Campsite and the promise of a very
enjoyable year ahead.
The advisors, Phyllis Glossman and Wendy Klein,
would like to thank the Pre-US Y’ers for making re¬
organizing a worthwhile venture. Thanks are also due
the CYL sponsors: Debbie Morris, Sherri Silverman
and Randy Silverberg.

The Jewish Single Adults Club of Jacksonville,
sponsored by the Jewish Community Council, has
been active in Jacksonville for the past year. The pur¬
pose of the Club is to bring together single Jewish
adults who wish to mix socially with other single
people. The Club also brings together new people
who have just come into the city.
Jacksonville has a definite need for such a club.
There are a great number of single adults in the city
who would ordinarily have no other way of meeting
Jewish single men and women.
The Club in the past has held dances, dinners and
barbeques. An event is held about once a month with
invitations going as far as Atlanta and Tampa. People
come from Tallahassee, Gainesville, Daytona and many
other cities around the northern part of Florida and
southern part of Georgia.
Come and join the fun, help us plan an event, meet
new people and renew old friendships!
