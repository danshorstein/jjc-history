If you were the wisest man in the world and could have but one wish
granted you, what would you pray for?
FOR PEACE?
FOR POWER?
FOR LONG LIFE?
FOR WEALTH?
FOR LEARNING?
The question is not hypothetical.
It was asked of King Solomon in his day.
He answered none of the above; instead — if there were only one thing
he could have — he asked to be able to tell the difference between good
and evil.
He asked for an understanding heart.
Why?
Today is the birthday of the world.
Whatever was our yesterday, today as every day we start anew.
Will we — as individuals, as families, as nations—
meet problems today we have not yet solved?

Must we, today, face evil as well as good?
Of course we must.
But can this evil defeat the good?
It cannot.
For the good is stronger than evil.
We can, therefore, without despair, without discouragement,
set ourselves to any problem we face today and tomorrow.
We need only realize that ours is not to complete the task,
yet neither can we neglect it.
