Troop 14 came away from the Ross Allen Jam¬
boree in Silver Springs with a first place ribbon. As
part of the theme “Follow the Rugged Road’' we con¬
structed a lean-to and several small animal traps. The
Troop also came away from the Mohawk District
Camporee in Hilliard with a second place ribbon, de¬
spite the fact we lost 150 points for late registration.
Four Scouts earned the Ner Tamid award and
the presentation was made by our Rabbi at Scout Sab¬
bath Services in February: Bruce Darlow, Mark Gend-
zier, Roy Lasris and Alan Trager. Dr. Harry Yergin
received the Shofar Award for outstanding service to
the synagogue and to Scouting. He is the first adult
in our community to receive this award.
Jeffrey Goldstein was the sole recipient of the
hard earned rank of Eagle Scout, the highest rank in
Scouting. He attained the rank during the summer and
presentation was made in September.
The troop went on its first real hike in quite a
while last February. We spent our first night at Camp
Leitman and after breaking camp the following morn¬
ing, we walked the ten miles to the Center Day Camp.
During the February hike we walked through a cold
biting wind and braved it through a low of 23 degrees
that night. In May we camped at Gold Head and saw
the temperature soar to 100 degrees. We had the du¬
bious distinction of having picked both the hottest
and coldest days of the year for our camp-outs.
