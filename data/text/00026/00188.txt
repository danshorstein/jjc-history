“The cycle of Brotherly Love,
No greater reward can be received than the joy and
satisfaction resulting from service to our brothers and
sisters. By such effort our own B‘nai B’rith and the
entire community will benefit.
B’nai B’rith is a world-wide organization of many
facets, respected and revered by all, and joined with
dignity by Jews everywhere.
The following are some of the far-flung activities
of B’nai B’rith:
1. Contribution to citizenship by training youth, co¬
operation with other groups.
2. Hillel Foundation—the means of moving the young
people on campus back to Jewish life.
3. Vocational service — assisting Jews toward econ¬
omic development.
4. Patriotism — B’nai B’rith went into action from
the Civil War through each of the conflicts. Volun¬
teer workers served in bringing hope and rehabilita¬
tion to the armed forces and veterans.
5.B’nai B’rith Youth Organization — Founded in 1924
because of a concern over the vast number of young
Jews who were drifting away from Judaism. The
leaders of tomorrow are trained in this organization.
6. The Anti-Defamation League, Defender of Rights,
was founded in 1913 by a brilliant lawyer. His
theory of understanding through enlightenment is
the foundation for the League’s approach to cur¬
rent problems. Must men hate?

The Samuel B. Wolfson B’nai B’rith Lodge No.
2588 is the newest member of the B’nai B’rith family
in Jacksonville. Our lodge was founded January 1,
1966. We received our charter June 25, 1966 with a
charter membership of 100 men. This past year has
seen the Wolfson chapter hold a splendid and success¬
ful fund raising affair November, B’nai B’rith Anni¬
versary celebration with the presentation of a cake for
children of the Hope Haven Hospital, and one of the
outstanding highlights was the conducting of a “Career
Day” program at Wolfson High School. This program
received such enthusiasm from the high schools that it
is going to be an annual program with lodge members
donating their time to counsel prospective college
students .
Many of our members attended the State B’nai
B’rith Convention in Daytona Beach, Fla. Robert I.
Roth was elected to the State office of Vice-President.
