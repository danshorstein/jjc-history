The Center Men’s Club has tried to follow in the
footsteps of its predecessors while adding new pro¬
gram ideas.
A number of dinner meetings were held which
evidenced the broad interests of the group. Judge
Marion Gooding was the guest speaker at the initial
meeting. The annual Paid-up Membership Dinner
Dance had Congressman Charles Bennett as guest.
Rabbi Jerome Lipnick and Cantor A. Marton turned
one meeting into a Passover Institute, furnishing the
men with the details of how to conduct a Seder rele¬
vant and meaningful. As usual the annual Hanukkah
Party left everyone with a good feeling.
One of the new programs launched was a luncheon
Torah study group. This took place at the Hotel
Roosevelt and was led by Rabbi Lipnick. The sub¬
ject of discussions was “The Prophets of Israel. What
do they have to say to us today?”. One of the events
most looked forward to was the annual Sunday School
Picnic which was held at the Campsite and which drew
a large and enthusiastic crowd.
A series of discussions was conducted on the role
S. ROBERT SHORSTEIN,
President
of the Club as the men’s organization of the congre¬
gation. We would like to consider the Men’s Club as
a cultural, religious and social arm of the men of the
congregation, similar to the role of the Sisterhood as
the organization of the women.
Once more the Men’s Club is looking forward to
a year of increased activity, enlarged membership, and
greater service to the Center.
