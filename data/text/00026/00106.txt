The members of the Charles Palot Club have been
busily engaged fulfilling the three-fold pupose of United
Synagogue Youth; namely Learning, helping others
and having a good time. With a total membership of
70 teenagers, this has been one of our most successful
years.
To fulfill the first fold, learning, we heard speak¬
ers on Jewish education, Israel, and held study ses¬
sions. For our second fold, helping others, we held ser¬
vices at River Garden and raised money for Building
Spiritual Bridges, which is USY’s national fund-rais¬
ing project. We also participated in the Hanukah and
Purim Fairs. For ourselves, the third fold, we held
dances, parties, dinner and socials.
The Charles Palot Club was well represented at
the past L.T.I., National Convention and Regional
Convention. This summer, Karen Gartner, a C.P.C/er
went on U.S.Y. on Wheels across the United States.
Certainly much praise and many thanks are due
our advisor, Miss Evelyn Brauner, for her efforts on
behalf of our club and to the past officers, who worked
so hard to make this club as great as it is.
