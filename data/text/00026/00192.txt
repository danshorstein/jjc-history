David Marón, Alan Harris, President,
and Jerry Shapiro
This past year, under the able leadership of Godols
Henry Rogozinski and Bob Feldman, has been a proud
and successful one for AZA. We have not only tripled
our membership, but more important, tripled the
spirit among our brothers. At the last regional conven¬
tion, we walked off with the “Most Spirited Chapter”
award and Bob Roulston, the James Madison of our
new constitution, won the Oratory Contest. Our big¬
gest honor came when our own Henry Rogozinski was
elected Regional Aleph Godol.
AZA is composed of a five fold system with a
phase to please everyone. Under the religious fold we
held an AZA Sabbath at the Center and held several
discussions; under culture, we saw several entertain¬
ing and educational movies; under community service,
we collected over $500 for Muscular Distrophy and
several hundred paper back books for our men in
Vietnam; under Athletic, we participated in several
sports and competed with B’nai B’rith in a softball
game; and under social, we held several stag get-
togethers and many parties — including the Sweet¬
heart Ball at the end of the year.
Our special thanks go to Mike Sternfield for pub¬
lishing such a fine newspaper. Our deepest gratitude
extends to our life member and past advisor, Howard
Margolis, for the fine job he performed.

GATORS ON THE GO
This past year for the B’nai B’rith Girls of
Jacksonville has been a wonderful and exciting year
through the leadership of Linda Hilsenrad.
Gators B. B. G., No. 398 of Jacksonville walked
away with all honors at the past Regional Conven¬
tion. To name two such awards are Best All Round
Chapter and Most Spirited. Besides those awards,
Carolyn Snyder won first in Oratory and Pat Kowitz
won in Storyteller.
In addition to conventions our Chapter has at¬
tended, we have taken part in many activities that
include Brownie Sales, raffles, People to People,
Heaven and Hell Dances, car washes, and many other
activities not to foregt the usual Muscular Dystrophy
for which we received national recognition by collect¬
ing over $500 in one afternoon. We also hosted a
workshop for 50 BBYO’s from four states which was a
great success.
We ended a wonderful year with the Installation
Luncheon at which the Debra Hoffman Best Girl
Award was presented to Fran Kass and the Susan Hil¬
senrad. Best MIT Award was given to Eve Aptheker.
We must not forget our Life Member Ceremany at the
beach and the End of the Year Party at Camp Leitman
to bring another year to a close that our Chapter will
never forget.
