GIRL SCOUTS
The Setzer Youth Education program is proud that our Girl Scout program has grown in the last few years.
Our youngest troop, Daisy Troop 1367, spent the year bonding, learning about working together and earning badges,
petals and leaves. Our Brownies, Troop 1371, also had fun learning all kinds of new skills as they earned badges
and did projects in our community as part of a Girl Scout Leadership Journey. The 4th & 5th grade girls, Junior
Troop 1372, tried a variety of new experiences and learned new skills to earn badges. They chose a variety of
issues they cared about, then developed projects that helped to make a difference as part of a Girl Scout Leadership
Journey. Of course all our troops had another successful year selling Girl Scout cookies around the community.
What makes the Girl Scout Troops special at the Jacksonville Jewish Center is the ability to combine Jewish
perspectives with the scouting program. The skills that our girls develop through scouting help to empower them
as they grow while maintaining their Jewish identity.
