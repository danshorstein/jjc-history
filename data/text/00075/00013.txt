Gilbert Kleiner, FSA
Executive Director
The Torah is full of stories where our ancestors journeyed from one place
to another to begin a new adventure and perform sacred duties. Many of us
have journeyed from one city to begin a new adventure here in Jacksonville.
My wife, Carol, and I have begun our new adventure and have completed our
journey to our new spiritual home here at the Jacksonville Jewish Center.
The warmth and friendship that we have experienced in our short time
here have made a great impression on us. Everyone has opened their arms to
welcome us as if we were family. The many connections that have been made
through family and friends from our former home in Baltimore are amazing.
One example is when a young mother came up to me at a Center event and
I introduced myself to her. She then told me her name and then a story about
her friend from Baltimore who knows me. Her friend is a close friend of one
of my daughters from childhood, and happened to be a young woman I have known since she was a young
child. The warmth expressed by this young mother and her family was another confirmation as to why I
am very fortunate and glad to be a new part of the Center family.
As we begin our new journey with the High Holy Days, I invite you, your family and your friends to
stop by to visit. And I invite you to join me in my adventure here at the Jacksonville Jewish Center by
finding out what excites you about being Jewish. Let’s begin that adventure together. I want to meet you,
talk with you and learn from you as to how we can continue to make the Center the place where your
spiritual needs will be found.
Carol and I and our families in California, New York and Connecticut wish you and your families a
L’Shanah Tovah Umetukah (A Happy and Sweet New Year).
B’Shalom and See you at the Center.
Gilbert Kleiner, FSA
