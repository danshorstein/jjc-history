ISRAEL: ANCESTRAL HOMELAND
This summer, 26 members of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center community
traveled to our ancestral homeland along
with Hazzan Holzer to explore our Jewish
heritage and learn how Israel is thriving in
countless ways.
Organized by the tour group Keshet,
the itinerary focused on sharing the wide
variety of flavors Israel has to offer- the
history of our people 2,000 years ago,
the road to independence, the struggle for
religious freedom, and the desire to be at
the peak of the high tech industry. Along the
way, our group bonded through camel rides
and parallel parking-buses, while ensuring
throughout that we would drink copious
amounts of water. Our guide, Morgi, gave
a well-rounded description of Israel today,
while being an expert educator on the
state’s history as well as the history of the
Holocaust. Highlights included: finding
2,000 year old coins, trying marzipan
rugelach for the first time, celebrating Ethan
and Christina Levine’s B’nei mitzvah in an
egalitarian service adjacent to the kotel,
watching Norma Bell hike up Masada,
singing at a kumzitz in the Golan, and
bonding as one kehilla. L’hitra-ot- we all
hope to be back to Israel soon!!
