SOCIAL JUSTICE
Our Social Justice Committee, known as Rodef Tzedek (pursuer of justice), had a banner year working with our
interfaith affiliate ICARE (Interfaith Coalition for Action, Reconciliation and Empowerment), to promote positive
change for our community. As the only synagogue among its 40 members, the Jacksonville Jewish Center’s team,
led by Kim Glasgal, attended community problem meetings as well as the great Nehemiah Assembly. Our members
fought for the rights of everyone in Jacksonville, dealing with issues of crime, support for our mental health services,
among others. Among ICARE’s achievements in the past year include:
• Our Superintendent expanding restorative justice to 144 schools. That is up from 97 last year. Over
8,000 students have benefited from the program. School suspensions have gone down and so have
violent crimes on campus.
• Our Mayor supported a wealth building model that will start employee owned businesses in Northwest
Jacksonville.
• Our Sheriff agreed to expand civil citations for nonviolent youth and do a public service announcement.
Since his commitment, civil citations have doubled. To date, 200 youth have gone through restorative
justice instead of being arrested. The public service announcement should come out this month.
• The Sheriff also committed to increase funding for the Jacksonville Reentry Center to $900,000 a year
to seek state grants that could increase the budget to $2.5 million by the 2020.
If you’d like to get more involved in social justice work for Jacksonville and the greater community,
please contact us!
