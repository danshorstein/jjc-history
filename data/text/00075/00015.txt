BE A TEAM PLAYER
My dear friends,
Fortunately I am long on thought, but short on words. Last November, I joined
a new team. Team Jacksonville Jewish Center. I did not know the other players,
coaches or fans. I joined apparently one year too soon as my home team finally won
the NBA title. Who knew?
My new team has welcomed me with open arms and offers of help. And about
the only skills I can’t improve on are my computer / technology statistics. God bless
Danielle.
Overall it is such a fantastic group of staff that really know and care about helping
each other. I could write for pages and pages about the wonderful atmosphere
regarding the Jacksonville Jewish Center. It is an exemplary place for prayer and
learning; for adults and children; for Jews and non-Jews.
I suspect the longer I write, the sooner you will stop reading. Allow me to say that one of the things that
makes a community unique is its ability to work together and helping each other in needful times. Yes, you know
minyan. We need you. Thank God that we are here to help you in your times of need. Please! Be there for us.
Be an important part of our lives when we need you. Please help us make a minyan. It doesn’t make us religious.
It does make us human and thoughtful. It’s not someone else’s job to make minyan, it’s yours. We are all busy,
we are all tired. Many of us have families and community obligations. I’m just asking you to put us on the list
once in a while to help others.
OK, you are done by now. So I will leave you with the hope that my wife will be joining me soon to help me
enjoy my life here and can see for herself that of which I speak. If this is not long enough, please let me know. I
will be ready to write a part II.
Shanah Tovah,
David Wolinsky
David Wolinsky
Ritual Director
