BOY SCOUT TROOP 14
Troop 14 continued to carry on the tradition of Jewish Scouting in a very successful 97th year for the Troop.
The Scouts attended summer camp at Boonesboro Village, a living history program at Camp Daniel Boone, North
Carolina where they experienced life skills and crafts that were common in the late-1700’s. Service projects for the
year included creation of an archery game and helping children camp at the North Florida Council Special Needs
Camporee and building an indoor garden for Hubbard House Thrift Store. A joint campout and hike with Cub Scout
Pack 14 at Fort Clinch was both fun and educational. Sam Goldstein became the 83rd Eagle Scout from Troop 14.
His Eagle Scout project was to design and plant a vegetable and butterfly garden for Chabad preschool. Many thanks
go to the Fleet family for establishing a fund at the Center this year for continued support of Scouting. Scouting is
open to all boys from 1st grade until 18 years of age. For more information, contact Scoutmaster Allan Andrew at
aandrew @ alum .mit .edu.
