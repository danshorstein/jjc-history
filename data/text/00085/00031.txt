TROOP 14
Under the guidance of Scoutmaster Joe Mizrahi and
Assistant Scoutmasters David Barnett and Erwin Gendzier,
Troop 14 met weekly during the 1946-47 school year to
increase their knowledge of scouting and scout craft and
advance individually to the highest scout ranks. In line
with this, Jack Eff became an Eagle Scout and Stanley
Kantor a Life Scout.
On Parent’s Night the charter was presented to the
Troop by Joel Richards, District Scout Commissioner.
The year’s highlight was a scout banquet given in the
Center auditorium Sunday, May 18, 1947 with approxi¬
mately three hundred present. All scout elements of the
Center were in attendance and the principal speaker was
Scout Executive, M. G. Boswell, of the North Florida
Council.
Many of the boys spent the first week in July at Camp
Echockotee.
Members of the Troop Committee are: Robert Gordon,
Chairman, Harry Katz, Perry Kantor, Mesod Bono, Rabbi
Tofiehl, Harry Eff.
NATHAN FRIEDLIN, CUBMASTER
JOSEPH MIZRAHI, SCOUTMASTER
CUB PACK 14
Under the guidance of Cub Master, Nathan Friedlin,
and Assistant Cub Master, Hyman Selber, the Cub Pack
made strides in training the young boys for a future in
scouting. Meetings were held weekly and knowledge
of scouting and scout craft was imparted by capable
instructors.
During the year many events were held in the form of
picnics and outings to various places of interest. The
cubs joined with the boy scout and girl scout organizations
in the mass scout banquet given on Sunday, May 18, 1947.
Members of the Cub Committee are: Charles Feinstein,
Chairman; David Torn, Mrs. Perry Safer, Ansel Chepenik,
Phil Sher and Mrs. Charles Feinstein, Chief Den Mother.
