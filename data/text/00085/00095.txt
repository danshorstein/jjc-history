Lag B’Omer (thirty-third day of the counting of the Omer).
These days are called Sefriah days when festive celebrations are
limited because of sad memories of oppressive treatment of the
Jews during this period of the year in the days of the Romans,
and in the Middle Ages. Lag B’Omer, according to tradition, was
the date of a victory by Bar Kokhba in his struggle against the
Romans, and these restrictions are suspended.
