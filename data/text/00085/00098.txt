Shabbat Hazon derives its name from the Haftorah of that day,
day, Isaiah, I, 1-27, which begins with the word Hazon—“Vision”.
Tisha B’Ab (ninth day of Ab) is a fast day which marks the
destruction of the first Temple in Jerusalem by the Babylonians
(586 B.G.E.) and also of the second Temple by the Romans
(70 B.C.E.) (*When the fast occurs on a Saturday, it is observed
on Sunday).
Shabbat Nahamu derives its name from the Haftorah of that
day, Isaiah XI, 1-26, which begins with the word Nahamu
“Comfort ye” read on the Sabbath after Tisha B’Ab.
