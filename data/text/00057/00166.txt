Bruce Adler
Sunday, October 26, 1997
Often described as a “Yiddish”
Danny Kaye, Bruce Adler is a
highly seasoned showman. The son
of Yiddish vaudeville legends,
Julius Adler and Henrietta
Jacobson, Adler is an exuberant,
joyful, power-packed talent who
can do anything from a knee-
rupturing Russian kazachok, to Gene Kelly’s umbrella-swinging
choreography from Singin ’ in the Rain, without losing an ounce of his
dazzle. Adler’s performance was sensational.
Jon Simon
Sunday, January 11, 1998
Jazz pianist Jon Simon was featured
in a cabaret-style performance. One
of America’s foremost contemporary
Jewish musical artists, Simon has
released several recordings that have
captivated audiences worldwide.
His masterful technique and
incredible improvisational skills are
truly extraordinary. Simon’s concert
was filled with heartfelt and humorous interpretations of songs we all
know and love, coupled with stories, anecdotes and humor.
Neshama Carlebach
Sunday, February 15,1998
Songstress Neshama Carlebach
follows in the tradition of her late
father, Rabbi Shlomo Carlebach.
Neshama’s talent and charisma
have endeared her to audiences
throughout the world. While
following in her father’s footsteps,
she has the unmistakable Carlebach
depth of soul and the ability to captivate, yet she has her own unique
artistic style. With a powerful voice that easily spans several octaves,
she is a natural-bom performer. She was electrifying.
