Solomon Schechter Day School
We, of the Solomon Schechter Day School of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center, welcome a new year with the
largest enrollment in our thirty-seven year history. The school
now has three Kindergarten Classes and double classes through
the fourth grade. In addition, we have expanded our Middle
School Program to include the seventh grade.
The school is utilizing every square foot of educational
space in the building available to it. Our classrooms are
dynamic places where careful attention is given to individual
learning styles and group sizes and teaching approaches are
varied so each child will be reached. A dedicated faculty leads
students through an enriched curriculum in which texts are only one source of information.
The richness of Jewish tradition permeates the school day. Through observance of daily prayer
and kashrut, in holiday observances, and through teaching of Mitzvot, Judaica becomes an integral
part of everyday life. Hebrew language study goes beyond just being a language program with the
goal of fluency.
Now we turn our vision to the future. We look forward with much
anticipation to the continued process of creating, together with our parents,
students and members of the congregation’s leadership, a school which
is truly educationally significant.
The Solomon Schechter Day School looks back with gratitude to
all those who sustained it during the early years, are maintaining its
identity today, and looks forward with confidence and enthusiasm to the
years ahead.
Evelyn Borstein
