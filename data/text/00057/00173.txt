If gratitude is the essence of
my reflections I need, especially, to
express hakarat ha-tov ...my
gratefulness to lay leaders whose
devotion and dedication to this
synagogue is so extraordinary. We
can be proud of the fact that we
are one of the finest congregations
in the country. I find it more than
fortuitous that I should be writing this
message as a new
administration, led by our
President Mark Shorstein
takes office. I can recall, as
if it were yesterday, the
warmth and cordiality with
which an invitation to serve
this congregation as its rabbi
was extended by Jack
Shorstein and his beloved
Sylvia, may her name be for
a blessing. Jack introduced
me to a group of leaders who
were conscientious and
devoted to the well-being of
our synagogue and to Jewish
life. It is extraordinary that
we have come full circle -that a
generation has passed and Jack and
Sylvia’s son Mark serves now as
president. That he is joined by his
beloved Beth who is president of our
always dynamic Center Sisterhood,
is especially meaningful to me.
Over these twenty-five years
I have had the pleasure of serving
with thirteen different congregational
presidents -Jack Shorstein, Charles
Mizrahi, Sheldon Gendzier,
Lawrence DuBow, Nate Krestul, Sol
Proctor, Dr. Joseph Honigman, Gene
Kornblum, Marcia Pollock, Jeff
Morris, Stuart Hecht, Evan Yegelwel
and now Mark Shorstein. These
devoted individuals represented a
large group of Center officers,
supporters and volunteers whose
hearts were moved in their
understanding of the needs of our
congregation.
Whatever my achievements,
nothing would have happened
without their help. Together we
were able to move this congregation
from its facility on 3rd and Silver in
the Springfield area to the beautiful
campus we have at our present
location. Together, with their
devotion and the financial support of
people of vision, we have built an
outstanding and beautiful synagogue
to serve our needs. Together we
expanded on the programs of our
Solomon Schechter Day School, our
afternoon Hebrew school and our
youth programs. Over the years
every initiative that I was prompted
to consider in my concern for the
needs of our membership was
supported and encouraged by our
lay leaders. We are a congregation
devoted to its religious traditions.
We have minyan services every
morning and evening every day of
the year. The changes we have
made with regard to the participation
of all of our members at services
were made with a view towards
change with discretion. We
continue to maintain a fundamental
focus of the importance of education
and our youth. We have also
expanded our education programs to
serve adults. We have enjoyed
years of Adult B’nai Mitzvah
classes, Sisterhood Seminars, Torah
Study classes, and Talmud classes.
From the pulpit and from the podium,
I was encouraged to teach. We
have personalized our large and
active synagogue by urging
membership in havurot, by creating
programs like the minyannaires, and
by encouraging the formation of the
Center’s Men’s Club to complement
a most active and dynamic Center
Sisterhood. We have opened our
congregation and community to the
world of Jewish culture with our
Performing Artists Series.
We have been challenged,
both intellectually and
emotionally, with many
scholars who shared our
Shabbaton and we have been
informed about the
fundamental issues that
effect the Jewish community
through our Setzer-Levit
programs. Our concerns are
all encompassing ...from the
youngest child to the mature
members of our
congregation who enjoy our
55+ programs and the many
other activities that we offer.
I could not have effected any
of the above were it not for the
cooperation, support and the
sensitivity on the part of our lay
leadership who trusted my instinct
as to what our congregational needs
were.
My personal thank you to those
who made possible, in this edition of
the yearbook, this most loving
expression of my twenty-five years
as your rabbi — Peggy Organes,
Raymond Cohen, Etta Drashin,
Renee Gordon and her committee,
Susan Letherman and Shirley
Bielski.
If I reflect on my blessings I
need to add my gratitude to a loving
wife who was always patient,
understanding and sensitive to the
demanding schedule of a rabbi who
serves a large congregation. I always
enjoyed Inge’s counsel and support;
