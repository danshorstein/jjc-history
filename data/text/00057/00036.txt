Scouting
Boy Scout Troop 14
Troop 14 carried on the scouting tradition at the Jacksonville Jewish Center with another year of outdoor
fun, adventure and activities in 1997-98. Bob Fleet served as Scoutmaster, and was assisted by Assistant
Scoutmasters Louis Schwartz and Billy Wilensky. Youth leadership was provided by Eli Fleet as Junior
Assistant Scoutmaster, Justin Jackrel as Senior Patrol Leader, and Jared Fleet
as Assistant Senior Patrol Leader.
The troop camped out this year at Stephen Foster State Park in the
Okeefenokee Swamp, aboard the USS Yorktown in South Carolina, at
Marineland, and at Fort Wilderness in Walt Disney World. On all campouts
kashrut and Shabbat are observed.
This year’s programs included visiting the new City Hall and attending a
City Council Meeting as part of the requirements for the Citizenship in the
Community Merit Badge, earning the Climbing Merit Badge at Peak Performers
Climbing Gym and working on the Safety Merit Badge with a member of the Northeast Florida Safety Council.
This summer, the scouts attended camp at Camp Powhatan in the Blue Ridge Mountains of Virginia.
They went rafting on the New River in West Virginia and earned many merit badges.
Troop 14 is open to any boy age 11 to 18 who enjoys camping, sports, and other outdoor activities. We
welcome you to come to our meetings at the Center on Thursday nights. The troop also welcomes participation
from adults interested in providing volunteer assistance. For more information call Bob Fleet at 268-2746
(H) or 730-8103 (W).
1998 - 1999 will the 80th year that Troop 14 has provided Jewish youth a Boy Scouting experience. We
look forward to a great 80th year!
