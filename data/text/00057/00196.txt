Inge and I had the privilege of
being bom into families that provided
us with life’s most precious and
sacred experiences. Our families
lived and breathed Jewish life. Being
a rabbi was really an extension of
my upbringing and my personality
and an expression of my love affair
with things Jewish. Our way in the
world has been gladdened by the
companionship of family and friends.
We are grateful for the joy and
understanding, the naches and
pleasure that our children Asher &
Sabrina, Shalva & David and our
grandchildren Jordana, Yoav Ezra &
Shira have brought us.
The Jewish community lives on
the frontier of a constantly changing
world, facing problems and
challenges that seem unprecedented.
We are concerned about a
weakening of those Jewish ideals
and standards to which former
generations were loyal. We have,
seemingly, paid a high price for our
integration in a free society. The
transmission of tradition from one
generation to the next, is no longer a
given. Some of our religious
traditions are regarded with the
skepticism resulting in a consequent
lessening of the values we have held
most dear. But, as we face the
future, we are confident that this
congregation will be equal to the task
that lies ahead; that our synagogue
will continue to provide a sense of
kedusha — a sacredness which
affirms the enduring meaning of
human existence and Jewish life.
A Jew was defined by Yehuda
Ha-Levi, a medieval and distinguished
poet and philosopher as a “prisoner
of hope”. Because we are prisoners
of hope we believe that the legacy
bequeathed to us over these last
some 100 years of our congregation
will be renewed for the generations
of the future. The American
synagogue has conveyed the
meaning of Jewish existence to the
largest Jewish community that has
ever lived in a free society. We
believe that the Jacksonville Jewish
Center can expand on the best of
our past as it inspires its sons and
daughters to Jewish idealism and
faithfulness in the future. We are
“prisoners of hope”. In the midst of
freedom and security we can
reaffirm our witness to God’s
presence and to our faith in Torah.
Let the record of the Jacksonville
Jewish Center fortify us with the
strength and determination to fulfill
this task for the decades ahead
AMEN.
— Rabbi David Gaffney
