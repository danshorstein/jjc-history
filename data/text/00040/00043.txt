Jacksonville welcomes return of Leb’s
Jacksonville’s old-timers remember
when the name Leb’s Restaurant was
synonymous with good eating, and it was
a disappointment to many when it closed
in the late 1960s.
Now those who enjoyed its fine cuisine
can take heart. The restaurant has been
reborn on the Southside as LiP Leb’s, on
Baymeadows Road just off San Jose
Boulevard.
Ira Sachs, the owner of LiP Leb’s,
knows what kind of act he has to follow.
“I was affiliated with them when I was
just getting started in the restaurant
business,” he says. “I was their steward
when they opened in the old Roosevelt
Hotel in 1946.
“I was with them until 1948, when I left
and moved to Atlanta. There, I was in
charge of food at the Progressive Club.”
Sachs remembers that Leb’s was
considered one of the best restaurants in
Jacksonville. “In my own opinion, it was
the finest in town—no question about it,”
he said. “It was just a fantastic place.”
LiP Leb’s, which opened in mid
December, is quite smaller than its
predecessor and its menu is more
limited.
“They served many more meals than
we do,” says Sachs. “We mainly serve
sandwiches, soups, delicatessen items,
and we have a bakery.”
There is a wide assortment of
sandwiches and other items to choose
from. The menu lists, among others:
imported ham; corned beef; pastrami,
chopped liver; roast beef; turkey; tuna
salad, and chicken salad. A combo, with
your choice of three fillings, is also
offered.
Diners can help themselves to the free
kosher-style salad bar, offering fresh-
barrel sauerkraut, fresh-barrel half-sour
pickles, plum tomatoes and pickled
beets.
All sandwiches are served on the
patron s choice from among six breads,
all baked on the premises: regular rye;
onion rye; eggroll; onion roll; kaiser roll;
and pumpernickel.
Sachs is not the only alumnus of Leb’s
working at the new place.
The head chef at Leb's, Russel
Reinhart, met his wife, Iris, while she was
a waitress there. He’s my head chef here,
now, and Iris is head waitress,” Sachs
said. “And Joann Starr, another waitress
at Leb’s, is working for me now.”
The Reinharts also worked with Sachs
in Atlanta after he moved there.
Sachs returned to Florida in 1950 to
work in Miami Beach, first at
Pumpernick’s and then the Virginian.
Reinhart also returned to Florida. “He
opened every Wolfie’s,” Sachs said.
“That’s a restaurant franchise with
outlets through the state, mostly in
South Florida.”
Sachs said he decided to open LiP
Leb’s “for my two sons, Mike and Jeff.”
Michael Sachs is manager of the
restaurant, while Jeff is working as the
sandwich and salads cook.
