Solomon Schechter Day School
An increasing number of students in our community are now experiencing
two quality educations: a dynamic General Studies program which far
exceeds the public school offerings integrated with an intensive and multi¬
dimensional Judaic Studies program. The effect on the children is readily
apparent. Our children are proud to be Jews and have their Jewish self¬
esteem supported by the backbone of religious knowledge and practice.
They identify with and support Israel and their mastery of the Hebrew
language continues to astound us.
The children have developed a love of learning that transcends any one
subject area. Most importantly, they are learning values that are incumbent
on us as parents, educators, and members of the community to pass on to
future generations: self-respect, respect for one another, respect for those
who are different, the importance of community. We feel that the Solomon
Schechter Day School is an enormous asset to this community.
It is our belief that the greatest impact of the Solomon Schechter Day School
lies in the future. Jewish leaders, professional, religious, and lay, assert that
day schools hold one of the very best answers to furthering Jewish
continuity.
We are very proud to be able to say that our school has a student body
ranging from the Jumping Jacks (our 2 year old program), Pre-School (3 and
4 year olds), Kindergarten, (5 year olds), to our Day School, first through the
seventh year grades. We look forward to each year with enthusiasm and
excitement as our numbers grow and add to our accomplishments.
