The State of Israel presents
The Medilerraiieaii - Dead Sea Canal Project
Israel announces its most ambitious economic project; the building of a Canal from the
Mediterranean to the Dead Sea for production of Hydro-Electric power.
The Canal will ultimately produce more than 20% of Israel’s total Energy needs and alleviate
to a larger extent the import of oil which in 1980 cost 25 billion dollars.
As water cascades from the High ground on the Mediterranean to the Dead Sea the Canal will
provide Hydro-Electric power which by the year 2000 should produce 3,000 megawatts of
electricity out of a national total of 10,000 megawatts.
The Canal will provide solar ponds to soak up heat from the sun, make water available for
cooling future power stations.
The Canal will also create lakes for tourist attractions, agricultural projects and fisheries and
establish industrial and building enterprises.
The Canal project lifts the importance of Israel Bonds to a higher than ever level. While more
than 5 billion dollars in Israel Bonds has been sold world Jewry will quickly produce another
billion dollars to provide the funds for this historic project.
