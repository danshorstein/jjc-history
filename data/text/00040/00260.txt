■ JWV is more than a veterans organiza¬
tion. More than a Jewish organization. It is
an amalgamation of Americans of Jewish
faith who share the common experience of
having served their country in time of war.
■ JWV was founded in 1896 to counteract
slurs against Jewish patriotism. Since then,
it has been in the forefront of the battle
against anti-Semitism, bigotry and preju¬
dice. In upholding American democratic
traditions, JWV fights injustice and discrim¬
ination of all kinds.
■ JWV contributes greatly to the growth of
a sympathetic understanding of Israel and
its problems among all Americans. It is out¬
spoken on issues that are affecting per¬
manent peace in the Middle East. Likewise,
its leadership role continues to help arouse
American public opinion against Soviet
Union’s anti-Jewish policies.
■ JlW’s views are well known to Congress
on such diverse subjects as foreign policy,
civil rights, defense spending, veterans
benefits, housing and education.
■ JWV, through its hospital, rehabilitation
and veterans service programs, assists the
veteran and his dependents in many ways.
It maintains veteran service offices, manned
by professional experts, in major cities
throughout the country.
■ JWV, through its child welfare program,
supports the Boy Scout organization, pro¬
vides summer camp scholarships for under¬
privileged children, sponsors athletic league
tournaments. Its program includes sponsor¬
ship of brotherhood rallies and the under¬
taking of civic betterment projects in local
communities.
