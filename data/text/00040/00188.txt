Objectives
1. To recognize the importance of Shabbat and holiday
observance.
2. To recognize the need for regular devotion both individually
and in groups.
3. To develop an appreciation and willingness to work for the
synagogue.
4. To increase the use of Hebrew in informal settings.
5. To establish a communal sense by supporting community
and civic activities.
6. To recognize the importance of Israel as a modern center for
world Jewry.
7. To develop a Jewish identity through music and dance.
8. To develop an appreciation of Jewish life.
KADIMA
General Aim: Jewish families today look to the synagogue
as the center for Jewish activity: religious, educational,
social and recreational. A major goal of the synagogue and
its school is the education of youth with a firm base of
knowledge; Jewish identification of youth, a sense of pride
in their heritage, and a committment to Judaism. The goal
of Kadima is to bring about the internalization of Jewish
values, attitudes and behavior patterns by supplementing
the child’s formal religious education with informal Jewish
learning experience.
