Founded in 1917, as the United Jewish Charities, J.F.C.S. is the oldest of the three social service agencies serving the Jewish
community. Its goals, as described in its by-laws, are to strengthen and/or restore family functioning and stability, to enable
individuals to approach their maximum potential and to help individuals and families lead happier and healthier lives.
In 1980, 880 individuals were served in one or more of its four programs, an increase of 355 persons over 1979. With the
establishment of the Kosher Nutrition Program the senior citizen programs increased by 284 to 490 individuals served.
Counselling and the New American programs increased by 47 to 254. With the further anticipated cut-backs in federal aid
programs, the demands on the agency in the coming year are expected to continue increasing. At this point we have no firm
knowledge of how many new New Americans can be expected.
Nathaniel Greenwood, a well trained, experienced, certified social worker joined the staff in June. His work is focussing on family
and individual counselling and Family Life Education. The priorities for the latter, recommended by the Planning Committee, are
single parent families, teenagers, and senior citizens. This committee further recommended that a formal job file be established
to aid our clients to locate work as quickly as possible and to aid employers in finding well qualified help. Your cooperation in this
worthwhile project is earnestly solicited.
