Our Cantor Emeritus, Abraham Marton, held his Choral Society Concert, and delighted his entire audience. The selection
of music, the professional manner in which it was presented, and the entire program was a delight for all who were there. We
were enthralled with the wide range of voices and everyone rose and ebbed with each strain. It is indeed our good fortune to have
Cantor Marton, who is still our leading force in Jewish music at the Jacksonville Jewish Center, and we wish him many more
years and many more concerts. Outstanding Center talents, Raymond Cohen, tenor and soprano Rosalind Klein were also
íeatured at the concert.

Minyanaires are men and women of all ages and life styles who attend one, some or every
Minyanaire Program.
The Minyanaire Program is held most Sundays at the Jacksonville Jewish Center. It begins
at 9 a.m. with a service held in the intimate atmosphere of the Witten Chapel. This is followed
by breakfast and the speaker of the day. We feature speakers on a variety of subjects that will
inform, excite, or entertain you. All Minyanaire Programs are free of chatge.
Minyanaires attend for various reasons. The brief service is inspirational. The friendship and
comraderie felt at breakfast is a pleasant change of pace from our usual morning routine. It’s
an opportunity to see old friends and meet new ones. There is no membership or on-going
obligation. Some may choose only a few programs to attend—some are there every week.
