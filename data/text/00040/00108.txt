Torah Readers
Not all progress is achieved by sudden leaps and bounds. Steady growth has many
unseen advantages. Regular worshipppers at Shabbat and Holiday services can observe
subtle advancements in quality of Torah Reading during the past year. This is a sign of
incalculable value for the beautification of the Mitzvah of public worship.
Bar Mitzvah was an incentive for individualized study has long been recognized, but is
quickly dissipated. The educational challenge lies, therefore, in arranging a pattern for
extended development. Of all synagogue skills only Torah reading allows for non-
repetitive, exacting preparation for the close to 500 different portions read through the year. The Torah schroll contains
no punctuation marks, vowels, accents, or portion endings. These must be laboriously memorized. A large percentage
of our Bar Mitzvah candidates are willing to meet this challenge and to continue to participate in this specialty.
The total list of readers, young and old, who recognize that reading the Torah is a self rewarding Mitzvah of high value,
has grown this year by the addition of the following Bar Mitzvahs: Haim Becker, Bart Chepenik, Bruce Gelman,
Jonathan Gray, Howard Markowitz, Michael Rosenblatt and Bradley Rosenthal.
We are thankful to all the readers who have continued to devote much of their time to this activity. They have made
attendance at services more meaningful.
