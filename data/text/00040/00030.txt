Sybil Ansbacher - Woman of Valor
The “Woman of Valor” honor
will be bestowed on Sybil Ansbach-
er during Simhat Torah Services.
Twenty-two years ago, Sybil came
to( Jacksonville as the new bride of
Lukie Ansbacher. Raised as a Re¬
formed Jew in Miami, Sybil was
anxious to learn more about Con¬
servative Judaism.
The first organization she joined
was the Center Sisterhood. After
serving on various committees she
became Cultural Vice-President and
then Sisterhood President. The So¬
cial Action committee was one of
the innovations bom of Sybil’s
presidency. Preceding Sisterhood
president, Blanche Slott and Sybil’s
Fund Raising Vice President and
close friend, Sylvia Shorstein, both
describe her as a “joy to work with.”
When her sons, Richard, Law¬
rence and Barry, were in school,
Sybil volunteered as a teacher’s
aide in the Day School and was
involved in many school activities.
She was an inspiring Kadima ad¬
visor for two years and a Den
Mother for Cub Scouts.
Involvement in the Center is a
family affair. Her husband, Lukie,
has been on the Executive Commit¬
tee for many years. Her interest
in youth activities inspired her
boys to become active in the Center
Youth programs where they have
held positions of leadership.
She is a past President of N.C.-
J.W., and now serves as Area 17
Chairwoman, leadership trainer and
trouble shooter. Civic-minded and
concerned for people, she has been
motivated to become active in many
civic organizations such as United
Way, Mental Health Association,
City HUD, and NCCJ, and more.
Sybil cares for her people, her
family and the world around her
and she strives to make it a better
place for alL

The Jacksonville Jewish Center take pride in its Daily Minyan.
Through the tireless efforts of the captains and co-captains, we
have minyans every morning and evening.
The anniversary date of the passing of a loved one presents an
opportunity to observe a Mitzvah, if not a double Mitzvah.
The Mitzvah of observing the Yahrzeit is obvious - the second
Mitzvah is the opportunity to insure a minyan so that others,
besides yourself, can also observe or attend a service for their own
reasons.
The Daily Minyan needs you. For a satisfying and rewarding
experience, attend once or twice a month on a regular basis.
If you plan to attend, call the captain of the day and time.
