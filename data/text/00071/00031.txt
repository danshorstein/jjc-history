SHABBAT AT SEA
For the weekend of January 19-22,2012, sixty members of our community took a unique voyage aboard the
Carnival Sensation for our first ever Shabbat at Sea. The ship stopped in Nassau, Bahamas, where many of our
passengers enjoyed visiting the sites of another country for the first time! Shabbat services were unforgettable.
We transformed the ship’s Michelangelo’s Lounge into a Makom Kadosh, a holy prayer space. After a lively
Kabbalat Shabbat service featuring Koltrain Light, we enjoyed Shabbat dinner together in the dining room.
Shabbat morning services were even more breathtaking, as our prayer space had an unbelievable view of the
Atlantic Ocean. Ilu Finu Malei Shira Kayam- “Could song fill our mouth as water fills the sea.” We recited
these words as we delved into a traditional Shabbat morning service. Havdalah was equally as powerful, as we
gathered to watch the sunset far off in the distance. Shabbat at Sea enabled all of us on board to get to know
each other better, have some fun, and experience moving prayer in the most unordinary of places.
