Dick Browdy recalls his first minyan in 1956
when he was recruited out of Hebrew School to go
to the chapel and help make a minyan. Behind the
chapel there was a coat room. But this was not to
hang your coat up when it was cold outside; this was
a room with extra suit coats that were given to men
so they could meet the minyan clress code.
If you were at services Saturday morning
and you felt a tap on your shoulder, you knew it
was Phillip Selber ensuring that you would be in
attendance for minyan Saturday night or Sunday
morning. For countless years he made sure there was
a minyan on the weekends.
Jack Silverman was captain for Sunday morning and
even when he was not able to attend he still made
calls each week for years to make sure the Sunday
morning minyan count was met.
Abe Fages was a speed reader when it came to
davening. People would say no one can read faster
than Abe Fages, even Abe Fages! Once he was late
for minyan which was very unusual, and when he
arrived he had gotten a speeding ticket because he
was afraid he was going to be late for minyan. That
was dedication.
Another major piece of our congregation’s
minyan history is the Minyan Club. Started by
Cantor Abraham Marton in the 1950s, the club was
created for boys transitioning into Bar Mitzvah age
and functioned as a learner’s service that was held on
Sunday morning at 9:00 am before Sunday school.
