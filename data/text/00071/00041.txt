IT WAS A GREAT YEAR TO BE IN SISTERHOOD!
Kim Glasgal
Sisterhood President
The new Sisterhood
board and members
would like to thank
and congratulate the
previous board for
its inspiring work the
last two years. We
accomplished goals
we set out early in our
terms and are happy
to report that last year
was an extremely
active and productive
year.
The mission of
Sisterhood is to support
Conservative Judaism, support Conservative Jews
and perform mitzvot in our community and around
the world. How did we do that this past year?
• Our dues and fundraisers provided support for
youth camps, USY on wheels, Youth group
programs, our Center schools and the
Sisterhood Kitchen;
• We had engaging educational Hot Topic
discussions about subjects ranging from
Interfaith Marriages to Who is a jew?;
• We were able to purchase a new walk-in freezer
for the Sisterhood kitchen

• We made scarves for needy adults and children;
• We collected for local agencies - food, games,
books, toiletries, even nail polish;
• We raised $18,000 for JTS through our Torah
Fund Campaign;
• We brought in fabulous speakers, including the
Women’s League Educator, Lisa Cogen, and
our own dynamo, Donna Orender;
• We baked hamantaschen for the schools and
provided volunteers at the Purim Carnival;
• We made leagrams and delivered flowers to
make joyous occasions a little more special;
• We even learned how to belly dance!
(okay this last one may not seem to support the
mission above, but it was in keeping with the
spirit of Purim!)
On top of all that, we had social events, such as
the Not-so-Newlywed game and game parties. These
social events serve to make us feel that much more at
home in our community.
Thank you Lara Werwa and the 2011-2012 Board
for making it all happen!
This year we will continue to work (and play)
to support our community - be a part of it! Please
join us at any of the events we have planned for this
year. Check out jjcsisterhood.com - we have many
changes coming!!
