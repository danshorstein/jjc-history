PURIM SHPIEL
On Erev Purim, the Jacksonville Jewish Center presented its first ever Purim Shpiel. A
purim shpiel is a comic dramatization that incorporates the story of the Book of Esther. Purim
Shpielers from our own community co-authored the script that parodied some musical classics.
Audience members didn’t know what to expect when they saw the advertisement for “Avenue
Shu.” Based upon the muppet-centric Broadway Musical Avenue Q, our performance brought
down the house filled with well over 400 people. Starring Count von Count as Haman and
Kermit the Frog as Mordechai, people of all ages raved about the creativity of the lyrics,
the innovative puppeteering, and the musical talents of our congregation. Kudos go to all of our singers,
puppeteers, band members, and set designers: Leslie Blumenfeld, Beth Fleet, Brad Segal, Beth Shorstein,
Cindy Wohl, Dan Wohl, Gayle Bailys, Shirley Bielski, Marilyn Garber, Helen Hill, Leonardo Maiman, Robin
Morris, Lois Tompkins, Dennis Mizrahi, Todd Morse, Aron Zimmerman, and Scott Zimmerman.
