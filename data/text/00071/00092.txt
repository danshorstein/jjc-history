The Jacksonville Jewish Center hosted a weekend
full of four events celebrating 50 years of education
and achievements at the Martin J. Gottlieb Day School
(formerly known as the Foundation School and the
Solomon Schechter Day School). Chairs, Bruce Horovitz
and Andrea Mail, and honorary chairs, Linda and Laurie
DuBow & Debbie and Mel Gottlieb, along with many
volunteers and Center staff, worked together for a year
to plan an inspirational 50th Anniversary Celebration.
The weekend began with Shabbat morning services,
co-chaired by Arlene Yegelwel and Marsha Pollock,
honoring the past and present teachers and faculty of
the Day School. In addition, Edith Horovitz received
the 2012 David Gaffney Leadership in Education Award
presented by visiting Rabbi Emeritus, David Gaffney.
The Student Reunion, “Chamesh de Mayo,” took
place Saturday evening with over 175 guests. Co¬
chairs Sheri Trager Weiss and Brent Trager along with
the committee, planned the evening that began with a
beautiful Havdalah service. Guests proceeded into the
social hall after receiving name tags displaying the
class that they were in when attending the Day School,
liana Manassee led Israeli dancing, something everyone
remembered doing while attending the Day School.
Sunday morning the PTA sponsored a Family Luau,
chaired by Rebecca Fixel. Guests swam in the Yegewel
Pool and were entertained by Polynesian dancers and the
MJGDS Dance Team, while eating a luau-themed lunch
under a giant tent.
The weekend culminated with the 50th Anniversary
Extravaganza. The Sunday evening event was co-chaired
by Andrea Mail and Jeanine Rogozinski. The evening
included a cocktail hour, a sit down dinner, a photo
gallery, and a slide show that brought smiles and tears to
the 375 guests.
