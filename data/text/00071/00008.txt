p—Celebrating Over a Century of Minyan^
It was an opportunity for the older boys to teach the
younger Bar Mitzvah candidates about putting on
tallit and tefillin as well as helping in training them
to lead the daily service. There would be breakfast
after services which was sponsored by the Men’s
Club - organized by Dave Safer and served by Annie
May. The Minyan Club had elected officers who ran
the services and recruited new members. In later
years the Minyan Club was directed by Mr. Leon
Zucker who kept the tradition of teaching the next
generation so they would know how to lead services
and attend minyan. Many of our service leaders
today were originally trained by Cantor Marton and
Mr. Zucker.
The Minyan Club of yesteryear may no longer
exist but the need to educate and sensitize a new
generation about the importance and value of
attending minyan is no less important.

Our current Minyan Captains are listed on the
following page, but there are many more whose
dedication to our minyan has ensured that our
congregants would have the opportunity to pray
with a community of peers and be able to say
Kaddish when they needed to.
In 2006, the Jacksonville Jewish Center became
an egalitarian synagogue and enabled the inclusion
of women in this important tradition. While there
were already women who attended minyan, now
they were able to be counted as part of the ten. This
brought a whole new group of dedicated participants
to daily minyan.
There is a L’dor V’dor aspect to being a Minyan
Captain. Many of our current captains listed below
inherited their dedication to daily prayer from their
fathers and carry on the tradition today. We thank all
of our current Minyan Captains for their dedicated
efforts in keeping the tradition of daily prayer alive
at the Jacksonville Jewish Center.
