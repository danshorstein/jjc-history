MEMBERSHIP COMMITTEE
The Membership Committee at the
Center wishes all of our Center family,
“L’Shana Tovah!” We on the committee
serve as the hosts and hostesses of the
synagogue. Our goal is to try to make
our entire membership feel welcome,
reaching out to unfamiliar faces when
in the building to connect.
This past year, it was a pleasure
to integrate our new friends from
Beth Shalom. We have celebrated
Shabbat together, we have explored the
insights and profound wisdom found
in our Torah text, we have gathered
together as a congregation to make a
difference in our community. We are
a congregational family dedicated to
deepening and enriching our connection
to Judaism.
We came together as family to
celebrate Jewish Holidays, whether it be all ages dancing on Simchat Torah, the Thanksgiving Kesher Service,
our wonderful Purim Shpiel and special dinner, a Latke Palooza, or Shavuot when we welcomed those who
become a Jew by Choice, and so much more.
We came together as family for Social Justice and Environmental Purposes on Community Spruce Up
Day, when members came to “spruce up the JJC property,” or a cruise on the St. Johns River with the River
Keeper, to donate food and other items for those in need through our Donate the Weight Food Drive and
Operation Isaiah.
We came together as family to be entertained by Ramat Gan, an Israeli dance troupe, a Yiddish musical
comedy film Der Purimspiler (The Jester), the Second Annual Laugh Out Loud Shabbat, as well as by Kol
Sasson, an a capella group from the University of Maryland. Many enjoyed the first Shabbat at Sea... and
the list goes on!
We came together as
family to learn in many
classes such as The
Fundamentals of Judaism,
Talmud Study, Coffee and
Torah, Nosh and Drash, and
the always wonderful Safer
Shabbaton.
These were just a few
examples of how we came
together as family and there
will be many more activities
in which to participate in the
New Year. We hope to see
you at many of them!
