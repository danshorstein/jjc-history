Executive
Director's
Message
Just as a house is not a home without
a family, the Jacksonville Jewish Center
is just a building without the devotion
and support of members and their
families—people with whom we laugh,
cry, celebrate simchas and share sorrows
as the years pass. As I reflect on the
past year, I am filled with pride for
the Center’s many accomplishments.
During this year of dedication, it is my
goal that as we feel pride in our
achievements we must also remember
the goals that are to be met for the
coming year. Someone once said,
“Nothing wilts faster than laurels that
have been rested upon.” We must persist
in all of our efforts to ensure that 5756
is another successful year for the
Jacksonville Jewish Center.
This year as we dedicate the building,
we pay tribute to more than just the

Executive Director
structural aspects of the Center—the
walls, paint and lights. We are
celebrating the purpose, achievements
and the commitment of many families
who uphold the traditions of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center. A building
alone is not capable of greatness without
the involvement of members and the
vision of its leaders. Much like children,
youth programs, adult education, special
events and schools require guidance and
tireless attention. We are priviledged
to have an excellent professional staff
and administrative staff that provide
support and encouragement to our many
dedicated volunteers.
As I review the activities that will occur
this year, I realize how swiftly children
grow, become a bar or bat mitzvah,
get married and become a part of our
Jewish community. Many of the
children who attend our schools are
likely to become married here, take
adult education classes and ready their
own children for the Center preschool
someday. L’Dor V’Dor, “from
generation to generation” the Center
continues to be “the Center of our
members’ lives.”
It is with pride that I continue to serve
as Executive Director of the
Jacksonville Jewish Center. May the
High Holidays fill each of us with a
sense of hope and peace, as we pray
together for a sweet year.
L’Shana Tovah.
