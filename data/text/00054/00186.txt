“Honor thy father
and mother that thy
days may be long...”
This is the fifth commandment and the
reward. The theme of respect and re¬
sponsibility for our forebears is central
to the organization of Jewish life.
It was this commandment which in¬
spired the founding of River Garden
Hebrew Home in 1946. It was this com¬
mandment which nourished the love
and care that over the years has made
River Garden a nationally recognized
model of excellence in its field.
The success of River Garden is based
on the tradition of meeting 100 per¬
cent of local Jewish community needs
and of serving rich and poor alike with
excellence. To be served, all you need
do is call. The River Garden Founda¬
tion accepts donations to help River
Garden continue to meet its mission.
River Garden’s programs of short-term
rehabilitative/respite care, long-term
residential care, Alzheimers’ and Adult
Day Care, serve over 180 people on
any given day. These programs are
guided by a devoted, professional full

River Garden is located on a 33 acre
Mandarin campus. In December 1994,
The Coves, an elegant yet affordable
independent retirement community,
welcomed its first residents.
River Garden is a community where
people live, love and care; a commu¬
nity of many lifestyles and of rich Jewish
content, and a community which every
day strives to live up to the mandate
of the Fifth Commandment.
