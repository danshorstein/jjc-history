JJC Thanks Marilyn Sandler,
Preschool Director Retires
After 34 Years
Marilyn Sandler
X r i e n d ,
Advisor... Aunt
Marilyn. These are
the only words that
can describe the re¬
lationship between
Marilyn Sandler
and the families
and teachers of the JJC Preschool. Marilyn
evolved into much more than a seasoned
Preschool Director. Not only did she coor¬
dinate curriculum, supervise teachers and
register children, she represented what many
of us feel to be an invaluable source of
advice and comfort - a velvet-gloved disci¬
plinarian to our children... part of our
extended families.
Marilyn feels fortunate to have been given
the opportunity to shape the Center’s Pre¬
school and to be touched by so many lives
in return. Her greatest success is knowing
that the children can laugh, play and thrive
in a environment rich in love and encour¬
agement much like their homes. Under
Marilyn’s leadership the Preschool and the
Solomon Schecter Day School had gained
the reputation of having a strong develop¬
mental curriculum taught by caring,
experienced teachers, in a warm, Jewish en¬
vironment. Her advice to the new leadership
following in her footsteps is to “just love
the children, have plenty of patience and
remember that the parents are giving you
their most precious treasures to care for.”
Marilyn plans to travel, visit her own chil¬
dren and do volunteer work for the Center
during her retirement. Plans are underway
to give Marilyn a grand retirement party
on September 10 in appreciation of her 34
years of dedication, leadership and love she
has shown in all aspects of her Center life.
From all of the staff, parents and children
of the Jacksonville Jewish Center we wish
Marilyn Sandler much happiness, satisfac¬
tion and joy from her retirement. She will
be missed!
Marilyn
Sandler
Preschool Director
