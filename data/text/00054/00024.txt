Sisterhood is a learning experience. It
is a way of life. It is family. It is edu¬
cation. It is discussion. It is
understanding. It is ideals and purpose.
Our goal is to continue to build a strong,
knowledgeable, vital Jewish commu¬
nity, right here for ourselves, and for
our people everywhere. We do this by
supporting our own educational endeav¬
ors, the welfare of our congregation
and its religious schools, by giving aid
and support to religious programs, spon¬
soring educational and cultural
activities, fostering Jewish sentiments
and observances in the home, encour¬
aging youth with guidance and
scholarships, and building the Jewish
Theological Seminary of America.
Phyllis Goldberg,

Sisterhood is so very important to the
Center and to each of us. Sisterhood
needs your support. Attend meetings.
Make phone calls. Help at meetings.
Plan events. Enjoy book reviews. Wor¬
ship and participate during
Sisterhood-Library Shabbat and
Women’s Minyan. Join the Sisterhood
Bat Mitzvah Class. Participate in Sis¬
terhood Seminar. Learn new skills.
Create a Judaica handcraft. Get out
mailings. Teach. Entertain. Nurture our
youth. Design publicity. Bake
Hammentaschen. Do crafts at the Purim
carnival. Enjoy our social events. Sup¬
port our fundraisers. Help in the Judaica
shop. Make new friends. Meet and vol¬
unteer with your old friends. Grow
personally and Jewishly.
Come and share your efforts, ideas, ex¬
periences, ability, enthusiasm, energy,
time and heart. You will be rewarded
with pride, spirit, the mitzvah of un¬
selfish devotion and the opportunity to
develop your own personal Jewish
lifestyle. Our families benefit, Judaism
benefits and future generations will ben¬
efit.
Best wishes for a happy, healthy New
Year.
